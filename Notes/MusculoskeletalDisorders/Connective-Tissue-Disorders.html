Connective Tissue Disorders

NCLEX-RN High-Yield Notes

1. Overview

Connective tissue disorders (CTDs) affect tissues that support and bind organs — cartilage, bones, ligaments, skin, blood vessels. Most are autoimmune (the immune system attacks the body's own tissues). Many are chronic with flares and remissions.

2. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

Definition: Chronic autoimmune disease causing widespread inflammation in skin, joints, kidneys, brain, and other organs.

Key Signs & Symptoms

  • Butterfly (malar) rash — across both cheeks and bridge of nose (hallmark)
  • Joint pain (arthralgia), symmetric
  • Fatigue — profound and debilitating
  • Photosensitivity — skin flare with UV exposure
  • Oral ulcers (painless)
  • Lupus nephritis — kidney involvement (proteinuria, hematuria, hypertension)
  • Serositis (pleuritis, pericarditis)
  • Positive ANA (antinuclear antibody) test — most sensitive

NCLEX Tips

  • Monitor BUN/Creatinine — for renal function (lupus nephritis)
  • Avoid sunlight; use sunscreen (SPF ≥30); wear protective clothing
  • Medications: Corticosteroids, Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil), Methotrexate, Belimumab
  • Rest during flares; balance activity with rest in remission
  • Monitor BP — hypertension common with renal involvement
SLE patients on immunosuppressants: increased infection risk — monitor for fever, report promptly.

3. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) — CTD Perspective

Autoimmune attack on synovial joints → inflammation → joint destruction. (See Musculoskeletal Conditions note for full RA detail.)

  • Morning stiffness >1 hour
  • Symmetric small joint swelling (fingers, wrists)
  • Swan-neck and boutonnière deformities
  • Rheumatoid factor (RF) and anti-CCP antibodies positive
  • Extraarticular: nodules, pulmonary fibrosis, Sjögren's overlap

4. Scleroderma (Systemic Sclerosis)

Definition: Autoimmune disease causing progressive hardening and tightening of skin and connective tissues due to excess collagen deposition.

CREST Syndrome (Limited Scleroderma)

  • C — Calcinosis (calcium deposits under skin)
  • R — Raynaud's phenomenon (fingers turn white → blue → red with cold/stress)
  • E — Esophageal dysfunction (dysphagia, reflux)
  • S — Sclerodactyly (thick, tight skin on fingers)
  • T — Telangiectasia (small dilated blood vessels on skin)

NCLEX Tips

  • Raynaud's: keep extremities warm; avoid cold and stress; calcium channel blockers (nifedipine)
  • Avoid smoking — causes vasoconstriction, worsens Raynaud's
  • Renal crisis (sclerodermal renal crisis) — sudden ↑ BP + ↓ urine output = emergency; treat with ACE inhibitors
  • Mouth exercises for limited mouth opening; liquid/soft diet for dysphagia
Scleroderma renal crisis: sudden severe hypertension + oliguria = emergency → ACE inhibitors.

5. Polymyositis / Dermatomyositis

Definition: Inflammatory diseases causing muscle weakness. Dermatomyositis also involves skin rash.

Signs & Symptoms

  • Proximal muscle weakness — difficulty raising arms, climbing stairs, rising from chair
  • Dermatomyositis: Heliotrope rash (purple/violet discoloration around eyelids)
  • Dermatomyositis: Gottron's papules (red/purple scaly bumps over knuckles)
  • Elevated CPK (creatine phosphokinase) — muscle breakdown marker
  • Dysphagia if pharyngeal muscles involved

NCLEX Tips

  • Corticosteroids — first-line treatment
  • Physical therapy to maintain strength
  • Monitor for aspiration risk (dysphagia) — HOB up for meals, soft diet
  • Elevated CPK = active muscle inflammation

6. Sjögren's Syndrome

Definition: Autoimmune attack on moisture-producing glands (lacrimal and salivary glands).

Signs & Symptoms

  • Dry eyes (keratoconjunctivitis sicca) — gritty, burning sensation
  • Dry mouth (xerostomia) — difficulty chewing/swallowing
  • Vaginal dryness
  • Fatigue, joint pain

NCLEX Tips

  • Artificial tears for dry eyes; lubricating eye drops/gels
  • Artificial saliva sprays; sip water frequently; sugarless gum
  • Excellent oral hygiene — high cavity risk with dry mouth
  • Avoid antihistamines — worsen dryness significantly
  • Vaginal lubricants for comfort

7. Ankylosing Spondylitis

Definition: Chronic inflammatory disease primarily affecting the spine and sacroiliac joints → progressive fusion.

Signs & Symptoms

  • Low back pain and stiffness — worse in the morning, improves with activity (opposite of mechanical back pain)
  • Decreased spinal mobility (can't touch toes)
  • Progressive kyphosis ("bamboo spine" on X-ray)
  • HLA-B27 positive (genetic marker)
  • Uveitis (eye inflammation) — associated finding

NCLEX Tips

  • Encourage good posture and daily stretching exercises
  • NSAIDs — first-line for pain and stiffness
  • Swimming is highly recommended (non-weight bearing; spinal mobility preserved)
  • Respiratory exercises to maintain chest expansion (ribs/thorax may fuse)
  • Sleep on firm mattress; avoid pillow under head (promotes kyphosis)

9. NCLEX Must-Know

  • ✅ SLE = butterfly rash + photosensitivity + lupus nephritis; monitor BUN/Cr; avoid sun
  • ✅ Scleroderma CREST; Raynaud's = keep warm; renal crisis = ACE inhibitor emergency
  • ✅ Dermatomyositis = heliotrope rash + Gottron's papules + proximal muscle weakness
  • ✅ Sjögren's = dry eyes + dry mouth; NO antihistamines
  • ✅ Ankylosing spondylitis = back pain worse in AM, better with activity; swimming best exercise
  • ✅ Biologics (TNF inhibitors): screen for TB before starting; avoid live vaccines
  • ✅ Methotrexate: teratogenic — birth control required; no alcohol; monitor LFTs
All CTDs: monitor for infection (immunosuppressant therapy); teach signs of infection to report.
