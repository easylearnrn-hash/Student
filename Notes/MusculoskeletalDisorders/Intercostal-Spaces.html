Intercostal Spaces (ICS)

NCLEX-RN High-Yield Notes — Anatomy & Clinical Landmarks

1. Definition

Intercostal spaces (ICS) = anatomical gaps located between two adjacent ribs.

Each space contains:

  • Intercostal muscles — assist in breathing
  • Intercostal nerves — innervate skin and muscle of thoracic wall
  • Intercostal artery and vein — supply thoracic wall
Naming: Each ICS is named after the rib above it. The 2nd ICS lies between rib 2 and rib 3.

2. Heart Valve Auscultation — "APE TO MAN"

Memorize the auscultation sites for the 4 heart valves (NCLEX favorite):

ValveLocationLandmark
A Aortic 2nd ICS, right sternal border Right of sternum, 2nd rib space
P Pulmonic 2nd ICS, left sternal border Left of sternum, 2nd rib space
E Erb's Point 3rd ICS, left sternal border General heart sounds (especially aortic regurgitation)
T Tricuspid 4th ICS, left sternal border Lower left sternal border
M Mitral (Apical) 5th ICS, left midclavicular line (MCL) Below left nipple line — apical pulse site
Mnemonic: "APE TO MAN" — A=Aortic, P=Pulmonic, E=Erb's, T=Tricuspid, M=Mitral (Apical). Goes from top to bottom, right to left across the chest.

3. Apical Pulse Assessment

  • Location: 5th ICS, left midclavicular line
  • Auscultate for 1 full minute before administering cardiac medications (especially Digoxin)
  • Hold Digoxin if apical pulse <60 bpm
  • Use diaphragm of stethoscope for normal S1/S2; bell for low-pitched murmurs
  • Best position: supine or left lateral decubitus (brings heart closer to chest wall)
Digoxin rule: Check apical pulse 1 full minute → hold if <60 bpm → notify provider.

4. Key Clinical Procedures by ICS

ProcedureICS LevelKey Points
Chest Tube Insertion 4th or 5th ICS, midaxillary line (MAL) Insert just above the upper border of the rib below to avoid neurovascular bundle; air removal: anterior; fluid: lateral/posterior
Pericardiocentesis 5th or 6th ICS, left sternal border Aspirates fluid from pericardial sac; used for cardiac tamponade
Thoracentesis 8th–10th ICS, posterior chest wall Patient: sit upright, leaning forward over table; insert above rib; removes pleural fluid
CRITICAL: Always insert needles/tubes just above the rib — the neurovascular bundle (nerve, artery, vein) runs in the costal groove on the underside (inferior margin) of each rib.

5. Chest Tube Nursing Care

  • Keep drainage system below chest level always
  • Expected: tidaling (water level fluctuates with breathing) — normal
  • Expected: intermittent bubbling in water seal — normal initially
  • Continuous bubbling in water seal = air leak — notify provider
  • If tube disconnects: submerge open end in sterile water (not clamp)
  • If tube pulls out: cover site with occlusive dressing (taped on 3 sides — flutter valve) → call provider
  • Never clamp without specific order — can cause tension pneumothorax

6. Intercostal Muscles and Breathing

MuscleDirectionFunction
External intercostalsFibers run down and forwardAssist inspiration — elevate ribs, expand thorax
Internal intercostalsFibers run up and forwardAssist expiration — depress ribs, compress thorax
During quiet breathing, the diaphragm does most of the work. Intercostals are important in forced breathing and when diaphragm is compromised (e.g., spinal cord injury).

7. Thoracentesis — Nursing Care

  • Position: sit upright leaning forward over bedside table (opens posterior ICS wider)
  • Stay still during procedure — unexpected movement can cause lung puncture
  • After procedure: chest X-ray to rule out pneumothorax
  • Monitor: respiratory status, breath sounds, oxygen saturation, pain
  • Report: decreased breath sounds, SOB, chest pain, tracheal deviation
  • Maximum safe removal: ~1,000–1,500 mL at one time (re-expansion pulmonary edema risk)

8. NCLEX Must-Know

  • ✅ APE TO MAN — Aortic (2nd R), Pulmonic (2nd L), Erb's (3rd L), Tricuspid (4th L), Mitral (5th L MCL)
  • ✅ Apical pulse = 5th ICS MCL — auscultate 1 full minute for Digoxin
  • ✅ Chest tube = 4th/5th ICS MAL; always insert ABOVE the rib
  • ✅ Thoracentesis = sit upright leaning forward; post-procedure CXR
  • ✅ Neurovascular bundle = UNDER each rib; insert ABOVE to avoid
  • ✅ Continuous bubbling in chest tube water seal = air leak (problem)
  • ✅ Tube dislodged from chest = occlusive dressing 3-sided
Neurovascular bundle mnemonic: VAN = Vein, Artery, Nerve — all run under (inferior margin of) each rib → always insert ABOVE the rib.
