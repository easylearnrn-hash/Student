Musculoskeletal Disorders — NCLEX Rapid Review

Master Summary Sheet — All Key MSK Topics

1. Emergency Conditions (Act Immediately)

  • 🚨 Compartment Syndrome — pain out of proportion + paresthesia + tense swollen limb → remove cast/bivalve → fasciotomy
  • 🚨 Fat Embolism — SOB + petechiae (chest/axillae) + confusion after long-bone fracture → O₂ + STAT notify
  • 🚨 Dislocation + Absent Pulse — vascular emergency; immobilize and call provider immediately
  • 🚨 Septic Arthritis — red, hot, swollen, painful joint + fever → immediate IV antibiotics + joint drainage
  • 🚨 Scleroderma Renal Crisis — sudden ↑ BP + oliguria → emergency ACE inhibitor treatment
  • 🚨 Hip Arthroplasty Dislocation — sudden severe hip pain + leg shortening + rotation → DO NOT flex/rotate; call provider

3. The 5 (or 6) P's of Neurovascular Assessment

PAssessmentAbnormal Finding
PainPain level; response to analgesics/elevationUnrelieved = compartment syndrome
PulseDistal pulse quality; compare bilaterallyAbsent/diminished = arterial compromise
PallorSkin color and temperaturePale/cool/mottled = poor perfusion
ParesthesiaSensation — tingling, numbnessNerve compression or ischemia
ParalysisMovement — can patient move distal digits?Nerve or muscle damage
PoikilothermiaTemperature of limbCool limb = ↓ arterial flow
Assess the 5 P's after every cast application, traction setup, surgery, or splinting. Document and report abnormals immediately.

6. Assistive Devices — Quick Summary

DeviceKey Rule
Crutches2–3 fingers below axilla; weight on hands; "up with good, down with bad"
CaneStrong side; moves WITH weak leg; up with strong first
WalkerAdvance 6–8"; affected leg first; never pull yourself up with it
Gait pattern for crutches — 4-point (safest), 3-point (non-weight bearing), 2-point (normal gait), swing-through (paraplegia).

9. Final NCLEX Pearls

  • 🎯 Neurovascular checks (5–6 P's) = #1 priority after any ortho procedure
  • 🎯 Pain out of proportion = compartment syndrome until proven otherwise
  • 🎯 Fat embolism triad = SOB + petechiae + confusion after long-bone fracture
  • 🎯 Traction weights: NEVER remove; always hang freely
  • 🎯 Cane on STRONG side; "up with good, down with bad" for all devices on stairs
  • 🎯 Alendronate: upright 30 min; empty stomach; esophagitis
  • 🎯 Methotrexate: teratogenic + hepatotoxic; no alcohol; monitor labs
  • 🎯 Septic arthritis = emergency → IV antibiotics immediately
  • 🎯 Osteomyelitis = S. aureus; bone biopsy = gold standard; 4–6 wks IV ABx
  • 🎯 RICE for soft tissue injuries; ice 24–48 hrs then heat
