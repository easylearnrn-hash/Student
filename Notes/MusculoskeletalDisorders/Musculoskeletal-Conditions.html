Musculoskeletal Conditions

NCLEX-RN High-Yield Notes

1. Overview

The musculoskeletal system includes bones, muscles, joints, tendons, ligaments, and cartilage. Its functions: movement, support, and protection. NCLEX focuses on mobility, safety, fractures, inflammatory disorders, and surgical care.

2. Osteoporosis

Definition: Bone becomes porous, brittle, and prone to fractures due to decreased bone density.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Aging, menopause (estrogen loss)
  • Low calcium/vitamin D intake
  • Long-term corticosteroid use
  • Female, small frame, Caucasian/Asian, smoking, sedentary lifestyle
  • Family history

NCLEX Tips

  • Encourage weight-bearing exercise (walking, stair-climbing) — stimulates bone formation
  • Calcium-rich diet (dairy, leafy greens) + Vitamin D supplementation
  • Fall prevention is priority: remove rugs, add night lights, use assistive devices
  • DEXA scan — measures bone mineral density

Medications

DrugClassKey NCLEX Teaching
Alendronate (Fosamax) Bisphosphonate Take in AM on empty stomach with full glass of water; stay upright 30 min after; risk of esophagitis
Calcitonin (nasal spray) Hormone Reduces bone resorption; SE: nasal irritation, flushing; alternate nostrils daily
Raloxifene (Evista) SERM Estrogen receptor modulator; increases DVT risk; avoid in women with clotting disorders

3. Osteoarthritis (OA)

Definition: Degeneration of joint cartilage from wear and tear (non-inflammatory, non-autoimmune).

Signs & Symptoms

  • Joint pain worsens with activity, improves with rest
  • Stiffness after rest, improves with movement
  • Heberden's nodes — bony enlargements at DIP joints (distal fingers)
  • Bouchard's nodes — bony enlargements at PIP joints (proximal fingers)
  • Crepitus (joint grinding)

NCLEX Tips

  • Low-impact exercise — swimming, walking (protect joints)
  • Ice for inflammation/acute pain; heat for morning stiffness
  • NSAIDs for pain — watch GI and kidney effects
  • Weight management — reduces joint load (especially knees/hips)
  • Assistive devices — cane, walker
  • Joint replacement (arthroplasty) if conservative therapy fails
OA = "Old Arthritis" — wear and tear; OA affects joints unevenly; RA is symmetric.

4. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

Definition: Chronic autoimmune disease causing systemic inflammation of synovial joints → joint damage and deformity.

Signs & Symptoms

  • Symmetric joint pain and swelling (both hands, both wrists)
  • Morning stiffness >1 hour (hallmark)
  • Fatigue, low-grade fever, weight loss (systemic)
  • Affects small joints first (fingers, wrists, MCP joints)
  • Deformities: swan-neck, boutonnière, ulnar deviation
  • Rheumatoid nodules

NCLEX Tips

  • Rest during flare-ups; gentle ROM during remission
  • Warm showers/moist heat for morning stiffness; cold packs for acute swelling
  • Splints to support and rest affected joints

Medications

5. Septic Arthritis

Medical emergency! Infection inside a joint space → rapid joint destruction if untreated.

Signs & Symptoms

  • Sudden onset: red, swollen, hot, extremely painful joint
  • Fever, chills, malaise
  • Markedly decreased ROM
  • Usually monoarticular — often knee or hip

Treatment

  • Immediate IV antibiotics (broad-spectrum: vancomycin, ceftriaxone) — start before culture results
  • Joint aspiration or surgical drainage to remove infected synovial fluid
  • Monitor WBC, CRP/ESR, joint cultures
  • Immobilize during acute phase; gentle movement after infection controlled

7. NCLEX Must-Know

  • ✅ OA = wear/tear, asymmetric, worsens with activity; RA = autoimmune, symmetric, morning stiffness >1 hr
  • ✅ Bisphosphonates (Alendronate): upright 30 min after taking, empty stomach, full glass water
  • ✅ Methotrexate: no alcohol, birth control, monitor LFTs + CBC
  • ✅ Biologics (TNF inhibitors): screen for TB first; increased infection risk
  • ✅ Septic arthritis = emergency → immediate IV antibiotics + joint drainage
  • ✅ Weight-bearing exercise promotes bone formation in osteoporosis
  • ✅ Fall prevention = priority nursing intervention for osteoporosis
OA: heat for stiffness, ice for acute pain. RA: ice for swollen joints, heat for morning stiffness.
