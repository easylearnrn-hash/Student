Anatomy · Otitis Media · Otitis Externa · Tinnitus · Vertigo · Meniere's · Hearing Aids

Ear Anatomy — 3 Main Parts

PartStructuresFunction
Outer EarPinna, ear canalCollects sound and directs it to the eardrum
Middle EarTympanic membrane, ossicles (malleus, incus, stapes), Eustachian tubeTransmits sound vibrations; Eustachian tube equalizes pressure
Inner EarCochlea (hearing), semicircular canals (balance), auditory nerveConverts vibration to nerve signal; maintains balance

Otitis Media — Middle Ear Infection

  • Cause: fluid trapped behind eardrum, often following cold or allergy
  • Symptoms: ear pain (worse at night), fever, tugging at ear (children), red/bulging tympanic membrane, hearing loss

Treatment

  • Warm compress
  • Acetaminophen or ibuprofen for pain/fever
  • Antibiotics if bacterial (amoxicillin first choice)
  • Myringotomy (ear tubes) if chronic or recurrent
Pinna technique for ear drops — pull down and back for children <3 years; up and back for >3 years and adults. Warm drops in hand before instillation. Keep position 2–3 minutes.
Untreated otitis media in children = risk of speech and language delay. Always follow up.

Otitis Externa — Swimmer's Ear

  • Cause: water exposure or trauma (e.g., cotton swabs)
  • Symptoms: pain when pulling/touching outer ear, swelling, redness, drainage, itching
  • Treatment: keep ear dry; antibiotic + steroid ear drops; NO Q-tips
Key distinction: otitis media = pain at rest; otitis externa = pain when pulling the ear.

Tinnitus — Ringing in the Ears

  • Cause: damage to inner ear or auditory nerve; ototoxic medications
  • Ototoxic drugs: aspirin (salicylates), loop diuretics (furosemide), aminoglycosides (gentamicin, tobramycin)
  • Symptoms: ringing, buzzing, humming — constant or intermittent; disrupts sleep and focus
  • Nursing care: review medication history; minimize noise/stress; refer to audiology

Vertigo — Spinning Sensation

  • Cause: vestibular dysfunction (inner ear or balance nerve)
  • Symptoms: dizziness (room spinning), nausea/vomiting, nystagmus (rapid involuntary eye movements)
  • Treatment: meclizine (antivertigo), ondansetron (anti-nausea), fall precautions, avoid sudden position changes

Meniere's Disease

Classic NCLEX Triad:
  1. Vertigo (severe, episodic)
  2. Tinnitus (ringing in ear)
  3. Sensorineural hearing loss (progressive)
  • Also: ear fullness/pressure, nausea, loss of balance
  • Cause: unknown; affects endolymphatic fluid in inner ear

Treatment

  • Low-sodium diet (reduces fluid retention in inner ear)
  • Diuretics
  • Avoid caffeine, alcohol, nicotine
  • Meclizine (dizziness), anti-nausea meds
  • Move slowly; fall precautions; surgery if severe

Hearing Loss — Types

TypeCauseClues
ConductiveBlockage in outer/middle ear (wax, infection, fluid)Muffled sounds; improved when someone speaks loudly
SensorineuralDamage to inner ear or auditory nerveTrouble hearing high-pitched sounds; often with tinnitus; presbycusis in elderly
MixedBoth types combinedMixed symptoms
For patients with hearing loss: speak slowly and clearly, face the patient directly, do NOT shout — this distorts sound. Use visual cues and written communication if needed.

Hearing Aid Teaching

  • Start wearing for short periods and gradually increase time
  • Turn off when not in use to preserve battery
  • Keep dry and clean daily
  • Remove before bathing or sleeping
  • Whistling sound = poor fit or earwax blockage
  • Replace battery regularly

NCLEX Ear Recap — Must-Memorize

  • 🎯 Otitis media = middle ear; red tympanic membrane; kids tug ear; risk of speech delay
  • 🎯 Otitis externa = outer ear pain with pulling; caused by moisture; no Q-tips
  • 🎯 Tinnitus = ototoxic meds: aspirin, furosemide, gentamicin
  • 🎯 Vertigo = dizziness + fall risk; meclizine; avoid sudden movements
  • 🎯 Meniere's = triad: vertigo + tinnitus + hearing loss; low-sodium diet
  • 🎯 Ear drops: <3 yrs = pull pinna down & back; >3 yrs = up & back
  • 🎯 Hearing aids: keep dry, remove at night, whistling = poor fit or wax
