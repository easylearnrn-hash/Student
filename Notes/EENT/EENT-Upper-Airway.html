EENT & Upper Airway — Comprehensive Review

Eye · Ear · Nose · Throat · Upper Airway Conditions & Care

Eye — Key Conditions

ConditionDescriptionNCLEX Key Points
Conjunctivitis (pink eye)Inflammation/infection of conjunctivaHighly contagious; hand hygiene; separate washcloths
CataractsCloudy lens → blurry visionPainless, gradual; common in elderly; requires surgery
Glaucoma↑ IOP → optic nerve damagePeripheral vision loss first; treat with eye drops (timolol); NO atropine
Macular DegenerationDamage to central retina (macula)Loss of central vision; common cause of blindness in elderly
Retinal DetachmentRetina separates from back of eyeSudden floaters, flashes, "curtain" over vision — medical emergency
MyopiaNearsightednessBlurry far vision
HyperopiaFarsightednessBlurry near vision
PresbyopiaAge-related near vision lossNormal after age 40–50

Post-Eye Surgery Care (e.g., Cataract)

  • Avoid bending, lifting, straining (↑ IOP)
  • No rubbing eyes
  • Wear eye shield at night
  • Administer prescribed eye drops as directed
  • Report immediately: pain, flashes, floaters, bleeding

Ear — Key Conditions

ConditionDescriptionNCLEX Notes
Otitis MediaMiddle ear infection; common in childrenPull pinna down & back (<3 yrs); warm compress; antibiotics
Otitis Externa (Swimmer's Ear)Outer ear canal infectionPain when pulling ear; keep dry; no Q-tips
TinnitusRinging in earsOtotoxic meds: aspirin, furosemide, aminoglycosides
VertigoSpinning sensation from vestibular dysfunctionFall risk; meclizine; avoid sudden movements
Meniere's DiseaseInner ear disorder — classic triadVertigo + tinnitus + sensorineural hearing loss; low-sodium diet; diuretics
Ear drop technique: Pull pinna down and back if age <3; up and back if age >3. Warm drops in hand first. Keep position 2–3 minutes.

Nose — Key Conditions

ConditionDescriptionNCLEX Points
Epistaxis (Nosebleed)Bleeding from nasal mucosaSit upright, lean forward, pinch nose 5–10 min; never tilt head back
SinusitisInflammation of sinusesFacial pressure; fluids, steam, nasal spray; antibiotics only if >10 days
Allergic RhinitisAllergy-caused nasal inflammationAntihistamines; nasal steroids (fluticasone); avoid triggers
Deviated SeptumCrooked nasal septum — obstructs airflowSeptoplasty if severe; frequent nosebleeds, snoring
Nasal FractureTrauma to noseElevate head; ice; monitor for CSF leak (halo sign or glucose-positive fluid)
CSF leak after nasal trauma = medical emergency → risk of meningitis. Test drainage for glucose or halo sign on gauze.

Throat & Upper Airway — Key Conditions

ConditionKey SignsNursing Care
PharyngitisRed throat, pain with swallowing, feverWarm saline gargles; hydration; antibiotics if strep
TonsillitisSwollen/red tonsils, white patches, bad breathMonitor post-op: frequent swallowing = first sign of bleeding; cold liquids; no straws
LaryngitisHoarseness, dry throat, tickling coughVoice rest; humidifier; hydration; no smoking/whispering
Oral Thrush (Candidiasis)White patches in mouthNystatin swish & swallow; occurs with antibiotics or immunosuppression
Sleep ApneaSnoring, apneic episodes, daytime fatigueCPAP; weight loss; avoid alcohol/sedatives
🚨 Epiglottitis — EMERGENCY: Drooling + stridor + tripod position + muffled voice. DO NOT inspect the throat — can trigger airway closure. Prepare for intubation. IV antibiotics. Stay with patient.

Suctioning — Key NCLEX Tips

  • Suction only on withdrawal, not while inserting
  • Limit suction to 10–15 seconds
  • Pre-oxygenate before suctioning
  • Monitor for hypoxia and bradycardia during procedure
  • Yankauer = oral (not sterile); nasopharyngeal & endotracheal = sterile

NCLEX Quick Recap

  • 🎯 Glaucoma = peripheral vision loss first; NO atropine (↑ IOP)
  • 🎯 Retinal detachment = painless sudden vision loss + "curtain" = emergency
  • 🎯 Post-eye surgery = no bending, lifting, rubbing
  • 🎯 Ear drops: <3 yrs = down & back; >3 yrs = up & back
  • 🎯 Meniere's = vertigo + tinnitus + hearing loss; low-sodium diet
  • 🎯 Nosebleed = sit upright, lean forward, pinch 5–10 min; never tilt back
  • 🎯 Epiglottitis = DO NOT inspect throat; airway prep; tripod + drooling
  • 🎯 Tonsillectomy post-op = frequent swallowing = bleeding; no straws; cold liquids
  • 🎯 Strep throat untreated → rheumatic fever (heart damage)
  • 🎯 Suctioning = 10–15 sec, pre-oxygenate, suction on withdrawal only
