EENT & Upper Airway Medications

Eye · Ear · Nose · Throat — Drug Summary for NCLEX

Eye Medications

DrugUseNCLEX Alert
Timolol (Beta-blocker eye drop)Glaucoma — ↓ intraocular pressure (IOP)Can cause bradycardia and hypotension if systemically absorbed; press inner canthus after instillation
Latanoprost (Prostaglandin analog)Glaucoma — ↑ aqueous drainageCan darken iris color permanently; promotes eyelash growth
AtropinePupil dilation (mydriatic)CONTRAINDICATED in glaucoma — raises IOP and worsens condition
Never administer atropine or anticholinergics to a patient with glaucoma — it increases IOP and can cause acute angle-closure crisis.

Ear Medications

DrugUseNCLEX Alert
AmoxicillinOtitis media (middle ear infection) — first-line antibioticGI upset; check for penicillin allergy
Acetaminophen / IbuprofenPain & fever with ear infectionsMonitor dose carefully in children; ibuprofen → renal/GI caution
Antibiotic + Steroid ear dropsOtitis externa (swimmer's ear)Local irritation; keep ear dry; warm drops before instillation
Gentamicin / Furosemide (ototoxic)—Ototoxic: can cause tinnitus and hearing loss; monitor hearing especially with IV forms
MeclizineVertigo, Meniere's disease — antivertigoDrowsiness, dry mouth; fall precautions
OndansetronNausea due to vertigoConstipation, headache
Ototoxic drugs = aspirin (high doses), furosemide, aminoglycosides (gentamicin, tobramycin). Monitor for tinnitus and hearing changes — report immediately.

Nose Medications

DrugUseNCLEX Alert
Diphenhydramine (1st-gen antihistamine)Allergic rhinitisSedating; avoid in elderly (Beers criteria); anticholinergic effects
Loratadine / Cetirizine (2nd-gen antihistamine)Allergic rhinitisLess sedating; preferred over 1st-gen for most patients
Oxymetazoline (nasal spray)Decongestant — shrinks nasal vesselsLimit to 3 days max → rebound congestion (rhinitis medicamentosa) if overused
Pseudoephedrine (oral decongestant)Nasal congestion↑ BP; insomnia; avoid in hypertension; controlled substance in some states
Fluticasone (nasal steroid)Allergic rhinitis, sinusitis — reduces inflammationUse consistently for best results; nosebleeds, dryness possible
Saline nasal sprayMoisturize dry mucosa, flush irritantsSafe for daily use; no rebound effect

Throat & Upper Airway Medications

DrugUseNCLEX Alert
Penicillin / AmoxicillinStrep throat, tonsillitis, pharyngitisComplete full course; allergy risk; GI upset
Acetaminophen / IbuprofenPain & fever with sore throat or post-tonsillectomyLiver caution (acetaminophen); renal/GI caution (ibuprofen)
Nystatin (oral antifungal)Oral thrush (Candida)Swish & swallow (or spit per order); mild GI upset; continue until gone
Dexamethasone (corticosteroid)Epiglottitis, croup — reduces airway swellingImmunosuppression; mood changes; never stop abruptly if chronic use
Racemic Epinephrine (nebulized)Croup — reduces subglottic swelling↑ HR, tremors; rebound edema may occur 2–4 hrs later — monitor
IV Antibiotics (ceftriaxone/ampicillin-sulbactam)Epiglottitis emergency treatmentAirway must be secured first; administer after intubation
CPAP (not a drug)Sleep apnea — maintains airway patencyRequires adherence; educate on proper fit, cleaning; avoid alcohol/sedatives

NCLEX Must-Knows — Medications

  • 🎯 Timolol eye drops = glaucoma; press inner canthus to prevent systemic absorption
  • 🎯 Atropine = CONTRAINDICATED in glaucoma (↑ IOP)
  • 🎯 Latanoprost = may permanently darken iris color — warn patient
  • 🎯 Oxymetazoline nasal spray = max 3 days → rebound congestion after that
  • 🎯 Ototoxic drugs: aspirin, furosemide, gentamicin/aminoglycosides — monitor hearing
  • 🎯 Nystatin = swish & swallow for oral thrush
  • 🎯 Strep throat = complete full antibiotic course; prevent rheumatic fever
  • 🎯 Racemic epi for croup = watch for rebound swelling 2–4 hrs post-treatment
