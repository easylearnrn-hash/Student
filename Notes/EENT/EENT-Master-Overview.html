Emergencies:

6. Gingivitis — Quick Facts

  • Mildest, most reversible form of periodontal disease
  • Cause: plaque accumulation; also hormones, Vit C deficiency, diabetes, phenytoin/cyclosporine/CCBs
  • Signs: red/swollen gums, bleeding with brushing, halitosis — painless in early stages
  • Treatment: dental cleaning, improved oral hygiene, treat underlying cause
  • Progression: gingivitis → periodontitis (bone loss — irreversible)
  • Drug-induced gingival hyperplasia: phenytoin, cyclosporine, nifedipine

7. Master NCLEX Pearls

  • 🎯 Glaucoma = NO atropine (↑ IOP); timolol = press inner canthus after drops
  • 🎯 Retinal detachment = painless + curtain = emergency surgery
  • 🎯 Ear drops: <3 yrs = down & back; >3 yrs = up & back
  • 🎯 Meniere's triad = vertigo + tinnitus + hearing loss; low-sodium diet
  • 🎯 Ototoxic: aspirin, furosemide, aminoglycosides
  • 🎯 Epistaxis = sit up, lean forward, pinch 5–10 min; NEVER tilt back
  • 🎯 CSF leak after nasal trauma = emergency (meningitis risk)
  • 🎯 Oxymetazoline = max 3 days; rebound congestion after
  • 🎯 Epiglottitis = DO NOT inspect throat; airway emergency
  • 🎯 Tonsillectomy = frequent swallowing = bleeding; no straws; cold/clear liquids
  • 🎯 Strep = complete antibiotics → prevent rheumatic fever
  • 🎯 Suctioning = 10–15 sec, pre-oxygenate, suction on withdrawal only
  • 🎯 Gingivitis = painless + bleeds easily; reversible with cleaning
  • 🎯 Phenytoin/cyclosporine = gingival hyperplasia (gum overgrowth)
