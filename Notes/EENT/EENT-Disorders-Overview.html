EENT Disorders — Full Overview

Glaucoma · Cataracts · Retinal Detachment · Otitis · Meniere's · Rhinitis · Strep · Epiglottitis

Eye Disorders

Glaucoma — ↑ IOP → Optic Nerve Damage

  • Open-Angle (most common): slow, painless peripheral vision loss — tunnel vision
  • Angle-Closure (emergency): sudden eye pain, blurry vision, halos, nausea/vomiting
  • Treatment: timolol (↓ IOP), latanoprost (↑ drainage), surgery if severe
NEVER give anticholinergics (atropine) in glaucoma — increases IOP and can cause acute angle-closure!

Cataracts — Cloudy Lens

  • Causes: aging, diabetes, smoking, UV exposure
  • Symptoms: blurry/dim vision, glare, halos, poor night vision
  • Treatment: cataract surgery (lens replacement)
  • Post-op: avoid heavy lifting, bending; no rubbing eyes; report flashes/pain

Retinal Detachment — Medical Emergency

  • Symptoms: flashing lights, floaters (black spots), "curtain-like shadow" over vision — painless
  • Treatment: emergency laser or cryotherapy surgery; lie face down post-op
Retinal detachment is painless but causes sudden vision loss — it is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY.

Ear Disorders

Otitis Media — Middle Ear Infection (Common in Kids)

  • Symptoms: ear pain (worse at night), fever, tugging at ear (infants), red/bulging tympanic membrane
  • Treatment: warm compress; acetaminophen/ibuprofen; amoxicillin if bacterial; myringotomy if chronic
Ear drops/pinna technique: Pull down & back if <3 years; up & back if >3 years. Risk: speech delay if untreated in children.

Meniere's Disease — Inner Ear Fluid Imbalance

  • Classic triad: Vertigo + Tinnitus + Sensorineural hearing loss
  • Also: ear fullness/pressure, nausea, loss of balance
  • Treatment: low-sodium diet, diuretics, avoid caffeine/alcohol/nicotine, meclizine, anti-nausea meds
  • Instruct patient to move slowly; fall precautions

Nose Disorders

Rhinitis (Allergic)

  • Symptoms: runny nose, sneezing, itchy eyes, postnasal drip
  • Treatment: antihistamines (loratadine, diphenhydramine), decongestants (pseudoephedrine), fluticasone nasal spray
  • Avoid long-term decongestant use — rebound congestion

Sinusitis

  • Symptoms: facial pain/pressure (worse bending forward), thick yellow/green discharge, headache
  • Treatment: hydration, steam, saline spray, decongestants; antibiotics only if symptoms >10 days

Throat & Upper Airway Disorders

Strep Throat (Bacterial)

  • Symptoms: sore throat + pain with swallowing + white patches on tonsils + swollen lymph nodes + fever
  • Treatment: penicillin or amoxicillin — complete full course
Untreated strep throat → rheumatic fever → permanent heart valve damage.

Tonsillitis

  • Symptoms: red/swollen tonsils, pus, difficulty swallowing
  • Post-tonsillectomy: avoid red liquids (mimics blood); no straws; cold liquids; frequent swallowing = first sign of bleeding

Epiglottitis — Airway Emergency

  • Symptoms: drooling, muffled voice, stridor (high-pitched breathing), tripod position, high fever
  • Cause: often Haemophilus influenzae type B in unvaccinated children
🚨 DO NOT inspect the throat — can trigger complete airway closure. Stay with patient, prepare for emergency intubation, IV antibiotics, O₂.

Final NCLEX Tips

  • 🎯 Glaucoma = high IOP → peripheral vision loss → NO atropine
  • 🎯 Retinal detachment = painless + "curtain" = emergency surgery
  • 🎯 Otitis media in kids = tugging ear; risk of speech delay
  • 🎯 Meniere's = vertigo + tinnitus + hearing loss; low-sodium diet
  • 🎯 Strep = antibiotics needed; prevent rheumatic fever (heart damage)
  • 🎯 Epiglottitis = drooling + tripod + stridor; DO NOT inspect throat
  • 🎯 Tonsillectomy = frequent swallowing after = bleeding; no straws; cold liquids only
