Master Summary: Abuse & Violence · Aging · End-of-Life Care

1. Abuse & Violence — Core Principles

Nurses are mandatory reporters. Suspect abuse? REPORT — do not investigate. This applies to children, elderly, and vulnerable adults.
TypeKey Clues
PhysicalLoop marks, cigarette burns, immersion lines, multiple healing stages
SexualSTIs, genital trauma, fear of touch
EmotionalWithdrawal, anxiety, low self-esteem
NeglectMalnutrition, poor hygiene, missed appointments
FinancialMissing possessions, bank changes, unpaid bills

Child Abuse Red Flags

  • Injuries inconsistent with story; delay in seeking care
  • Multiple stages of healing — highest priority red flag
  • Spiral fracture in non-mobile infant; sexualized behavior in young child

Elder Abuse Red Flags

  • Unexplained bruises, over/under-medication, poor hygiene, caregiver hostility
  • Caregiver answers all questions; patient appears fearful

IPV

  • All genders/relationships; frequent injuries + vague explanations; partner controls conversation
  • Competent adult: provide support + resources; autonomy is respected — cannot force report

Nursing Do's & Don'ts

  • ✅ Use open-ended questions; document exact quotes; provide privacy; ensure safety
  • ❌ Do NOT confront abuser; do NOT promise confidentiality; do NOT ask leading questions

Mental Health in Aging

ConditionKey Difference
DepressionPoor sleep, appetite, energy — underdiagnosed in elderly
DeliriumSudden onset, fluctuating consciousness — caused by illness/meds/infection; REVERSIBLE

Erikson's stage 65+ = Ego Integrity vs. Despair — reflection on life's meaning.

3. End-of-Life Care — Key Points

TypeWhenKey Point
Palliative CareAny time during illnessCan be given with curative treatments
Hospice CareLife expectancy <6 monthsCurative treatment stops; comfort only

Physical Changes Near Death

  • Respiratory: Cheyne-Stokes breathing (deep → shallow → apnea); "death rattle"
  • Cardiac: weak pulse, ↓ BP, cool extremities
  • Skin: mottling (purplish marbling), cyanosis of fingers/toes
  • Neuro: increasing sleep, ↓ alertness, possible hallucinations (normal)
  • GI/GU: ↓ urine output, ↓ appetite

Comfort Nursing Interventions

  • Morphine: eases pain AND dyspnea (air hunger) — appropriate and expected
  • Death rattle: anticholinergics (atropine sublingual) or repositioning — educate family
  • Oral care: mouth swabs, moisten lips
  • Do NOT force food or fluids — this is not comfort care
  • Allow cultural rituals, family presence, spiritual support

4. Master NCLEX Pearls

  • 🎯 Suspect abuse? Report — do not investigate
  • 🎯 Child/elder abuse = mandatory report; competent adult IPV = support + resources only
  • 🎯 Multiple stages of healing in a child = highest red flag
  • 🎯 Do NOT promise confidentiality; do NOT confront abuser
  • 🎯 Normal aging = slower, not diseased; delirium = sudden/reversible; dementia = gradual/progressive
  • 🎯 Presbycusis = speak slowly, face patient — do NOT shout
  • 🎯 Start low, go slow = medication principle in elderly (↓ renal clearance)
  • 🎯 Erikson: 65+ = Ego Integrity vs. Despair
  • 🎯 Palliative = anytime; Hospice = <6 months + curative treatment stopped
  • 🎯 Morphine at EOL = appropriate; do NOT withhold for fear of hastening death
  • 🎯 Do NOT force food/fluids at end of life
  • 🎯 Always advocate for dignity, comfort, and patient autonomy
