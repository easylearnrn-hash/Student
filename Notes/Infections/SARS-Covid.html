SARS & Covid-19

Coronavirus respiratory illnesses — airborne transmission, no specific antiviral (SARS), N95 required

Overview

  • SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) = SARS-CoV; emerged 2002–2003 in China
  • COVID-19 = SARS-CoV-2; emerged 2019; global pandemic
  • Both are single-stranded RNA coronaviruses
  • Primarily attack the lower respiratory tract → pneumonia/ARDS

Transmission

  • Primary: Respiratory droplets (close contact <6 ft)
  • Healthcare settings: Airborne (aerosol-generating procedures — intubation, bronchoscopy, suctioning)
  • Contact with contaminated surfaces (fomites)
  • Incubation: SARS = 2–10 days; COVID-19 = 2–14 days (average 5)

Clinical Stages (SARS/COVID-19)

StageTimingSymptoms
Early/MildDays 1–7Fever (>100.4°F), cough, myalgia, malaise, headache
Pulmonary PhaseDays 7–10Shortness of breath, hypoxia, persistent cough, pneumonia
Severe/LateDays 10+ARDS, respiratory failure, multi-organ dysfunction, septic shock

Diagnosis

  • PCR (RT-PCR) — gold standard; best in early infection
  • Rapid antigen test — quick screen (less sensitive)
  • Serology (antibodies) — for past exposure, not acute diagnosis
  • Chest X-ray / CT scan — bilateral infiltrates ("ground-glass" opacities)
  • CBC: leukopenia, lymphopenia typical
  • ABG: hypoxemia, possible respiratory alkalosis early

Treatment

Supportive Care (Both):

  • Supplemental O₂ (target SpO₂ ≥94%)
  • IV fluids for hydration
  • Acetaminophen for fever
  • Corticosteroids (dexamethasone) — reduce inflammation, especially in severe COVID
  • Prone positioning for ARDS
  • Mechanical ventilation if needed

COVID-19 Specific:

  • Remdesivir — antiviral (moderate–severe, hospitalized)
  • Paxlovid (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir) — early treatment outpatient
  • Anti-IL-6 therapy (tocilizumab) — cytokine storm
  • Vaccines — mRNA (Pfizer/Moderna), adenoviral vector (J&J)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

SARS = no specific antiviral; supportive only. COVID has antivirals (remdesivir, Paxlovid) and vaccines. Both require N95 + negative pressure room.

Isolation — NCLEX PRIORITY

  • Airborne + Contact precautions
  • N95 respirator (not surgical mask) — especially for AGPs
  • Negative-pressure room — door must remain CLOSED
  • Gown, gloves, eye protection (goggles/face shield)
  • Limit visitors; minimize staff entering room
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

SARS/COVID = N95 + negative-pressure room. This is airborne disease for NCLEX purposes — surgical mask is INSUFFICIENT.

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Both SARS and COVID = Airborne + Contact precautions
  • N95 + negative-pressure room (especially AGPs)
  • SARS = no specific antiviral, no vaccine
  • COVID = remdesivir, dexamethasone, vaccines available
  • Main complication = ARDS → may need intubation
  • PCR = best early diagnostic test
  • Chest CT = ground-glass bilateral infiltrates
  • Prone positioning helps oxygenation in ARDS
