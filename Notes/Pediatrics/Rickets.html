Pediatrics

Rickets

Defective bone mineralization due to vitamin D deficiency — NCLEX overview

Definition

Rickets is a pediatric disorder characterized by defective mineralization of the growing bones due to vitamin D deficiency, calcium, or phosphate imbalance. (Mineralization = process of hardening bones with minerals like calcium and phosphate)

📌
NCLEX Tip

Rickets = soft bones in children due to vitamin D deficiency. In adults, the same condition is called osteomalacia.

Etiology / Causes

  • Vitamin D deficiency (poor diet, lack of sun exposure)
  • Malabsorption syndromes (celiac disease, cystic fibrosis)
  • Chronic kidney disease (interferes with vitamin D activation)
  • Premature birth (lower stores of vitamin D)
  • Exclusive breastfeeding without vitamin D supplementation

Risk Factors

  • Dark-skinned children (reduced skin production of vitamin D)
  • Limited sunlight exposure
  • Poor dietary intake (vegan diets, lactose intolerance)
  • Children in northern climates or with covered skin for cultural reasons

Signs & Symptoms — RICKETS Mnemonic

RICKETS Mnemonic
R
Rosary chest — swelling at costochondral junctions ("rachitic rosary")
I
Indented chest — Harrison's groove
C
Curved bones — bowed legs (genu varum), knock-knees (genu valgum)
K
Knock knees — valgus deformity
E
Enlarged wrists and ankles — due to bone growth plate abnormalities
T
Teeth issues — delayed eruption, enamel defects
S
Soft skull (craniotabes), stunted growth

Diagnostic Tests

  • ↓ Serum calcium
  • ↓ Serum phosphate
  • ↑ Alkaline phosphatase (elevated in active bone turnover)
  • ↓ 25-hydroxyvitamin D — most accurate test for vitamin D levels
  • X-rays: show cupping/fraying of metaphysis, widened growth plates, bone softening

Treatment & Management

  • Vitamin D supplementation (D2 or D3)
  • Calcium and phosphate supplements as needed
  • Sunlight exposure (15–30 mins/day without sunscreen on face and arms)
  • Balanced nutrition: Dairy, fortified cereals, eggs, fish
  • Surgery in severe deformities (rare)

Nursing Interventions — NCLEX Focus

  • Educate caregivers on vitamin D sources (sunlight, food, supplements)
  • Administer supplements as prescribed
  • Encourage outdoor play with safe sun exposure
  • Monitor growth and development regularly
  • Reinforce follow-up labs to check treatment progress
  • Assess for bone pain or delayed milestones
  • Monitor for seizures in severe hypocalcemia
NCLEX Practice Q&A

Q: A toddler presents with bowing of the legs, delayed walking, and irritability. Labs show low calcium and low vitamin D. What condition is most likely?
A: Rickets

Q: Which meal best supports bone growth in a child with Rickets?
A: Fortified milk, egg, and cereal (rich in vitamin D and calcium)
