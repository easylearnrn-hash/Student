Pediatric Developmental Stages

Milestones, Erikson's psychosocial stages, and red flags — NCLEX overview

Why It Matters

Pediatric nursing often focuses on whether a child is hitting expected milestones at the right developmental stage. Delays or regressions may signal illness, disability, or stress — recognizing what's normal helps you notice what's not.

Infant: Birth–1 Year

Physical & Motor
  • Rapid growth: doubles by 6 months, triples by 1 year
  • Anterior fontanel closes by 12–18 months
  • Rolls over: 4–6 months
  • Sits up: 6–8 months
  • Crawls: ~9 months
  • Stands & cruises: ~10–12 months
Cognitive & Language
  • Object permanence develops (~6–9 months)
  • Coos by 6 weeks
  • Babbles by 4–6 months
  • Says first words by 12 months
  • Erikson: Trust vs. Mistrust
  • Baby learns if needs will be met

Toddler: 1–3 Years

Physical & Motor
  • Slower growth; pot belly and wide stance normal
  • Walks alone ~12–15 months
  • Climbs stairs, kicks ball ~2 years
  • Builds tower, throws ball ~2–3 years
Cognitive & Language
  • Egocentric thinking
  • Parallel play (alongside, not with, other children)
  • 2-word phrases by age 2
  • Vocabulary explosion after 2 years
  • Erikson: Autonomy vs. Shame/Doubt
  • "Me do it!" — wants independence

Preschooler: 3–6 Years

Physical & Motor
  • Growth slows, loses baby fat
  • Runs well, hops, skips
  • Uses scissors, copies shapes
Cognitive & Language
  • Magical thinking — believes thoughts cause events
  • Animism — believes objects are alive
  • Full sentences; asks "why?" constantly
  • Knows name, age, and colors
  • Erikson: Initiative vs. Guilt
  • Wants to try new things; feels guilt when scolded

School-Age: 6–12 Years

Physical & Cognitive
  • Steady growth; begins to lose baby teeth
  • Puberty signs may start near end (esp. girls)
  • Concrete operational thinking — reasons logically about real things
  • Understands cause and effect, conservation
Social & Psychosocial
  • Cooperative play
  • Peer approval becomes important
  • Erikson: Industry vs. Inferiority
  • Focuses on success, schoolwork, learning skills

Adolescent: 12–18 Years

Physical & Cognitive
  • Puberty: growth spurts, sexual maturity
  • Girls usually start earlier than boys
  • Abstract thinking develops
  • Questions rules and identity
Social & Psychosocial
  • Peer relationships are top priority
  • May experiment with identity, risk-taking
  • Erikson: Identity vs. Role Confusion
  • Figuring out who they are

NCLEX Must-Knows

  • Head lag should disappear by 4 months — if not, may signal neurologic delay
  • Toilet training readiness starts around 18–24 months
  • Regression is normal during stress (toddler may start wetting again when hospitalized)
  • Magical thinking → preschoolers may blame themselves for illness
  • Let toddlers make safe choices (e.g., "red cup or blue?")
  • School-age kids want to master skills — involve them in care
  • Teens care most about peers, privacy, and control

Developmental Red Flags — Call Provider If:

🚨
Red Flags

No babbling by 9 months • Not walking by 18 months • No 2-word phrases by 2 years • Loss of milestones (used to speak/walk but now can't)
