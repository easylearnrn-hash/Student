Pediatrics — Cardiovascular

Pediatric Cardiovascular Nursing

Congenital heart defects, heart failure, medications, and family teaching

Acyanotic Defects (Normal O₂ Saturation)

Blood shunts Left → Right (oxygenated blood recirculates through lungs). Child is pink but may have increased respiratory effort and poor growth.

ASD — Atrial Septal Defect

Hole between atria. Often asymptomatic in childhood. May hear a murmur. Repair if large (closure device or surgery).

VSD — Ventricular Septal Defect

Most common congenital heart defect. Hole between ventricles. Loud harsh murmur. Small VSDs may close spontaneously. Large = surgery.

PDA — Patent Ductus Arteriosus

Fetal vessel connecting aorta to pulmonary artery fails to close after birth. Continuous "machine-like" murmur. Common in premature infants. Treatment: indomethacin (closes PDA) or surgical ligation.

Cyanotic Defects (Low O₂ Saturation)

Blood shunts Right → Left — unoxygenated blood enters systemic circulation. Child is blue (cyanotic), especially when crying or during activity.

Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF) — 4 Defects
  1. VSD (ventricular septal defect)
  2. Pulmonary stenosis (narrowed pulmonary valve)
  3. Overriding aorta (straddles the VSD)
  4. Right ventricular hypertrophy

Signs: cyanosis, clubbing of fingers/toes, polycythemia (high RBC from chronic hypoxia).

🔴
Tet Spells (Hypercyanotic Episodes)

Sudden blue/hypoxic episodes in TOF. Triggered by crying, feeding, defecation. Action: Place child in knee-to-chest position (increases SVR → forces blood through pulmonary circulation). Give O₂, morphine, IV propranolol. Calm the child.

Heart Failure Signs in Infants and Children

  • Tachycardia (compensatory)
  • Tachypnea, dyspnea, retractions
  • Poor feeding — tires during feeding (sweating, stops often)
  • Failure to thrive (FTT)
  • Hepatomegaly (liver enlargement)
  • Periorbital or peripheral edema
  • Diaphoresis (especially during feeding)

Pre/Post-Operative Care

Pre-op
  • Monitor SpO₂ and cyanosis
  • Daily weight; monitor intake/output
  • Support feeding (high-calorie formula)
  • Prevent infection; up-to-date vaccines
Post-op
  • Continuous cardiac monitoring, vitals, SpO₂
  • Assess for arrhythmias
  • Incision care; watch for infection/dehiscence
  • Encourage deep breathing; incentive spirometry
  • Pain management; reduce agitation (increases O₂ demand)

Family Teaching

  • Weigh daily at same time on same scale; report gain >0.5 lb/day
  • Give medications on schedule; never skip doses
  • High-calorie feedings (supplement breastmilk or high-calorie formula)
  • Signs of distress: poor feeding, fast breathing, color changes, fewer wet diapers
  • Activity restrictions (as ordered); avoid strenuous play until cleared
  • SBE prophylaxis (antibiotics before dental/invasive procedures if applicable)

NCLEX Summary

VSD/ASD/PDA = acyanotic (pink, breathing fast, L→R shunt). TOF = cyanotic (blue, clubbing, R→L shunt, surgery). Tet spell = knees to chest. Digoxin = always check HR before giving; toxicity = nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, loss of appetite, confusion, weakness, fatigue, dizziness, blurred vision, yellow/green vision, halos, spots, or blind spots, slow, fast, or irregular pulse. Heart failure in infants = diaphoresis with feeding, poor weight gain, hepatomegaly.
