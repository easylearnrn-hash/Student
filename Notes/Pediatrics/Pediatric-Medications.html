Pediatrics

Pediatric Medications Overview

Key drugs by system — indications, side effects, and NCLEX pearls

1. Newborn Care Medications

Vitamin K (Phytonadione)

Prevents bleeding in newborns (vitamin K deficiency)

Side effects: pain at injection site

Erythromycin Eye Ointment

Prevents eye infection from gonorrhea/chlamydia

Side effects: mild redness or irritation

2. Pediatric Diabetes Medications

Insulin

(regular, lispro, glargine)

Type 1 diabetes management

SE: hypoglycemia, weight gain

Glucagon

Severe hypoglycemia when unconscious

SE: nausea, vomiting

Dextrose (D50)

Emergency treatment for low blood sugar

SE: vein irritation, hyperglycemia

3. Pediatric Cardiac Medications

Digoxin

Improves contractility in heart failure

SE: bradycardia, vomiting, toxicity — check apical pulse!

Furosemide (Lasix)

Reduces fluid overload in heart failure

SE: dehydration, electrolyte imbalance

Captopril (ACE Inhibitor)

Lowers afterload in congenital heart disease

SE: hypotension, cough, hyperkalemia

4. Seizure, Neurologic & ADHD Meds

Lorazepam, Diazepam

Seizure rescue meds (status epilepticus)

SE: sedation, respiratory depression

Phenytoin, Levetiracetam, Valproic Acid

Seizure prevention

SE: gingival hyperplasia, liver toxicity, dizziness

Baclofen, Botulinum Toxin

Cerebral palsy — reduce spasticity

SE: muscle weakness, sedation

Methylphenidate, Amphetamines (Adderall)

ADHD — increase attention span

SE: ↓ appetite, insomnia, ↑ HR

5. Respiratory Medications

Albuterol

Rescue inhaler for asthma

SE: tremors, tachycardia

Racemic Epinephrine

Croup — reduces airway swelling fast

SE: ↑ HR, rebound swelling

Palivizumab (Synagis)

Prevents RSV in high-risk infants

SE: fever, injection site pain

6. GI & Nutrition Medications

Polyethylene Glycol (Miralax)

Constipation relief

SE: diarrhea, gas

Omeprazole

GERD or acid reflux in children

SE: headache, abdominal pain

Phenylketonuria Formula

Special diet to prevent brain damage in PKU

SE: none if properly used

Pancrelipase

CF enzyme replacement — take with meals, do not chew!

SE: GI upset

7. Skin & Infection Medications

Zinc Oxide

Diaper rash prevention

SE: very minimal

Nystatin

Yeast diaper rash

SE: skin irritation

Permethrin Cream

Lice or scabies treatment

SE: mild skin burning

Antibiotic Ear/Eye Drops

Otitis media, conjunctivitis

SE: redness, mild burning
