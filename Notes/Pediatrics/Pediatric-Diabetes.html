Pediatric Diabetes Mellitus

Type 1, Type 2, DKA, insulin types, hypoglycemia, sick day rules

Type 1 vs Type 2

Type 1 (Most Common in Children)
  • Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells
  • Absolute insulin deficiency
  • Onset: childhood/adolescence
  • Treatment: insulin required (no oral meds alone)
Type 2 (Increasing in Children)
  • Insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency
  • Associated with obesity, sedentary lifestyle
  • Treatment: diet/exercise, metformin, ± insulin

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL
  • Random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with symptoms
  • HbA1c ≥ 6.5%
  • 2-hour OGTT ≥ 200 mg/dL

Hyperglycemia — The 3 Ps

  • Polyuria — frequent urination
  • Polydipsia — excessive thirst
  • Polyphagia — increased hunger
  • + Weight loss, fatigue, blurry vision

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

Life-threatening complication. No insulin → body breaks down fat → ketones (acids) accumulate in blood.

Signs of DKA
  • Kussmaul respirations — deep, rapid, labored breathing (compensating for acidosis)
  • Fruity/acetone breath (from ketones)
  • Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
  • Dehydration, sunken eyes
  • Confusion, lethargy → coma if untreated
  • BG often >250 mg/dL; ketones in urine/blood

Treatment: IV normal saline (fluid resuscitation) → IV regular insulin drip → potassium replacement (K⁺ drops when insulin given) → close monitoring.

🔴
DKA = Emergency

Do NOT give insulin subcut first — IV fluid bolus first to treat hypovolemia. Then IV insulin drip. Monitor potassium closely — hypokalemia is a DKA treatment complication.

Glargine (Lantus) — NCLEX Must Know

No peak = no hypoglycemic spike. Do NOT mix with other insulins. Given at bedtime or same time daily. Rotate injection sites to prevent lipodystrophy (lumpy/hard skin).

Hypoglycemia (<70 mg/dL)

Signs: Shakiness, diaphoresis (sweating), pallor, headache, hunger, irritability, confusion, difficulty concentrating → seizures if untreated.

15–15 Rule
  1. Give 15g of fast-acting carbohydrates (4 oz juice, glucose tablets, regular soda)
  2. Wait 15 minutes
  3. Recheck blood glucose
  4. If still <70 — repeat

Unconscious/cannot swallow = glucagon injection IM or IN (nasal). Call 911 if unresponsive.

Sick Day Rules

  • Never skip insulin even if not eating (illness increases BG)
  • Check blood glucose every 3–4 hours
  • Check urine/blood ketones if BG >240 mg/dL
  • Stay hydrated with fluids
  • If vomiting and cannot keep fluids down → call provider/go to ER
📝
NCLEX Summary

DKA = emergency → IV fluids first, then IV insulin, then K⁺ replacement. Glargine = no peak, don't mix. Rotate injection sites. Exercise lowers BG → watch for hypoglycemia. Hypoglycemia treatment = 15g carbs → wait 15 min → recheck. Unconscious = glucagon. Never skip insulin when sick.
