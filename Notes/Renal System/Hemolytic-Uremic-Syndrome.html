Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)

Triad of hemolytic anemia + thrombocytopenia + acute kidney injury — most common in children under 5

Classic Triad E. coli O157:H7 Avoid Antibiotics + Antidiarrheals Children Under 5

1. Classic Triad of HUS

1. Microangiopathic Hemolytic Anemia

RBCs are destroyed as they pass through damaged microvasculature → ↓ Hgb + schistocytes (fragmented RBCs) on smear

2. Thrombocytopenia

Platelets consumed in microclots → ↓ platelet count → petechiae, bleeding risk

3. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

Microthrombi occlude renal microvasculature → ↓ GFR → oliguria/anuria, ↑ creatinine

2. Cause & Pathophysiology

  • Most common trigger: E. coli O157:H7 (Shiga toxin–producing E. coli, STEC)
  • Source: undercooked ground beef, contaminated produce, unpasteurized juices
  • Shiga toxin → damages endothelium → microthrombi → hemolysis + platelet consumption + renal injury
  • Begins with bloody diarrhea → 5–10 days later → HUS triad develops
Timeline: Bloody diarrhea → 5–10 days → HUS triad (anemia + thrombocytopenia + AKI)

3. Mnemonic — "HUS = HURTS"

HUS = HURTS

  • H — Hemolytic anemia (schistocytes on blood smear)
  • U — Uremia (↑ BUN/creatinine from AKI)
  • R — RBC fragmentation (microangiopathic)
  • T — Thrombocytopenia (↓ platelets)
  • S — Shiga toxin from E. coli O157:H7

4. Signs & Symptoms

  • Prodrome: watery → bloody diarrhea, abdominal cramps, fever (5–10 days before HUS)
  • Pale, jaundiced, fatigue (hemolytic anemia)
  • Petechiae, easy bruising (thrombocytopenia)
  • Oliguria or anuria — hallmark of AKI onset
  • Edema, HTN
  • Irritability, neurological symptoms (confusion, seizures) — severe cases

5. Diagnostics

TestExpected Finding
CBC↓ Hgb (anemia), ↓ platelets
Peripheral blood smearSchistocytes (fragmented RBCs) — hallmark finding
BMP↑ BUN, ↑ creatinine, electrolyte imbalances
UrinalysisHematuria, proteinuria, casts
Stool cultureE. coli O157:H7 (STEC)
LDH↑ Elevated — reflects RBC destruction

6. Treatment

🚫 AVOID ANTIBIOTICS — worsen Shiga toxin release → increase risk of HUS progression
🚫 AVOID ANTIDIARRHEALS (e.g., loperamide) — slow gut motility → increase toxin absorption → worsen HUS
  • ✅ Supportive care: fluid management, electrolyte correction
  • ✅ Dialysis (peritoneal or hemodialysis) if AKI is severe
  • ✅ Blood transfusions for severe anemia
  • ✅ Platelet transfusions only if life-threatening bleeding
  • ✅ Eculizumab (monoclonal antibody) — for atypical HUS (complement-mediated)

7. Nursing Care

  • ✅ Monitor I&O strictly — hourly urine output; watch for oliguria/anuria
  • ✅ Daily weights — detect fluid retention
  • ✅ Monitor BP — hypertension from fluid overload
  • ✅ Monitor CBC, BUN, creatinine, electrolytes closely
  • ✅ Watch for neurological changes — confusion, seizures indicate cerebral involvement
  • ✅ Bleeding precautions (thrombocytopenia) — soft toothbrush, no IM injections
  • ✅ Provide emotional support to child and parents

8. Prevention

  • Cook ground beef to internal temperature ≥160°F (71°C)
  • Wash hands thoroughly after handling raw meat
  • Avoid unpasteurized milk and juice
  • Wash produce carefully
  • Prevent cross-contamination in kitchen
🔑 Key Takeaway: Triad = hemolytic anemia + thrombocytopenia + AKI. Caused by E. coli O157:H7 (undercooked meat). Schistocytes on blood smear. AVOID antibiotics and antidiarrheals — both worsen HUS. Treatment is supportive; dialysis if needed.
