Pheochromocytoma

Adrenal medulla tumor → excess catecholamines → hypertensive crisis

Do NOT Palpate Abdomen Alpha-Blocker FIRST Classic Triad: H-P-D Adrenalectomy

1. What Is Pheochromocytoma?

  • Rare tumor of the adrenal medulla — secretes excess catecholamines (epinephrine + norepinephrine)
  • Causes continuous or episodic, life-threatening hypertension
  • Usually benign and unilateral; rarely bilateral or malignant

2. Classic Triad (PHEO)

  • P — Palpitations (racing heart)
  • H — Headache (from sudden BP spike)
  • E — Episodic sweating (diaphoresis)
  • O — hypertension (paroxysmal, severe)

Also: tremors, anxiety/panic attacks, chest pain, hyperglycemia, weight loss

Triggers

  • Stress, surgery, exercise
  • Tyramine-rich foods (aged cheese, wine)
  • MAO inhibitors, decongestants

3. Diagnosis

TestPurpose
Plasma-free metanephrinesMost sensitive screening test
24-hour urine catecholaminesConfirms excess hormone secretion
CT/MRI abdomenLocates adrenal tumor
Clonidine suppression testDifferentiates from other HTN causes

4. Treatment — Pre-Op Priority Order

  1. Alpha-blockers FIRST (e.g., phenoxybenzamine) — dilate vessels, lower BP, prevent intra-op HTN crisis
  2. Beta-blockers AFTER (e.g., propranolol) — control tachycardia; NEVER start beta before alpha (causes unopposed alpha → worsens HTN)
  3. High-sodium diet + IV hydration — prevents orthostatic hypotension post-op
  4. Surgery: Adrenalectomy — primary treatment
Post-Op Watch: Hypotension (catecholamine drop) + Hypoglycemia

5. NCLEX Nursing Priorities

🚫 DO NOT PALPATE THE ABDOMEN — triggers catecholamine surge → hypertensive crisis
  • ✅ Monitor BP frequently — can spike dangerously high
  • ✅ Calm, quiet environment — prevent stress-induced episodes
  • ✅ Avoid triggers (tyramine foods, emotional stress, certain meds)
  • ✅ Alpha-blocker before beta-blocker (order matters!)
  • ✅ Monitor glucose — hyperglycemia pre-op; hypoglycemia post-op
  • ✅ Lifelong follow-up — risk of recurrence or metastasis

6. Complications

ComplicationExplanation
Hypertensive crisisLife-threatening BP spike → stroke, MI, organ damage
ArrhythmiasExcess catecholamines disrupt heart rhythm
Post-op hypotensionSudden catecholamine withdrawal after tumor removal
Adrenal insufficiencyRare — if both adrenal glands are removed
🔑 Key Takeaway: Adrenal medulla tumor → excess catecholamines → paroxysmal HTN, headache, palpitations, diaphoresis. Never palpate abdomen. Alpha-blocker BEFORE beta-blocker. Post-op watch: hypotension + hypoglycemia.
