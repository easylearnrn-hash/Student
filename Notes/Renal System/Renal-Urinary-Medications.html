Renal & Urinary Medications

Medications used for UTIs, renal calculi, AKI, CKD, and dialysis management

Phenazopyridine = Orange Urine Phosphate Binders WITH Meals Avoid Nephrotoxics in CKD Insulin + Dextrose for K+

1. UTI / Cystitis Medications

DrugClass / UseKey Nursing Points
Nitrofurantoin (Macrobid)Antibiotic — cystitisTake with food; avoid if GFR <45; urine turns dark yellow/brown; complete full course
TMP-SMX (Bactrim)Antibiotic — cystitis/pyeloMonitor for rash (Stevens-Johnson syndrome); adequate hydration; avoid in G6PD deficiency
Ciprofloxacin (Cipro)Fluoroquinolone — complicated UTI/pyeloDo NOT take with dairy or antacids (reduces absorption); tendon rupture risk; avoid in pregnancy
Phenazopyridine (Pyridium)Urinary analgesic (NOT antibiotic)Relieves dysuria/burning; orange/red urine — warn patients; do NOT use >2 days; not for infection treatment

2. Renal Calculi (Kidney Stones)

DrugUseKey Points
Tamsulosin (Flomax)Alpha-1 blocker — facilitates stone passageRelaxes ureteral smooth muscle → helps stone pass; orthostatic hypotension risk; rise slowly
NSAIDs (ketorolac, ibuprofen)Analgesic for ureteral spasmShort-term pain relief; avoid if renal impairment
Opioids (morphine, hydromorphone)Moderate–severe renal colic painIV for hospital setting; monitor for sedation
AllopurinolUric acid stones preventionReduces uric acid production; adequate hydration required
Hydration is KEY: Encourage 2–3 L/day — dilutes urine and facilitates stone passage/prevention

3. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) Medications

DrugUseKey Points
Furosemide (Lasix)Loop diuretic — fluid/edema managementMonitor I&O, electrolytes; ototoxicity at high doses; hypokalemia risk
Sodium Polystyrene Sulfonate (Kayexalate)Hyperkalemia treatmentExchanges Na+ for K+ in gut; onset 2–6 hrs; sorbitol added to prevent constipation
Insulin + Dextrose (D50)Acute hyperkalemia — shift K+ into cellsFast onset (30 min); temporary measure; monitor blood glucose; give dextrose to prevent hypoglycemia
Calcium GluconateCardiac protection in hyperkalemiaStabilizes cardiac membrane — FIRST drug given for life-threatening hyperkalemia (peaked T-waves, wide QRS)
Sodium bicarbonateCorrect metabolic acidosis; shift K+ into cellsTemporary measure; raises pH → K+ moves intracellular
Hyperkalemia Treatment Order: Calcium Gluconate (protect heart) → Insulin + Dextrose (shift K+ in) → Kayexalate (remove K+ from body)

4. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Medications

DrugUseKey Points
ACE inhibitors / ARBsBP control; reduce proteinuria; slow CKD progressionMonitor potassium (hyperkalemia risk); monitor creatinine; avoid in bilateral renal artery stenosis
Erythropoietin (EPO / Epoetin alfa)CKD anemia — stimulates RBC productionMonitor Hgb — goal 10–12 g/dL; thrombosis risk if Hgb too high; iron supplementation often needed concurrently
Phosphate Binders (sevelamer, calcium carbonate)Prevent hyperphosphatemiaTake WITH MEALS — binds dietary phosphate in gut; do NOT crush sevelamer
Vitamin D (calcitriol)CKD mineral bone diseaseCKD → kidneys can't activate Vitamin D → hypocalcemia → secondary hyperparathyroidism; calcitriol is active form
Iron supplementsCKD anemia (adjunct to EPO)IV iron preferred in CKD/dialysis patients (better absorption); oral causes GI upset

5. Dialysis-Related Medications

DrugConsideration
AntihypertensivesHold before hemodialysis — dialysis removes excess fluid → BP drops; taking antihypertensive beforehand = dangerous hypotension
HeparinUsed in HD circuit to prevent clotting; monitor for bleeding; protamine sulfate = reversal agent
Water-soluble vitamins (Vit B, C)Removed by dialysis — supplement daily
NCLEX Alert: Never give antihypertensives before hemodialysis unless specifically ordered — risk of life-threatening hypotension

6. Drugs to Avoid in Renal Impairment

  • 🚫 NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) — reduce renal blood flow; worsen AKI/CKD
  • 🚫 Aminoglycosides (gentamicin, tobramycin) — nephrotoxic; use with caution; monitor trough levels
  • 🚫 Metformin — hold if GFR <30; risk of lactic acidosis
  • 🚫 IV contrast dye — risk of contrast-induced nephropathy; pre-hydrate and use minimum volume
  • 🚫 Potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone) — use with caution; risk of dangerous hyperkalemia in CKD
🔑 Key Takeaway: Phenazopyridine = orange urine (analgesic, NOT antibiotic). Tamsulosin helps pass stones. Kayexalate + Insulin/Dextrose for hyperkalemia. Phosphate binders WITH meals. EPO for CKD anemia (monitor Hgb). Hold antihypertensives before HD. Avoid NSAIDs/aminoglycosides in renal failure.
