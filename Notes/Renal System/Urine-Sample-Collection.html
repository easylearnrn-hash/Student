Urine Sample Collection

The method of collection directly affects accuracy — correct technique prevents contamination and false results

Midstream Clean-Catch = Gold Standard 24-hr: Keep on Ice Catheter Port — NOT the Bag First-Morning = Most Concentrated

1. Midstream Clean-Catch (Preferred for Culture)

1

Wash hands thoroughly

2

Clean the perineum/urethral meatus with antiseptic wipes — front to back for women; circular motion for men (retract foreskin if uncircumcised)

3

Begin voiding into the toilet — the first stream flushes out contaminants

4

Catch the midstream urine — hold the sterile container in the stream mid-void without touching the rim

5

Finish voiding into toilet; cap the container immediately

6

Label the specimen with patient name, date/time, and send to lab ASAP (within 30 min or refrigerate)

Key: Patient must NOT touch inside of container or lid. Refrigerate if not sent within 30 minutes.

2. Catheterized Specimen

NEVER collect from the drainage bag — bag urine is stagnant, contaminated, and not fresh
  • Clamp the catheter tubing for 15–30 minutes to allow urine to collect
  • Cleanse the sampling port with alcohol
  • Aspirate urine from the port using a sterile syringe
  • Transfer to a sterile specimen container
  • Unclamp tubing and send specimen to lab

3. 24-Hour Urine Collection

Used to measure: creatinine clearance, protein levels, hormones (cortisol, catecholamines), electrolytes

1

Discard the first morning void — this is NOT collected (note the time)

2

Collect ALL urine for the next 24 hours into the large collection jug

3

Keep container on ice or refrigerated throughout collection — prevents bacterial growth

4

Collect the final void at the same time as the start (24 hours later)

5

Label and send to lab

If any void is missed → RESTART the entire collection from the beginning

5. NCLEX Nursing Priorities

  • ✅ Teach patient correct perineal cleaning technique before collection
  • ✅ Midstream clean-catch = preferred for urine cultures
  • ✅ First-morning urine = most concentrated = best for pregnancy tests
  • ✅ 24-hr urine: discard FIRST void, collect ALL after, keep on ice
  • ✅ If catheter present: use sampling port, NEVER the bag
  • ✅ Refrigerate or send specimen within 30 minutes
  • ✅ If any 24-hr void is missed → restart entire collection
🔑 Key Takeaway: Midstream = clean perineum → start void → catch middle stream. 24-hr = discard first void, keep on ice, restart if missed. Catheter = sample from the port (NEVER the bag). First-morning = best for pregnancy.
