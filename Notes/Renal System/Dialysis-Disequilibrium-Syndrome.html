Dialysis Disequilibrium Syndrome (DDS)

Rare but dangerous neurological complication — rapid urea removal creates osmotic shift → cerebral edema → ↑ ICP

Confusion During Dialysis = NEVER Normal First HD Session Highest Risk Slow Dialysis Prevents It Mannitol + Hypertonic Saline

1. Pathophysiology

  1. Rapid hemodialysis quickly removes urea from the blood
  2. Urea clearance from the brain lags behind blood clearance
  3. This creates an osmotic gradient — brain remains hypertonic compared to blood
  4. Water moves into brain cells (osmosis) → cerebral edema
  5. ↑ Intracranial pressure (ICP) develops → neurological symptoms
Key concept: It's not the dialysis itself that causes DDS — it's dialyzing TOO FAST in a patient with very high BUN

2. Symptom Progression

Symptoms worsen as ICP increases — occurs during or shortly after dialysis:

Headache
→
Nausea / Vomiting
→
Restlessness / Confusion
→
Blurred Vision
→
Seizures
→
Coma
NCLEX CRITICAL: Any confusion, headache, or neurological change DURING dialysis = NEVER normal → STOP or SLOW dialysis + notify provider IMMEDIATELY

3. High-Risk Patients

  • 🔴 First hemodialysis session — most common time (body not adapted)
  • 🔴 Very high pre-dialysis BUN — greater the osmotic gradient, higher the risk
  • 🔴 Pediatric patients — smaller blood volume; rapid fluid shifts more impactful
  • 🔴 Elderly patients — reduced compensatory mechanisms
  • Pre-existing neurological conditions (head injury, brain tumor)
  • Severe uremia (BUN >150 mg/dL)

4. Mnemonic — "SLOW DIALYSIS"

SLOW DIALYSIS — Prevention Is Key

  • S — Slow the dialysis rate (gradual urea removal)
  • L — Lower blood flow rate for first-time patients
  • O — Osmotic agents: mannitol to draw water out of brain
  • W — Watch for neurological changes (headache, confusion)
  • D — Dialysis: shorter duration for first sessions
  • I — Immediate action if symptoms develop
  • A — Alert provider — do NOT wait to see if it resolves
  • L — Look for: headache, N/V, restlessness, seizures
  • Y — Your priority = stop or slow dialysis if DDS suspected
  • S — Saline (hypertonic 3%) or mannitol to treat cerebral edema
  • I — ICP monitoring if severe
  • S — Safety measures: seizure precautions, padded side rails

5. Treatment

InterventionPurpose
Slow / stop dialysis rateFirst action — reduces ongoing urea removal and osmotic gradient
Mannitol IVOsmotic diuretic → draws water out of brain → reduces cerebral edema → ↓ ICP
Hypertonic saline (3% NaCl)Raises serum osmolality → pulls water from brain cells
Anticonvulsants (benzodiazepines)If seizures occur — lorazepam or diazepam IV
Supportive careO2 therapy, side-lying position if vomiting, seizure precautions
Discontinue dialysis (severe DDS)If coma or uncontrolled seizures — emergency management

6. Prevention Strategies

  • ✅ Slow dialysis rate for first sessions (shorter duration, lower blood flow)
  • ✅ Target modest BUN reduction (25–30%) in first session — not complete clearance
  • ✅ Use bicarbonate dialysate rather than acetate
  • ✅ Increase dialysate sodium concentration to slow osmotic shift
  • ✅ Prophylactic mannitol for very high-risk patients
  • ✅ Frequent neurological assessments during HD session

7. NCLEX Nursing Priorities

HIGHEST PRIORITY: Patient on HD becomes confused, reports headache, or is restless → NOTIFY PROVIDER and REDUCE dialysis rate immediately — do NOT continue at the same rate
  • ✅ Continuous neurological assessment during all dialysis sessions
  • ✅ First HD session = highest risk → shorter duration, lower rate
  • ✅ Seizure precautions — padded rails, suction at bedside, O2 available
  • ✅ Keep mannitol and hypertonic saline available for high-risk patients
  • ✅ Educate patient and family: headache or confusion during dialysis = report immediately
  • ✅ Monitor vitals, neuro status, and LOC throughout session
🔑 Key Takeaway: Rapid urea removal → osmotic shift → water enters brain → cerebral edema → ↑ ICP. Highest risk: first HD session + very high BUN. Symptoms: headache → N/V → confusion → seizures → coma. Treatment: slow dialysis + mannitol + hypertonic saline. Confusion during dialysis = NEVER normal → notify provider NOW.
