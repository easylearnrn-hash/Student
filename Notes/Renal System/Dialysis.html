Dialysis

Mechanical filtering of waste, fluid, and electrolytes when the kidneys can no longer do so — initiated at CKD Stage 5 (GFR <15)

Feel Thrill + Hear Bruit = Fistula Patent Cloudy PD Fluid = Peritonitis Hold BP Meds Before HD No BP/IV on Fistula Arm

1. Two Types of Dialysis

Hemodialysis (HD)

  • Blood is removed, filtered through a dialyzer (artificial kidney), then returned
  • Frequency: 3× per week, 3–5 hours per session
  • Usually done in dialysis center (or home HD)
  • Rapid fluid/waste removal
  • Requires vascular access

Peritoneal Dialysis (PD)

  • Dialysate instilled into peritoneal cavity; peritoneal membrane acts as filter
  • Waste and fluid diffuse into dialysate → drained out
  • Done at home (CAPD: 4–5×/day; APD: machine overnight)
  • Slower, gentler — better hemodynamic stability
  • Requires peritoneal catheter

2. Hemodialysis — Vascular Access

Access TypeDescriptionNotes
AV FistulaSurgical connection of artery + vein under the skin; usually in forearmPREFERRED — longest lasting, lowest infection risk; takes 6–8 weeks to mature
AV GraftArtificial tube (synthetic) connecting artery and veinUsed when veins aren't adequate; higher infection + clotting risk than fistula
Central Venous CatheterLarge catheter in jugular, subclavian, or femoral veinTemporary — highest infection risk; used in emergencies or while fistula matures
Fistula Assessment: Palpate for thrill (vibration/buzzing = blood flowing) + Auscultate for bruit (whooshing sound). Absence = possible clot → notify provider stat.
🚫 Fistula Arm Rules: NO blood pressure measurements, NO IV lines, NO blood draws, NO tight clothing or jewelry on fistula arm — may damage or occlude access.

3. Hemodialysis — Nursing Care

Before Dialysis

  • Hold antihypertensive medications before HD — BP drops significantly during dialysis → hypotension risk
  • Assess weight (compare to last post-dialysis dry weight)
  • Assess access site for thrill and bruit
  • Assess vital signs baseline

During Dialysis (Complications to Monitor)

  • Hypotension — most common complication; due to rapid fluid removal → slow rate, give NS bolus if needed
  • Muscle cramps (rapid electrolyte shifts)
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Bleeding at access site — apply pressure
  • Disequilibrium Syndrome — headache, confusion, restlessness, nausea from rapid fluid shifts in brain; occurs during or after HD

After Dialysis

  • Monitor BP — may still be low post-procedure
  • Assess access site for bleeding or hematoma
  • Record weight (post-dialysis "dry weight" = target)

4. Peritoneal Dialysis — Nursing Care

Procedure Steps

  • Warm dialysate to body temperature before instilling (cold dialysate = cramps; never microwave)
  • Use strict sterile technique at all times — #1 priority to prevent peritonitis
  • Instill dialysate → dwell → drain → record outflow

Monitoring Output

  • Outflow should be clear to straw-colored
  • Cloudy/turbid outflow = peritonitis → report immediately
  • Blood-tinged outflow may be normal with new catheter (a few exchanges)
  • If outflow < inflow: check for kinks in tubing, reposition patient (side-to-side, ambulate)
⚠️ Peritonitis Signs: Cloudy dialysate + abdominal pain + fever + rebound tenderness → send fluid for culture → IV/IP antibiotics STAT. Peritonitis is the most common and dangerous complication of PD.

PD Types

  • CAPD (Continuous Ambulatory PD) — manual exchanges 4–5×/day; patient does at home while awake
  • APD (Automated PD) — machine (cycler) performs exchanges overnight while patient sleeps

6. Laboratory Goals on Dialysis

LabGoalWhy
BUN / Creatinine↓ (reduced each session)Dialysis removes waste — effective clearance indicates adequate treatment
Potassium (K+)↓ (3.5–5.0 normal)Prevent cardiac arrhythmias; K+ removed with each session
Hemoglobin (Hgb)10–12 g/dL (with EPO)EPO therapy to correct anemia of CKD; avoid over-correction (→ clots)
Dry WeightMaintained at targetFluid removed each HD session to reach "dry weight" — no edema, no fluid overload

7. NCLEX Must-Knows

  • ✅ AV Fistula = PREFERRED access — feel thrill + hear bruit each shift
  • ✅ NO BP, IV, or blood draws on fistula arm
  • ✅ Hold antihypertensives before HD — hypotension is most common complication
  • ✅ PD: warm dialysate first; sterile technique always
  • ✅ Cloudy PD outflow = peritonitis → report immediately
  • ✅ Dialysis begins at Stage 5 CKD (GFR <15)
  • ✅ Disequilibrium syndrome = rapid fluid shifts in brain during HD → headache/confusion
  • ✅ EPO given to treat anemia — target Hgb 10–12 g/dL
🔑 Key Takeaway: Dialysis filters waste/fluid when kidneys fail. HD: 3×/week, external dialyzer, AV fistula preferred (feel thrill + hear bruit, no BP/IV on that arm), hold BP meds before. PD: peritoneal membrane, sterile technique, warm dialysate, cloudy output = peritonitis emergency. Both begin at Stage 5 CKD.
