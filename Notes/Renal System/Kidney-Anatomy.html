Kidney Anatomy

Two bean-shaped retroperitoneal organs — filter ~180 L of blood per day

Nephron = Functional Unit Retroperitoneal Right Lower than Left Renin → BP Control

1. Location & Overview

  • Located in the retroperitoneal space (behind the peritoneum) in the lower back
  • Right kidney is slightly lower than the left — displaced by the liver
  • Each kidney is approximately the size of a fist (~10–12 cm long)
  • Protected by the ribcage, fat, and fascia

2. Major Kidney Functions

FunctionHow
Filter blood / form urine~1 million nephrons per kidney filter plasma continuously
Fluid & electrolyte regulationReabsorb/excrete Na+, K+, Ca2+, Cl– as needed
Blood pressure controlSecretes Renin → activates RAAS → raises BP
RBC productionSecretes Erythropoietin (EPO) → stimulates bone marrow
Vitamin D activationConverts inactive Vit D → active calcitriol → calcium absorption
Acid-base balanceExcretes H+ ions, reabsorbs HCO3– to maintain pH

3. Internal Structures

1
Cortex — outer layer; contains the glomeruli and proximal/distal tubules of nephrons
2
Medulla — inner region; made of renal pyramids; where urine concentrates (Loop of Henle resides here)
3
Renal Pelvis — funnel-shaped; collects urine from the calyces → funnels it into the ureter
4
Calyces (singular: calyx) — small cup-shaped chambers that collect urine from renal pyramids → pass to renal pelvis

4. The Nephron — Functional Unit

Each kidney has ~1 million nephrons. The nephron filters blood to produce urine.

PartLocationFunction
GlomerulusCortex (in Bowman's capsule)Initial filtration of blood — filters water, ions, glucose, urea into filtrate
Proximal Convoluted Tubule (PCT)CortexReabsorbs ~65% water, all glucose, amino acids, electrolytes
Loop of HenleMedullaConcentrates urine — reabsorbs water (descending) and Na+/Cl– (ascending)
Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT)CortexRegulates K+, Na+, Ca2+ — responds to aldosterone (Na+ reabsorption)
Collecting DuctMedulla → renal pelvisFinal water reabsorption — controlled by ADH (antidiuretic hormone)
Aldosterone (from adrenal cortex) → DCT → reabsorbs Na+ / excretes K+ → ↑ water retention → ↑ BP
ADH (from posterior pituitary) → collecting duct → reabsorbs water → concentrated urine

5. Blood Flow Through the Kidney

  1. Renal artery — brings oxygenated, unfiltered blood into kidney
  2. Afferent arteriole — delivers blood to the glomerulus
  3. Glomerulus — filters blood (water + small molecules out → filtrate)
  4. Efferent arteriole — carries remaining blood away from glomerulus
  5. Peritubular capillaries — surround tubules; allow reabsorption back into blood
  6. Renal vein — returns filtered, cleaned blood to systemic circulation

6. NCLEX Must-Knows

  • ✅ Nephron = basic filtering unit (1 million per kidney)
  • ✅ Glomerulus filters blood — if damaged → protein/albumin leaks into urine (proteinuria)
  • ✅ Renin = raises blood pressure (secreted when BP drops)
  • ✅ Erythropoietin (EPO) = stimulates RBC production; CKD → ↓ EPO → anemia
  • ✅ Kidney injury = fluid/electrolyte imbalance + waste (BUN/creatinine) buildup
  • ✅ Right kidney is slightly lower than left (liver pushes it down)
  • ✅ ADH controls water reabsorption in the collecting duct (↑ ADH → concentrated urine)
  • ✅ Aldosterone works in DCT — retains Na+, excretes K+
🔑 Key Takeaway: Kidneys filter blood, form urine, regulate electrolytes, control BP (renin), make RBCs (EPO), and activate Vitamin D. Nephron = functional unit. Glomerulus filters first. Loop of Henle concentrates. Collecting duct = ADH-controlled final water reabsorption. Right kidney is lower than left.
