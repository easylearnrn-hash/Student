Core Medication Classifications

Know the class → know the action, uses, and what to monitor — without memorizing every individual drug.
GI · Pain · Fever · Inflammation 9 classes
  • Antiemetics
    Prevent or relieve nausea and vomiting
  • Antipyretics
    Reduce fever
  • Anti-Inflammatories
    Reduce inflammation
  • NSAIDs
    Reduce pain, inflammation, and fever (Non-Steroidal)
  • Corticosteroids
    Suppress inflammation and immune response
  • Antacids
    Reduce stomach acid (e.g. Calcium carbonate)
  • Antidiarrheals
    Reduce GI motility
  • Laxatives
    Promote bowel movement
  • Antispasmodics
    Relieve smooth muscle spasms
Cardiovascular · Blood 9 classes
  • Antihypertensives
    Lower blood pressure
  • Antiarrhythmics
    Regulate abnormal heart rhythms
  • Antilipemics
    Lower cholesterol levels
  • Anticoagulants
    Prevent blood clot formation
  • Antiplatelets
    Prevent platelet aggregation
  • Thrombolytics
    Break down existing blood clots
  • Vasodilators
    Relax blood vessels
  • Vasopressors
    Increase blood pressure
  • Diuretics
    Remove excess fluid via kidneys
Endocrine · Metabolic 5 classes
  • Hypoglycemics
    Lower blood glucose
  • Hyperglycemics
    Increase blood glucose
  • Thyroid Hormones
    Replace or supplement thyroid function
  • Antithyroid Agents
    Reduce thyroid hormone production
  • Antianemics
    Increase RBC production (e.g. Iron, EPO)
Respiratory · ENT 6 classes
  • Bronchodilators
    Open and widen airways
  • Antitussives
    Suppress cough
  • Expectorants
    Loosen mucus
  • Mucolytics
    Break down mucus
  • Decongestants
    Reduce nasal congestion
  • Antihistamines
    Block histamine (H1) receptors
Immune · Infectious Disease 6 classes
  • Antiinfectives
    Treat bacterial, viral, fungal infections
  • Antiretrovirals
    Treat HIV
  • Antineoplastics
    Treat cancer (Chemotherapy agents)
  • Immunosuppressants
    Suppress immune system activity
  • Immunostimulants
    Stimulate immune response
  • Vaccines (Immunizations)
    Provide acquired immunity
CNS · Psychiatric 10 classes
  • Antidepressants
    Treat depression
  • Antipsychotics
    Treat psychosis
  • Anxiolytics
    Reduce anxiety
  • Mood Stabilizers
    Stabilize bipolar disorder
  • Sedatives
    Induce calming effect
  • Hypnotics
    Induce sleep
  • Stimulants
    Increase CNS activity
  • Anticonvulsants
    Control seizure activity
  • Muscle Relaxants
    Reduce skeletal muscle spasm
  • Anticholinergics
    Decrease secretions, reduce spasms
Pain · Optics · Antidotes 4 classes
  • Narcotics / Analgesics
    Relieve moderate-to-severe pain
  • Antidotes
    Reverse toxic effects of medications
  • Miotics
    Constrict the pupil (e.g. glaucoma)
  • Mydriatics
    Dilate the pupil (e.g. eye exams)
