Immunodeficiency
Primary & Secondary — NCLEX Overview

NCLEX-focused study note — immune failure · SCID · HIV/AIDS · infection prevention

What It Is

Immunodeficiency is a condition where the immune system is weakened or not functioning properly, leading to increased risk of infections, cancers, and even autoimmune diseases.

Primary Immunodeficiency
Genetic / Congenital — born with weak or absent immune components
Examples: SCID, Bruton's, DiGeorge
Secondary Immunodeficiency
Acquired — develops later in life due to disease, medications, or other causes
Examples: HIV/AIDS, chemotherapy, steroids
💡
NCLEX Memory Tip

Primary = Genetic (From Birth) — "P for Primary = P for Parents gave it"
Secondary = Acquired (Develops Later) — "S for Secondary = S for Something caused it"

💡
SCID — Most Severe Primary Immunodeficiency

No B cells + no T cells = zero immune defense. Only cure is bone marrow transplant. Must be in protective isolation.

Secondary Immunodeficiency — Common Causes

Top Causes
  • HIV/AIDS — destroys CD4+ T cells
  • Cancer — leukemia, lymphoma attack immune cells
  • Chemotherapy & radiation — kill fast-growing immune cells
Other Causes
  • Corticosteroids (prednisone) — suppress immune response
  • Malnutrition — lack of protein/vitamins weakens immunity
  • Diabetes — hyperglycemia impairs WBC function
  • Burns, major surgery, trauma
🚨
NCLEX Must-Know Causes

Steroids (prednisone) + chemotherapy = major causes of iatrogenic (treatment-caused) immunosuppression.
HIV/AIDS = most common infectious cause of secondary immunodeficiency.

Clinical Manifestations

Mild to Moderate
  • Frequent colds, sinus infections, ear infections
  • Slow-healing wounds
  • Fatigue, weight loss
Severe (Life-Threatening)
  • Opportunistic infections — PCP, CMV, fungal, TB
  • Recurrent pneumonia
  • Chronic diarrhea
  • Oral thrush, candidiasis
  • Kaposi's sarcoma (in HIV)
🚨
Opportunistic Infections — Biggest Threat

Infections that don't normally harm healthy people but become life-threatening in immunodeficient patients:
PCP (Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia) · CMV retinitis · Oral candidiasis (thrush) · Toxoplasmosis · Tuberculosis · Cryptococcal meningitis

Diagnostics

TestWhat It Detects

HIV testing (ELISA, Western Blot, RNA)Confirm HIV infection; Western Blot = confirmatory test
CD4+ T-cell countHIV staging: <200 cells/μL = AIDS diagnosis

Treatment Overview

  • 💉 IVIG (IV Immunoglobulin) — provides passive immunity for antibody deficiencies
  • 🦴 Bone marrow transplant — curative for SCID
  • 💊 Antibiotic prophylaxis — prevent infections before they happen (e.g., TMP-SMX for PCP)
  • 💊 Antiretroviral therapy (ART) — for HIV/AIDS; prevents CD4 decline; must be taken lifelong
  • 🚫 Avoid live vaccines — immunocompromised patients cannot handle live attenuated viruses
🚨
No Live Vaccines — Critical NCLEX Rule

Live attenuated vaccines are CONTRAINDICATED in all immunocompromised patients:
MMR · Varicella · FluMist (nasal influenza) · Shingles (Zostavax) · Rotavirus · Yellow fever

Inactivated vaccines (flu shot, pneumococcal, COVID, hepatitis B) are SAFE and recommended.

Preventing Infections — Nursing Care

Do These
  • 🤲 Hand hygiene — #1 prevention tool
  • 😷 Use masks in public / crowded places
  • 🥩 Cook meat and eggs fully (no raw foods)
  • 🍎 Wash produce thoroughly
  • 💉 Stay up to date on inactivated vaccines
  • 📊 Monitor CBC, labs regularly
Avoid These
  • 🚫 Live vaccines (MMR, varicella, nasal flu)
  • 🌱 No gardening — soil contains Aspergillus (fungal infection)
  • 🐱 No cat litter — Toxoplasma gondii risk
  • 🤒 Avoid sick contacts
  • 🌊 No raw shellfish, no deli meats

NCLEX Nursing Care Priorities

  • 🌡️ Monitor for infection signs — fever, cough, redness, wounds not healing; report immediately
  • 💉 Vaccines — encourage inactivated; educate on live vaccine avoidance
  • 🍽️ Adequate nutrition — protein and vitamins boost immune function
  • 💊 Medication adherence — ART for HIV must not be missed; IVIG schedule adherence
  • 🧼 Infection prevention education — handwashing, diet restrictions, environmental precautions
  • 🛡️ Protective isolation if indicated (SCID, severe neutropenia)
  • 🧠 Psychosocial support — chronic illness, stigma (especially HIV), depression risk
