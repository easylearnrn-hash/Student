Infection Control & Isolation Precautions

Standard · Contact · Droplet · Airborne · PPE · Disease-specific isolation reference

Overview: Four Levels of Precautions

LevelRoute BlockedPPE RequiredRoom Requirement
Standard Blood and body fluids (all patients, all times) Gloves (when touching blood/fluid); mask/goggles if splash risk; gown if soiling likely No special room requirement
Contact Direct or indirect contact with patient/environment Gown + gloves on room entry; dedicated equipment Private room preferred; dedicated equipment
Droplet Respiratory droplets >5 µm (travel <3 feet) Surgical mask within 3 feet; gloves, gown as needed Private room or cohort; keep door closed
Airborne Airborne particles ≤5 µm (travel >3 feet, linger in air) N95 respirator (fit-tested); negative-pressure AIIR room AIIR — Airborne Infection Isolation Room (negative pressure, 6–12 air exchanges/hr, air vented outside)
NCLEX Tip: Standard precautions are used for ALL patients, ALL the time regardless of diagnosis. They are the foundation on which all other precautions are added.

Standard Precautions — Disease Examples

Standard Precautions Only
  • CMV (cytomegalovirus)
  • Hepatitis B (HBV)
  • Hepatitis C (HCV)
  • HIV / AIDS
  • Aspergillosis (immunocompromised patient)
  • Anthrax (cutaneous or inhalation)

Contact Precautions — Disease Examples

Contact Precautions — Gown + Gloves
  • MRSA (methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus)
  • VRE (vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus)
  • C. diff (Clostridioides difficile) — soap and water, not alcohol gel
  • Hepatitis A (if incontinent)
  • RSV (respiratory syncytial virus — pediatric)
  • Scabies and Lice (pediculosis)
  • Herpes Zoster (shingles) — localized, immunocompetent
  • Rotavirus, Salmonella, E. coli O157:H7
  • Wound infections with multidrug-resistant organisms
NCLEX Tip: Contact precautions = gown AND gloves upon entering the room — not just when touching the patient!

Droplet Precautions — Disease Examples

Droplet Precautions — Surgical Mask
  • Influenza A and B
  • Pertussis (whooping cough)
  • Mumps
  • Rubella (German measles)
  • Rubeola / Measles — initial droplet, then airborne
  • Neisseria meningitidis (bacterial meningitis)
  • Streptococcal pharyngitis (group A strep)
  • Epiglottitis (Haemophilus influenzae)
  • Diphtheria
  • Mycoplasma pneumonia
NCLEX Tip: Droplet precautions require a surgical/procedure mask (NOT N95) when within 3 feet. Keep door closed.

Airborne Precautions — Disease Examples

Airborne Precautions — N95 + AIIR
  • Tuberculosis (TB) — classic airborne
  • Measles (Rubeola) — most contagious known disease; airborne for 2 hours in room
  • Chickenpox (Varicella) — airborne + contact
  • Disseminated herpes zoster (in immunocompromised)
  • SARS / COVID-19
  • Avian influenza (H5N1)
  • Monkeypox (airborne + contact)
NCLEX Tip: Airborne precautions = N95 respirator (fit-tested) + AIIR (negative pressure room). Remember: MATC — Measles, Anthrax (inhalation, no isolation), TB, Chickenpox.

AIIR — Airborne Infection Isolation Room

🏥
AIIR Requirements
  • Negative pressure — air flows inward (into room) to prevent particles from escaping
  • 6–12 air exchanges per hour
  • Air vented directly to outdoors OR through HEPA filter before recirculation
  • Door must remain closed at all times
  • Limit visitors; visitors must wear N95 before entering
  • Patient wears surgical mask when transported outside the room

PPE Donning and Doffing Order

Order Matters — Avoid Self-Contamination
Donning (Putting On)Doffing (Removing)
1. Gown1. Gloves (most contaminated)
2. Mask or N952. Goggles/Face shield

Perform hand hygiene before donning AND after doffing each piece

NCLEX Must-Know Summary

⭐
Key Takeaways
  • Standard precautions: every patient, every time — blood/body fluids
  • Contact: gown + gloves — MRSA, VRE, C. diff, RSV, Scabies
  • Droplet: surgical mask — Flu, Mumps, Rubella, Pertussis, N. meningitidis
  • Airborne: N95 + AIIR — TB, Measles, Chickenpox, SARS
  • C. diff: soap and water ONLY — no alcohol gel
  • AIIR: negative pressure, door closed, 6–12 air exchanges/hr
  • Doffing: remove gloves first, mask last
