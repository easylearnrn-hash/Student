Rheumatoid Arthritis
(RA)

NCLEX-focused study note — synovial inflammation · joint destruction · DMARDs · systemic involvement

What It Is

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) is a chronic autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks the synovial membrane (lining of joints that produces lubricating fluid), causing:

Acute
Synovitis — inflammation of joint lining → pain, swelling, warmth
Chronic
Pannus formation — inflamed granulation tissue erodes cartilage and bone → permanent joint destruction
💡
RA vs Osteoarthritis (OA) — Key NCLEX Differentiator

RA: morning stiffness >1 hour · symmetric · inflammatory · systemic symptoms · small joints first
OA: morning stiffness <30 minutes · asymmetric · degenerative · no systemic symptoms · weight-bearing joints

Key Characteristics

Joint Pattern
  • Symmetric — both hands, both knees affected equally
  • Small joints first — fingers (PIP, MCP), wrists, then larger joints
  • Spares DIP joints (unlike OA or psoriatic arthritis)
Morning Stiffness
Greater than 1 hour — key NCLEX distinguisher from OA
Improves with movement/activity throughout the day

Clinical Manifestations

Joint Symptoms

  • Morning stiffness >1 hour — hallmark feature
  • Joint pain, swelling, warmth, redness
  • Decreased range of motion
  • Subcutaneous nodules — firm lumps under skin over bony prominences

Joint Deformities (Late Disease)

Ulnar Deviation
Fingers drift toward the ulnar (pinky) side
Swan-Neck Deformity
PIP hyperextension + DIP flexion
Boutonnière Deformity
PIP flexion + DIP hyperextension

Systemic / Extra-articular Symptoms

Constitutional
  • Fatigue, low-grade fever
  • Weight loss, anorexia
  • Morning stiffness throughout
Organ Involvement
  • Eyes — dry eyes (Sjögren's), scleritis
  • Lungs — ILD, pleural effusion
  • Heart — pericarditis
  • Vessels — vasculitis

Treatment — Stepwise Approach

Step 1 — Symptom Relief

  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) — reduce pain and swelling; GI bleeding risk; take with food
  • Corticosteroids — short-term bridging during flares; avoid long-term due to side effects
  • 🌡️ Warm compresses/showers for morning stiffness; cold packs for acute inflammation

Step 2 — Disease-Modifying (DMARDs)

DMARDs — Slow or Halt Disease Progression
DrugMonitoring RequiredKey Side Effects
Methotrexate (first-line)CBC, LFTs monthly → then q3 monthsHepatotoxicity, stomatitis, bone marrow suppression, teratogenic
HydroxychloroquineAnnual eye examsRetinal toxicity (rare but serious)
SulfasalazineCBC, LFTsGI upset, hepatotoxicity
LeflunomideLFTs, BPHepatotoxicity, hypertension, teratogenic

Step 3 — Biologics (if DMARDs Fail)

  • TNF inhibitors — etanercept, adalimumab, infliximab
  • IL-6 inhibitors — tocilizumab
  • B-cell depletion — rituximab

All biologics → significant immunosuppression → high infection risk → TB screening required before starting!

🚨
Methotrexate Monitoring — Critical NCLEX Points

Watch for: hepatotoxicity (↑ LFTs), stomatitis (mouth sores), bone marrow suppression (↓ WBC, RBC, platelets)
Supplement with folic acid to reduce side effects.
Teratogenic — must use reliable contraception; do NOT use in pregnancy.

Nursing Interventions

  • 🌡️ Heat therapy for stiffness (warm shower/bath on waking) · Cold therapy for acute swelling
  • 😴 Rest during flares — balanced with low-impact exercise (swimming, walking) during remission
  • 🧰 Assistive devices — button hooks, jar openers, raised toilet seats, grab bars (preserve joint function)
  • 💊 Medication education — compliance is critical; explain DMARD monitoring schedules
  • 🦠 Infection monitoring — DMARDs/biologics suppress immunity; teach infection signs
  • 🤲 Protect joints — avoid activities that stress small joints; use large joints for heavy tasks
  • 💪 ROM exercises — daily gentle range of motion; PT/OT referral
  • 🧠 Psychosocial — chronic pain impacts mood and function; assess depression/anxiety

Complications

Disease Complications
  • Joint deformity and contractures
  • Cervical spine instability (atlantoaxial subluxation) → spinal cord injury risk
  • Vasculitis
  • ILD → respiratory failure
Treatment Complications
  • Methotrexate: hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression
  • Steroids: hyperglycemia, osteoporosis, cushing-like features
  • Biologics: serious infections (TB, fungal)
