SLE (Lupus) Medications

Hydroxychloroquine
Antimalarial
Long-term flare prevention; used in ALL lupus patients. Takes months to show full effect.
Side effects: Retinal toxicity (rare but irreversible if missed)
Nursing: Annual ophthalmologic exams required. Smoking reduces effectiveness.
Prednisone
Corticosteroid
Rapid control of severe inflammation and flares. Use short-term.
Side effects: Infection risk, hyperglycemia, osteoporosis, adrenal suppression
Nursing: Never stop abruptly — taper slowly. Monitor blood glucose.
NSAIDs
(Ibuprofen, Naproxen)
Anti-inflammatory
Mild inflammation, joint pain, fever management.
Side effects: GI bleeding, ulcers, kidney impairment
Nursing: Always take with food or milk. Monitor renal function in lupus nephritis.
Azathioprine / Mycophenolate
Immunosuppressant
Severe disease, lupus nephritis; steroid-sparing agents.
Side effects: Significant infection risk; bone marrow suppression
Nursing: Avoid live vaccines. Monitor CBC and watch for infection signs.
Belimumab
Biologic (B-cell inhibitor)
Targets B-lymphocyte stimulator (BLyS) → reduces antibodies and inflammation.
Side effects: Depression risk, injection site reactions; monitor for serious infections
Nursing: Assess for depression and suicidal ideation. Takes up to 6 months for full effect.

Rheumatoid Arthritis Medications

Methotrexate
DMARD (first-line)
Slows/halts disease progression; most widely used DMARD for RA.
Side effects: Hepatotoxicity (liver damage), stomatitis (mouth sores), bone marrow suppression, teratogenic
Nursing: Monitor LFTs + CBC monthly then q3 months. Supplement with folic acid. Contraindicated in pregnancy — reliable contraception required.
TNF Inhibitors
(Etanercept, Adalimumab)
Biologic DMARD
Used when DMARDs fail. Suppress immune response by blocking TNF-alpha.
Side effects: Serious infection risk, reactivation of TB, lymphoma risk
Nursing: TB screening (PPD/IGRA) REQUIRED before starting. No live vaccines.

Myasthenia Gravis Medications

Pyridostigmine (Mestinon)
Cholinesterase inhibitor
Increases ACh at neuromuscular junction → improves muscle strength. Drug of choice for MG.
Side effects: GI cramps, diarrhea, excessive secretions (SLUDGE if too much)
Nursing: Give EXACTLY on time — never delay. Schedule 30–45 min before meals to aid swallowing. Atropine is the antidote for overdose (cholinergic crisis).
IVIG / Plasmapheresis
Immunotherapy
Short-term treatment for myasthenic crisis or severe exacerbation. IVIG blocks harmful antibodies; plasmapheresis removes them.
Side effects: IVIG — headache, chills, rare kidney injury. Plasmapheresis — BP drops, bleeding, infection risk
Nursing: Monitor vital signs during infusion. Check access site for plasmapheresis.

Guillain-Barré Syndrome Medications

IVIG
IV Immunoglobulin (first-line)
Blocks autoantibodies that are attacking myelin; shortens duration of paralysis.
Side effects: Headache, fever, chills, rare renal injury
Nursing: Monitor VS during infusion. Adequate hydration before infusion.
Plasmapheresis
Blood filtration
Removes antibodies from blood. Equally effective as IVIG.
Side effects: Hypotension, bleeding, infection, electrolyte imbalance
Nursing: Monitor BP closely; check access site; hypocalcemia risk.
🚨
Corticosteroids — NOT Used for GBS

Unlike most autoimmune conditions, steroids are NOT effective in GBS and may worsen outcomes. Use only IVIG or plasmapheresis.

Thyroid Disorder Medications

Methimazole / PTU
Antithyroid (Graves')
Block thyroid hormone synthesis. PTU also blocks peripheral conversion of T4→T3 (used in thyroid storm and pregnancy).
Side effects: Hepatotoxicity (esp. PTU), agranulocytosis (↓ WBCs — rare but dangerous)
Nursing: Monitor LFTs, CBC. Teach to report sore throat/fever immediately (agranulocytosis sign).
Levothyroxine
Thyroid hormone replacement (Hashimoto's)
Synthetic T4 replacement for hypothyroidism. Lifelong therapy.
Side effects: Tachycardia, insomnia, palpitations if overdosed
Nursing: Take on empty stomach in AM. Check TSH q6–12 weeks after dose changes. Signs of overdose = hyperthyroid symptoms.

Universal Nursing Rules for Immunosuppressants

  • 🚫 No live vaccines — MMR, varicella, FluMist, shingles, rotavirus — all contraindicated
  • 🦠 Infection vigilance — even a mild fever needs evaluation; opportunistic infections are life-threatening
  • 🥗 No raw foods for severely immunosuppressed patients (raw meat, unwashed produce)
  • 🌱 No gardening / cat litter — Aspergillus (soil) and Toxoplasma (cat feces) risk
  • 💊 Medication adherence — never stop abruptly (especially steroids — adrenal crisis)
  • 📋 Regular lab monitoring — CBC, LFTs, renal function per drug protocol
