Autoimmune Disorders · NCLEX Overview

Autoimmune Diseases
Comprehensive NCLEX Overview

Pathophysiology · assessment · interventions · complications — all high-yield autoimmune conditions

General Concepts

  • The immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own healthy cells (loss of self-tolerance)
  • Multifactorial causes — genetics, infections, environmental triggers
  • Usually chronic and progressive — worsens over time
  • Treatment: immunosuppressants + anti-inflammatory medications
  • Chronic inflammation → permanent organ damage over time

Common Autoimmune Diseases — NCLEX Overview

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
Immune attacks synovial joints → inflammation → joint deformity
  • Symmetric joint pain, swelling
  • Morning stiffness >1 hour
  • Subcutaneous nodules, fatigue, fever
Tx: DMARDs (methotrexate) · NSAIDs · steroids (short-term) · biologics
Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)
Immune attacks multiple organs via immune complexes
  • Butterfly (malar) rash, photosensitivity
  • Nephritis, pericarditis, pleuritis
  • Thrombocytopenia, CNS involvement
Tx: Hydroxychloroquine · corticosteroids · NSAIDs · immunosuppressants
Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
Immune attacks myelin sheath of CNS nerves → nerve damage
  • Muscle weakness, difficulty walking
  • Diplopia, nystagmus, paresthesia
  • Bladder dysfunction (urgency/retention)
Tx: Corticosteroids (acute) · immunomodulators · physical therapy
Myasthenia Gravis (MG)
Antibodies attack ACh receptors at neuromuscular junction
  • Ptosis, diplopia, dysphagia
  • Weakness worse with activity, better with rest
  • Myasthenic crisis → respiratory failure
Tx: Pyridostigmine · corticosteroids · plasmapheresis/IVIG
Graves' Disease (Hyperthyroidism)
Immune overstimulates TSH receptors → excess thyroid hormone
  • Exophthalmos (bulging eyes)
  • Tachycardia, hypertension, heat intolerance
  • Weight loss, goiter
Tx: Methimazole/PTU · beta-blockers · radioactive iodine therapy
Hashimoto's Thyroiditis (Hypothyroidism)
Immune destroys thyroid gland → low thyroid hormone
  • Fatigue, bradycardia, cold intolerance
  • Weight gain, constipation
  • Dry skin, hair thinning
Tx: Levothyroxine (lifelong)
Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM)
Immune destroys pancreatic beta cells → insulin deficiency
  • Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)
  • Weight loss, fruity breath
Tx: Insulin therapy (lifelong) · blood glucose monitoring
Celiac Disease
Immune reacts to gluten → damages small intestine villi
  • Diarrhea, bloating, steatorrhea
  • Malabsorption, anemia, weight loss
Tx: Strict gluten-free diet — no medication

NCLEX Priorities — All Autoimmune Diseases

  • 🏥 Patient education — medication compliance, avoid triggers (stress, infections, sun)
  • 🦠 Prevent infections — immunosuppressants → ↑ infection risk; teach hand hygiene; NO live vaccines
  • 😴 Manage fatigue — rest periods between activities; energy conservation
  • 📊 Monitor complications — system-specific monitoring (see table above)
  • 💊 Medication safety — watch for steroid side effects, DMARD toxicity (bone marrow, liver)
  • 🧠 Psychosocial support — chronic illness = depression, anxiety, body image concerns
🚨
No Live Vaccines — Critical Rule

Immunosuppressed patients (on steroids, DMARDs, biologics) must NOT receive live attenuated vaccines:
MMR · Varicella · Nasal flu (FluMist) · Shingles (Zostavax) · Yellow fever

💡
Inactivated vaccines ARE safe

Flu shot (injected), pneumococcal, COVID, Tdap, hepatitis B — recommended for immunosuppressed patients.
