Leopold Maneuvers

Four systematic abdominal palpation steps in the 3rd trimester (36+ weeks) to determine fetal lie, presentation, position, and engagement.
Mnemonic: "FPPP" — Feel Position Presentation and Part
Mnemonic: "Feel My Lovely Baby" — Fundus (1st), Midsection (2nd), Lower segment (3rd), Bottom (4th)

📋 Before you begin: Ask patient to empty bladder first. Supine with knees slightly flexed. Use warm hands.
1
Fundal Grip

Palpate UPPER abdomen. Identify what's in the fundus (top of uterus).

  • Head = round, firm, moves independently
  • Buttocks = softer, more irregular
  • Determines: Fetal presentation (cephalic vs breech)
2
Lateral Grip

Hands on BOTH SIDES of abdomen.

  • One hand feels smooth, firm back
  • Other hand detects small parts (hands, feet)
  • Determines: Fetal position (e.g., left occiput anterior)
3
Pawlik's Grip

Grasp LOWER abdomen just above pubic symphysis.

  • Determine if presenting part is engaged
  • Confirms presentation (head or buttocks)
  • Determines: Engagement
4
Pelvic Grip

Face mother's feet, slide hands downward to find cephalic prominence.

  • Same side as back → head extended (not ideal)
  • Opposite side from back → head flexed (ideal)
  • Determines: Fetal descent & attitude

Key Terms

TermMeaning
LieLongitudinal (parallel) vs Transverse (sideways)
PresentationPart entering pelvis first (cephalic = head, breech = buttocks)
PositionDirection baby faces (e.g., LOA = Left Occiput Anterior)
EngagementPresenting part reaches ischial spines = station 0
AttitudeDegree of flexion of fetal head (flexed = normal/ideal)
