Weeks 1–28 (1st and early 2nd trimester)Every 4 weeks
Weeks 28–36 (late 2nd / early 3rd trimester)Every 2 weeks
Weeks 36–40 (3rd trimester to delivery)Every week

Memory Trick

4 weeks → 2 weeks → 1 week = "Monthly, Biweekly, Weekly"

Trimester-by-Trimester Overview

🩵 1st Trimester (Weeks 1–13)

  • Confirm pregnancy (hCG, TVUS)
  • Calculate EDD (Naegele's Rule)
  • Initial labs (see below)
  • Pap smear, STI screen
  • Start prenatal vitamins + folic acid
  • Genetic screening: NIPT, nuchal translucency (11–14 wks)
  • Counseling: avoid alcohol, smoking, cat litter
  • Discomforts: nausea, fatigue, breast tenderness, frequency

💛 2nd Trimester (Weeks 14–27)

  • Fundal height measurement begins
  • Iron supplementation if anemic
  • Discomforts: round ligament pain, backache, heartburn, constipation
  • Most women feel best in 2nd trimester

🩷 3rd Trimester (Weeks 28–40)

  • GBS screening (35–37 weeks)
  • Repeat CBC, antibody screen if Rh-negative
  • RhoGAM if Rh-negative (28 weeks + after delivery)
  • Kick counts start (28 weeks)
  • NST, BPP, AFI as indicated
  • Cervical checks near term
  • Discomforts: edema, Braxton Hicks, dyspnea, insomnia, urinary frequency
  • Birth plan discussions

Fundal Height — McDonald's Rule

McDonald's Rule: Fundal height in centimeters ≈ gestational age in weeks (±2 cm, from 18–32 weeks)

Gestational AgeFundal Height Location
12 weeksJust above symphysis pubis
16 weeksHalfway between symphysis and umbilicus
20 weeksAt the umbilicus (20 cm)
36 weeksNear the xiphoid process (36 cm)
40 weeks (lightening)Drops slightly as baby engages — returns toward 36 cm level

Clinical Notes

  • Measure with tape from symphysis pubis to top of fundus (in cm)
  • Fundal height smaller than expected → FGR, oligohydramnios, wrong dates
  • Fundal height larger than expected → macrosomia, multiple gestation, polyhydramnios

Antepartum Fetal Monitoring Tests

Fetal Kick Counts (Cardiff Method)

Started at 28 weeks. Mother counts fetal movements at the same time each day after a meal (when baby most active).

MethodGoalReport If
Cardiff "Count-to-Ten"10 movements in 2 hoursFewer than 10 in 2 hours → call provider
Alternative method3 movements in 30 minutesNo 3 movements → call provider immediately

Non-Stress Test (NST)

ResultDefinitionAction
Reactive (Normal)≥2 accelerations of ≥15 bpm lasting ≥15 seconds in 20 minutesReassuring — continue monitoring per schedule
Nonreactive (Abnormal)Fails to meet acceleration criteria in 40 minutesFurther evaluation → BPP, CST, or delivery if indicated

⚠️ Danger Signs — Report to Provider Immediately

  • Vaginal bleeding (any amount after 1st trimester)
  • Severe headache or visual changes
  • Sudden swelling of face/hands (preeclampsia)
  • Abdominal pain or cramping
  • Decreased or absent fetal movement
  • Fever >100.4°F (38°C)
  • Burning with urination (UTI)
  • Gush or trickle of fluid from vagina (PROM)

NCLEX Priority Summary

Key Facts

  • Folic acid: 400–800 mcg/day → started BEFORE conception → prevents neural tube defects
  • GBS positive: IV penicillin in labor (NOT antepartum treatment — colonization returns)
  • Rubella non-immune: Vaccinate postpartum ONLY (live vaccine)
  • RhoGAM: Give at 28 weeks + within 72 hours after any sensitizing event (bleeding, delivery, amniocentesis)
  • ASB in pregnancy: Always treat — risk of ascending UTI/pyelonephritis and preterm labor
  • NST reactive = GOOD (normal). NST nonreactive = needs further evaluation.
  • Fundal height: cm ≈ weeks (18–32 weeks). At 20 weeks = at umbilicus.
  • Kick counts: 10 kicks in 2 hours after meal; report if fewer than 10
  • Left lateral position: Best for uteroplacental perfusion; avoids aortocaval compression
  • Hepatitis B+ mother: Newborn gets vaccine + HBIG within 12 hours of birth
