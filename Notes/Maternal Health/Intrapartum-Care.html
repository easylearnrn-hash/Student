Intrapartum Care

Maternal Health · Stages of labor, labor complications, pain relief

Stages of Labor

Stage 1 — Labor Begins

Cervix dilates 0–10 cm; contractions begin.

Nursing: Monitor FHR, contractions, pain relief

Stage 2 — Pushing & Birth

Mom pushes, baby is born!

Nursing: Guide pushing, monitor fetal distress, assist delivery

Stage 3 — Placenta Delivery

Placenta expelled (usually within 30 minutes)

Nursing: Monitor for bleeding, fundal massage

Stage 4 — Recovery (1–2 hrs)

First hours after birth

Nursing: Watch for hemorrhage, vital signs, bonding

3 Phases of Stage 1 Labor

Latent Phase · 0–5 cm
  • Mild contractions 5–30 min apart, 30–45 sec
  • Mom: excited, talkative
Transition Phase · 8–10 cm
  • Very strong, 2–3 min apart, 45–90 sec
  • Mom: irritable, shaking, urge to push
  • Most intense phase

Labor Progress Assessment

AssessmentScaleNCLEX Note
Cervical Dilation0–10 cm10 cm = fully dilated = ready to push
Cervical Effacement0–100%Measures how thin the cervix is
Station−3 to +30 = engaged (head in pelvis); +3 = crowning

NCLEX Tip

"100% effaced, 10 cm dilated, +3 station" = Ready to push! | Contractions >5 in 10 minutes → STOP oxytocin (Pitocin)

Labor Complications

ComplicationKey ClueNursing Response
Cord Prolapse Umbilical cord comes out before baby → sudden fetal bradycardia Knee-chest position, push baby's head UP, emergency C-section!
Shoulder Dystocia Baby's shoulder stuck after head is born McRoberts maneuver (legs hyper-flexed), suprapubic pressure
Uterine Hyperstimulation Contractions >5 in 10 minutes, fetal distress STOP oxytocin, reposition, O₂, notify provider
Fetal Distress (Late Decels) FHR drops after contraction ends Left lateral position, O₂, stop oxytocin, IV fluids, prepare for delivery

Pain Relief in Labor

MethodKey NCLEX Points
Epidural (most common)Given at active labor (6–7 cm); watch for hypotension; preload with IV fluids; monitor bladder (urinary retention)
IV Opioids (Morphine, Fentanyl)Give early in labor; NOT close to delivery (neonatal respiratory depression)
Non-pharmacologicBreathing techniques, positioning, massage, hydrotherapy, birthing ball
General Anesthesia (C-section)Emergency only; risk of aspiration; NPO before; wedge under right hip (prevent supine hypotension)
