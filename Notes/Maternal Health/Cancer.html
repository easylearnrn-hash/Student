Gynecologic & Breast Cancers

Cervical Cancer
  • Caused by HPV types 16 & 18 (most common STI-related cancer)
  • Risk factors: Early sexual activity, multiple partners, smoking, weak immune system (HIV), no HPV vaccine
  • Screening: Pap smear (age 21, q3y); HPV co-test from age 30 (q5y)
  • Late signs: Postcoital bleeding, watery/bad-smelling vaginal discharge, pelvic pain
  • Tx: LEEP (removes abnormal tissue), surgery, chemo/radiation if advanced
  • NCLEX: Recommend HPV vaccine ages 9–26; teach regular Pap testing; encourage safe sex
Ovarian Cancer — "Silent Killer"
  • Symptoms vague → often unnoticed until late stage
  • Risk factors: Age >50, family history, BRCA1/BRCA2, early menarche/late menopause, nulliparity, fertility treatments
  • Symptoms: Bloating, pelvic/belly pain, feeling full quickly, urinary frequency, ascites (fluid in belly → tighter clothes)
  • Diagnosis: No routine screening; transvaginal ultrasound + CA-125 blood test
  • Tx: Surgery (removal of ovaries and uterus) + chemotherapy
  • NCLEX: Know the vague warning signs; recognize BRCA risk; watch for DVT/PE post-op
Endometrial (Uterine) Cancer
  • Most common gynecologic cancer
  • Risk: ↑ estrogen unopposed, obesity, PCOS, tamoxifen use, nulliparity, early menarche/late menopause
  • Key sign: Painless postmenopausal bleeding 🚨
  • Diagnosis: Transvaginal US (measure uterine lining thickness) + endometrial biopsy
  • Tx: Hysterectomy ± radiation/chemo
  • NCLEX: Always report postmenopausal bleeding; promote healthy weight; safe hormone use
Breast Cancer
  • Risk: BRCA1/2, female age >50, family history (early onset), estrogen use, alcohol, obesity, first pregnancy after 30 or nulliparity
  • Screening: BSE monthly (1 wk after period); Mammogram starting age 40–50 (q1–2y)
  • Symptoms: Painless lump (upper outer quadrant), nipple retraction/discharge, peau d'orange skin, dimpling
  • Tx: Lumpectomy or mastectomy; radiation, chemo, tamoxifen (hormonal therapy)
  • Post-op: NO BP/IV/labs on affected arm; elevate arm; watch for lymphedema; gentle ROM exercises
  • Tamoxifen → raises uterine cancer risk

🔑 NCLEX Cancer Strategy Recap

  • Postmenopausal bleeding = Think endometrial cancer
  • Pap smear = cervical cancer screening (NOT ovarian or uterine)
  • CA-125 + transvaginal US = ovarian cancer check
  • Tamoxifen = raises risk for uterine cancer
  • BRCA mutation = higher breast & ovarian cancer risk
  • Teach self-exams, screenings, and signs to report early
