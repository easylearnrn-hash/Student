Ovarian Cycle Phases

Follicular Phase · Day 1–13
  • FSH stimulates follicles to grow
  • Dominant Graafian follicle matures
  • Rising estrogen → positive feedback → LH surge
  • Granulosa + theca cells produce estrogen
Ovulation · Day 14
  • LH surge → follicle ruptures → egg released
  • Egg swept into fallopian tube by fimbriae
  • Fertilization site: ampulla of fallopian tube
  • Egg lives ~24 hours
Luteal Phase · Day 15–28
  • Ruptured follicle → corpus luteum
  • Corpus luteum secretes progesterone + estrogen
  • If no fertilization → corpus luteum dies → cycle restarts
  • If fertilization → hCG maintains corpus luteum

NCLEX Tips

  • Estrogen = Proliferative phase | Progesterone = Secretory phase
  • LH surge = Ovulation | Corpus luteum = Progesterone factory
  • Fertilization = ampulla of fallopian tube
  • Most fertile window: Day 9–16 | Sperm survives up to 5 days
  • Menstruation = drop in progesterone + estrogen
