Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO₄) — NCLEX Favorite!

What it doesPrevents/treats seizures in preeclampsia/eclampsia
Toxicity Signs↓ RR (<12/min), ↓ deep tendon reflexes, ↓ urine output, lethargy, flushing
AntidoteCalcium gluconate (keep at bedside!)
Nursing careMonitor hourly: DTRs, respiratory rate, I&O, fetal monitoring

HELLP Syndrome (Complication of Severe Preeclampsia)

H
Hemolysis
EL
Elevated Liver enzymes
LP
Low Platelets

Signs: RUQ pain, N/V, malaise, bleeding. Life-threatening → can cause liver rupture or stroke.

Nursing Priorities Summary

ActionRationale
Monitor BP closelyWatch for worsening HTN
Administer MgSO₄Prevent seizures
Watch for MgSO₄ toxicity signsPrevent respiratory arrest
Keep calcium gluconate at bedsideMgSO₄ antidote
Monitor I&O strictlyPrevent fluid overload; watch oliguria
Left lateral positionImproves blood flow to placenta
Fetal monitoringAssess fetal wellbeing
Prepare for deliveryONLY CURE for preeclampsia/eclampsia is delivery of baby & placenta

Eclampsia Seizure Response

  • Ensure airway safety
  • Turn to side (lateral position)
  • Administer MgSO₄ (if not already)
  • Prepare for emergency delivery
  • Notify provider immediately
