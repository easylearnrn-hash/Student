Intrapartum Care, Leopold Maneuvers & VEAL CHOP

Maternal Health · Stages of labor, FHR patterns, Leopold maneuvers, labor complications

Stages of Labor

Stage 1 — Labor Begins

Cervix dilates 0–10 cm; contractions begin.

Nursing: Monitor FHR, contractions, pain relief

Stage 2 — Pushing & Birth

Mom pushes, baby is born!

Nursing: Guide pushing, monitor fetal distress, assist delivery

Stage 3 — Placenta Delivery

Placenta expelled (usually within 30 minutes)

Nursing: Monitor for bleeding, fundal massage

Stage 4 — Recovery (1–2 hrs)

First hours after birth

Nursing: Watch for hemorrhage, vital signs, bonding

3 Phases of Stage 1 Labor

Latent Phase · 0–5 cm
  • Mild contractions 5–30 min apart, 30–45 sec
  • Mom: excited, talkative
Transition Phase · 8–10 cm
  • Very strong, 2–3 min apart, 45–90 sec
  • Mom: irritable, shaking, urge to push
  • Most intense phase

VEAL CHOP — FHR Pattern Interpretation

Mnemonic matching FHR decelerations with their causes. Normal FHR = 110–160 bpm.

VEAL (FHR Pattern)
CHOP (Cause + Action)
V = Variable Decelerations
Sudden V/U/W-shaped dips
C = Cord Compression 🟡
Action: Reposition, O₂, amnioinfusion
E = Early Decelerations
Gradual dip, mirrors contraction
H = Head Compression 🟢
Action: None — benign during labor
A = Accelerations
Brief rise in FHR
O = OK! Reassuring 🟢
Action: None — healthy baby sign
L = Late Decelerations
Gradual dip AFTER contraction ends
P = Placental Insufficiency 🔴
Action: Turn mom to side, STOP oxytocin, O₂, IV fluids, notify provider

Late Decels = Emergency!

Late decels = baby NOT getting oxygen → Fix FAST: Reposition (left side), Stop oxytocin, Give O₂ (8–10 L/mask), IV fluids, notify provider, prepare for delivery.

Leopold Maneuvers

Four systematic abdominal palpation steps in the 3rd trimester (36+ weeks) to determine fetal lie, presentation, position, and engagement.
Mnemonic: "FPPP" — Feel Position Presentation and Part
Mnemonic: "Feel My Lovely Baby" — Fundus (1st), Midsection (2nd), Lower segment (3rd), Bottom (4th)

📋 Before you begin: Ask patient to empty bladder first. Supine with knees slightly flexed. Use warm hands.
1
Fundal Grip

Palpate UPPER abdomen. Identify what's in the fundus (top of uterus).

  • Head = round, firm, moves independently
  • Buttocks = softer, more irregular
  • Determines: Fetal presentation (cephalic vs breech)
2
Lateral Grip

Hands on BOTH SIDES of abdomen.

  • One hand feels smooth, firm back
  • Other hand detects small parts (hands, feet)
  • Determines: Fetal position (e.g., left occiput anterior)
3
Pawlik's Grip

Grasp LOWER abdomen just above pubic symphysis.

  • Determine if presenting part is engaged
  • Confirms presentation (head or buttocks)
  • Determines: Engagement
4
Pelvic Grip

Face mother's feet, slide hands downward to find cephalic prominence.

  • Same side as back → head extended (not ideal)
  • Opposite side from back → head flexed (ideal)
  • Determines: Fetal descent & attitude

Key Terms

TermMeaning
LieLongitudinal (parallel) vs Transverse (sideways)
PresentationPart entering pelvis first (cephalic = head, breech = buttocks)
PositionDirection baby faces (e.g., LOA = Left Occiput Anterior)
EngagementPresenting part reaches ischial spines = station 0
AttitudeDegree of flexion of fetal head (flexed = normal/ideal)

Labor Complications

ComplicationKey ClueNursing Response
Cord Prolapse Umbilical cord comes out before baby → sudden fetal bradycardia Knee-chest position, push baby's head UP, emergency C-section!
Shoulder Dystocia Baby's shoulder stuck after head is born McRoberts maneuver (legs hyper-flexed), suprapubic pressure
Uterine Hyperstimulation Contractions >5 in 10 minutes, fetal distress STOP oxytocin, reposition, O₂, notify provider
Fetal Distress (Late Decels) FHR drops after contraction ends Left lateral position, O₂, stop oxytocin, IV fluids, prepare for delivery

Pain Relief in Labor

MethodKey NCLEX Points
Epidural (most common)Given at active labor (6–7 cm); watch for hypotension; preload with IV fluids; monitor bladder (urinary retention)
IV Opioids (Morphine, Fentanyl)Give early in labor; NOT close to delivery (neonatal respiratory depression)
Non-pharmacologicBreathing techniques, positioning, massage, hydrotherapy, birthing ball
General Anesthesia (C-section)Emergency only; risk of aspiration; NPO before; wedge under right hip (prevent supine hypotension)
