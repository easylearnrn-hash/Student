Menstrual Disorders

DisorderDefinitionKey FeaturesTreatment
Dysmenorrhea
Primary / Secondary		 Painful menstruation Primary: no underlying cause (prostaglandins cause cramping). Secondary: caused by endometriosis, fibroids, PID NSAIDs (first-line), hormonal contraceptives, heat therapy
Amenorrhea
Primary / Secondary		 Absence of menstruation Primary: no period by age 15 with secondary sex characteristics. Secondary: cessation >3 months in previously menstruating woman Treat underlying cause; hormonal therapy if indicated
Menorrhagia Abnormally heavy/prolonged periods >80 mL blood loss; changing pad/tampon hourly for hours; fatigue, anemia NSAIDs, hormonal therapy, tranexamic acid; surgery if severe
Metrorrhagia Irregular bleeding between periods Spotting or bleeding at unpredictable times Identify cause (polyps, infection, cancer); hormonal therapy
Oligomenorrhea Infrequent periods (>35 days between cycles) Common in PCOS, thyroid disorders, extreme athletes Treat underlying cause

Memory Tips

  • menorrhagia = Massive flow (too heavy)
  • metrorrhagia = Midcycle bleeding (irregular timing)
  • amenorrhea = Absence (no period)
  • oligomenorrhea = Only occasionally (rare/infrequent)

PMS & PMDD

PMS (Premenstrual Syndrome)PMDD (Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder)
TimingLuteal phase (1–2 weeks before period)Same — luteal phase
Physical SxBloating, breast tenderness, fatigue, headacheSame physical symptoms
Mood SxIrritability, mood swings (mild–moderate)Severe depression, anxiety, hopelessness, anger, suicidal ideation
Functional ImpactMild disruptionSignificant impairment in daily life/relationships
TreatmentExercise, diet, calcium, NSAIDs, OCPSSRIs (first-line), OCPs, cognitive behavioral therapy
NCLEX Alert: If a patient reports severe depression or mood changes that improve within days of menstruation starting, suspect PMDD. SSRIs are first-line treatment — they can be given continuously or only during the luteal phase.

Endometriosis

Endometrial tissue grows outside the uterus (ovaries, fallopian tubes, peritoneum). Responds to hormonal cycle → bleeds with no exit → inflammation, scarring, adhesions.

FeatureDetails
Classic TriadDysmenorrhea + Dyspareunia (pain during sex) + Infertility
Additional SxPelvic pain throughout cycle, dyschezia (painful defecation), rectal bleeding, fatigue
DiagnosisLaparoscopy (gold standard); ultrasound may show endometriomas ("chocolate cysts")
Medical TxNSAIDs, combined OCP, progestins, GnRH agonists (Lupron — induces temporary menopause), danazol
Surgical TxLaparoscopic excision/ablation; hysterectomy + oophorectomy (definitive cure)
FertilityLeading cause of infertility in women; surgery improves fertility outcomes

Key NCLEX Points

  • Endometriosis ≠ endometritis (endometritis = uterine infection, often postpartum)
  • GnRH agonists → medical menopause → may cause hot flashes, osteoporosis (add-back therapy with estrogen)
  • Chocolate cysts = endometriomas on the ovaries (dark blood-filled cysts)

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

Hormonal disorder — excess androgens + insulin resistance → irregular ovulation → multiple small follicular cysts on ovaries.

FeatureDetails
Diagnostic Criteria
(Rotterdam — 2 of 3)		1. Irregular ovulation/anovulation
2. Clinical or biochemical hyperandrogenism
3. Polycystic ovaries on ultrasound
SymptomsOligomenorrhea/amenorrhea, hirsutism (excess hair), acne, obesity, acanthosis nigricans, infertility
Labs↑ LH:FSH ratio (>2:1), ↑ androgens (testosterone), ↑ insulin, ↑ fasting glucose
ComplicationsType 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, endometrial cancer (from unopposed estrogen), cardiovascular disease, infertility
TreatmentWeight loss (even 5% improves symptoms), OCP (regulate cycle), metformin (insulin resistance), clomiphene (fertility), spironolactone (hirsutism)

NCLEX High-Yield

  • PCOS = most common cause of anovulatory infertility
  • LH:FSH ratio >2:1 or >3:1 is classic for PCOS
  • Metformin is NOT just for diabetics — it's first-line for PCOS with insulin resistance
  • Endometrial cancer risk ↑ because anovulation → no progesterone → unopposed estrogen builds up endometrium

Perimenopause & Menopause

Details
PerimenopauseTransition period (2–10 years before menopause); irregular cycles, fluctuating hormones, vasomotor symptoms begin
Menopause12 consecutive months without a period; average age 51; diagnosed retrospectively
SymptomsHot flashes, night sweats, vaginal atrophy (dryness, dyspareunia), mood changes, sleep disruption, decreased libido, osteoporosis risk
Labs↑ FSH (>40 IU/L is diagnostic), ↑ LH, ↓ estrogen, ↓ AMH
TreatmentHRT (hormone replacement therapy — estrogen ± progesterone), vaginal estrogen cream, SSRIs for hot flashes (if HRT contraindicated), calcium + Vitamin D for bone health
HRT ContraindicationsBreast/uterine cancer history, unexplained vaginal bleeding, DVT/PE history, liver disease, stroke history

Key Points

  • If uterus is intact → always add progesterone to estrogen HRT (prevents endometrial cancer)
  • After hysterectomy → estrogen-only HRT is safe
  • ↑ FSH = the key lab for confirming menopause (hypothalamus/pituitary trying to stimulate non-responsive ovaries)
  • Teach: weight-bearing exercise + calcium (1200 mg/day) + Vit D for osteoporosis prevention
