Postpartum Care

Maternal Health · Postpartum recovery, lochia, PPH, complications, perineal care, breastfeeding, BUBBLE-EE

Postpartum Recovery Stages

Puerperium = time from birth until body returns to pre-pregnancy state (~6 weeks). Highest risk for complications = First 24 hours!

StageTimeframeKey Focus
Immediate (Stage 4 Labor)First 1–2 hours after birthMonitor for hemorrhage, vital signs, fundus check, bonding
Early PostpartumFirst 24 hoursPain management, uterine involution, lochia assessment
Late Postpartum2 days – 6 weeksEmotional adjustment, breastfeeding, return of menstruation
Extended Postpartum6 weeks – 6 monthsFull physical & emotional recovery

Uterine Involution

  • Immediately after birth: Fundus at level of umbilicus (belly button)
  • Descends 1 cm/day (1 finger width per day)
  • By Day 10: Fundus should no longer be felt
  • "Boggy" (soft) uterus = Uterine atony → hemorrhage risk → Perform fundal massage!

Lochia — Postpartum Discharge

Lochia Rubra · Day 1–3

Bright red, small clots

Normal. Large clots = Report!

Lochia Serosa · Day 4–10

Pink/brown, watery

Normal healing. Heavy bleeding = Report!

Lochia Alba · Day 10 – 6 weeks

Yellowish-white, no odor

Foul smell = Infection (Endometritis)

🚨 Sudden return to bright red bleeding after lochia has lightened = Possible hemorrhage!

Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)

PPH = Blood loss >500 mL (vaginal) or >1000 mL (C-section).

The 4 T's — Causes of PPH

TCauseIntervention
ToneUterine atony (soft, boggy uterus) — most commonFundal massage → oxytocin (Pitocin) → IV fluids
TissueRetained placentaManual removal, D&C (Dilation & Curettage)
TraumaLacerations, uterine ruptureSurgical repair
ThrombinClotting disorder (DIC)Blood transfusion, treat cause

First action for PPH

Fundal massage first! If ineffective → administer oxytocin (Pitocin). Methylergonovine (Methergine) used for persistent PPH — avoid in preeclampsia (causes hypertension).

Postpartum Perineal Care

InterventionTimingPurpose
Ice packsFirst 24 hoursReduces swelling and inflammation

Peri-bottle (warm water spray)After each void/BMCleanses perineum, prevents infection

NCLEX Key Points

  • No tampons or intercourse for 6 weeks — risk of infection!
  • Ice first 24 hrs → switch to sitz baths after 24 hrs

Postpartum Breast Care & Breastfeeding Education

SituationCare / Teaching
Breastfeeding Moms
  • Nurse every 2–3 hours
  • Use lanolin cream for sore nipples
  • Apply warm compress before feeding to help let-down
  • Feed within 1st hour of life
  • Signs of good latch: wide mouth, nipple + areola in mouth, no pain
  • Output: ≥6 wet diapers/day by day 5
Formula-Feeding Moms
  • Wear a tight/supportive bra
  • Avoid nipple stimulation
  • Apply cold compresses for engorgement relief
  • Use cabbage leaves in bra for engorgement
🚨 Engorgement peaks at 3–5 days postpartum — cold compresses help relieve pain!

Mastitis vs. Engorgement

Mastitis: Painful, hard, red breast + fever → antibiotics + warm compress + CONTINUE breastfeeding!
Engorgement: Bilateral fullness, no fever → feed frequently, cold compress between feeds.

BUBBLE-EE — Postpartum Nursing Assessment

A systematic head-to-toe postpartum assessment framework:

LetterAssessmentWhat to Monitor
BBreastsEngorgement, nipple pain, signs of mastitis
UUterusFundal height, firmness, position (should be midline & firm)
BBladderUrinary retention, voiding pattern (empty bladder before assessment)
BBowelsConstipation, hemorrhoids, passage of first stool
LLochiaAmount, color, odor — Abnormal: heavy bleeding, foul smell
EEpisiotomy / PerineumREEDA: Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation
EExtremitiesDVT signs: pain, swelling, redness, warmth in legs
EEmotionsBaby blues vs. PPD vs. psychosis — assess support system

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Fundus should be firm, midline, and decreasing in height each day
  • A full bladder can displace the fundus to one side — empty bladder first!
  • No tampons or sex for 6 weeks — infection risk
  • Folic acid (400 mcg/day) prevents neural tube defects in next pregnancy
