Follicle Development — Detailed

Stimulated by GnRH (hypothalamus) → FSH + LH (anterior pituitary):

StageDescription
Primordial folliclesTiny sacs holding immature eggs — 10–20 begin to mature each cycle
Primary follicleFollicle develops; surrounded by granulosa cells (produce estrogen) and theca cells (support estrogen production)
Secondary follicleContinues to grow; granulosa & theca cells produce increasing estrogen
Graafian follicleDominant mature follicle; estrogen peaks → triggers LH surge → ovulation
Zona PellucidaProtective shell around egg that allows only 1 sperm to enter — "locks" (polyspermy block) after fertilization
Corpus Luteum"Yellow body" made of cholesterol; formed from ruptured follicle → secretes progesterone + some estrogen

Quick Memory Tips

  • FSH = Follicle Starter Hormone
  • LH = Let's Hatch (triggers ovulation)
  • Estrogen = Endometrium rebuilds (proliferative phase)
  • Progesterone = Pregnancy hormone (secretory phase)
  • Zona Pellucida = Zipper closed after 1 sperm gets in
  • Granulosa cells = estrogen factory inside the follicle
  • Theca cells = support granulosa cells in producing estrogen

Fertilization & Implantation Steps

  • 1
    Ovulation & Egg Release

    LH surge → Graafian follicle ruptures → egg released → fimbriae sweep egg into fallopian tube (infundibulum). Egg lives ~24 hours.

  • 2
    Fertilization (Day 14)

    Sperm travels: Vagina → Cervix → Uterus → Fallopian Tube. One sperm penetrates zona pellucida → forms zygote. Only 1 out of 200–500 million sperm makes it. Zona pellucida locks after fertilization.

  • 3
    Cell Division & Travel (Day 1–5)

    Zygote → 2-cell → 4-cell → 8-cell → Morula (Day 3–4, solid ball of 16 cells) → Blastocyst (Day 5–6, hollow structure)

  • 4
    Blastocyst Structure

    Embryoblast (inner cell mass) → becomes the baby
    Trophoblast (outer layer) → becomes the placenta

  • 5
    Implantation (Day 6–10)

    Blastocyst embeds into uterine lining → trophoblast releases hCG → hCG tells corpus luteum to keep producing progesterone → maintains thick, blood-rich uterine lining.

NCLEX Key Distinctions

  • Presumptive = felt by the woman (subjective)
  • Probable = observed by the examiner (objective)
  • Positive = direct, definitive confirmation
  • Only positive signs confirm pregnancy — fetal heartbeat, ultrasound, provider feeling fetal movement
