Structural / Reproductive Organ Disorders

🔥 Endometriosis

Endometrial tissue grows OUTSIDE the uterus (ovaries, fallopian tubes, pelvic wall, bowel, bladder)

  • Cyclic pelvic pain (before/during periods)
  • Dysmenorrhea, dyspareunia (painful intercourse)
  • Infertility, bowel/bladder symptoms
  • Diagnosis: Laparoscopy (definitive)
  • Tx: NSAIDs, OCPs, GnRH agonists, laparoscopic surgery, hysterectomy (if severe/done with childbearing)
  • NCLEX: estrogen-dependent; GnRH → hot flashes, ↓ bone density
🩸 Uterine Fibroids (Leiomyomas)

Benign smooth muscle tumors of uterus; common in women 30–50

  • Heavy/prolonged periods (menorrhagia)
  • Pelvic pain/pressure, constipation, urinary frequency
  • Enlarged, irregular uterus
  • Types: Submucosal (most bleeding), Intramural, Subserosal
  • Diagnosis: Ultrasound (TVUS), MRI for surgical planning
  • Tx: OCPs, GnRH agonists (shrink pre-surgery), UAE, myomectomy, hysterectomy
  • NCLEX: Monitor for anemia; educate on NON-cancerous nature
⚠️ Ovarian Cysts

Fluid-filled sacs on ovary; usually from failed/incomplete ovulation

  • Most common: Functional cysts (follicular or corpus luteum)
  • Often asymptomatic; large → pelvic pain, bloating, pressure
  • Rupture: sudden sharp pain, light bleeding
  • Torsion: severe pain, N/V — EMERGENCY 🚨
  • Diagnosis: Ultrasound, hCG to rule out pregnancy
  • Tx: Observation (small/simple), NSAIDs, OCPs, cystectomy, emergency surgery for torsion/rupture
🦠 Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

Ascending infection from untreated STIs (chlamydia/gonorrhea) → uterus, tubes, ovaries

  • Fever, chills, lower abdominal pain
  • Purulent cervical discharge
  • Cervical motion tenderness (CMT) — key clinical sign
  • Dyspareunia, N/V in severe cases
  • Complications: Infertility, ectopic pregnancy, tubo-ovarian abscess
  • Tx: Ceftriaxone + doxycycline ± metronidazole; treat partners; pelvic rest
