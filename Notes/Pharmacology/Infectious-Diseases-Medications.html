PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Truvada (Tenofovir + Emtricitabine)
  • For HIV-negative high-risk individuals
  • Daily oral; must be HIV-negative before starting

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • Start within 72 hours of exposure
  • 28-day course
  • 3-drug regimen
  • Occupational and non-occupational exposures

Meningitis Treatment

  • Bacterial: Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin — start IMMEDIATELY (don't wait for LP results)
  • Add Ampicillin if Listeria suspected (immunocompromised, elderly, pregnant)
  • Dexamethasone — before or with first antibiotic dose → reduces cerebral edema
  • Viral (HSV): Acyclovir IV
  • Droplet precautions until 24 hrs of antibiotics
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Bacterial meningitis = IMMEDIATE antibiotics + Dexamethasone. Droplet precautions first 24 hrs. LP should NOT delay treatment.

NCLEX Must-Know

  • HIV PEP: within 72 hours; 28-day course; 3-drug regimen (antiretroviral/antiviral therapy)
  • Bacterial meningitis: immediate Ceftriaxone (cephalosporin) + Vancomycin + Dexamethasone
  • MRSA: Vancomycin IV; Contact precautions
  • C. diff (C. difficile / Clostridioides difficile): Fidaxomicin/Metronidazole; Contact precautions; no alcohol with Metronidazole
  • Gonorrhea: dual therapy with Ceftriaxone (cephalosporin) + Azithromycin
  • Syphilis: Penicillin G; Herxheimer reaction expected day 1
  • Fluoroquinolones (ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin): avoid in children/pregnancy; risk tendon rupture
  • Antifungals (fluconazole, amphotericin B): monitor liver function; amphotericin = nephrotoxic
