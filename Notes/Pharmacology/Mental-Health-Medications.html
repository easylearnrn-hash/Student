Antidepressants

🧠
NCLEX KEY:

SSRIs take 4–6 weeks for full effect. Serotonin syndrome = agitation, hyperthermia, tremors, diarrhea — if two serotonergic drugs combined. MAOIs + tyramine = hypertensive crisis.

Antianxiety Medications

Benzodiazepines (ends in -pam/-lam)

Alprazolam, Lorazepam, Diazepam, Clonazepam

  • ↑ GABA → calming effect
  • SE: drowsiness, fall risk, respiratory depression
  • Dependency potential
  • NEVER stop abruptly (seizure risk)
  • Antidote: Flumazenil

Buspirone

  • Stimulates serotonin receptors
  • No sedation, no dependency
  • Takes 2–4 weeks to work
  • SE: dizziness, nausea, headache
  • Best choice for chronic anxiety

Antipsychotics

First-Generation (Typical)

Haloperidol, Chlorpromazine, Fluphenazine

  • Blocks dopamine (D2)
  • Treats positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
  • SE: EPS (extrapyramidal symptoms)
  • Tardive dyskinesia (lip-smacking, writhing)
  • NMS (neuroleptic malignant syndrome)

Second-Generation (Atypical)

Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine, Clozapine, Aripiprazole

  • Blocks dopamine + serotonin
  • Treats positive AND negative symptoms
  • SE: weight gain, metabolic syndrome, sedation
  • Clozapine: weekly CBC for agranulocytosis (↓ WBCs)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

EPS = acute dystonia, akathisia, parkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia. Treat with Benztropine (anticholinergic). Clozapine = agranulocytosis → weekly WBC monitoring required.

Mood Stabilizers

Lithium

  • Treats bipolar disorder (manic episodes)
  • Therapeutic range: 0.6–1.2 mEq/L
  • Narrow therapeutic index — requires close monitoring
  • Toxicity signs: tremors, confusion, N/V, ataxia, seizures
  • Stay well hydrated; Na⁺ depletion increases Lithium levels → toxicity

Anticonvulsants (also mood stabilizers):

  • Valproic acid — monitor LFTs; teratogenic (spina bifida)
  • Carbamazepine — monitor CBC; ↓ Na⁺ (SIADH)
  • Lamotrigine — watch for Stevens-Johnson syndrome (severe rash)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Lithium toxicity → check level; ensure patient is hydrated. NSAIDs and diuretics increase Lithium levels. Low salt diet → Li⁺ accumulates → toxicity.

Stimulants (ADHD)

  • Methylphenidate (Ritalin), Amphetamine salts (Adderall)
  • ↑ dopamine + norepinephrine → improves focus
  • SE: ↓ appetite, insomnia, ↑ HR/BP, abuse potential
  • Give with food; monitor growth in children

Other Mental Health Meds

Propranolol (Beta blocker)

  • Social/performance anxiety; essential tremor
  • SE: bradycardia, fatigue

Prazosin

  • PTSD nightmares
  • SE: dizziness, orthostatic hypotension

NCLEX Must-Know

  • SSRIs: 4–6 weeks for effect; serotonin syndrome if combined with other serotonergic agents
  • MAOIs: avoid tyramine-rich foods → hypertensive crisis
  • TCAs: cardiac arrhythmias in overdose; anticholinergic SE
  • Benzodiazepines: no abrupt stop; antidote = Flumazenil
  • Lithium: narrow range (0.6–1.2); hydration key; toxicity = tremors, confusion
  • Clozapine: weekly CBC monitoring (agranulocytosis)
  • Haloperidol: EPS + tardive dyskinesia; treat EPS with Benztropine
