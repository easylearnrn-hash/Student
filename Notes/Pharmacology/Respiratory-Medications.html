🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Albuterol = rescue (blue "save" inhaler). Salmeterol/Formoterol = maintenance ONLY — never use for acute attacks.

Corticosteroids

Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)

Fluticasone (Flovent), Budesonide (Pulmicort)

  • Prevent airway inflammation
  • NOT for acute attacks
  • SE: oral thrush, dysphonia
  • Rinse mouth after every use!

Oral / IV Corticosteroids

Prednisone, Methylprednisolone

  • Acute asthma/COPD exacerbations
  • SE: ↑ blood glucose, weight gain, mood swings
  • Don't stop abruptly (taper)

Combination Inhalers

  • Advair — Fluticasone (ICS) + Salmeterol (LABA) → asthma/COPD maintenance
  • Symbicort — Budesonide (ICS) + Formoterol (LABA) → asthma/COPD maintenance
  • Rinse mouth after using (ICS component)
  • Not for acute attacks

Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist

Montelukast (Singulair)

  • Asthma/allergic rhinitis prevention (oral)
  • SE: nightmares, mood changes, neuropsychiatric events (especially in children)
  • Not for acute attacks

Mucolytics & Cough Medications

Guaifenesin

  • Expectorant — thins mucus
  • For productive (wet) cough
  • Drink plenty of fluids

Dextromethorphan

  • Antitussive — suppresses cough reflex
  • For dry, non-productive cough

Acetylcysteine

  • Breaks down thick mucus
  • Used for CF, chronic bronchitis
  • Also antidote for Acetaminophen (Tylenol) overdose

Racemic Epinephrine

  • Nebulized for croup (laryngotracheobronchitis)
  • SE: tachycardia; watch for rebound swelling

Emergency Respiratory Medications

  • Epinephrine IM — anaphylaxis, refractory asthma (use thigh!)
  • Palivizumab (Synagis) — RSV prevention in high-risk infants (IM monthly, Nov–March)
  • Dexamethasone — severe croup, epiglottitis

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Albuterol = rescue (SABA); Salmeterol = maintenance (LABA) — NEVER LABA for acute
  • Inhaled steroids → rinse mouth to prevent oral thrush
  • Acetylcysteine = both mucolytic AND Tylenol OD antidote
  • Epinephrine IM = first-line anaphylaxis
  • Montelukast SE: neuropsychiatric (mood changes, nightmares)
  • Anticholinergics: dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention
