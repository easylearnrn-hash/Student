NCLEX KEY:

Psyllium MUST be taken with adequate water (impaction risk if dry). Lactulose SE = diarrhea/gas — expected for hepatic encephalopathy treatment.

Antiemetics

Ondansetron (Zofran)

  • 5-HT3 antagonist
  • Post-op nausea, chemo-induced N&V, pregnancy
  • SE: constipation, QT prolongation

Promethazine (Phenergan)

  • Antihistamine / antidopaminergic
  • SE: sedation, EPS, respiratory depression in children
  • IV use associated with tissue necrosis — use IM/PO

Metoclopramide (Reglan)

  • Prokinetic — ↑ GI motility
  • Gastroparesis, GERD
  • SE: EPS, drowsiness, tardive dyskinesia (long-term)

Scopolamine patch

  • Motion sickness prevention
  • SE: dry mouth, blurred vision

Antidiarrheals

Loperamide (Imodium)

  • Slows GI motility (opioid-like)
  • Chronic diarrhea; NOT infectious diarrhea (prolongs illness)

Bismuth subsalicylate (Pepto-Bismol)

  • Anti-diarrheal, anti-nausea
  • SE: black stools, black tongue (harmless)
  • Contains salicylate — avoid in children/teens (Reye's syndrome)

Special GI Agents

Lactulose

  • Hepatic encephalopathy — ↓ ammonia absorption
  • SE: diarrhea, bloating (expected)
  • Goal: 2–3 soft stools/day

Octreotide

  • Bleeding esophageal varices
  • ↓ portal pressure
  • SE: hyperglycemia, hypoglycemia

H. pylori Treatment

Triple Therapy: Amoxicillin + Clarithromycin + PPI (14 days)

Complete full course even if symptoms improve. Test for eradication after treatment.

NCLEX Must-Know

  • PPIs = best for GERD/ulcers; take before meals
  • Misoprostol → uterine contractions; AVOID in pregnancy
  • Sucralfate = empty stomach; separate from other meds
  • Ondansetron SE: constipation, QT prolongation
  • Psyllium: must drink plenty of water
  • Lactulose SE: diarrhea — expected (not toxic)
  • Pepto-Bismol: black stools — harmless
