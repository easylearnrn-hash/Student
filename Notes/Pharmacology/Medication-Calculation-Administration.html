Dosage formulas, IV rates, unit conversions, and NCLEX safety rules

Basic Dose Formula

D ÷ H × Q = X
Dose Ordered (D) ÷ Dose on Hand (H) × Quantity (Q) = Amount to Give (X)
Example: Order 500 mg; on hand 250 mg per 5 mL
→ (500 ÷ 250) × 5 = 10 mL

Weight-Based Dosing (Pediatrics)

  1. Convert lbs to kg: lbs ÷ 2.2 = kg
  2. Calculate dose: mg/kg × weight (kg) = total dose
  3. Divide by number of doses per day
Example: Order 10 mg/kg; child weighs 22 lbs
→ 22 ÷ 2.2 = 10 kg → 10 mg × 10 kg = 100 mg
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

If ordered dose is outside the safe dosage range → hold and notify the provider. Never assume it's correct.

IV Rate Calculations

mL/hr (for IV pump):

Total volume (mL) ÷ Time (hr) = mL/hr
1000 mL over 8 hr → 1000 ÷ 8 = 125 mL/hr

gtt/min (gravity drip):

(Volume × Drop factor) ÷ Time (min) = gtt/min
500 mL in 2 hr, 15 gtt/mL → (500 × 15) ÷ 120 = 63 gtt/min

Common drop factors: 10, 15, 20 gtt/mL (macro); 60 gtt/mL (micro/pediatric)

Rounding Rules

Pediatric medsRound to hundredth (0.01) for precision
IV gtt/minAlways round to whole number

NCLEX Safety Rules

  • Use leading zeros: Write 0.5 mL — NOT .5 mL
  • No trailing zeros: Write 5 mL — NOT 5.0 mL
  • Double-check high-alert meds: insulin, heparin, opioids, chemo
  • Label all syringes
  • If dose seems too large or too small → STOP, recheck, call provider

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Formula: D/H × Q = X (dose ordered ÷ dose on hand × quantity = amount to give)
  • Pediatrics: always weight-based (mg/kg), convert lbs → kg first
  • IV rate (mL/hr) = volume ÷ hours (pump)
  • gtt/min = (volume × drop factor) ÷ minutes (gravity); gtts = drops
  • Know conversions cold: 1 kg = 2.2 lb, 1 tsp = 5 mL, 1 g = 1000 mg
  • Leading zeros YES; trailing zeros NO
  • Always follow the 6 rights: right patient, drug, dose, route, time, documentation
