Burn Care Medications

MedicationUseKey Notes / NCLEX

NCLEX KEY:

Silver sulfadiazine — ALWAYS check sulfa allergy first; NOT used on face. Pre-medicate with analgesics before burn dressing changes.

Skin Infection Medications

Bacterial Skin Infections

  • Mupirocin (Bactroban) — topical; impetigo, MRSA decolonization
  • Bacitracin — minor cuts and abrasions
  • Oral Amoxicillin/Dicloxacillin — mild cellulitis
  • IV Vancomycin — MRSA cellulitis

Inflammatory / Allergic Skin

  • Hydrocortisone (topical) — mild eczema/dermatitis
  • Triamcinolone — moderate-severe eczema
  • Diphenhydramine — allergic itch (sedating)
  • Calamine lotion — chicken pox, poison ivy

Fungal Skin Infections

Topical Antifungals

  • Clotrimazole (Lotrimin) — athlete's foot, ringworm, jock itch
  • Miconazole — similar spectrum
  • Terbinafine — onychomycosis (nail fungus); oral also available

Oral Antifungals

  • Fluconazole — widespread/systemic fungal infections
  • SE: hepatotoxicity — monitor LFTs
  • Drug interactions (CYP450)

Parasitic Skin Infections

Pediculosis (Lice)

  • Permethrin 1% (Nix) — apply to hair; leave 10 min; rinse; repeat in 7–10 days
  • Check for and remove nits (comb)
  • Wash all bedding/clothing in hot water

Scabies

  • Permethrin 5% (Elimite) — apply neck to toe; leave 8–14 hours; wash off
  • Treat all close contacts simultaneously
  • Wash all clothing/bedding
  • Itching may persist 2–4 weeks after successful treatment
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Lice = Permethrin 1%; Scabies = Permethrin 5%. Scabies itching persisting after treatment does NOT mean treatment failed.

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Burns: Lactated Ringer's (LR) = first-line fluid (Parkland formula)
  • Silver sulfadiazine: check sulfa allergy; not for face
  • Mafenide (Sulfamylon): painful; can cause metabolic acidosis
  • Pre-medicate before burn dressing changes (pain control)
  • Permethrin 1% = lice; Permethrin 5% = scabies
  • Topical corticosteroids: used for eczema/dermatitis; not for infections
