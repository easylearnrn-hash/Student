Autoimmune & Inflammatory Disorder Medications

SLE, RA, MS, myasthenia gravis, thyroid disorders, GBS, and Lyme disease pharmacology

Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

  • NSAIDs — mild joint pain/fever; SE: GI bleeding, renal damage
  • Prednisone / corticosteroids — flares; SE: ↑ BG, infection risk, osteoporosis
  • Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil) — disease-modifying; reduces flares
  • SE: retinal toxicity → annual eye exams required
  • Azathioprine / Mycophenolate — immunosuppressants for severe disease
  • Belimumab (Benlysta) — biologic; B-lymphocyte stimulator inhibitor
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Hydroxychloroquine → annual ophthalmology exams (retinal toxicity). Sun protection critical in SLE.

Lyme Disease

  • Doxycycline — first-line (adults, children >8 yr); 14–21 days
  • Amoxicillin or Cefuroxime — pregnancy, children <8 (doxycycline discolors teeth)
  • IV Ceftriaxone — late Lyme with neurological or cardiac involvement

Anthrax (Bacillus anthracis)

  • Ciprofloxacin — first-line treatment and post-exposure prophylaxis
  • Doxycycline — alternative (60-day course)
  • Anthrax antitoxin — inhalation anthrax (life-threatening)
  • Vaccine — military, laboratory workers at high risk

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

DMARDs (Disease-Modifying)

  • Methotrexate — first-line DMARD; weekly low-dose
  • SE: hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression, teratogenic
  • Monitor LFTs, CBC; take folic acid supplement

Biologics (TNF Inhibitors)

  • Etanercept, Adalimumab (Humira), Infliximab
  • Screen for TB before starting (reactivation risk)
  • SE: serious infections, lymphoma risk

Symptom Relief

  • NSAIDs — pain/inflammation
  • Corticosteroids — acute flares (bridge therapy)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Methotrexate + folic acid (reduces toxicity). TNF inhibitors → TB screening mandatory before starting. No live vaccines.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

Acute Flares

  • High-dose IV Methylprednisolone
  • Shortens duration, not long-term course

Disease-Modifying Therapy

  • Interferons (Interferon beta-1a/1b) — injection site reactions, flu-like SE
  • Glatiramer acetate — injection site reactions
  • Natalizumab, Ocrelizumab — newer biologics

Myasthenia Gravis

  • Pyridostigmine (Mestinon) — acetylcholinesterase inhibitor; improves muscle strength
  • Take on schedule; SE: excessive secretions (SLUDGE: salivation, lacrimation, urination, defecation, GI distress, emesis)
  • Plasmapheresis / IVIG — for myasthenic crisis
  • Avoid aminoglycosides, beta blockers (worsen symptoms)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Myasthenic crisis = respiratory muscle failure → ICU, ventilator support. Cholinergic crisis (overdose of pyridostigmine) = SLUDGE symptoms.

Thyroid Autoimmune Conditions

Graves' Disease (Hyperthyroid)

  • Methimazole / PTU — block synthesis
  • Beta-blockers — ↓ HR, tremors (symptomatic)
  • Radioactive Iodine (I-131)

Hashimoto's (Hypothyroid)

  • Levothyroxine — replacement therapy
  • Morning, empty stomach; monitor TSH

Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)

  • IVIG — immunoglobulin to halt immune attack on myelin
  • Plasmapheresis — removes antibodies
  • Anticoagulants — DVT prevention (immobility)
  • Monitor respiratory function closely (ascending paralysis can involve diaphragm)
  • Corticosteroids NOT effective in GBS
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

GBS → respiratory muscle involvement → monitor RR, O₂ sat, and prepare for ventilator support. Steroids are NOT used.

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Hydroxychloroquine: annual eye exams (retinal toxicity)
  • Methotrexate: hepatotoxicity; take folic acid; teratogenic
  • TNF inhibitors: TB screen before starting; no live vaccines
  • Pyridostigmine: SLUDGE symptoms if overdose
  • GBS: IVIG + plasmapheresis; NO steroids; watch respiratory
  • Doxycycline: not in pregnancy/children <8 (Lyme disease)
