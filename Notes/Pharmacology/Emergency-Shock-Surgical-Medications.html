🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Anaphylaxis → Epinephrine IM FIRST (lateral thigh). Septic shock → IV antibiotics FIRST, then 30 mL/kg NS bolus.

Key Vasopressors & Inotropes

Norepinephrine (Levophed)

  • First-line vasopressor for septic shock
  • ↑ vasoconstriction → ↑ BP
  • SE: ↓ renal perfusion if excess

Dopamine

  • Low dose → ↑ renal/mesenteric blood flow
  • High dose → ↑ HR + vasoconstriction
  • Used in cardiogenic + septic shock

Dobutamine

  • Inotrope → ↑ contractility + ↑ CO
  • Cardiogenic shock
  • SE: tachycardia, ↑ O₂ demand

Epinephrine IM

  • Anaphylaxis: 0.3 mg IM (lateral thigh)
  • Cardiac arrest (ACLS)
  • SE: tachycardia, hypertension

Pre-Operative Medications

  • Antibiotics (prophylactic) — given 30–60 min before incision; prevent surgical site infection
  • Midazolam / Lorazepam — sedation, anxiety reduction; respiratory depression; have reversal ready (Flumazenil)
  • Ondansetron (Zofran) — prevent post-op N&V
  • Atropine — reduces oral/respiratory secretions during anesthesia
  • Ketamine — dissociative anesthetic; SE: emergence delirium, hallucinations
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Prophylactic antibiotics = within 60 min before incision. NPO at least 6–8 hours before surgery (2 hours for clear liquids).

Post-Operative Pain Management

Opioids

  • Morphine, Hydromorphone (Dilaudid), Fentanyl
  • SE: respiratory depression, ↓ BP, urinary retention
  • Monitor RR (hold if <12)
  • Antidote: Naloxone (Narcan)

Non-opioid

  • Ketorolac (Toradol) — NSAID; watch for bleeding, GI upset
  • Acetaminophen — hepatotoxicity risk in overdose
  • Celecoxib — COX-2 inhibitor; CV risk

Post-Operative Urinary Retention

  • Bladder scan first — confirm retention (>400–500 mL)
  • Void if possible (privacy, running water, warm compress)
  • Straight catheterization if unable to void
  • Bethanechol (cholinergic) — stimulates bladder contraction

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Anaphylaxis: Epinephrine IM lateral thigh FIRST
  • Septic shock: IV antibiotics FIRST, then 30 mL/kg NS
  • Norepinephrine = first-line vasopressor for septic shock; vasopressin = adjunct vasopressor
  • Pre-op antibiotics within 60 min of incision
  • Post-op opioids: monitor RR; hold if <12; antidote = Naloxone
  • NPO ≥6–8 hrs before surgery (2 hrs clear liquids)
  • Incentive spirometer post-op: prevents atelectasis and pneumonia — deep breathing every 1–2 hrs
