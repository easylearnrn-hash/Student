Nursing Drug Dosage Math

Essential formulas, conversions, and safe dose calculations

Core Formulas

D/H × Q = X
Desired ÷ Have × Quantity = Amount to give
mL/hr = Total Volume (mL) ÷ Total Time (hr)
gtt/min = (Volume × Drop Factor) ÷ Time (minutes)
🧠
Memorize:

D/H × Q = dose; mL/hr = volume ÷ hours; gtt/min uses drop factor.

IV Drip Rate & Drop Factors

Common Drop Factors (gtt/mL):

  • Macrodrip: 10 gtt/mL
  • Macrodrip: 15 gtt/mL
  • Macrodrip: 20 gtt/mL
  • Microdrip: 60 gtt/mL

Example:

1000 mL over 8 hr, 20 gtt/mL:
1000 ÷ 8 = 125 mL/hr
(125 × 20) ÷ 60 = 41.7 → 42 gtt/min

Pediatric Dosage

  • Convert weight: lbs ÷ 2.2 = kg
  • Calculate dose: mg/kg × weight (kg) = total dose
  • Check against safe dose range (SDR) — HOLD if outside range, notify provider
mg/kg × weight(kg) = dose

Example: Child weighs 44 lb (= 20 kg). Order: 10 mg/kg. Dose = 10 × 20 = 200 mg

Rounding Rules

Tablets / Capsules

  • Round to nearest 0.5 tab
  • Never cut capsules

Liquids / IV

  • Liquids: round to 0.1 mL
  • gtt/min: round to whole number

Decimal Safety Rules

  • Leading zero — YES: write 0.5 mg (not .5 mg)
  • Trailing zero — NO: write 5 mg (never 5.0 mg)
  • U (units) → write "units" to avoid confusion with 0
  • μg (micrograms) → write "mcg"
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Trailing zero errors = 10× overdose risk. Always use leading zeros for doses <1.

Safe Dose Range Check

  • Always check SDR before administration
  • If dose is BELOW minimum → call provider, not enough therapeutic effect
  • If dose is ABOVE maximum → HOLD and notify provider immediately
  • Document action taken

NCLEX Must-Know

  • D/H × Q = X is the universal tablet/liquid formula
  • Weight-based dosing (mg/kg): always convert lbs → kg first (1 kg = 2.2 lb) for pediatric patients
  • IV rate (mL/hr) = volume ÷ hours (for infusion pumps)
  • gtt/min = (volume × drop factor) ÷ time in minutes; gtts = drops per minute
  • Ratio proportion method (ratio-proportion): dose on hand / quantity = dose ordered / X
  • Know conversions cold: 1 kg = 2.2 lb, 1 tsp = 5 mL, 1 g = 1000 mg
  • Leading zeros YES (0.5); trailing zeros NO (5 not 5.0)
