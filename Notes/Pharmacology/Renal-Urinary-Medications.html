Kidney Stones (Nephrolithiasis)

  • Tamsulosin (Flomax) — alpha blocker; relaxes ureters → helps pass stones
  • SE: dizziness, orthostatic hypotension, retrograde ejaculation
  • Pain management — Ketorolac (NSAID), opioids for severe pain
  • Hydration — 2–3 L/day to flush stone
  • Strain urine to catch stone for analysis

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

  • Furosemide (Lasix) — convert oliguric AKI to non-oliguric phase; monitor output
  • Kayexalate (Sodium polystyrene) — binds K⁺ in GI tract; use for hyperkalemia
  • Insulin + Dextrose (D50) — shifts K⁺ into cells (temporary fix)
  • Calcium gluconate — cardiac membrane protection in hyperkalemia
  • AVOID nephrotoxins: NSAIDs, IV contrast (use NAC pre-treatment), aminoglycosides
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Hyperkalemia treatment order: Calcium gluconate (heart protection) → Insulin+D50 (shift K⁺ into cells) → Kayexalate (remove K⁺ from body).

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Blood Pressure Control

  • ACE inhibitors or ARBs — first-line in CKD with proteinuria
  • Slow disease progression; reduce proteinuria

Anemia

  • Erythropoietin (EPO) — stimulate RBC production
  • Monitor Hgb (target 10–12 g/dL); ↑ HTN risk
  • Also give iron supplementation

Bone/Mineral Management

  • Phosphate binders (CaCO₃, sevelamer) — take WITH meals
  • Vitamin D supplementation
  • Avoid aluminum-containing antacids

Fluid/Electrolyte

  • Loop diuretics (furosemide) — fluid management
  • Restrict Na⁺, K⁺, phosphorus in diet

Hemodialysis Considerations

  • Hold antihypertensives BEFORE dialysis (hypotension risk during session)
  • No blood pressure or IV in fistula arm
  • Assess fistula: palpate thrill (vibration) + auscultate bruit (whooshing sound)
  • Report absent thrill/bruit → clotted fistula
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Thrill (palpate) + bruit (auscultate) confirm fistula patency. No BP cuff on fistula arm — ever!

Peritoneal Dialysis

  • Strict sterile technique during exchanges
  • Warm dialysate before infusing (comfort, better clearance)
  • Cloudy return = PERITONITIS — report immediately, culture
  • Monitor fluid balance; weigh before/after

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Phenazopyridine = orange urine (normal, warn patient); not an antibiotic
  • Nitrofurantoin: avoid in renal failure
  • Tamsulosin: orthostatic hypotension; fall risk
  • AKI hyperkalemia: Calcium gluconate first (cardiac protection)
  • Avoid NSAIDs and aminoglycosides in renal impairment
  • Phosphate binders: take WITH meals
  • Peritoneal dialysis cloudy return = peritonitis
