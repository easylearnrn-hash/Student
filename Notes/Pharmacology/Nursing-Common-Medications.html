Top Drug Interactions to Know

Avoid Combinations:

  • Nitroglycerin + Sildenafil (Viagra) → severe hypotension
  • MAOIs + SSRIs → serotonin syndrome
  • ACE + ARB + K⁺-sparing diuretics → hyperkalemia
  • Metformin + IV contrast → lactic acidosis
  • Warfarin + NSAIDs → bleeding risk ↑↑

Food Interactions:

  • Warfarin ↔ Vitamin K foods (consistency is key)
  • Statins ↔ Grapefruit (↑ drug levels → toxicity)
  • MAOIs ↔ Tyramine foods → hypertensive crisis
  • Calcium/iron ↔ Levothyroxine (↓ absorption)
  • Antibiotics ↔ dairy/antacids (↓ absorption)

Therapeutic Drug Levels (Memorize)

  • Digoxin: 0.5–2 ng/mL (toxicity >2)
  • Lithium: 0.6–1.2 mEq/L (toxicity >1.5)
  • Phenytoin: 10–20 mcg/mL
  • Vancomycin trough: 10–20 mcg/mL
  • Heparin aPTT: 60–100 sec (1.5–2.5× normal)
  • Warfarin INR: 2–3 (2.5–3.5 for mechanical valves)

Antidotes Reference

  • Heparin → Protamine sulfate
  • Warfarin → Vitamin K (phytonadione)
  • Opioids → Naloxone (Narcan)
  • Benzodiazepines → Flumazenil
  • Acetaminophen OD → Acetylcysteine (N-acetylcysteine)
  • Digoxin toxicity → Digibind (Digoxin Immune Fab)
  • MgSO₄ overdose → Calcium gluconate
  • Organophosphate → Atropine + Pralidoxime

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Digoxin: hold if apical pulse <60; therapeutic range 0.5–2 ng/mL
  • Nitroglycerin: never with sildenafil; dark glass bottle
  • Heparin: monitor aPTT + platelets (HIT risk); antidote = protamine
  • Vancomycin: slow infusion prevents red man syndrome
  • Metoprolol: hold if HR <50 or SBP <90; never stop abruptly
  • Statins: avoid grapefruit; monitor for muscle pain (rhabdomyolysis)
