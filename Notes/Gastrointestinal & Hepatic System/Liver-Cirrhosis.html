Liver Cirrhosis

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · Chronic Liver Disease

What Is Cirrhosis?

Cirrhosis is a chronic, progressive liver disease where healthy liver tissue is replaced by fibrotic (scar) tissue, impairing liver function and causing multi-system complications.

Causes

  • Alcoholism — chronic alcohol damages hepatocytes
  • Viral Hepatitis B & C — long-term inflammation → scarring
  • NAFLD (Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease) — linked to obesity, diabetes
  • Bile Duct Disorders — blocked bile flow damages liver
  • Autoimmune Hepatitis — immune system attacks liver cells

Liver Functions Affected

  • Detoxifies drugs & toxins
  • Metabolizes nutrients (glucose, fats, proteins)
  • Produces clotting factors & albumin (for fluid balance)
  • Stores fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K, B12, Iron)

Complications

1. Portal Hypertension

Scarred liver restricts portal vein blood flow → ↑ pressure → leads to:

  • Esophageal & Gastric Varices — dilated veins that can rupture
  • Splenomegaly — spleen traps WBCs/platelets → ↑ infection & bleeding risk
  • Ascites — fluid accumulation in abdomen

2. Esophageal Varices

  • Dilated veins in esophagus due to portal hypertension
  • Rupture → hematemesis (vomiting blood) & melena (black stools)
  • Treated with: Beta-blockers (propranolol) or TIPS shunt
Bleeding esophageal varices = life-threatening emergency

3. TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt)

  • Surgically reroutes blood from portal vein → central vein, bypassing the liver
  • Reduces portal pressure & prevents variceal bleeding

4. Hepatic Encephalopathy

  • Liver fails to clear ammonia → crosses blood-brain barrier → brain swelling
  • Symptoms: Confusion, asterixis (hand-flapping tremor), hepatic fetor (musty breath), coma
  • Treated with: Lactulose — binds ammonia → excreted via stool

5. Cerebral Edema & Wernicke Encephalopathy

  • Cerebral Edema — ammonia-induced brain swelling; life-threatening → monitor neuro status
  • Wernicke Encephalopathy — thiamine (B1) deficiency (especially alcoholics)
  • Wernicke symptoms: confusion, poor coordination, vision problems
  • Treatment: IV Thiamine (B1) to prevent brain damage

Nursing Interventions

Prevent Bleeding

  • Soft toothbrush
  • Monitor for melena & hematemesis
  • Check platelets, PT/INR

Treat Encephalopathy

  • Lactulose (↓ ammonia)
  • Monitor mental status & ammonia levels

Manage Ascites & Edema

  • Low sodium diet
  • Diuretics (Spironolactone, Furosemide)
  • Paracentesis for severe ascites

Nutrition

  • High-calorie, moderate protein
  • Low protein if encephalopathy present
  • Low sodium, low fat
  • Vitamins: A, D, E, K, B12, Iron

NCLEX Key Takeaways

  • Always monitor for GI bleeding (hematemesis, melena)
  • Watch for confusion → hepatic encephalopathy
  • Monitor fluid overload — ascites, edema
  • TIPS shunt prevents bleeding from esophageal varices
  • Liver transplant is the only definitive cure for end-stage cirrhosis
