Cystic Fibrosis (CF)

Autosomal recessive CFTR gene mutation → thick, sticky mucus → lungs, pancreas, GI, reproductive

Autosomal Recessive CFTR Gene Sweat Chloride >60 Airway Clearance First

1. Pathophysiology

Defective CFTR gene → abnormal chloride transport → dehydration of secretions → thick mucus:

  • Lungs → respiratory infections, airway obstruction
  • Pancreas → duct obstruction → malabsorption of fats, proteins, fat-soluble vitamins
  • GI tract → meconium ileus (newborns), bowel obstruction risk
  • Reproductive → male infertility (absent/obstructed vas deferens), thick cervical mucus in females
  • Sweat glands → salty skin (↑ sodium chloride loss)

2. Clinical Manifestations

Respiratory Symptoms

  • Chronic cough with thick sputum
  • Recurrent infections (Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Staphylococcus aureus)
  • Wheezing, dyspnea
  • Barrel chest — late sign
  • Clubbing of fingers — late sign of chronic hypoxia
  • Nasal polyps and sinus infections

GI Symptoms

  • Meconium ileus — first sign in newborns (thick stool blocking intestines)
  • Steatorrhea — fatty, foul-smelling stools (fat malabsorption)
  • Failure to thrive — weight loss despite good appetite
  • Vitamin deficiencies — A, D, E, K (fat-soluble vitamins)
  • Pancreatic insufficiency → CF-related diabetes (CFRD)

Other Symptoms

  • Salty-tasting skin (sodium loss in sweat)
  • Male infertility
  • Delayed puberty in females

3. Diagnostic Tests

  • Sweat chloride test (gold standard) → >60 mmol/L in children; >40 mmol/L in infants
  • Genetic testing for CFTR mutation
  • Chest X-ray → hyperinflation, mucus plugging, bronchiectasis
  • Pulmonary function tests (PFTs) → reduced FEV1, obstructive pattern
  • Fecal elastase test → checks pancreatic insufficiency

4. Treatment & Nursing Care

Airway Clearance (PRIORITY)

  • Chest physiotherapy (CPT): postural drainage, percussion, vibration
  • High-frequency chest wall oscillation (vest therapy)
  • Positive expiratory pressure (PEP) devices (Flutter, Acapella)

Inhaled Medication Order

1. Bronchodilator (Albuterol) → opens airways
2. Mucolytic (Dornase alfa / Hypertonic saline) → thins mucus
3. Airway Clearance (CPT / vest therapy)
4. Inhaled Antibiotic (Tobramycin) — given LAST

Key Medications

  • Dornase alfa (Pulmozyme) — mucolytic, breaks down DNA in thick mucus
  • Hypertonic saline (7%) — hydrates airways
  • Pancrelipase (Creon/Zenpep) — pancreatic enzyme replacement, taken WITH meals
  • Fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K
  • CFTR modulators — Ivacaftor (Kalydeco), Lumacaftor/ivacaftor (Orkambi)
  • Inhaled tobramycin, aztreonam (Pseudomonas infections)

Nutrition

  • High-calorie, high-protein diet
  • Increased salt intake (compensates for sodium loss)
  • Frequent snacks and meals
  • NG/GT tube feedings if severe malnutrition

5. NCLEX Priority Interventions

  • ✅ Airway clearance is ALWAYS priority
  • ✅ Administer bronchodilator BEFORE chest physiotherapy
  • ✅ Give pancreatic enzymes WITH meals/snacks — not before or after
  • ✅ Monitor for respiratory infections (low-grade fever, increased cough)
  • ✅ Sprinkle pancreatic enzymes on acidic foods (e.g., applesauce) to protect enzyme activity
  • ✅ Encourage high-calorie diet & fat-soluble vitamins
  • ✅ Assess for meconium ileus in newborns
  • ✅ Monitor for CFRD (CF-related diabetes)
  • ✅ Inhaled antibiotics used LAST after all airway clearance
  • ✅ Infection control: hand hygiene, contact precautions (resistant bacteria), avoid contact with other CF patients

6. NCLEX Sample Questions

Q1 — Prioritization: Child with CF has increased cough, fever, decreased appetite. First intervention?

✅ Obtain a sputum culture — identify organism before starting antibiotics

Q2 — Nutrition: How to give pancreatic enzyme replacement?

✅ Sprinkle on acidic foods like applesauce — protects enzyme activity

Q3 — Pharmacology: Drug to thin mucus secretions in CF?

✅ Dornase alfa (Pulmozyme) — mucolytic enzyme breaks down DNA in thick mucus

Q4 — Growth: 10-year-old with CF, short stature and slow growth?

✅ "The child may need supplemental tube feedings at night" — malnutrition is common

🔑 Key Takeaway: CFTR gene → thick mucus → lungs + pancreas + GI. Sweat chloride >60 = diagnosis. Airway clearance first. Bronchodilator → mucolytic → CPT → antibiotic (order matters!). Pancreatic enzymes WITH food. Fat-soluble vitamins. Watch for CFRD.
