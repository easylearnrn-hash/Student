Dumping Syndrome

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · Post-Surgical Complication

What Is Dumping Syndrome?

Dumping Syndrome occurs when food (especially sugars) moves too quickly from the stomach to the small intestine after eating — particularly after gastric surgery.

Pathophysiology: Stomach contents "dump" into the small intestine undigested → osmotic fluid shift into the intestine → hypovolemia + GI symptoms

Common Causes

  • Gastric surgery: Billroth I or II, Roux-en-Y gastric bypass
  • Pyloric sphincter removal or damage (the valve between stomach and small intestine)
  • Vagotomy
  • Increased gastric motility

Two Phases

⏰ Early Dumping — 15–30 min after eating

Cause: Fluid shift into intestine (osmotic)

  • 💨 Diarrhea
  • 🤢 Nausea
  • ❤️ Tachycardia (fast HR)
  • 🔄 Dizziness
  • 😓 Diaphoresis (sweating)
  • 🔺 Cramping, bloating
⏰ Late Dumping — 1–3 hours after eating

Cause: Rapid sugar absorption → insulin surge → hypoglycemia

  • 🍬 Weakness
  • 🧠 Dizziness
  • 🥴 Confusion
  • 🥵 Sweating
  • 🍭 Hunger

Signs & Symptoms (Combined)

  • Nausea, bloating, diarrhea
  • Diaphoresis (sweating)
  • Weakness, dizziness, fainting
  • Tachycardia, cramping
  • Hypoglycemia symptoms (late phase)

Nursing Interventions & Education

Diet Modifications

  • High-protein, low-carb meals
  • 🚫 Avoid simple sugars (juice, soda, sweets)
  • Small, frequent meals
  • Add fat/protein to delay gastric emptying
  • 🚫 No fluids with meals — wait 30–60 min after eating

Positioning

📉 Recline (semi-Fowler's or lying down) for 30–60 min after meals → slows gastric emptying

Other Teaching

  • Eat slowly and chew thoroughly
  • Avoid very hot or very cold foods
  • Add fiber (e.g., pectin) to slow digestion
  • Late phase: watch for hypoglycemia → keep a small snack handy

NCLEX Tips

Early Dumping: Dizziness, palpitations, diarrhea RIGHT AFTER meals (15–30 min) → fluid shift
Late Dumping: Symptoms 2–3 hours after meals → check blood glucose! → hypoglycemia
