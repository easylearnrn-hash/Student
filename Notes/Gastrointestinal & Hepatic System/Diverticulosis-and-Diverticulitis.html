Diverticulosis & Diverticulitis

LLQ Pain CT Scan Gold Standard High-Fiber Prevention NPO During Flare

1. Pathophysiology

  • Diverticulosis = Presence of diverticula (small pouches in the colon wall) — often asymptomatic.
  • Diverticulitis = Inflammation or infection of the diverticula — trapped stool/bacteria inside.

Risk Factors

  • Low-fiber diet → constipation and straining
  • Aging (weakened intestinal walls)
  • Obesity, smoking, lack of exercise
  • NSAID and opioid use (increase complication risk)

2. Symptoms

Classic Symptoms

  • LLQ pain — most common finding
  • Fever and chills (infection)
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Bloating and gas

Severe / Complication Symptoms

  • Peritonitis → rigid abdomen, high fever, severe pain
  • Abscess → persistent fever, worsening pain despite antibiotics
  • Perforation → sudden severe pain, hypotension, shock
  • Fistula → abnormal connection between colon and bladder/vagina

3. Diagnosis

  • CT scan with contrast — best imaging test to confirm diverticulitis
  • CBC → ↑ WBC (infection marker)
  • Colonoscopy — NOT during acute flare; done later to assess diverticula

4. Treatment

Mild Cases (Outpatient)

  • NPO or clear liquid diet (rests the bowel)
  • Oral antibiotics: ciprofloxacin + metronidazole
  • Acetaminophen for pain — avoid NSAIDs (worsen bleeding)
  • Gradually increase fiber once symptoms improve

Severe Cases (Hospitalization)

  • NPO → bowel rest
  • IV fluids (prevent dehydration)
  • IV antibiotics
  • NG tube if severe vomiting/bloating
  • Surgery (Colectomy) — for perforation, abscess, or obstruction

5. NCLEX Priorities

Acute Flare Management

  • ✅ NPO or clear liquids — rest the bowel
  • ✅ IV antibiotics and pain control
  • ✅ Avoid laxatives and enemas (↑ colon pressure)
  • ✅ Monitor for peritonitis and perforation

Long-Term Prevention

  • ✅ High-fiber diet (fruits, vegetables, whole grains)
  • ✅ Increase fluid intake
  • ✅ Regular exercise
  • 🌰 Seeds & nuts are NOT restricted — older teaching told patients to avoid seeds/nuts (thought to lodge in diverticula), but current evidence does NOT support this restriction; no need to avoid seeds, nuts, corn, or popcorn

6. Complications to Watch For

⚠ Peritonitis — Rigid board-like abdomen, severe pain, high fever, tachycardia
⚠ Abscess / Fistula — Persistent fever despite antibiotics; fecaluria (stool in urine) if colovesical fistula
⚠ Bowel Obstruction — Severe bloating, vomiting, no bowel movements
