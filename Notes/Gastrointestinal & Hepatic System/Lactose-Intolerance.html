Lactose Intolerance

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · GI Disorder

Cause

Lactose intolerance results from a lactase deficiency — lactase is the enzyme that breaks down lactose (a sugar found in milk).

Undigested lactose ferments in the colon → causes osmotic diarrhea, gas, and bloating.

Symptoms

  • Diarrhea (osmotic — water retention in bowel)
  • Flatulence (gas from fermentation)
  • Abdominal bloating and distension
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Dyspepsia (indigestion)
No fever or systemic symptoms — helps rule out infectious cause

Diagnosis

  • Hydrogen breath test — positive if exhaled hydrogen increases after lactose ingestion
  • Lactose tolerance test — blood glucose fails to rise after lactose ingestion

Dietary Management

Avoid

  • Milk, cream
  • Soft cheeses
  • Regular yogurt

Usually Tolerated

  • Aged cheeses (cheddar, parmesan, gouda)
  • Lactose-free milk
  • Soy, oat, almond milk alternatives
  • Gradual reintroduction to test personal tolerance

Supplements

  • Lactase enzyme (Lactaid) — take before dairy intake
  • Calcium & Vitamin D — prevent bone loss (key supplements)
  • Vitamin A, B12, K, Magnesium — supplement if signs of deficiency

Pediatric Note

  • Common after GI infections in toddlers
  • Switch to soy-based or lactose-free formulas
  • Usually temporary in infants but can persist in some populations

Nursing Considerations

  • Teach label reading — lactose is often hidden in processed foods and medications
  • Monitor for nutrient deficiencies (calcium, vitamin D, B12)
  • Educate on alternative calcium and protein sources
  • Ensure adequate hydration if diarrhea is present
