Central Line vs PICC Line

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · note_gi_16

Definitions

Central Line (CVC)

Catheter inserted directly into a large central vein (subclavian, internal jugular, or femoral) with tip in the Superior Vena Cava (SVC) near the right atrium.

Used for: long-term/high-volume infusion, vesicant drugs, TPN, hemodynamic monitoring (CVP)

PICC Line

Inserted peripherally (basilic, brachial, or cephalic vein in upper arm) and threaded to the SVC.

Used for: long-term antibiotics, outpatient chemo/TPN, poor peripheral access. Placed bedside by trained RN.

💡 Both are central access devices — tip rests in central circulation. Difference = insertion site & duration.

Complications (Both)

ComplicationNCLEX Notes
CLABSI (central line-associated bloodstream infection)Strict sterile technique for dressing & access; scrub the hub
ThrombosisSwelling, pain, redness in limb
Catheter occlusionFlush with saline/heparin per protocol
Air embolismClamp line; left lateral Trendelenburg; 100% O₂
PneumothoraxMore common with subclavian/jugular central lines; monitor for respiratory distress after insertion

Nursing Care Priorities

  • Confirm tip placement by chest X-ray before first use
  • Maintain sterile technique for dressing changes
  • Scrub the hub (antiseptic cap or alcohol before access)
  • Flush per protocol (saline or saline + heparin)
  • Assess for infection: redness, drainage, swelling, fever
  • Change transparent dressings every 7 days (or sooner if soiled)
  • Clamp line when not in use (unless continuous infusion)
  • PICC: Avoid BP measurement or venipuncture in that arm
  • Central line in chest: monitor for respiratory distress post-insertion (pneumothorax)

Mnemonic: C.L.E.A.N. (Central Access Care)

CConfirm placement before use (chest X-ray) LLumen flush per protocol EEvaluate site for infection AAseptic technique always NNever use until placement verified

⚡ NCLEX Priorities

  • New PICC/central line → confirm tip with chest X-ray before any infusion
  • Sudden shortness of breath + ↓SpO₂ after central line insertion → pneumothorax or air embolism
  • Air embolism → left lateral Trendelenburg + 100% O₂ + clamp line
  • PICC arm: no BP, no venipuncture, no IV in same arm
  • CLABSI prevention = sterile dressing changes + scrub the hub
NCLEX Q: Priority before starting IV antibiotics through new PICC? → Verify tip placement with chest X-ray.
