SBO vs LBO

Small Bowel Obstruction vs Large Bowel Obstruction — plus Paralytic Ileus

NPO + NG Tube Hypoactive Bowel Sounds No Laxatives Avoid Opioids

1. SBO vs LBO Comparison

Feature SBO (Paralytic Ileus) LBO (Large Bowel)
Cause Post-op, stroke — neurogenic/functional Tumor, volvulus, fecal impaction, stricture
Onset Abrupt, acute Gradual
Symptoms Pain, distention, no flatus, nausea/vomiting (early) Pain, distention, absolute constipation, vomiting (late, may be fecal-smelling)
Bowel Sounds Absent / hypoactive in all 4 quadrants High-pitched tinkling above obstruction → then silent
Stool Stuck due to paralysis Absolute constipation — no stool or gas
Associated Lab Hypokalemia → metabolic acidosis Hyponatremia → metabolic alkalosis
Treatment NPO, NG tube, IV fluids, correct electrolytes, ± prokinetics (partial only), avoid opioids NPO, NG tube, IV fluids + electrolyte correction, surgery often required, avoid opioids

2. Paralytic Ileus vs LBO — Detailed

Paralytic Ileus

  • Neurogenic — no physical blockage; bowel is paralyzed
  • Common causes: Post-abdominal surgery, opioids, electrolyte imbalance (hypokalemia), spinal trauma
  • X-ray: Gas in both small AND large intestines; uniform distention
  • Pain: Dull, generalized abdominal discomfort

Large Bowel Obstruction (LBO)

  • Mechanical blockage in the large intestine
  • Common causes: Colon cancer, volvulus, diverticulitis, fecal impaction, hernia
  • X-ray/CT: Air-fluid levels, distended colon, no gas beyond blockage
  • Pain: Crampy, severe, intermittent
  • Monitor for perforation/sepsis: abdominal rigidity, fever, elevated WBC

3. Postoperative Bowel Sound Timeline

  • < 2 days post-op → Absent bowel sounds = normal (due to anesthesia)
  • > 3 days post-op → Absent bowel sounds = suspect paralytic ileus → intervene
  • After stroke → Absent bowel sounds suggests paralytic ileus

4. NCLEX Tips

  • ✅ Paralytic ileus often follows abdominal surgery or opioid use
  • ✅ Absent bowel sounds = ileus; Tinkling → silent = obstruction
  • ✅ NG tube for BOTH → relieves distention and vomiting
🚫 Never give laxatives or stimulate peristalsis in complete obstruction — may cause perforation
🚫 Avoid opioids — slow gut motility and worsen both SBO and LBO

5. Symptoms Common to Both

  • Pain
  • Abdominal distention
  • Lack of flatus
  • Hypoactive bowel sounds in all 4 quadrants
🔑 Key Takeaway: SBO = acute + paralytic (functional). LBO = gradual + mechanical. Both → NPO + NG tube + IV fluids. Absent bowel sounds >3 days post-op = paralytic ileus. Never give laxatives in complete obstruction.
