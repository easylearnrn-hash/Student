Cholecystitis & Cholelithiasis

Gallbladder Inflammation & Gallstones

1. Definitions

Cholecystitis
Inflammation of the gallbladder (small organ under the liver that stores bile)
Cholelithiasis
Gallstones — hardened deposits of bile blocking the cystic duct

2. Pathophysiology

Causes

  • Gallstones blocking the cystic duct (tube connecting gallbladder to bile duct) — most common
  • Acalculous cholecystitis — inflammation without gallstones (from trauma, sepsis, or fasting)

Untreated Consequences

  • ⚠️ Gangrene — dead tissue from lack of blood flow
  • ⚠️ Perforation — hole in gallbladder → infection
  • ⚠️ Peritonitis — infection of the abdominal lining

3. Symptoms

Classic Symptoms

  • 🟡 RUQ pain (right upper quadrant)
  • 🫁 Murphy's Sign — pain when pressing under right ribcage on deep breath
  • ↗️ Pain radiating to right shoulder or back
  • 🤢 Nausea/vomiting (especially after fatty foods)
  • 🌡️ Fever, chills (infection)

Obstructive Signs

  • 🟡 Jaundice — yellowing of skin & eyes
  • 🌑 Dark urine — excess bilirubin
  • ⬜ Clay-colored stools — no bile in intestines
  • 😣 Pruritus — itchy skin from bile buildup

Chronic Cholecystitis Symptoms

  • 😮‍💨 Dyspepsia (indigestion) — recurring upper GI discomfort
  • 🧈 Fat intolerance — bloating and discomfort after eating fatty foods
🚨 Jaundice + dark urine + clay stools = obstructed bile flow!

4. Diagnosis

TestWhat It Shows
UltrasoundFirst-line! Sees gallstones or inflammation
HIDA ScanChecks if gallbladder is emptying properly
CT ScanUsed if perforation suspected
↑ WBCSigns of infection
↑ BilirubinBile flow blocked
↑ ALP, AST, ALTLiver/bile duct involvement

5. Treatment

Nonsurgical (Mild Cases)

  • 🚫 NPO status — rest the gallbladder
  • 💧 IV fluids — prevent dehydration
  • 💊 IV antibiotics — treat infection
  • 💉 Pain management (NSAIDs or opioids like morphine)
  • 🧪 NG tube — if severe nausea/vomiting

Surgical — Cholecystectomy (Gallbladder Removal)

  • Laparoscopic cholecystectomy — minimally invasive, quicker recovery (preferred)
  • Open cholecystectomy — if severe infection or complications
  • T-tube placement — drains bile if bile duct swollen or blocked

6. Nursing Care

Pre-operative

  • Keep NPO — no eating/drinking
  • Manage pain — avoid fatty foods, administer pain meds
  • Monitor for bile duct obstruction signs: jaundice, clay stools, dark urine

Post-operative

  • Encourage early ambulation — prevents gas pain after laparoscopic surgery
  • Monitor for infection — fever, redness at incision site
  • Advance diet slowly — start with clear liquids, avoid fatty foods
  • T-tube care (if placed):
✅ Keep tube below the surgical site
✅ Monitor output — should be greenish bile (not cloudy or bloody)
⚠️ Never clamp T-tube without a physician order

7. Complications to Watch For

ComplicationSignsNCLEX Note
Peritonitis Rigid abdomen, severe pain, fever Surgical emergency!
Pancreatitis Severe epigastric pain, ↑ amylase & lipase Can occur from blocked bile flow
Post-Cholecystectomy Syndrome Persistent pain after surgery May indicate retained stones or bile duct injury

8. NCLEX Must-Know Summary

✅ Murphy's sign = pain on deep breath + RUQ palpation
✅ Ultrasound = first-line imaging
✅ NPO before surgery; early ambulation after
⚠️ Jaundice + dark urine + clay stools = bile obstruction
⚠️ Never clamp T-tube without order
⚠️ T-tube output: green bile = normal; cloudy/bloody = abnormal
