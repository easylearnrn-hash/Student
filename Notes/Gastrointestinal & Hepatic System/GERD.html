GERD

Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease

1. What is GERD?

  • Stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing irritation and damage
  • Caused by a weak or overly relaxed Lower Esophageal Sphincter (LES)
  • Chronic acid reflux → esophagus damage → risk of ulcers and cancer
🚨 NCLEX: GERD is a chronic condition — requires long-term lifestyle changes & treatment!

2. Causes & Risk Factors

Primary Cause

  • Weak LES — the main cause
  • Hiatal hernia (stomach pushes through diaphragm)

Triggers

  • Obesity (extra abdominal pressure)
  • Pregnancy (hormones + pressure)
  • Smoking & alcohol (relax LES)
  • Spicy, fatty, acidic foods
  • Large meals
💡 Anything that relaxes the LES or increases stomach pressure can trigger GERD!

3. Symptoms

  • 🔥 Heartburn — burning chest pain, worse after eating or lying down
  • 🔄 Regurgitation — acid/food backs up to throat or mouth
  • 😤 Dysphagia — difficulty swallowing
  • 🗣️ Hoarseness — vocal cord irritation
  • 😮‍💨 Chronic cough or sore throat
  • 😮 Bitter taste in mouth
  • 💨 Bad breath (halitosis)
🚨 Heartburn more than twice a week = likely GERD!

4. Diagnosis

TestWhat it Does
EGD (Upper Endoscopy)Camera looks at esophagus for damage or ulcers — Gold Standard!
pH Monitoring (24-hour)Measures acid levels in the esophagus
Barium Swallow X-rayContrast liquid + X-ray to check for reflux
Manometry TestMeasures LES muscle strength
✅ EGD = gold standard for diagnosing GERD complications!

5. Treatment

A. Lifestyle Changes (FIRST LINE!)

Avoid trigger foods Small frequent meals No lying down 2–3 hrs after eating Elevate head of bed Lose weight Quit smoking

Trigger foods: spicy, fatty, fried, citrus, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol

💡 Lifestyle changes BEFORE medications!

B. Medications

Drug ClassExamplesWhat it DoesNCLEX Note
PPIsOmeprazole, Pantoprazole, EsomeprazoleBlocks acid productionTake before meals; long-term → osteoporosis, C. diff
H2 BlockersFamotidine (Pepcid)Reduces stomach acidTake before meals; less effective than PPIs
AntacidsTums, Maalox, MylantaNeutralizes acidFast but temporary; can cause electrolyte imbalances
ProkineticsMetoclopramide (Reglan)Speeds gastric emptyingRisk of tardive dyskinesia
⚠️ PPIs long-term → osteoporosis & C. diff infection risk!

C. Surgical Treatment (Severe GERD)

  • Nissen Fundoplication — wraps stomach top around esophagus to strengthen LES
  • LINX Device — magnetic ring around LES prevents reflux
💡 Surgery only for GERD unresponsive to lifestyle changes & medications

6. Complications (If Untreated)

  • ⚠️ Esophagitis — inflammation, pain, ulcers
  • ⚠️ Barrett's Esophagus — pre-cancerous lining change
  • ⚠️ Esophageal Strictures — narrowing → dysphagia
  • ⚠️ Aspiration Pneumonia — acid enters lungs
  • ⚠️ Esophageal Cancer — long-term GERD risk
🚨 Barrett's Esophagus = HIGHER risk for esophageal adenocarcinoma! Needs regular endoscopy!

7. Nursing Care

  • Encourage small, frequent meals
  • Teach patient: no lying down after eating (wait 2–3 hrs)
  • Monitor for aspiration signs: coughing while eating, wheezing
  • Reinforce medication adherence — PPIs work best before meals
  • Educate on complications: Barrett's Esophagus needs regular endoscopy

8. NCLEX Must-Know Summary

✅ GERD = weak LES → acid refluxes into esophagus
✅ Triggers = spicy, fatty, caffeine, alcohol, smoking
✅ First line = lifestyle changes, then PPIs
⚠️ PPIs long-term → osteoporosis
⚠️ Barrett's Esophagus → esophageal cancer risk
⚠️ Aspiration pneumonia = coughing/wheezing
