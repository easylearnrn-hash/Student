Gastric & Peptic Ulcers

Clinical Focus: Management & Nursing Care

1. Best Practices for Feeding

✅ Eat five small meals daily for better GI health — reduces acid load on the stomach

2. Gastric & Peptic Ulcers Overview

Cause

  • Mucus layer damage from: fatty foods, alcohol, H. pylori, stress
  • Protective stomach lining erodes → exposed to acid

Symptoms

Epigastric pain immediately post-eating Bloating Dyspepsia Abdominal distention

3. Treatment

A. Protective (Reduce Acid & Coat Ulcer)

  • Sucralfate coating
  • Antacids
  • H2 blockers
  • PPIs (Proton Pump Inhibitors)

B. Healing (Eradicate Cause)

  • Lifestyle changes
  • Triple Therapy for H. pylori: Amoxicillin + Clarithromycin + Omeprazole
  • Enteric-coated medications (protects stomach from direct irritation)
💡 Triple therapy = must complete the full antibiotic course!

4. Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPI) — Detail

Examples

Omeprazole Esomeprazole Lansoprazole Dexlansoprazole Pantoprazole Rabeprazole

Rules

  • Short-term only (2–3 weeks)
  • Take on empty stomach (before meals)

Risks

  • ⚠️ Osteoporosis → supplement with calcium + Vitamin D3
  • ⚠️ C. difficile infection
  • ⚠️ Pneumonia
🚨 Long-term PPI use → osteoporosis, C. diff, pneumonia risk. Supplement Ca + Vit D3!

5. Gastric Ulcer Rupture / Perforation

🚨 EMERGENCY — Requires immediate surgical intervention!

Signs & Symptoms

  • 🔥 Severe pain
  • 🩸 Bloody vomit (hematemesis)
  • 🌡️ Fever
  • 💪 Board-like abdomen (peritonitis)
  • 🩸 Melena (tarry black stools)
💡 Board-like rigid abdomen = peritonitis — surgical emergency!

6. Indications for Surgery

  • Refractory ulcers (not responding to meds)
  • Perforation
  • Obstruction
  • Active/uncontrolled bleeding
  • Suspicion of malignancy
  • Giant ulcers

7. Gastric Surgery Variants

ProcedureIndicationNotes
Gastric Bypass Severe GERD, obesity, ulcers Pre-surgery: NPO + patient education
Vagotomy Reduces acid overproduction Cuts vagus nerve
Pyloroplasty Ulcer-induced narrowing Widens stomach outlet

8. NCLEX Must-Know Summary

✅ 5 small meals daily for GI health
✅ Triple therapy: Amoxicillin + Clarithromycin + Omeprazole
✅ PPIs: empty stomach, short-term (2–3 weeks)
⚠️ Board-like abdomen = peritonitis = emergency
⚠️ PPI risks: osteoporosis, C. diff, pneumonia
⚠️ Surgery if: bleeding, perforation, obstruction, malignancy
