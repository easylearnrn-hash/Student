Appendicitis

RLQ Pain McBurney's Point Appendectomy No Laxatives/Enemas

1. Pathophysiology

Inflammation of the appendix — usually caused by obstruction:

  • Fecalith (hardened stool blocking the appendix)
  • Bacterial overgrowth / infection
  • Lymphoid hyperplasia (swelling of immune tissue inside the appendix)

If untreated → rupture → peritonitis → sepsis

2. Symptoms

Early Symptoms

  • Periumbilical pain (around the belly button) — migrates to RLQ
  • Mild nausea, loss of appetite

Classic Symptoms

  • RLQ pain (right lower quadrant)
  • McBurney's point tenderness — pain when pressing over the appendix
  • Rebound tenderness — pain when releasing pressure
  • Rovsing's sign — RLQ pain when pressing on the LLQ
  • Low-grade fever (<100.4°F)
  • Nausea and vomiting

Rupture Symptoms — EMERGENCY

  • Sudden relief of pain (when appendix bursts) → followed by worsening diffuse pain
  • High fever, tachycardia, hypotension (sepsis)
  • Rigid, board-like abdomen

3. Diagnosis

  • Physical exam: McBurney's point, rebound tenderness, Rovsing's sign
  • CBC: ↑ WBC (elevated white blood cells = infection)
  • Ultrasound — first-line for children and pregnant women
  • CT scan — most accurate diagnosis for adults

4. Treatment

  • NPO status (nothing before surgery)
  • IV fluids (prevent dehydration)
  • IV antibiotics (prevent infection)
  • Appendectomy:
    • Laparoscopic — minimally invasive, faster recovery
    • Open — if appendix has ruptured
  • IV analgesics for pain management

5. NCLEX Priorities

Preoperative Nursing Care

  • ✅ Keep NPO (nothing by mouth)
  • ✅ Avoid laxatives/enemas — can cause rupture!
  • ✅ Avoid applying heat — worsens inflammation
  • ✅ Monitor for sudden pain relief — may indicate rupture
  • ✅ Start IV fluids, explain procedure

Postoperative Nursing Care

  • ✅ Early ambulation (prevents blood clots and gas pain)
  • ✅ Monitor for infection: fever, redness, swelling at incision
  • ✅ Advance diet slowly: clear liquids → solid foods
  • ✅ Pain control + encourage deep breathing

6. Complications

⚠ Peritonitis (abdominal infection after rupture) — Rigid abdomen, high fever, tachycardia, rebound tenderness
⚠ Sepsis (widespread infection) — Hypotension, confusion, altered mental status
