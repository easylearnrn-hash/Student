Vegan & Vegetarian Diets

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · Nutrition

🟢 Vegan Diet

Only plant-based foods; excludes ALL animal-derived products (meat, dairy, eggs, honey)

Nutrient Considerations

  • Iron — non-heme iron: lentils, chickpeas, tofu, enriched grains
    Combine with Vitamin C (citrus, peppers) to ↑ absorption
  • Protein — beans, lentils, tofu, quinoa, tempeh, nuts, seeds
  • Vitamin B12 — NOT in plants → supplements or fortified foods required
  • Calcium — fortified plant milks, leafy greens (kale, bok choy), tofu, almonds
  • Vitamin D — sunlight, fortified foods, or supplements
  • Omega-3 (ALA) — flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts; consider algae-based DHA supplements
💡 NCLEX: Always assess vegan patients for B12 deficiency — may present as neuropathy or anemia
🟡 Vegetarian Diet

Plant-based + may include eggs and dairy; excludes meat, poultry, and fish

Nutrient Considerations

  • Typically meet protein, B12, and calcium needs more easily than vegans
  • Iron — still monitor, especially in menstruating women
  • Vitamin B12 — especially in lacto-vegetarians (dairy only, no eggs)
  • Protein — less concern but emphasize variety

NCLEX-Focused Points

  • Monitor for anemia, fatigue, neurological symptoms → B12 or iron deficiency
  • Ensure NG or hospital diets respect patient dietary preferences
  • Educate vegan patients on label reading (hidden animal-derived ingredients)
  • Collaborate with dietitian for complex cases (e.g., pregnant vegan patients)
