Colostomy

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · note_gi_20

What Is a Colostomy?

A surgical opening (stoma) created from the colon (large intestine) to the abdominal wall to allow stool to exit the body through a colostomy bag.

  • Used when part of the colon or rectum is diseased or removed
  • Can be temporary or permanent depending on the indication

Types of Colostomies

TypeLocationStool ConsistencyNotes
AscendingRight abdomenLiquid — digestive enzymes still activeSkin care critical (enzymes damage skin)

SigmoidLower left abdomenNormal/formedOften permanent (e.g., after rectal cancer)
🧠 NCLEX Tip: The lower the stoma on the GI tract → the more formed the stool.

Indications

  • Colorectal cancer
  • Diverticulitis
  • Trauma to colon or rectum
  • Bowel obstruction
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)

Stoma Assessment: Normal vs Abnormal

AssessmentNormal ✅Abnormal ⚠️
Stoma colorPink/red, moist (well-perfused)Pale, dusky, purple, black → ischemia/necrosis 🚨
Stoma shapeSlightly protruding, round or ovalSunken/retracted, herniated, prolapsed, severely swollen
BleedingSmall amount when touched (minimal)Continuous or heavy bleeding
Drainage/outputSigmoid = formed · Ascending = liquidExcessive output (fluid imbalance risk) · No output (obstruction)
Peristomal skinIntact, no rednessRedness, irritation, rash, breakdown (contact dermatitis)
Odor/gasMild odor, some gas is normalFoul odor (infection) · Excessive gas (bag ballooning)
Stoma function post-opBegins within 2–4 daysNo function after 5 days → notify provider
⚠️ Black/necrotic stoma = EMERGENCY → Notify HCP immediately! Poor perfusion = ischemia.

Nursing Care & Patient Teaching

Pouching & Appliance

  • Empty bag when ⅓ full to prevent leakage
  • Change pouching system every 3–7 days (or as needed)
  • Cut wafer ⅛ inch larger than stoma to protect skin
  • Avoid oily/moisturizing soaps — they interfere with pouch adhesion
  • Use adhesive remover wipes (non-alcohol) for residue removal

Diet & Lifestyle

  • Avoid gas-producing foods (beans, broccoli, carbonated drinks) if needed
  • Encourage hydration to prevent constipation
  • Colostomy does not usually restrict activities or travel
  • Resume normal diet unless contraindicated

Cleaning Steps

  1. Gently peel off old pouch/barrier
  2. Inspect stoma and skin (redness, swelling, breakdown)
  3. Clean with warm water + mild, fragrance-free soap
  4. Rinse thoroughly (soap residue irritates skin and affects adhesive)
  5. Pat dry — skin must be completely dry before applying new pouch
  6. Apply new pouch with proper fit
⛔ Do not use alcohol on the stoma itself — too harsh and drying. Use non-alcohol adhesive remover wipes.

Psychosocial Support

  • Body image concerns are common → provide empathy and support
  • Teach how to use, empty, and change the appliance independently
  • Encourage support groups if coping is difficult
  • Assess readiness to learn self-care before teaching
High output colostomy → monitor for fluid and electrolyte imbalance (especially K⁺, Na⁺).
