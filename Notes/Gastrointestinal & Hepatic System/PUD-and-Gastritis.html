Peptic Ulcer Disease & Gastritis

1. What is Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)?

  • Open sores (ulcers) develop in the lining of the stomach or upper small intestine (duodenum)
  • Caused by stomach acid and digestive enzymes damaging the protective lining
💡 Ulcers = breaks in stomach/intestine lining due to too much acid or not enough protection!

2. Causes & Risk Factors

  • 🦠 H. pylori infection — most common cause!
  • 💊 Long-term NSAID use (ibuprofen, naproxen, aspirin) — weaken stomach lining
  • 🚬 Smoking & alcohol — irritate stomach lining
  • 😤 Stress & spicy foods — do NOT cause ulcers but can worsen symptoms
  • 🧬 Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome — rare tumor causing excessive stomach acid
🚨 H. pylori = #1 cause of ulcers! Needs antibiotics!

3. Types of Peptic Ulcers

TypeLocationPain TimingFood Effect
Gastric Ulcer Inside the stomach Pain worsens with eating NOT relieved by food — may worsen
Duodenal Ulcer (most common) First part of small intestine Worse on empty stomach & at night Relieved by eating or antacids
💡 Duodenal ulcers feel better with food! Gastric ulcers feel worse!

4. Symptoms of PUD

  • 🔥 Burning/gnawing epigastric pain
  • 🌙 Pain worse at night (duodenal)
  • 💨 Bloating, belching
  • ⚖️ Weight loss (gastric ulcers)
  • 🤢 Nausea, vomiting
  • 🩸 Melena — black, tarry stools
  • 🩸 Hematemesis — coffee-ground vomit
🚨 Bloody stool OR vomit = GI Bleed! Emergency!

5. Diagnosis

TestWhat It Does
EGD (Upper Endoscopy)Best test! Camera examines stomach & duodenum for ulcers
H. pylori Test (blood, breath, stool, biopsy)Detects H. pylori infection
Barium Swallow X-rayContrast + X-ray to check for ulcers
✅ EGD = best test for diagnosing ulcers and their complications!

6. Treatment of PUD

A. Lifestyle Changes

  • ✅ Avoid NSAIDs → use acetaminophen instead
  • ✅ Stop smoking & limit alcohol
  • ✅ Reduce stress
  • ✅ Eat small, frequent meals
💡 NSAIDs = high risk for ulcers! Educate patients to take with food or avoid!

B. Medications

Drug ClassExamplesWhat It DoesNCLEX Note
PPIsOmeprazole, PantoprazoleBlocks acid productionTake before meals; long-term → osteoporosis
H2 BlockersFamotidine (Pepcid)Reduces acidSafer for long-term use
AntacidsTums, MaaloxNeutralizes acidFast but temporary only
Antibiotics (H. pylori)Amoxicillin + Clarithromycin + MetronidazoleKills H. pyloriMust complete full course!
Mucosal ProtectantsSucralfate, MisoprostolCoats & protects ulcerTake on empty stomach

C. Surgical Treatment (Severe Cases)

  • Vagotomy — cuts vagus nerve to reduce acid production
  • Pyloroplasty — widens stomach outlet if ulcer causes narrowing
🚨 Surgery only for severe ulcers with complications (bleeding, perforation)!

7. Complications of PUD

  • ⚠️ GI Bleeding — hematemesis (vomiting blood), melena (black stool)
  • ⚠️ Perforation — ulcer erodes through stomach wall → peritonitis
  • ⚠️ Gastric Outlet Obstruction — narrowing from scarring
🚨 Sudden severe abdominal pain = Ulcer perforation! Emergency!

8. Gastritis Overview

💡 Gastritis = irritation of the stomach. PUD = open sores. Different!

Causes

  • H. pylori (most common)
  • NSAIDs
  • Alcohol
  • Stress
  • Autoimmune disorders

Symptoms

  • Burning/gnawing stomach pain
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Black tarry stools (if bleeding)

Treatment

  • Remove the cause (stop NSAIDs, alcohol)
  • PPIs, H2 Blockers, Antacids
  • If H. pylori present → antibiotics
✅ H. pylori → antibiotics required. NSAID-induced gastritis → just stop the drug!

9. NCLEX Must-Know Summary

✅ H. pylori = #1 cause — needs antibiotics!
✅ NSAIDs = high ulcer risk — take with food or avoid
✅ Duodenal ulcer = better with food; Gastric = worse
⚠️ Melena/hematemesis = GI bleed — emergency!
⚠️ PPIs long-term → osteoporosis
⚠️ Sudden severe pain = perforation — emergency!
