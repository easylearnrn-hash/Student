Physical Examination Sounds

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · Assessment

NCLEX tests your ability to identify normal vs abnormal sounds during physical assessment — lung, heart, bowel, and vascular — and associate them with clinical conditions.

Lung Sounds (Auscultation)

Stethoscope over chest and back

✅ Normal
  • Vesicular — soft, low-pitched over peripheral lung fields
  • Bronchial — louder, over trachea
  • Bronchovesicular — intermediate, over major bronchi
❗ Abnormal (Adventitious)
  • Crackles (rales) — popping sounds → pneumonia, CHF, pulmonary edema
  • Wheezes — high-pitched whistling → asthma, COPD
  • Rhonchi — low-pitched snoring → secretions in large airways (clears with cough)
  • Stridor — harsh, high-pitched upper airway EMERGENCY
  • Pleural friction rub — grating → pleuritis

Heart Sounds (Auscultation)

5 auscultation points: Aortic, Pulmonic, Erb's Point, Tricuspid, Mitral

✅ Normal
  • S1 ("lub") — AV valves close → start of systole
  • S2 ("dub") — semilunar valves close → start of diastole
❗ Abnormal
  • S3 — early diastole (ventricular gallop) → may indicate heart failure in adults
  • S4 — late diastole (atrial gallop) → stiff ventricle (HTN, MI)
  • Murmur — whooshing, turbulent flow → valve stenosis or regurgitation
  • Click/Snap — prosthetic valves or stenosis
  • Pericardial friction rub — scratching → pericarditis

Bowel Sounds (Auscultation)

Diaphragm of stethoscope in all 4 quadrants

✅ Normal
  • Irregular, high-pitched gurgles every 5–15 seconds
❗ Abnormal
  • Hyperactive — rapid, loud → early obstruction, diarrhea
  • Hypoactive — slow, infrequent → post-op, ileus
  • Absent — no sounds in 5 min → EMERGENCY paralytic ileus or obstruction

Vascular Sounds (Bruits)

Bell of stethoscope over carotid, abdominal aorta, femoral arteries

  • Bruit — turbulent swishing → narrowing (stenosis) or aneurysm
  • Carotid bruit → stroke risk
  • Abdominal bruit → possible abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA)

Percussion Sounds

Tapping body surfaces to assess underlying structures

SoundMeaning / Example
ResonantNormal lung sound (hollow)
HyperresonantAir-trapped lung — emphysema, pneumothorax
DullFluid or solid — pneumonia, mass, liver
FlatOver bone or muscle
TympanyAir-filled space — stomach, bowel

NCLEX Key Points

  • EMERGENCY Stridor = airway obstruction
  • Crackles = CHF or fluid in lungs
  • Bruits = vascular narrowing or aneurysm
  • Murmurs = valvular disease
  • Absent bowel sounds + distention = paralytic ileus or obstruction
  • Always compare bilaterally (lung, heart, vascular sounds)
