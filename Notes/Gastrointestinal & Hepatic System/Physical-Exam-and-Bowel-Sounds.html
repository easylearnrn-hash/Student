Physical Exam & Bowel Sounds

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · note_gi_18

Four Physical Examination Techniques

  1. Inspection — looking at the body to spot visible problems (swelling, discoloration, unusual shapes).
    Done first — avoids changing signs that could be altered by touching or tapping.
  2. Auscultation — listening with stethoscope (heart, lungs, digestive system).
    Done before percussion/palpation — prevents altering internal sounds.
  3. Percussion — tapping on the body to detect fluid, air, or solid masses.
    Done before palpation — avoids causing pain that could change findings.
  4. Palpation — using hands to feel abdomen for lumps, pain, stiffness.
    Done last — can cause pain/tenderness, ensuring most accurate assessment.
⚠️ Abdominal exam order = I → A → P → P (Inspection → Auscultation → Percussion → Palpation)
Unlike other body systems, auscultation precedes percussion and palpation in the abdomen!

Bowel Sound Auscultation

Finding Description Clinical Significance
Normal Gurgling or rumbling; 5–15 clicks/gurgles per minute Active intestinal peristalsis
Hyperactive Loud, high-pitched, rushing sounds ("borborygmi") Diarrhea, gastroenteritis, early bowel obstruction, laxative use, hunger
Hypoactive Decreased/sluggish sounds Post-op ileus, constipation, peritonitis, medication side effect (opioids)
Absent No sounds heard after 5 minutes of auscultation in all 4 quadrants 🚨 EMERGENCY — paralytic ileus, bowel obstruction, peritonitis; report immediately
Auscultate all 4 quadrants for at least 5 minutes per quadrant before declaring bowel sounds absent.

Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA)

During auscultation, a swishing or bruit sound over the abdomen may indicate an AAA.

  • Caused by turbulent blood flow through the aneurysm
  • Bruit = swishing sound → abnormal finding → requires further medical evaluation
  • Never palpate a suspected AAA forcefully (risk of rupture)
⚠️ Bruit over abdomen = suspect AAA → do not aggressively palpate → notify provider immediately.

⚡ NCLEX Priorities

  • Abdominal exam = Inspection → Auscultation → Percussion → Palpation
  • Absent bowel sounds = EMERGENCY → report immediately
  • Auscultate bowel sounds for at least 5 full minutes before documenting as absent
  • Abdominal bruit = suspect AAA → do not palpate → notify provider
  • Hyperactive sounds in post-op patient → early return of bowel function (positive finding)
  • Hypoactive post-op = expected initially; absent after 72 hrs = concern
