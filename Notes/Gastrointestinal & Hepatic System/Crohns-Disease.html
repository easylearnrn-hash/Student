Crohn's Disease

Chronic Inflammatory Bowel Disease — Anywhere in the GI Tract

1. What is Crohn's Disease?

  • Chronic autoimmune inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)
  • Can affect any part of the GI tract from mouth to anus
  • Most commonly affects the terminal ileum (last part of small intestine)
  • Involves transmural inflammation — all layers of the intestinal wall
  • No cure — management only (unlike Ulcerative Colitis)

2. Pathophysiology & Key Characteristics

Tissue Changes

  • Transmural inflammation → ulceration, scarring, strictures
  • Fistulas — abnormal connections between organs
  • Abscesses — pus-filled infections
  • Granulomas on biopsy

Endoscopy Appearance

  • 🪨 Cobblestone appearance — raised inflamed areas between deep ulcers
  • 🎯 Skip lesions — healthy tissue between inflamed areas
  • Bowel wall thickening on CT/MRI
💡 "C" for Crohn's = Cobblestone + Can happen anywhere + Chronic (no cure)

3. Signs & Symptoms

GI Symptoms

  • 🟡 RLQ abdominal pain (terminal ileum involvement)
  • 💩 Chronic diarrhea — often non-bloody (can be bloody)
  • ⚖️ Weight loss (from malabsorption)
  • 💨 Steatorrhea (fatty, greasy stool)

Systemic Symptoms

  • 😴 Fatigue and fever
  • 🩸 Anemia (low RBCs)
  • 👄 Mouth ulcers (aphthous ulcers)
  • 📏 Delayed growth in children

4. Lab Findings & Diagnostics

Labs

  • ↑ ESR & CRP — inflammation markers
  • ↓ Hgb/Hct — anemia
  • ↓ Albumin — malnutrition/malabsorption
  • ↑ WBC — infection/inflammation
  • Positive fecal occult blood

Imaging

  • Colonoscopy — cobblestone mucosa, skip lesions, biopsies
  • CT/MRI — bowel wall thickening, fistulas

5. Nutrition Teaching

✅ Low-residue (low fiber) diet during flare-ups
✅ High-calorie, high-protein diet
✅ Small, frequent meals
⚠️ Avoid dairy, fatty/fried foods, spicy food, alcohol
⚠️ May require TPN if unable to absorb nutrients

7. Surgical Management

⚠️ Surgery is NOT curative for Crohn's — disease can recur after surgery!

Indications

  • Resection — removal of diseased bowel segment
  • Fistula repair
  • Abscess drainage
  • Strictureplasty — widening of narrowed areas

8. Complications

  • Malabsorption — B12, folate, iron deficiencies
  • Fistulas (abnormal organ connections)
  • Abscesses, intestinal obstruction
  • Colon cancer risk (long-term disease)
  • Strictures (bowel narrowing)
  • Psychosocial: depression, body image concerns

9. Nursing Priorities (Flare)

  • NPO → NG tube if vomiting/obstruction suspected
  • IV fluids and electrolytes
  • Administer corticosteroids as ordered
  • Monitor bowel sounds and stool output
  • Daily weights and I&O
  • Monitor for perforation/sepsis: sudden severe pain, fever, tachycardia
  • Educate on medication adherence
  • Avoid laxatives and high-fiber foods during flares

11. NCLEX Must-Know Summary

✅ Anywhere in GI tract — most common: terminal ileum (RLQ pain)
✅ Transmural, skip lesions, cobblestone
✅ Low-fiber + high-protein diet during flares
✅ Biologics (infliximab) for severe disease
⚠️ No cure — surgery does NOT cure (unlike UC)
⚠️ Fistulas = hallmark complication
⚠️ B12, folate, iron deficiencies common
⚠️ Corticosteroids: taper slowly!
