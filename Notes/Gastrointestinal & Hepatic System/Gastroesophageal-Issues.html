Gastroesophageal Issues

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · note_gi_15

Esophageal Anatomy

  • Epiglottis — prevents food from entering the airway during swallowing; epiglottitis caused by droplet infection
  • Esophagus — muscular tube connecting throat to stomach; has Lower Esophageal Sphincter (LES) at bottom
  • Unlike the stomach, the esophagus lacks a protective mucus layer → vulnerable to acid damage

GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease)

LES fails to function properly → stomach acid flows back into the esophagus.

Causes / Risk Factors

  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Fatty & spicy foods
  • Peppermint
  • Dairy products
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Hiatal hernia (stomach protrudes through diaphragm)
  • Certain medications (NSAIDs)

Symptoms

  • Heartburn
  • Epigastric pain
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Regurgitation
  • Burping
  • Chest pain
  • Acid taste/smell
  • Bloating

Treatment — Lifestyle Modifications

  • Elevate head of bed (30–45°)
  • Wear loose clothing
  • Modify diet (avoid triggers)
  • Quit smoking
  • Lose weight if obese

Medications

Drug ClassExamplesKey NCLEX Notes
Antacids Tums, Maalox Neutralize acid; avoid taking other meds at the same time (wait ≥1 hour)
PPIs (Proton Pump Inhibitors) Omeprazole, Esomeprazole Reduce acid production; take on empty stomach; short-term use (2–3 weeks) to avoid osteoporosis, C. diff, pneumonia; supplement Ca + Vit D3
H2 Blockers Ranitidine, Famotidine Decrease acid production; less potent than PPIs
⚠️ PPIs → long-term use risk: osteoporosis, C. diff, pneumonia. Supplement calcium + Vitamin D3.

Esophageal Cancer

  • Chronic acid exposure → Barrett's esophagus (metaplasia) → adenocarcinoma
  • Risk factors: GERD, obesity, tobacco use
Barrett's esophagus = pre-cancerous lesion from chronic GERD → requires endoscopic surveillance.

Esophageal Atresia & Tracheoesophageal Fistula (TEF)

Definitions

  • Esophageal Atresia — esophagus does not connect to stomach (congenital)
  • TEF — abnormal connection between esophagus and trachea
  • Often in preterm infants (GI system not fully developed)

Symptoms (3 C's + more)

  • Coughing
  • Choking
  • Cyanosis during feeding
  • Drooling (excessive)
  • Apnea
  • Distended abdomen
  • Frothy saliva
Management: Immediate NPO (nothing by mouth) + surgical intervention.

⚡ NCLEX Priorities

  • PPIs = take on empty stomach; avoid long-term use
  • Antacids = separate from other meds by ≥1 hour
  • Barrett's esophagus = chronic GERD complication → cancer risk
  • Esophageal atresia/TEF in neonate → NPO immediately, prepare for surgery
  • Preterm baby + coughing/choking/cyanosis during feeding → suspect TEF
