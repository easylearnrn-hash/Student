Overview

The Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) controls involuntary body functions — heart rate, digestion, respiration, pupil size. It has two opposing branches that keep the body in balance.

💥 Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS)

"Fight or Flight"

  • Activates the body for emergencies and stress
  • Neurotransmitter: Norepinephrine (+ epinephrine from adrenal medulla)
  • Receptors: Adrenergic (alpha and beta)
🌿 Parasympathetic Nervous System (PNS)

"Rest and Digest"

  • Calms the body; conserves energy; promotes normal functions
  • Neurotransmitter: Acetylcholine (ACh)
  • Receptors: Cholinergic (muscarinic, nicotinic)

Mnemonics

💥 Sympathetic — "A-B-C"

A — Adrenergic (norepinephrine/epinephrine)

B — Bronchodilation, BP ↑, Blood sugar ↑

C — Cardiac ↑ HR, Cold (vasoconstriction = cool extremities)

🌿 Parasympathetic — "S-L-U-D-D"

S — Salivation

L — Lacrimation (tears)

U — Urination

D — Digestion

D — Defecation

NCLEX Practice Questions

Q1

A patient with asthma is given albuterol. Which part of the ANS does this drug stimulate?

✅ Sympathetic — albuterol is a beta-2 adrenergic agonist → causes bronchodilation (opens airways)

Q2

Atropine is administered to a patient with bradycardia. What effect is expected?

✅ Increased heart rate — atropine is anticholinergic, blocks PNS (vagal tone) → allows SNS to increase HR

Q3

Which finding indicates parasympathetic stimulation?

✅ Increased bowel sounds + urinary urgency — PNS promotes "rest and digest" → GI motility + bladder contraction
