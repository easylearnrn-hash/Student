Neurology · Diagnostics

Neuro Imaging & Diagnostics
CT · MRI · EEG · Lumbar Puncture

NCLEX-focused study note — when to order each test · contraindications · patient preparation

Overview

Neurological diagnostics are ordered to identify the cause of altered mental status, neurological deficits, seizures, head trauma, or suspected CNS pathology. Choosing the right test at the right time is a core NCLEX skill.

CT Scan (Computed Tomography)

What It Does
  • Uses X-rays to create cross-sectional brain images
  • Fast — results in minutes
  • First-line in emergencies (trauma, stroke)
Best Detects
  • Hemorrhage (bleeds) — appears bright white
  • Skull fractures
  • Mass effect (tumor, swelling)
  • Hydrocephalus (fluid buildup)
⭐
NCLEX #1 Rule — CT Before tPA

CT scan must always be ordered first in suspected stroke to rule out hemorrhage before administering tPA (tissue plasminogen activator). Giving tPA to a hemorrhagic stroke patient is fatal.

CT With Contrast

Pre-Contrast Nursing Assessment
  • Ask about allergies — iodine, shellfish (contrast contains iodine)
  • Check kidney function (BUN, creatinine) — contrast is nephrotoxic
  • Contrast helps highlight blood vessels and tumors

MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)

What It Does
  • Uses strong magnets + radio waves (no radiation)
  • Takes 30–60 minutes — not for acute emergencies
  • Best for soft tissue detail
Best For
  • Brain tumors, MS plaques
  • Brain stem and spinal cord lesions
  • Soft tissue injuries
  • Posterior fossa pathology
🚫
MRI Absolute Contraindications

Pacemakers · Metal implants/clips (cochlear implants, surgical clips, shrapnel) · Cochlear implants. Screen ALL patients before entering MRI room — the magnetic field can be lethal with ferromagnetic devices.

EEG (Electroencephalogram)

What It Measures
  • Electrical activity of the brain (brain waves)
  • Non-invasive — electrodes placed on scalp
Used For
  • Seizure diagnosis and classification
  • Brain death determination
  • Sleep disorders (monitoring sleep stages)
  • Encephalopathy evaluation

EEG Preparation — Nursing Instructions

  • ✅ Hair must be clean, dry, and free of oils/products
  • ✅ Patient can eat — do NOT fast (hypoglycemia alters brain waves)
  • 🚫 Hold sedatives and stimulants (caffeine, alcohol) before test — as ordered
  • ✅ Seizure meds may be held if ordered (to capture seizure activity)

Lumbar Puncture (Spinal Tap)

Purpose
  • Collects cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) for analysis
  • Diagnoses: meningitis, MS, subarachnoid hemorrhage
Patient Position
  • Lateral side-lying fetal position (most common) — knees to chest, chin down
  • OR sitting upright, leaning forward over a table
  • Goal: open intervertebral space for needle insertion
🚨
Contraindication — Increased ICP

Lumbar puncture is contraindicated when ICP is elevated. Sudden release of CSF pressure from below can cause brain herniation — a rapidly fatal complication. Always confirm CT scan is normal before LP if ICP is suspected.

Post-LP Nursing Care

  • Keep patient flat (prone or supine) for 1–4 hours to prevent post-LP headache (CSF leak)
  • Encourage oral fluids to replenish CSF volume
  • Monitor for headache, numbness, tingling, or bladder dysfunction
  • Assess puncture site for bleeding or leakage
