What Is Parkinson's Disease?

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is a chronic, progressive neurodegenerative disorder affecting movement. It results from the loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra of the brain.

Pathophysiology
  • Degeneration of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra
  • ↓ Dopamine → ↑ acetylcholine effect → abnormal motor activity
  • Result: tremors, rigidity, bradykinesia, postural instability

Classic "TRAP" Signs

T
Tremor
At rest — "pill-rolling" motion of thumb and fingers
R
Rigidity
Cogwheel stiffness — jerky resistance to passive movement
A
Akinesia
Absence or slowing of voluntary movement (bradykinesia)
P
Postural Instability
Unsteady gait, festination (shuffling), fall risk

Other Symptoms

Motor
  • Shuffling gait, short steps (festination)
  • Stooped, forward-leaning posture
  • Masked face — reduced facial expression (hypomimia)
  • Drooling (excess saliva — bradykinesia of swallowing)
  • Dysarthria (soft, slurred speech)
Non-Motor
  • Dysphagia — aspiration risk
  • Constipation
  • Orthostatic hypotension — fall risk on standing
  • Depression, sleep disturbances
  • Dementia (late-stage)

Diagnosis

Primarily clinical — based on the TRAP symptom triad. A positive response to levodopa therapy helps confirm diagnosis. MRI/CT may rule out other causes.

Medications

DrugClass / MechanismNCLEX Notes
Levodopa/Carbidopa (Sinemet) Dopamine precursor Cornerstone Therapy Levodopa → converts to dopamine in brain. Carbidopa → prevents breakdown in bloodstream (lets more reach brain).
Take before meals or on empty stomach. Avoid high-protein meals. Monitor for orthostatic hypotension. Watch for dyskinesias (involuntary movements) with long-term use.
Ropinirole, PramipexoleDopamine agonistsMimic dopamine in brain; used alone or with levodopa
Selegiline, RasagilineMAO-B inhibitorsSlow dopamine breakdown; neuroprotective
EntacaponeCOMT inhibitorBoosts effect of levodopa; extends its duration
BenztropineAnticholinergicReduces tremors and rigidity. Caution — avoid in elderly (confusion, urinary retention, constipation)
⭐
Levodopa/Carbidopa NCLEX Must-Knows

• Take on empty stomach (food reduces absorption)
• Avoid vitamin B6 in large amounts (reduces levodopa effect)
• Orthostatic hypotension — teach to rise slowly
• On-off effect — periods of adequate control alternating with loss of control
• Dyskinesias — involuntary movements with long-term use → report to provider

Nursing Interventions

Safety — Falls
  • Assistive devices — cane, walker
  • Raised toilet seats, grab bars
  • Remove rugs and trip hazards
  • Monitor orthostatic hypotension
  • Allow extra time for ADLs
Nutrition & Swallowing
  • Small, frequent, easy-to-chew meals
  • Thickened liquids for dysphagia
  • Elevate HOB during eating
  • Suction at bedside — aspiration risk
Rehab & Communication
  • Physical therapy and ROM exercises
  • Speech therapy for dysarthria
  • Occupational therapy for ADLs
  • Monitor for depression
