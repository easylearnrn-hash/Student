What Is Vertigo?

Vertigo is the false sensation of spinning or motion when the body is actually still. It originates from the inner ear (vestibular system). Ménière's disease is one of the most clinically important causes on NCLEX.

What Is Ménière's Disease?

Ménière's disease is a chronic inner ear disorder caused by excess endolymph fluid buildup in the labyrinth, which interferes with hearing and balance signals to the brain.

It is chronic and episodic — attacks come and go over time. Currently has no cure.

Symptom 1
🌀

Symptom 2
🔔
Tinnitus

Symptom 3
🔇
Sensorineural Hearing Loss
Fluctuating; typically one-sided (unilateral)
⭐
NCLEX Classic Triad — Must Memorize

Vertigo + Tinnitus + Fluctuating unilateral sensorineural hearing loss = Ménière's disease. This triad appears frequently on NCLEX. Add ear fullness/pressure as a 4th associated symptom.

Additional symptoms during episodes: nausea and vomiting, fullness or pressure in ear, nystagmus (involuntary rapid eye movements), ataxia (unsteady balance), severe fatigue after episodes.

Triggers That Worsen Symptoms

Dietary Triggers — Avoid
  • High sodium — increases endolymph fluid retention
  • Caffeine (coffee, tea, sodas)
  • Alcohol
  • Tobacco/nicotine
Lifestyle Triggers
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Rapid head movements or position changes
  • Bright lights and loud sounds during attacks
  • Fatigue or sleep deprivation

Nursing Interventions

Safety — Falls Priority
  • Fall precautions — #1 priority with vertigo
  • Assist with ambulation; use side rails
  • Remove trip hazards, rugs
  • Instruct to lie down during attacks
During Acute Attacks
  • Quiet, dark room
  • Minimize head and eye movement
  • Keep emesis basin at bedside
  • Administer ordered antivertigo/antiemetic meds
Patient Teaching
  • Low-sodium diet — limit <2,000 mg/day
  • Avoid caffeine, alcohol, tobacco
  • Change positions slowly
  • Vestibular rehab exercises for balance
  • Compliance with diuretics

Surgical Options (If Medications Fail)

ProcedureDescriptionHearing Effect
Endolymphatic shuntDrains excess endolymph fluid from inner earHearing preserved
Vestibular nerve sectionCuts the vestibular nerve to stop vertigo signalsHearing preserved
LabyrinthectomyRemoves the labyrinth (balance organs); last resortPermanent hearing loss
