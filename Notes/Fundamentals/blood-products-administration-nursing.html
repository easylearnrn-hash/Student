ADMINISTRATION OF BLOOD PRODUCTS

💉 Blood products save lives by replacing what the body is missing.

This guide covers the critical nursing responsibilities for safe blood transfusions.

WHY GIVE BLOOD PRODUCTS?

Types of Blood Products

Blood products replace what the body is missing:

  • Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBCs): Red blood cells to carry oxygen
  • Platelets: Cells that help blood clot
  • Plasma: Liquid part of blood with clotting factors
  • Cryoprecipitate: Very rich in specific clotting proteins like fibrinogen

PRE-CHECKS BEFORE GIVING BLOOD

Important Pre-Administration Checks

  • Doctor's order: Need a prescription
  • Type and crossmatch: Lab test to find matching donor blood
  • Consent form signed: Patient agrees
  • Vital signs taken: Temperature, blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate before starting
  • IV access: Usually large-bore IV → 18 or 20 gauge

🧠 NCLEX TIP: If fever before starting → call doctor before giving blood.

HOW TO ADMINISTER BLOOD SAFELY

Safe Administration Steps

1. Check blood product with another nurse:

  • Right patient
  • Right blood type
  • Right unit

2. Start blood slowly:

  • Run first 15 minutes very slowly
  • Watch for transfusion reactions (body reacting badly to the blood)
  • Stay with patient for first 15 minutes

3. Infusion guidelines:

  • Infuse within 4 hours (never longer—risk of bacteria growing)
  • Only use normal saline (NS) (0.9% sodium chloride) to prime and flush IV
  • No meds in the same IV line during transfusion!

⚠️ CRITICAL RULE: Stay with the patient for the first 15 minutes of transfusion. Most reactions occur early!

TRANSFUSION REACTION SIGNS

Signs of Transfusion Reaction (NCLEX LOVES THIS)

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Back pain
  • Dark urine (hemoglobinuria): Breakdown of red cells
  • Hypotension: Low blood pressure
  • Tachycardia: Fast heart rate
  • Difficulty breathing (dyspnea)

🧠 NCLEX TIP: If reaction suspected →

  1. STOP transfusion
  2. Start NS with new tubing
  3. Notify doctor
  4. Stay with patient

SPECIAL BLOOD ADMINISTRATION FACTS

Important Storage & Handling Facts

  • Platelets: Must be used quickly → room temperature storage
  • PRBCs: Must be refrigerated → cold storage
  • Irradiated blood: Treated with radiation, used for patients with weak immune systems

COAGULATION STUDIES (CLOTTING TESTS)

Test Normal Range What It Means
PT (Prothrombin Time) 11-13.5 seconds Time to form clot (extrinsic pathway)
INR (International Normalized Ratio) 0.8-1.1 Standardized clotting test; 2-3 if on warfarin
aPTT (Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time) 30-40 seconds Clotting time (intrinsic pathway)

🧠 NCLEX TIP: High INR = bleed risk. Low INR = clot risk.

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR NCLEX:

  • Check ID and blood type carefully before transfusion (two-nurse verification)
  • Vital signs: Before, during, and after blood transfusion
  • STOP transfusion immediately if reaction suspected
  • Never add meds into blood tubing
  • Only use normal saline (0.9% NS) to prime and flush
  • Stay with patient for first 15 minutes
  • Infuse within 4 hours maximum
  • Know basic lab values (CBC, coagulation, electrolytes)
  • Know transfusion reaction symptoms and steps to take

🩸 MEMORY TRICK: "Stop-Saline-Stay-Notify"

If transfusion reaction → STOP transfusion → Start NS with new tubing → STAY with patient → NOTIFY doctor
