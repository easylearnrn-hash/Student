AMPUTATION NURSING CARE

💡 Amputation care requires physical, emotional, and rehabilitative nursing support.

This guide covers the essential nursing care for amputation patients from pre-op through rehabilitation.

WHAT IS AMPUTATION?

Definition

Amputation is the surgical removal of all or part of a limb (arm, leg, fingers, toes).

It can be:

  • Elective (planned): Example: from poor blood flow
  • Traumatic (accident): Example: car crash or crush injury

WHY DO PEOPLE NEED AMPUTATION?

Common Causes

  • Poor circulation (ischemia): Lack of blood flow
  • Infection (gangrene): Dead tissue
  • Trauma: Accidents
  • Cancer: Bone tumors
  • Frostbite: Freezing damage

🧠 NCLEX TIP: Diabetes is a top cause → leads to poor circulation and infection.

PRE-OP (BEFORE SURGERY) CARE

Pre-Operative Nursing Care

  • Explain the procedure to the patient
  • Assess circulation: Pulses, color, temperature
  • Consent form must be signed
  • Teach about phantom limb sensation: Feeling the missing part (this is normal!)
  • Emotional support: Grieving is normal!

POST-OP (AFTER SURGERY) CARE

Post-Operative Nursing Care

1. Monitor for bleeding and swelling

  • Check surgical site: drainage, color, temperature

2. Pain control:

  • Real pain at surgical site
  • Phantom pain: Pain felt in missing limb (treat with medications)

3. Positioning:

  • Elevate stump (residual limb) for first 24-48 hours → helps prevent swelling
  • Avoid contractures (tight muscles that freeze the joint):
    • No pillow under stump long-term
    • Encourage lying flat (prone) several times a day

🧠 NCLEX TIP: First 24-48 hrs: elevate the stump → after that, avoid elevation!

⚠️ POSITIONING TRICK QUESTION:

First 24-48 hours: Elevate stump (reduces swelling)

After 48 hours: Lie flat/prone (prevents contractures)

REHABILITATION

Rehabilitation & Recovery

  • Physical therapy: Strengthen other muscles
  • Stump care:
    • Keep clean and dry
    • Avoid lotion unless ordered
  • Prepare for prosthesis (artificial limb):
    • Compression bandages shape stump
  • Emotional support: Grief, depression, and body image issues are common

COMMON COMPLICATIONS

Watch For These Complications

  • Infection: Monitor surgical site for redness, warmth, drainage
  • Delayed healing: Poor blood flow can slow recovery
  • Phantom limb pain: Pain in missing limb (treat with meds)
  • Contractures: Joint freezes in wrong position (prevent with positioning)

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR NCLEX:

  • Phantom pain is REAL → treat it with medications (pain relievers, antidepressants, anti-seizure meds)
  • Contracture prevention → lie prone (on stomach) several times a day
  • Compression bandages → shape stump for prosthesis
  • Emotional support is critical → allow grieving
  • Assess color, warmth, and pulses frequently after surgery
  • Positioning changes at 48 hours:
    • First 24-48 hrs = elevate
    • After 48 hrs = flat/prone
  • Stump care: Keep clean and dry, no lotion unless ordered
  • Diabetes is the #1 cause of amputation

🦵 MEMORY TRICK: "Elevate Early, Flatten Forever"

First 24-48 hours → Elevate (reduce swelling)

After 48 hours → Flatten/prone (prevent contractures)
