MASLOW'S HIERARCHY OF NEEDS

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs is a key concept in nursing and is frequently tested on the NCLEX.

It helps prioritize patient care by addressing needs in order of importance, from basic physiological needs to self-actualization.

THE FIVE LEVELS OF MASLOW'S HIERARCHY

1⃣ PHYSIOLOGICAL NEEDS (MOST BASIC, HIGHEST PRIORITY)

  • Airway, breathing, circulation (ABCs)
  • Oxygenation
  • Nutrition and hydration
  • Elimination
  • Temperature regulation
  • Pain management

2⃣ SAFETY AND SECURITY

  • Risk for falls
  • Infection control
  • Medication safety
  • Protection from harm (e.g., abuse, self-harm, medical errors)

3⃣ LOVE AND BELONGING

  • Emotional support
  • Family involvement
  • Social relationships

4⃣ SELF-ESTEEM

  • Encouraging independence
  • Promoting confidence
  • Supporting body image concerns

5⃣ SELF-ACTUALIZATION (LOWEST PRIORITY ON NCLEX)

  • Personal growth
  • Fulfilling potential

NCLEX PRIORITIZATION TIPS

⚠️ CRITICAL RULES:
  • Always prioritize physiological needs first, especially ABCs
  • Safety is the next priority if no immediate physiological threats exist
  • Psychosocial needs (love, esteem, and self-actualization) are addressed after physical and safety needs are met
✅ MEMORY TIP:

When two patients need help, ask yourself: "Who will die first if I don't help them?"

Physiological and safety needs always come before emotional or self-esteem needs.

NCLEX PRACTICE QUESTIONS

QUESTION 1: PHYSIOLOGICAL NEEDS (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

A nurse is caring for a client who was in a motor vehicle accident and is now experiencing severe shortness of breath. What is the priority nursing intervention?

A) Administering pain medication.

B) Placing the client in a high-Fowler's position and applying oxygen. ✅

C) Encouraging the client to take deep breaths.

D) Providing emotional support to reduce anxiety.

Rationale:

Airway and breathing take priority according to Maslow's Hierarchy. ABCs always come first.

QUESTION 2: SAFETY AND SECURITY NEEDS

A nurse is assigned to care for a confused elderly client who keeps trying to get out of bed without assistance. What intervention should the nurse implement first?

A) Apply wrist restraints to prevent falls.

B) Request a family member to stay with the client.

C) Place the call light within reach and use a bed alarm. ✅

D) Administer a sedative as prescribed.

Rationale:

Ensuring safety without unnecessary restraints or sedation is the priority. Bed alarms and call lights are the least restrictive interventions.

QUESTION 3: LOVE AND BELONGING NEEDS

A 35-year-old client with terminal cancer is crying and states, "I feel so alone." What is the best nursing action?

A) Offer to call a spiritual advisor for the client.

B) Sit with the client and actively listen. ✅

C) Encourage the client to think positively.

D) Provide pain medication to help the client relax.

Rationale:

Addressing emotional needs and providing companionship supports love and belonging. Therapeutic presence is key.

QUESTION 4: SELF-ESTEEM NEEDS

A client recovering from a stroke is frustrated because they can no longer independently button their shirt. Which intervention best promotes self-esteem?

A) Encourage the client to practice buttoning shirts despite frustration. ✅

B) Provide Velcro clothing to make dressing easier.

C) Tell the client that being able to button a shirt is not important.

D) Perform the dressing task for the client to reduce stress.

Rationale:

Encouraging independence helps build self-esteem. While Velcro clothing may be practical, promoting independence through practice supports psychological well-being.

QUESTION 5: SELF-ACTUALIZATION NEEDS (LOWEST PRIORITY)

A nurse is caring for a retired teacher who expresses a desire to teach underprivileged children as part of a community service project. How should the nurse respond?

A) "That's wonderful! How can I support you in achieving this goal?" ✅

B) "Right now, your focus should be on your recovery first."

C) "Maybe you should wait until you feel 100% better before considering this."

D) "You should find something less stressful to do."

Rationale:

Self-actualization focuses on achieving personal growth and fulfillment. Supporting the patient's goals promotes holistic care.

HIGH-YIELD NCLEX STRATEGIES

🔥 KEY TAKEAWAYS:
  • ABCs ALWAYS come first – Airway, Breathing, Circulation
  • If no life-threatening physiological issues, focus on safety
  • Psychosocial needs are important but addressed AFTER physical needs
  • In NCLEX questions, look for keywords like "priority", "first", "immediate"
  • When in doubt, ask: "Which need must be met for survival?"
  • Remember: You can't teach a patient who can't breathe, and you can't promote self-esteem if they're falling
💡 CLINICAL APPLICATION:
  • Use Maslow's Hierarchy to prioritize multiple patients in triage or assignment scenarios
  • Apply the hierarchy when creating nursing care plans
  • Remember that pain management is physiological, not just comfort
  • Safety concerns (falls, medication errors) always trump teaching or emotional support
  • Once basic needs are met, addressing psychosocial needs promotes holistic healing
