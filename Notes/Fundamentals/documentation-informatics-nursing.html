DOCUMENTATION & INFORMATICS

What is Documentation?

Documentation is recording everything about patient care in a legal medical record.

🧠 NCLEX Golden Rule: If you didn't chart it, it didn't happen!

BASIC RULES FOR DOCUMENTATION

Accurate

Exact, correct information

Objective

Stick to facts (no opinions)

Complete

Include all important information

⏰ Timely

Chart as soon as possible after care

Legible

Clear writing (most charting is electronic now)

✅ Good Example: "Patient states pain is 8/10"

❌ Bad Example: "Patient seems fussy"

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Always document patient teaching and their response.

WHAT TO INCLUDE IN DOCUMENTATION

Always Document These:

  • Assessments (example: vital signs, lung sounds)
  • Nursing interventions (example: dressing changes, giving meds)
  • Patient's response (example: pain went from 8 to 3)
  • Changes in condition
  • Communication with providers (example: you called the doctor and what was said)
  • Refusal of treatment

WHAT NOT TO INCLUDE

Never Document These:

  • Never chart for someone else
  • No opinions, blame, or criticism
  • No guessing or fake entries
  • No erasing or deleting

INFORMATICS (Technology in Nursing)

What is Informatics?

Informatics = using computers and technology to manage health information.

Examples of Informatics Tools

Electronic Health Record (EHR)

Online patient chart

Bar-code Medication Administration (BCMA)

Scanning meds and patient ID before giving

Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS)

Computer alerts that guide decisions

Telehealth

Virtual health care visits

KEY INFORMATICS SAFETY POINTS

Protect Patient Privacy

  • Confidentiality: Follow HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) → protects patient privacy
  • Passwords: Never share logins
  • Log off computer when done
  • Only access charts you need for your job

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Accessing patient info without a need = HIPAA violation!

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

Key Points to Remember

  • Chart facts only, no opinions
  • Chart immediately after care
  • HIPAA protects patient information
  • Use technology correctly — log out, no sharing passwords
  • Document refusals and who you notified

MEMORY TRICKS

  • "If you didn't chart it, it didn't happen" — Always document!
  • HIPAA = "Hide Information, Protect All Accurately"
  • Log out = "Lock Out Unauthorized Tampering"
  • Document the 3 R's: Response, Refusal, Reported (to whom)
