CLIENT POSITIONING

What is Client Positioning?

Client positioning = how we place the patient's body to help breathing, healing, comfort, and prevent complications.

🧠 NCLEX Tip: The right position can save lives or improve recovery!

COMMON POSITIONS AND WHEN TO USE THEM

1⃣ FOWLER'S POSITION (Sitting Up in Bed)

Type Angle Used For
Low Fowler's 15–30° Resting, easy breathing
Semi-Fowler's 30–45° Feeding, prevent aspiration (choking)
High Fowler's 60–90° Severe breathing problems (like COPD), NG tube insertion

🧠 NCLEX Tip: High Fowler's = best for breathing problems!

2⃣ SUPINE POSITION (Lying Flat on the Back)

Used For:

  • Sleeping
  • After some surgeries (unless ordered otherwise)

3⃣ PRONE POSITION (Lying on the Stomach)

Used For:

  • COVID-19 severe lung problems (helps lungs open)
  • After some spinal surgeries

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Prone = belly down = better oxygenation sometimes.

4⃣ LATERAL (Side-Lying) POSITION (Lying on One Side)

Used For:

  • Preventing pressure ulcers
  • Seizure recovery (helps open airway)

5⃣ SIMS' POSITION (Left Side-Lying, Right Knee Bent)

Used For:

  • Giving enemas
  • Rectal exams

6⃣ TRENDELENBURG POSITION (Head Lower Than Feet)

Bed tilted so head is lower than feet

Used For:

  • Shock (temporary if low blood pressure)
  • Central line insertion (helps fill neck veins)

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Trendelenburg = head down to improve blood flow to heart.

7⃣ REVERSE TRENDELENBURG POSITION (Head Higher Than Feet)

Bed tilted so head is higher than feet

Used For:

  • After some surgeries to reduce pressure on brain or abdomen

8⃣ ORTHOPNEIC POSITION (Sitting Up, Leaning Over Table)

Used For:

  • Severe breathing problems (makes it easier to expand lungs)

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

High Fowler's

Best for breathing problems

Sims' Position

Left side-lying for enemas

Trendelenburg

Head down for low blood pressure/shock

Orthopneic

Sitting up, leaning forward for severe breathing issues (tripod position)

QUICK POSITION GUIDE

  • Breathing problems? → High Fowler's or Orthopneic
  • Enema or rectal exam? → Sims' position (left side-lying)
  • Shock or low blood pressure? → Trendelenburg (temporarily)
  • Prevent aspiration during feeding? → Semi-Fowler's
  • COVID-19 ARDS? → Prone (belly down)
  • Seizure recovery? → Lateral (side-lying)
  • Post-surgery (general)? → Supine (unless contraindicated)

MEMORY TRICKS

  • High Fowler's = "High for Breathing" (elevated helps lungs)
  • Sims' = "Sims Enemas" (left side for rectal procedures)
  • Trendelenburg = "Trend Down" (head trends down for shock)
  • Prone = "Prone to Belly" (face down position)
  • Orthopneic = "Ortho-Breathe" (tripod position for breathing)
