PRECAUTIONS

STANDARD PRECAUTIONS

Standard precautions = basic infection control for ALL patients, all the time, no matter what!

Includes:

  • Hand hygiene (wash or sanitize hands)
  • Gloves when touching blood, body fluids, broken skin, or mucous membranes
  • Mask, gown, eye protection if splashing is likely
  • Safe sharps disposal (never recap needles!)
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

Use standard precautions with every single patient — even if they look healthy.

TRANSMISSION-BASED (SPECIAL) PRECAUTIONS

When a patient has a known infection, you add extra protections based on how the germ spreads.

There are three types:

1⃣ AIRBORNE PRECAUTIONS

(For very tiny germs that float in the air)

Used for:

  • Tuberculosis (TB)
  • Measles
  • Chickenpox (Varicella)

What to do:

  • Private room with negative pressure (air sucked out so germs don't float away)
  • N95 respirator mask (special tight mask)
  • Keep door closed
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

Airborne = N95 mask and negative pressure room!

2⃣ DROPLET PRECAUTIONS

(For bigger germs that travel about 3-6 feet when coughing, sneezing, talking)

Used for:

  • Influenza (flu)
  • Mumps
  • Meningitis
  • COVID-19 (early stages, depending on hospital policy)

What to do:

  • Private room (or share with same infection)
  • Surgical mask when within 3 feet
  • Patient wears mask if leaving room

3⃣ CONTACT PRECAUTIONS

(For germs spread by touching)

Used for:

  • MRSA (Methicillin-Resistant Staphylococcus Aureus)
  • C. diff (Clostridioides difficile)
  • VRE (Vancomycin-Resistant Enterococcus)
  • Open wounds with drainage

What to do:

  • Private room (or share with same infection)
  • Gloves and gown when entering room
  • Equipment stays in room or is disinfected
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

C. diff = wash hands with soap and water, NOT alcohol gel.

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

✅ KEY TAKEAWAYS:
  • Standard precautions = always (every patient)
  • Airborne = N95 + negative pressure
  • Droplet = surgical mask within 3 feet
  • Contact = gloves and gown
  • Handwashing (soap + water) needed after C. diff!
💡 CLINICAL APPLICATION:
  • Always check the isolation sign on the patient's door before entering
  • Don PPE (put on) before entering the room, doff (take off) after leaving
  • Remember: Gloves come off LAST when removing PPE
  • For C. diff: Use soap and water because alcohol doesn't kill spores
  • N95 fit testing is required annually for healthcare workers
  • When in doubt, err on the side of more protection
