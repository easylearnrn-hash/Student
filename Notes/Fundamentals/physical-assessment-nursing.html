PHYSICAL ASSESSMENT

Physical assessment is the hands-on check a nurse performs to gather facts about a patient's health.

You observe, touch, listen, and sometimes tap to find problems early and provide accurate nursing care.

STEPS OF PHYSICAL ASSESSMENT

ALWAYS IN THIS ORDER!

Inspection → Palpation → Percussion → Auscultation

Exception: For the abdomen → Inspect, Auscultate, Percuss, Palpate

(To avoid disturbing bowel sounds)

INSPECTION (LOOK)

Description: Looking carefully with your eyes to observe the patient's physical condition.

What to Check:

  • Body size, shape, and color
  • Look for swelling, redness, bruises, rashes
  • Watch how the patient moves and acts
  • Note symmetry (is left side same as right?)
  • Observe posture and body alignment
  • Check for any obvious abnormalities

Always done FIRST to get baseline information before touching or manipulating the body.

PALPATION (FEEL)

Description: Using hands to feel skin, muscles, and organs.

What to Check:

  • Temperature: Hot, warm, or cold (use back of hand/dorsum for temperature)
  • Texture: Rough, smooth, dry, moist
  • Tenderness: Pain when touched
  • Swelling: Edema, masses, lumps
  • Pulses: Strength and regularity
  • Organ size: Liver, spleen enlargement
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

Use the back of your hand (dorsum) to check temperature - it's more sensitive!

PERCUSSION (TAP)

Description: Tapping on the body to hear different sounds.

Sounds You'll Hear:

  • Dull: Solid organs or fluid (like liver)
  • Resonant: Air-filled structures (like normal lungs)
  • Tympanic: Hollow, drum-like sound (like stomach/intestines)
  • Flatness: Very dull (muscle, bone)
  • Hyperresonance: Excessive air (pneumothorax)

Purpose: Helps identify fluid, air, or solid masses under the skin and assess organ boundaries.

AUSCULTATION (LISTEN)

Description: Using a stethoscope to listen to internal body sounds.

What to Listen For:

  • Heart sounds: Lub-dub rhythm, murmurs, irregular beats
  • Lung sounds: Breathing in/out, crackles, wheezes, diminished sounds
  • Bowel sounds: Gurgles in abdomen (all 4 quadrants)
  • Blood vessels: Bruits (abnormal whooshing sounds)
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

Always listen over bare skin — NOT over clothes! Clothing creates artifact sounds.

IMPORTANT AREAS TO ASSESS

System What to Check
Neuro (Brain/Nerves) Level of consciousness (awake? alert?), Pupils (PERRLA: Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation)
Respiratory (Lungs) Rate, depth, breath sounds (crackles, wheezes, clear)
Cardiac (Heart) Heart sounds, blood pressure, pulses (rate, rhythm, strength)
Gastrointestinal (Stomach/Bowels) Bowel sounds, abdomen soft or firm, tenderness, distention
Musculoskeletal Muscle strength, joint movement, range of motion
Skin Color, warmth, moisture, turgor (elasticity), wounds, lesions

HEAD-TO-TOE ASSESSMENT

📍 WHAT IS IT?

A head-to-toe assessment is a full check of the body, starting from the head and moving down to the toes. It gives a complete picture of the patient's health!

Always start at the top and move down systematically.

STEP-BY-STEP HEAD-TO-TOE ASSESSMENT

1⃣ GENERAL SURVEY

(First quick look at the whole person)

  • Level of consciousness (awake? alert? oriented?)
  • Appearance (clean? well-groomed? breathing easily?)
  • Behavior (calm? anxious? confused?)
  • Vital signs: Temperature, heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation

2⃣ HEAD AND FACE

  • Inspect scalp, hair, face (color, swelling, injuries)
  • Check eyes: PERRLA (Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation)
  • Check ears, nose, throat (clear? infections? drainage?)
  • Palpate skull and facial bones for tenderness or deformities

3⃣ NECK

  • Palpate for lumps, swollen glands (lymph nodes)
  • Check for jugular vein distention (JVD) → heart problem sign
  • Check neck range of motion (can they turn head?)
  • Assess for tracheal deviation

4⃣ CHEST (RESPIRATORY AND CARDIAC)

  • Respiratory: Look at breathing effort (easy or labored?)
  • Listen to lungs (front and back) for breath sounds
  • Normal: Clear, vesicular sounds
  • Abnormal: Crackles (fluid), wheezes (narrowing), diminished
  • Cardiac: Listen to heart sounds (regular beat? murmurs? extra sounds?)
  • Check for chest pain, palpitations

5⃣ ABDOMEN

  • INSPECT: Shape, scars, swelling, distention
  • AUSCULTATE: Bowel sounds in all 4 quadrants (before touching!)
  • PERCUSS: Dullness or tympany
  • PALPATE: Tenderness, firmness, lumps, organ enlargement
🧠 NCLEX TIP:

For abdomen → Look, Listen, then Feel!

6⃣ ARMS AND HANDS

  • Check skin color, temperature, turgor (elasticity → hydration test)
  • Palpate radial pulses (both arms)
  • Check grip strength (both hands)
  • Look for swelling, wounds, IV sites
  • Check capillary refill (<2 seconds = normal)

7⃣ LEGS AND FEET

  • Inspect and palpate skin condition
  • Palpate pulses: Dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses
  • Check strength (push and pull feet)
  • Look for swelling (edema), wounds, discoloration
  • Check for calf tenderness (DVT assessment)

8⃣ NEUROLOGICAL (QUICK CHECK)

  • Orientation: Person, place, time, situation
  • Hand grips and foot pushes equal on both sides?
  • Sensation (numbness? tingling?)
  • Glasgow Coma Scale if needed
  • Pupil response (PERRLA)

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

✅ KEY POINTS:
  • Inspect first in most areas (except abdomen: auscultate first)
  • Assess bilateral (both sides) and compare
  • Document what you see, feel, hear — clearly and objectively
  • Report abnormal findings right away
  • Head-to-toe = systematic approach from top to bottom
  • PERRLA: Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation
  • Use back of hand for temperature assessment
  • Listen over bare skin with stethoscope
🧠 MEMORY TRICKS:
  • "I-P-P-A" = Inspection, Palpation, Percussion, Auscultation
  • "Look Before You Touch" = Always inspect first!
  • "Abdomen = Listen First" = Auscultate before palpating
  • "PERRLA" = Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation
  • "Top to Bottom" = Head-to-toe assessment direction
