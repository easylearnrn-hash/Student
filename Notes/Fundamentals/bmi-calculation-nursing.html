BMI CALCULATION

📏 Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool used to assess weight status.

This guide covers BMI calculation formulas and categories essential for NCLEX-RN.

HOW TO CALCULATE BMI

U.S. UNITS FORMULA (Pounds & Inches)

BMI = (Weight in pounds × 703) ÷ (Height in inches)²

Example:

Weight: 150 lbs, Height: 65 inches (5'5")

BMI = (150 × 703) ÷ (65)² = 105,450 ÷ 4,225 = 24.96

Result: BMI = 25 (Overweight)

METRIC UNITS FORMULA (Kilograms & Meters)

BMI = Weight in kilograms ÷ (Height in meters)²

Example:

Weight: 68 kg, Height: 1.65 meters

BMI = 68 ÷ (1.65)² = 68 ÷ 2.72 = 25.0

Result: BMI = 25 (Overweight)

BMI CATEGORIES (USED ON NCLEX)

Standard BMI Classifications

Memorize these categories for NCLEX questions about nutrition, health risks, and patient teaching.

Category BMI Range Health Risk
Underweight Less than 18.5 Malnutrition, weak immune system
Normal (Healthy) 18.5 – 24.9 Lowest health risk
Overweight 25 – 29.9 Increased risk for chronic diseases
Obese (Class I) 30 – 34.9 High risk for diabetes, hypertension
Obese (Class II) 35 – 39.9 Very high risk for comorbidities
Morbidly Obese (Class III) 40 or more Extremely high risk; may need surgery

NCLEX-RN TIPS

🎯 CRITICAL POINTS FOR NCLEX:

  • BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic test
  • Watch for questions linking BMI with comorbidities (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, heart disease)
  • Expect to apply BMI values when giving dietary, lifestyle, or health promotion advice
  • Know the cutoff values to recognize obesity-related risks
  • BMI ≥ 30 = Obese (increased risk for chronic conditions)
  • BMI ≥ 40 = Morbidly obese (may qualify for bariatric surgery)

CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

BMI Category Associated Conditions Nursing Interventions
Underweight (<18.5) Malnutrition, anemia, osteoporosis, weakened immunity Assess nutrition, encourage high-calorie diet, monitor weight
Normal (18.5-24.9) Lowest risk for weight-related health problems Maintain healthy lifestyle, balanced diet, regular exercise
Overweight (25-29.9) Pre-diabetes, hypertension, elevated cholesterol Encourage weight loss (5-10%), diet modification, exercise
Obese (≥30) Type 2 diabetes, heart disease, stroke, sleep apnea, joint problems Comprehensive weight management, lifestyle changes, possible medication
Morbidly Obese (≥40) Severe chronic conditions, reduced life expectancy Refer for bariatric surgery evaluation, intensive weight management

QUICK REFERENCE SUMMARY

🧮 CALCULATION TIPS:

  • U.S. Formula: Multiply weight (lbs) by 703, then divide by height (inches) squared
  • Metric Formula: Divide weight (kg) by height (meters) squared
  • Remember 703: This is the conversion factor for U.S. units
  • Height squared means: Height × Height (e.g., 65 × 65 = 4,225)

🎯 NCLEX HIGH-YIELD FACTS:

  • BMI 30+ = Obese → Screen for diabetes, HTN, high cholesterol
  • BMI 40+ = Morbidly obese → Consider bariatric surgery referral
  • BMI <18.5 = Underweight → Assess for eating disorders, malabsorption
  • Waist circumference is also important (not just BMI):
    • Women: >35 inches = increased risk
    • Men: >40 inches = increased risk
  • BMI limitations: Doesn't account for muscle mass (athletes may have high BMI but low fat)
