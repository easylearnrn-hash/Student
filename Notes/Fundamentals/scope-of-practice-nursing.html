SCOPE OF PRACTICE

WHAT IS SCOPE OF PRACTICE?

Scope of practice refers to the legal and professional boundaries that define what a nurse is allowed to do based on:

  • Licensure level (RN vs LPN/LVN vs CNA/UAP)
  • State Nurse Practice Act (NPA) (state-specific legal rules)
  • Education and training
  • Facility policies and procedures
💡 CRITICAL RESPONSIBILITY:

You are responsible for knowing what you legally and ethically can and cannot do in your role.

DELEGATION VS ASSIGNMENT

(NCLEX MUST-KNOW)

Concept Definition
Assignment The distribution of routine care/workload (e.g., assigning patients)
Delegation Transferring responsibility (not accountability) for a task to someone else
✅ KEY PRINCIPLE:

You may delegate the task, but the RN remains accountable for the outcome.

5 RIGHTS OF DELEGATION

These help ensure safe and appropriate delegation:

  1. Right Task → Is the task appropriate for delegation?
  2. Right Circumstance → Is the patient's condition stable?
  3. Right Person → Is the delegatee trained and qualified?
  4. Right Direction/Communication → Are clear, specific instructions provided?
  5. Right Supervision/Evaluation → Is the nurse available to monitor and evaluate?

NCLEX TIP: WHAT CAN BE DELEGATED?

TO LPNS/LVNS (UNDER RN SUPERVISION)

  • Monitor stable patients
  • Administer oral, IM, SubQ meds
  • Reinforce teaching already done by RN
  • Perform wound care, sterile dressing changes
  • Collect data (e.g., vitals, I&O, blood glucose)
  • Perform ECG, catheter insertion

TO UAPS/CNAS

  • Assist with ADLs (bathing, dressing, feeding)
  • Vitals on stable patients
  • Ambulation, repositioning, range of motion (ROM)
  • Specimen collection (e.g., urine, stool)
  • Empty Foley bags, record I&O
  • Non-sterile hygiene care (oral care, toileting)

TASKS THAT CANNOT BE DELEGATED

Task Reason
Assessment or nursing judgment Requires critical thinking → RN only
Teaching (initial or new) RN must assess learning needs and effectiveness
Unstable or new patients RN must monitor due to unpredictable needs
Care plan creation/adjustment RN responsibility
Medication titration or IV pushes (most) Complex skill — RN only (unless LPN trained and allowed by state law)

MNEMONIC: "DELEGATE" — WHAT TO CONSIDER BEFORE DELEGATING

  • D – Does the task require nursing judgment?
  • E – Evaluate the patient's stability
  • L – Legal scope of the delegatee
  • E – Experience/education of the staff
  • G – Guidelines/policies of facility
  • A – Assign tasks only within capability
  • T – Time for supervision and follow-up
  • E – Ensure clear communication

SAMPLE NCLEX QUESTIONS

❓ The nurse is assigning tasks to a UAP. Which is appropriate to delegate?

✅ Measuring I&O for a stable patient

❌ Teaching the patient how to perform insulin injections

❓ Which task can the RN delegate to an LPN?

✅ Administering IM pain medications to a stable post-op patient

❌ Creating a nursing care plan

❓ A UAP reports a patient's blood pressure is 190/110. What should the RN do first?

✅ Reassess the blood pressure personally

(RNs must validate abnormal findings from UAPs)

FINAL NCLEX TAKEAWAYS

✅ KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER:
  • RNs are responsible for assessment, planning, teaching, clinical judgment, unstable patients
  • LPNs can care for stable patients, do meds, simple wound care, and reinforce teaching
  • UAPs can assist with basic care tasks (ADLs, vitals, hygiene) on stable patients
  • Never delegate assessment, teaching, or clinical judgment
  • Use the 5 Rights of Delegation before assigning a task
