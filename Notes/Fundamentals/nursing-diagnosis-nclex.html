NURSING DIAGNOSIS

What is a Nursing Diagnosis?

A nursing diagnosis is a clinical judgment (nurse's decision) about a patient's response to a health problem.

🧠 NCLEX Tip: It's about what the patient feels or needs, not about the disease itself.

PARTS OF A NURSING DIAGNOSIS

The PES Format (Always in 3 Pieces)

P = Problem

E = Etiology

S = Symptoms

P = Problem

What's wrong

E = Etiology

What's causing it
(written as "related to")

S = Symptoms

How you know
(written as "as evidenced by")

EXAMPLE OF FULL NURSING DIAGNOSIS

Complete Example:

"Impaired physical mobility related to pain after surgery as evidenced by refusal to walk, grimacing, slow movement"

Problem: Impaired physical mobility

Etiology: Related to pain after surgery

Symptoms: As evidenced by refusal to walk, grimacing, slow movement

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Never include medical diagnoses like "pneumonia" directly — focus on the patient's needs (ex: "ineffective airway clearance related to secretions").

COMMON NURSING DIAGNOSES FOR NCLEX

Diagnosis What It Means
Impaired gas exchange Trouble moving oxygen and carbon dioxide
Risk for infection Higher chance of infection happening
Acute pain Sudden, short-term pain
Chronic pain Long-term, ongoing pain
Impaired skin integrity Damaged or broken skin
Ineffective coping Trouble handling stress or emotions
Deficient fluid volume Dehydration (not enough fluids)
Risk for falls More likely to fall

TIPS FOR WRITING GOOD NURSING DIAGNOSES

  • Focus on the patient's response, not the doctor's disease label
  • Use NANDA-I (North American Nursing Diagnosis Association International) list — NCLEX tests only official terms
  • Add "Risk for" when no symptoms are showing yet, but danger is there

Important: "Risk For" Diagnoses

"Risk for" diagnoses do NOT have "as evidenced by" — because the problem hasn't happened yet!

EXAMPLES: CORRECT VS. INCORRECT

✅ Correct:

"Risk for infection related to surgical incision."

✅ Correct:

"Impaired skin integrity related to immobility as evidenced by redness on sacrum."

❌ Incorrect:

"Pneumonia related to lung infection."

(This is a medical diagnosis, not a nursing diagnosis)

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD REMINDERS

Key Points to Remember

  • Nursing diagnoses = patient problems, not diseases
  • Use PES format: Problem, Etiology (cause), Symptoms
  • Risk diagnoses = no symptoms yet
  • Focus on what you can treat or manage as a nurse

QUICK COMPARISON TABLE

Type Format Example
Actual Diagnosis Problem + Etiology + Symptoms (PES) "Acute pain related to surgical incision as evidenced by grimacing and rating pain 8/10"
Risk Diagnosis Risk for + Problem + Etiology (NO symptoms) "Risk for falls related to altered mobility"
Medical Diagnosis ❌ NOT a nursing diagnosis "Diabetes mellitus" (This is the doctor's diagnosis)

MEMORY TRICKS

  • PES = "Perfect Evidence Shows" the nursing diagnosis format
  • "Related to" = R/T (common abbreviation for etiology)
  • "As evidenced by" = AEB (common abbreviation for symptoms)
  • "Risk for" = No symptoms yet (future problem prevention)
