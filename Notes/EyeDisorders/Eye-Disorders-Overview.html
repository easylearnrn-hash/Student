Pain vs. Painless — Critical Triage

Painful Red Eye (Urgent/Emergency)

  • Acute angle-closure glaucoma — severe pain, N/V, halos
  • Keratitis — foreign body sensation, photophobia
  • Iritis/Uveitis — deep ache, photophobia, perilimbal flush
  • Endophthalmitis — post-op, hypopyon, severe pain
  • Scleritis — deep boring pain, tender globe
  • Orbital cellulitis — proptosis, restricted movement, fever

Painless Vision Changes

  • Open-angle glaucoma — gradual peripheral loss
  • Cataracts — progressive blur, glare
  • AMD — central scotoma, metamorphopsia
  • Retinal detachment — curtain, flashes/floaters (painless but URGENT)
  • Diabetic retinopathy — vitreous hemorrhage (sudden painless loss)
  • CRVO/CRAO — sudden painless vision loss

Post-Op Eye Surgery — Universal Precautions

Applies to All Eye Surgeries

  • Eye shield at night — prevents rubbing
  • No bending below waist, no lifting >5–10 lbs
  • No Valsalva maneuver (straining, coughing hard)
  • No rubbing the eye
  • Administer drops on exact schedule
  • Dark glasses outdoors (UV + photophobia)
  • Report: sudden pain, vision loss, increasing redness, purulent discharge
🚨 Universal Post-Op Emergency: Pain + vision loss + purulent discharge after ANY eye surgery = endophthalmitis. Immediate ophthalmology evaluation is required.
Amsler Grid — Home Monitoring Tool

Amsler Grid (AMD Self-Monitoring)

  • Used at home to detect changes in central vision — especially for dry AMD patients
  • Hold grid 12–14 inches from eyes; cover one eye at a time; look at center dot
  • Normal: all lines straight, no missing areas, center dot visible
  • Abnormal: wavy/distorted lines (metamorphopsia), missing/blurry areas, center dot missing
  • Any change = call provider immediately — may indicate conversion to wet AMD
⚠️ NCLEX: A patient with dry AMD calls the nurse saying their Amsler grid lines look wavy. Priority action: notify ophthalmologist STAT — this indicates possible wet AMD conversion requiring urgent anti-VEGF evaluation.
NCLEX Priority Conditions

Emergencies (Act Now)

  • Acute angle-closure glaucoma
  • Retinal detachment
  • Central retinal artery occlusion (CRAO) — 90-min window
  • Endophthalmitis (post-surgical)
  • Chemical burns to eye
  • Orbital cellulitis with proptosis

Urgents (Same Day)

  • New flashes/floaters (retinal tear?)
  • Sudden monocular vision loss
  • Keratitis with ulceration
  • Corneal foreign body (metallic)
  • Hypopyon
