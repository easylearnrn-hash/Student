Eye Disorders — NCLEX Rapid Review

Master Summary Sheet — All Key Eye Topics

1. Emergency Eye Conditions (Act Fast!)

These require immediate action — do NOT delay:
  • EMERGENCY Acute Angle-Closure Glaucoma (AACG) — sudden severe eye pain, halos, HA, N/V, ↑IOP → treat within hours to save vision
  • EMERGENCY Retinal Detachment — painless curtain/shadow over vision + flashes + floaters → immediate surgical repair
  • EMERGENCY Chemical Eye Injury — immediate saline irrigation ×15–20 min; do NOT wait for orders
  • URGENT Keratitis — severe eye pain + photophobia + blurred vision → ophthalmology referral same day
  • URGENT Wet AMD sudden vision change — new metamorphopsia or sudden central vision loss → same-day ophthalmologist
  • URGENT Diabetic Retinopathy — new floaters/curtain in diabetic patient → report immediately

4. Critical NCLEX Don'ts (Contraindications)

  • ❌ NO atropine/anticholinergics in glaucoma → dilates pupil → blocks outflow → IOP crisis
  • ❌ NO corticosteroid drops in HSV keratitis → worsens corneal ulceration → perforation risk
  • ❌ NO bending/straining/lifting after cataract or retinal surgery → raises IOP
  • ❌ NO contact lenses during active eye infection
  • ❌ NO tap water for contact lens rinsing → Acanthamoeba risk
  • ❌ NO eye drop before nasolacrimal occlusion → systemic absorption (especially beta-blockers)
  • ❌ NO ointment before drops → barrier prevents drop absorption

5. Key Nursing Priorities

  • All eye drops: nasolacrimal occlusion 30–60 sec; don't touch tip; drops before ointment; 5 min between drops
  • All diabetics: annual dilated eye exam regardless of symptoms
  • Post-eye surgery: eye shield at night; no rubbing; avoid heavy lifting; report sudden pain or vision loss
  • Falls prevention: orient to environment; clock method for meals; consistent placement of items; adequate lighting
  • Infection control: hand hygiene; no sharing towels or drops; contact precautions for viral/bacterial conjunctivitis
  • Amsler grid: AMD patients check daily; report new distortion immediately

7. Final NCLEX Pearls

  • 🎯 Curtain/shadow over vision = retinal detachment = CALL MD NOW
  • 🎯 Wavy lines (metamorphopsia) = wet AMD = CALL MD NOW
  • 🎯 Sudden severe eye pain + halos + HA/N/V = AACG = EMERGENCY
  • 🎯 Eye pain + photophobia + blurred vision = keratitis (NOT conjunctivitis)
  • 🎯 Diabetics → annual dilated eye exam even without symptoms
  • 🎯 Glaucoma → NO atropine, NO anticholinergics, NO medications that dilate the pupil
  • 🎯 Neonatal eye prophylaxis = erythromycin ointment 0.5% within 1 hour of birth
  • 🎯 Nasolacrimal occlusion prevents SYSTEMIC absorption of eye drops
  • 🎯 Drops BEFORE ointment; 5 min between different drops
  • 🎯 Poor vision = high fall risk → always implement safety measures
