Eye Disorders

Eye Anatomy

Structural foundations of the visual system — the basis for understanding all eye pathology

External Structures

Protective Structures

  • Eyelids (palpebrae) — protect, lubricate, spread tear film
  • Conjunctiva — thin mucous membrane; bulbar (covers sclera) + palpebral (lines lids)
  • Lacrimal apparatus — lacrimal gland (superotemporally), puncta, canaliculi, nasolacrimal duct → drains to nasal cavity
  • Extraocular muscles (6) — control eye movement; CN III, IV, VI

Tear Film (3 Layers)

  • Lipid layer — outermost; from Meibomian glands; prevents evaporation
  • Aqueous layer — middle; from lacrimal gland; nutrients, antibodies
  • Mucin layer — innermost; from goblet cells; wets corneal surface

Anterior Segment

Structures

  • Cornea — avascular, transparent; 5 layers (epithelium, Bowman's, stroma, Descemet's, endothelium); refracts 2/3 of light entering eye
  • Anterior chamber — between cornea and iris; filled with aqueous humor
  • Iris — pigmented diaphragm; controls pupil size via sphincter (CN III) and dilator (sympathetic)
  • Pupil — aperture in iris
  • Posterior chamber — between iris and lens
  • Lens — biconvex, avascular; held by zonules (suspensory ligaments) attached to ciliary body; focuses near objects (accommodation)
  • Trabecular meshwork + Canal of Schlemm — aqueous outflow pathway; ↑ resistance = glaucoma
Posterior Segment

Structures

  • Vitreous humor — 80% of globe volume; gel-like; 99% water + collagen; supports retina
  • Retina — 10 layers; photoreceptors (rods + cones) in outer nuclear layer
  • Macula — central retina; area of highest visual acuity
  • Fovea centralis — center of macula; highest cone density; 20/20 vision point
  • Optic disc — blind spot; no photoreceptors; optic nerve exits here
  • Choroid — vascular layer; blood supply to outer retina
Photoreceptors

Rods (~120 million)

  • Peripheral retina
  • Scotopic (dim light/night) vision
  • Achromatic (no color)
  • High sensitivity, low acuity
  • Rhodopsin (requires Vitamin A)

Cones (~6 million)

  • Concentrated in fovea/macula
  • Photopic (bright light) vision
  • Color vision (S/M/L wavelengths)
  • High acuity, low sensitivity
  • Color deficiency = cone dysfunction
Visual Pathway

Retina → Cortex

  • Retina → Optic nerve (CN II) → Optic chiasm (nasal fibers cross) → Optic tract → Lateral geniculate nucleus (thalamus) → Optic radiation → Primary visual cortex (V1, occipital lobe)
  • Chiasm lesion → bitemporal hemianopia (pituitary tumor)
  • Optic tract lesion → contralateral homonymous hemianopia
  • Pupillary reflex → pretectal nucleus → CN III → sphincter pupillae
Aqueous Humor Circulation

Flow Pathway

Produced by ciliary body epithelium → posterior chamber → through pupil → anterior chamber → trabecular meshwork → canal of Schlemm → episcleral veins → systemic circulation.

  • Normal IOP: 10–21 mmHg
  • Obstruction at any point → ↑ IOP → glaucoma
