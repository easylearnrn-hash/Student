Ophthalmic Medication Administration

NCLEX-RN High-Yield Notes

1. Eye Drop Technique — Step by Step

1
Wash hands thoroughly before and after administration.
2
Have patient tilt head back and look up toward the ceiling.
3
Pull down lower eyelid with non-dominant hand to create a conjunctival pocket (lower conjunctival sac).
4
Hold dropper 1–2 cm above the eye. Do NOT touch the dropper tip to the eye, eyelashes, or any surface (prevents contamination).
5
Instill drop into the conjunctival sac (NOT directly on the cornea — causes reflex blinking and loss of drop).
6
Ask patient to close eyes gently — do NOT squeeze or rub (can expel medication).
7
Nasolacrimal occlusion: Press inner corner of eye (lacrimal punctum) with clean fingertip for 30–60 seconds to prevent systemic absorption.
8
Blot excess with clean tissue; wash hands.
Most common NCLEX question: "Which technique prevents systemic absorption of eye drops?" → Nasolacrimal (punctal) occlusion for 30–60 seconds.

2. Eye Ointment Technique

  1. Wash hands; warm tube in hands briefly to soften ointment.
  2. Tilt head back; pull down lower eyelid.
  3. Apply a ¼ to ½ inch thin ribbon of ointment along the conjunctival sac (inner to outer).
  4. Do NOT touch tube tip to eye.
  5. Close eye gently; roll eye to distribute.
  6. Warn patient: temporary blurred vision is expected after ointment (the greasy base blurs vision).
  7. Apply ointment after eye drops, not before.
Schedule ointments at bedtime when possible — blurred vision less problematic during sleep.

3. Timing Between Multiple Eye Medications

SequenceWait TimeRationale
Eye drop → Eye drop5 minutesAllows first drop to be absorbed before second drop flushes it out
Eye drop → Ointment5–10 minutesDrop absorbs first; ointment seals in
Ointment → Ointment10–15 minutesFirst ointment absorbs partially before next layer applied
Order: Always instill eye drops BEFORE eye ointment. Ointment creates a barrier that blocks drop absorption if given first.

4. Common Ophthalmic Drug Classes

Drug / ClassExamplesUseSide Effects / NCLEX Points
Beta-Blockers Timolol (Timoptic) Reduce IOP in glaucoma — ↓ aqueous humor production Systemic absorption → bradycardia, hypotension, bronchospasm. Contraindicated in asthma, COPD, heart block.
Prostaglandin Analogs Latanoprost (Xalatan), Bimatoprost (Lumigan) First-line for POAG — ↑ aqueous outflow Iris color change (permanent), increased eyelash growth, eye redness. Administer at bedtime.
Alpha-2 Agonists Brimonidine (Alphagan) Reduce IOP — ↓ production + ↑ outflow Drowsiness, dry mouth, local allergy. Avoid in children (CNS depression).
Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors Dorzolamide (Trusopt), Acetazolamide (oral) Reduce IOP — ↓ aqueous humor production Metallic taste, paresthesia (oral), sulfa allergy cross-reactivity. Monitor electrolytes with oral form.
Antibiotic drops Erythromycin, Ciprofloxacin, Tobramycin Bacterial conjunctivitis / keratitis Complete full course; use from inner canthus outward when wiping.
Mydriatics / Cycloplegics Atropine, Phenylephrine, Tropicamide Dilate pupil for eye exams; cycloplegia for some conditions Contraindicated in narrow-angle glaucoma; photosensitivity, dry mouth, increased HR, urinary retention. Blurred vision after exam.
Corticosteroids Prednisolone acetate, Dexamethasone Inflammation (uveitis, post-surgical) ↑IOP with prolonged use, delayed healing, risk of cataracts, mask infections. Avoid in HSV keratitis.
Anti-VEGF (Intravitreal) Bevacizumab (Avastin), Ranibizumab (Lucentis), Aflibercept (Eylea) Wet AMD, diabetic macular edema, PDR Intravitreal injection by ophthalmologist; watch for endophthalmitis (severe eye pain + redness + vision loss after injection).

5. Contact Lens Precautions

  • Remove contact lenses before instilling ANY eye drops
  • Wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting lenses (some drugs may be absorbed by lens material)
  • For Prostaglandin analogs (Xalatan) — remove lenses, wait 15 minutes, then reinsert
  • Do NOT wear contacts during active eye infection
  • Never rinse/store contacts in tap water (Acanthamoeba risk)

6. Patient Education

  • Always wash hands before handling eye medications or touching eyes
  • Do NOT touch dropper tip to any surface — prevents contamination
  • Use nasolacrimal occlusion every time (30–60 sec)
  • Wait 5 minutes between different eye drops
  • Do NOT drive or operate machinery if vision is blurred after drops/ointment
  • Complete the full antibiotic course even if symptoms improve
  • Store medications properly (some require refrigeration — e.g., Xalatan)
  • Report immediately: sudden vision changes, severe pain, increased redness, worsening symptoms
  • Discard eye drops per manufacturer recommendations (typically 28–30 days after opening)

7. NCLEX Must-Know

  • ✅ Nasolacrimal (punctal) occlusion 30–60 sec = prevents systemic absorption
  • ✅ Drop into conjunctival SAC — not directly on cornea
  • ✅ Do NOT touch dropper tip to eye or any surface
  • ✅ Drops before ointment; 5 min between drops
  • ✅ Remove contacts before drops; wait 15 min before reinserting
  • ✅ Timolol → bradycardia/bronchospasm in susceptible patients
  • ✅ Atropine drops → contraindicated in glaucoma
  • ✅ Steroid drops → ↑IOP + avoid in HSV keratitis
  • ✅ Prostaglandins (Xalatan) → permanent iris color change is possible
Key rule: Drops before ointment. Press inner corner. Wait 5 minutes between drops. Never touch the tip.
