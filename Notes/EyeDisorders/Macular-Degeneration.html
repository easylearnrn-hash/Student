Macular Degeneration (AMD)

NCLEX-RN High-Yield Notes

1. Definition

Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD) = progressive damage to the macula (central portion of the retina) → loss of central vision.

Peripheral (side) vision is typically preserved.

Macula = responsible for sharp central vision, color vision, and fine detail. Optic nerve = Cranial Nerve II carrying visual signals to the brain.

2. Types

TypePrevalenceMechanismProgression
Dry (Non-exudative)~90%Thinning of macula + accumulation of drusen (yellowish protein/lipid deposits under retina)Slow, gradual
Wet (Exudative / Neovascular)~10%Abnormal blood vessel growth (neovascularization) → vessels leak blood/fluid under retina → damages maculaRapid, severe
Wet AMD = less common but more dangerous → can cause sudden and severe central vision loss.

3. Risk Factors

  • Age >60 years (most important)
  • Family history
  • Smoking (↓ blood flow to retina)
  • Hypertension, hyperlipidemia, obesity
  • Light-colored eyes (less UV protection)
  • Prolonged UV exposure
  • Poor diet (low antioxidants, omega-3s)

4. Signs & Symptoms

Dry AMD

  • Gradual blurring of central vision
  • Difficulty recognizing faces or reading fine print
  • Need for brighter lighting
  • Drusen visible on fundoscopic exam

Wet AMD

  • Sudden onset of central vision loss
  • Dark or blurry central spot (blind spot)
  • Metamorphopsia — straight lines look wavy or bent (hallmark sign)
  • Rapidly progressing vision loss
NCLEX cue: "Straight lines appear wavy" or "distorted vision" → Wet AMD → report immediately.

5. Diagnosis

  • Amsler Grid Test — patient looks at grid; wavy or missing lines = AMD. Used daily at home for monitoring.
  • Fundoscopic exam — visualizes drusen, abnormal vessels, macular changes
  • OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography) — cross-sectional retinal imaging; detects fluid/swelling
  • Fluorescein Angiography — dye injection to highlight leaky/abnormal vessels (Wet AMD)

6. Treatment

Dry AMD — Slow Progression

  • AREDS2 supplements — Vitamin C, Vitamin E, Zinc, Copper, Lutein, Zeaxanthin
  • Stop smoking immediately
  • Control blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Diet: leafy greens, fish (omega-3s)
  • Use Amsler grid daily to monitor for changes
  • Good lighting when reading

Wet AMD — Prompt Treatment

  • Anti-VEGF injections (intravitreal): Bevacizumab (Avastin), Ranibizumab (Lucentis), Aflibercept (Eylea) — block abnormal vessel growth and leakage
  • Photodynamic Therapy (PDT) — inject Verteporfin (light-sensitive drug) + laser activation to seal leaking vessels
  • Laser Photocoagulation — high-energy laser destroys abnormal vessels (rarely used now)
Anti-VEGF injections are the gold standard for wet AMD. VEGF = Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor that drives abnormal vessel growth.

7. NCLEX Must-Know

  • ✅ AMD affects central vision — peripheral vision preserved
  • ✅ Dry = gradual (drusen); Wet = sudden (neovascularization, leakage)
  • ✅ Wavy/distorted lines = metamorphopsia = Wet AMD = emergency
  • ✅ Amsler grid daily — report changes to provider
  • ✅ AREDS2 supplements for dry AMD
  • ✅ Anti-VEGF injections for wet AMD
  • ✅ Quit smoking — most modifiable risk factor
  • ✅ No cure — management prevents progression
Dry AMD: "No cure, slow down with vitamins." Wet AMD: "Urgent anti-VEGF injections."
