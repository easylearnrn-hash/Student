What Is Mental Health Nursing?

Mental health nursing is about caring for patients with emotional, behavioral, or psychiatric disorders — conditions that affect thinking, mood, or behavior.

Core Focus
  • Safety
  • Therapeutic communication
  • Medication management
Core Goal
  • Crisis intervention
  • Coping support
  • Help patients function, not just treat illness

Therapeutic Communication

TechniqueExample

Use silenceLet the patient think and talk
Reflect feelings"It sounds like you feel scared."
Restate"You're saying you don't feel safe?"
✗ Avoid Giving advice"You should…"
✗ Avoid Asking "Why?""Why did you do that?" — feels judgmental
💡
Goal of Therapeutic Communication

Build trust and let the patient explore feelings safely. The focus is always on the patient's needs, not the nurse's.

Safety First — Suicide, Violence & Crisis

Suicide Risk Assessment

Always assess risk directly. Never avoid the subject.

Ask Directly

"Are you thinking of hurting yourself?"

  • Red flags: Plan, means, past attempts, hopelessness, withdrawal
  • Patient says "I want it to end" → stay with them + notify team immediately
Safety Interventions
  • One-to-one supervision (someone with them at all times)
  • Remove sharp objects, belts, cords

Assault / Violence Risk

  • Signs: clenched fists, pacing, yelling
  • Stay calm, give space, use clear short statements
  • Never turn your back on an aggressive patient

Crisis Intervention

Crisis = moment of breakdown / loss of coping. Goal: stabilize first, then help regain control.

Mental Status Exam (MSE)

A structured way to assess brain function across 9 domains:

AreaWhat You Check
AppearanceClean? Dressed? Groomed?
BehaviorCalm? Agitated?

Common Psychiatric Disorders — Snapshot

Depression

Low mood, loss of interest, fatigue, suicidal thoughts. Always assess for suicide risk.

Bipolar Disorder

Mania = high energy, no sleep, risky behavior. Depression = low energy, sadness. Protect during mania.

Schizophrenia

Positive: hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech. Negative: flat affect, poor hygiene. Do not argue with delusions.

Anxiety Disorders

Excess worry, racing heart, tense muscles. Use calm voice, breathing techniques, coping skills.

OCD

Obsessions = unwanted thoughts. Compulsions = repeated actions. Don't stop rituals abruptly — reduce gradually.

PTSD

Flashbacks, nightmares, startle easily. Avoid triggers, use grounding techniques.

Therapeutic Milieu

A safe, structured healing environment that supports recovery:

  • Structure, safety, and social support
  • Clear rules and roles
  • Encourage group participation
  • Limit stimulation when needed
  • Helps patients feel secure and build social skills

NCLEX High-Yield Tips

🚨
Never Ignore Suicidal Words

Always assess and stay with the patient. Safety is the first priority — above feelings, above all else.

💡
Do Not Argue With Delusions or Hallucinations

Acknowledge the patient's experience without reinforcing it. Redirect gently.

📋
Set Limits — Clearly and Kindly

Use firm, calm boundaries for manic or aggressive patients. Avoid power struggles.
