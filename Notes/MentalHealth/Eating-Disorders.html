Anorexia Nervosa — Detailed

Subtypes
  • Restricting type — caloric restriction, fasting, excessive exercise
  • Binge-purge type — restriction plus episodes of bingeing and/or purging

Physical Complications of Anorexia

Cardiovascular
  • Bradycardia
  • Hypotension (orthostatic)
  • Prolonged QT interval
  • Cardiac dysrhythmias
  • Heart failure (refeeding)
Metabolic / Endocrine
  • Amenorrhea
  • Low estrogen / testosterone
  • Hypothermia
  • Osteoporosis
  • Hypoglycemia

Refeeding Syndrome

Refeeding Syndrome — Critical NCLEX Topic

Occurs when a severely malnourished patient is fed too quickly. As carbohydrates are introduced, insulin causes rapid cellular uptake of electrolytes, causing severe drops in serum levels.

  • Most dangerous: ↓ Phosphorus (hypophosphatemia) — can cause cardiac failure, respiratory failure
  • Also: ↓ potassium, ↓ magnesium, ↓ thiamine
  • Prevention: Slow, gradual refeeding; monitor electrolytes closely; supplement as needed
🚨
NCLEX Priority — Refeeding Syndrome

Monitor phosphorus, potassium, magnesium closely when beginning refeeding. Cardiac monitoring is essential. Do NOT overfeed rapidly.

Bulimia Nervosa — Detailed

Diagnostic Criteria
  • Binge episodes (large amount + sense of loss of control) ≥ once a week for 3 months
  • Compensatory behavior to prevent weight gain: vomiting, laxatives, diuretics, fasting, excessive exercise
  • Self-evaluation unduly influenced by body shape and weight
  • Does NOT occur exclusively during anorexia episodes

1Monitor vital signs and electrolytesCardiac dysrhythmias are the leading cause of death
2Monitor weight daily (same time, same clothes)Tracks progress; detects water-loading or weight manipulation
3Sit with patient during and after meals (≥1 hour)Prevents purging immediately after eating
4Remove laxatives, diet pills from roomSafety — prevents purging methods
5Therapeutic relationship, non-judgmentalReduces shame; promotes trust
6Set behavioral contracts with agreed-upon weight gain goalsProvides structure; promotes patient involvement in recovery
7Monitor I&ODetects laxative abuse or vomiting patterns
8Do NOT focus on food or weight during conversationsReduces power struggle; eating disorders are about control, not food
💡
Therapeutic Approach — Anorexia

Avoid power struggles over food. Use a non-confrontational approach. Focus on feelings and emotions, not food or weight. Eating disorders are about control and distorted self-image.

NCLEX Must-Know Tips

⭐
Key Takeaways
  • Anorexia = highest mortality of all psychiatric disorders
  • Anorexia priority: cardiac monitoring — bradycardia, QT prolongation, arrhythmias
  • Bulimia signs: Russell's sign, parotid enlargement, dental erosion, hypokalemia
  • Refeeding syndrome: ↓ phosphorus — cardiac/respiratory failure; feed slowly
  • Sit with patient ≥1 hour post-meal to prevent purging
  • Bupropion is contraindicated in eating disorders
  • Focus on emotions, not food — eating disorders are about control
