Antidepressants — SSRIs

Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors — first-line treatment for depression, anxiety, OCD, PTSD

DrugKey NotesWatch For
Fluoxetine (Prozac)Longest half-life (4–6 weeks); approved for children ≥8 years; weekly dosing availableSerotonin syndrome if combined with MAOIs; wait 5 weeks washout
Sertraline (Zoloft)Most prescribed; good for anxiety, PTSD, OCD, PMS; safe in pregnancyGI upset common initially; sexual dysfunction
Paroxetine (Paxil)Strongest anticholinergic effects of SSRIs; most sedatingAvoid abruptly stopping — discontinuation syndrome; teratogenic
Escitalopram (Lexapro)Well-tolerated; fewer drug interactionsQT prolongation at high doses
Citalopram (Celexa)Simple dosing; used in elderlyQT prolongation — max dose 20 mg in elderly/hepatic impairment
⚠️
Black Box Warning — All Antidepressants

Increased risk of suicidal thoughts and behaviors in children, adolescents, and young adults (up to age 24) during initial treatment. Monitor closely, especially in first 1–4 weeks of therapy.

💡
SSRI Teaching Points
  • Takes 2–6 weeks for full therapeutic effect
  • Do NOT stop abruptly — taper to avoid discontinuation syndrome (FINISH: Flu-like symptoms, Insomnia, Nausea, Imbalance, Sensory disturbances, Hyperarousal)
  • Avoid alcohol, MAOIs, St. John's Wort
  • Sexual dysfunction is common — discuss with provider before stopping

Antidepressants — TCAs (Tricyclics)

Older antidepressants; used when SSRIs/SNRIs fail; also used for chronic pain, neuropathy, insomnia

DrugUseCritical Side Effects
Amitriptyline (Elavil)Depression, migraine prevention, neuropathic painAnticholinergic effects (dry mouth, urinary retention, constipation, blurry vision), sedation, orthostatic hypotension, QT prolongation
NortriptylineDepression, neuropathic painFewer anticholinergic effects than amitriptyline
Imipramine (Tofranil)Depression, enuresis (bedwetting) in childrenSame as amitriptyline; cardiotoxic in overdose
🚨
TCA Overdose — Lethal!
  • TCAs are highly dangerous in overdose — cardiotoxicity, seizures, death
  • Treat with sodium bicarbonate for cardiac effects
  • Use with caution in suicidal patients — limit prescription quantity
  • Anticholinergic toxidrome: "Hot as a hare, blind as a bat, dry as a bone, mad as a hatter, red as a beet"

Antidepressants — MAOIs

Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors — last-line; strict dietary restrictions required

DrugUseCritical Interactions
Phenelzine (Nardil)Treatment-resistant depression, atypical depression, social phobiaTyramine-rich foods → hypertensive crisis; SSRIs/SNRIs → serotonin syndrome; sympathomimetics
Tranylcypromine (Parnate)Treatment-resistant depressionSame as phenelzine — most activating MAOI
Selegiline (EMSAM)Depression (patch); also used in Parkinson'sLow-dose patch: fewer dietary restrictions; high dose: same restrictions
⚠️
MAOI Dietary Restrictions — Tyramine
  • Avoid: aged cheeses, cured meats, fermented foods, wine/beer (especially tap), soy products, fava beans, bananas, avocados
  • Tyramine + MAOI = hypertensive crisis: severe headache, neck stiffness, palpitations, N/V — can cause stroke
  • Washout: 14 days before/after starting another serotonergic drug (5 weeks for fluoxetine)

⚠️
Lithium Toxicity — Early Signs (NCLEX)
  • Early (1.5–2.0): Coarse tremor, GI distress (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea), polyuria, lethargy
  • Moderate (2.0–2.5): Confusion, ataxia, slurred speech, tinnitus
  • Severe (>2.5): Seizures, coma, cardiac dysrhythmias, death
  • Hold if patient has fever, diarrhea, vomiting, or decreased fluid intake — risk of toxicity increases

🚨
Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS) — Any Antipsychotic
  • FEVER: Fever, Encephalopathy, Vital sign instability, Elevated CK/enzymes, Rigidity (lead-pipe)
  • Occurs with any antipsychotic; life-threatening
  • Onset: days to weeks after starting/increasing dose
  • Treatment: stop drug immediately; Dantrolene; Bromocriptine; cooling; supportive care

DrugClassKey Notes

💡
ADHD Stimulant Nursing Teaching
  • Take in the morning to avoid insomnia
  • Monitor growth (height/weight) regularly
  • Report chest pain, palpitations, decreased appetite, mood changes
  • Do not double dose if missed
  • High abuse potential — schedule II controlled substance
