Med-Surg Priorities

Pain management, infection control, fluids, surgical risks, post-op complications, and blood transfusion

1⃣ Pain Management

PCA Pump (Patient-Controlled Analgesia)

  • Allows patients to self-administer preset doses of pain meds
  • Common with opioids (morphine, hydromorphone)
  • Lockout interval prevents overdose
  • Family must NOT press the button — risk of oversedation

Opioid Safety & Reversal

  • Monitor for: respiratory depression (RR <12/min), sedation, low SpO₂
  • Naloxone (Narcan): opioid antidote — may need repeat dose; monitor closely
  • Side effects: constipation, sedation, nausea

Nonpharmacologic Interventions

  • Positioning, massage, distraction, music therapy
  • Heat/cold (NOT on surgical wounds)
  • Breathing techniques

2⃣ Infection Control & Wound Care

Sterile Technique

  • Used in invasive procedures (dressing changes, catheter insertions)
  • "Sterile touches sterile only"
  • Anything out of sight or wet = contaminated

SSI Prevention

  • Antibiotics pre-op
  • Chlorhexidine wash
  • Maintain normothermia, blood sugar control
  • Monitor: redness, swelling, purulent drainage

Wound Drainage Types

TypeAppearanceSignificance
SerousClear, wateryNormal

SerosanguineousPink, wateryNormal early healing

Wound Healing Stages

  1. Inflammatory (0–3 days): swelling, redness
  2. Proliferative (3–21 days): tissue grows (granulation)
  3. Maturation/Remodeling (weeks–months): scar formation

4⃣ Types of Surgery & Surgical Risks

Types of Surgery

  • Elective: planned (e.g., hernia repair)
  • Urgent: within 24–48 hrs (e.g., appendectomy)
  • Emergency: immediate (e.g., ruptured aneurysm)

Risk Factors

  • Advanced age
  • Comorbidities: diabetes, hypertension, heart disease
  • Obesity, smoking, malnutrition

6⃣ Blood Transfusion Nursing Care

Crossmatching

  • Type & screen → ABO/Rh determination
  • Type & cross → performed before actual transfusion

Transfusion Reactions — STOP & ACT

  1. Stop the transfusion immediately
  2. Start NS through new tubing
  3. Notify HCP and blood bank
  4. Monitor: chills, fever, back pain, hypotension, tachycardia

Monitoring During Transfusion

  • Stay with patient for first 15 minutes
  • Take baseline and post-vitals
  • Infuse within 4 hours of receiving blood
  • Use Filtered Y-Tubing with normal saline only

7. NCLEX Prioritization Framework

ABC Framework — Airway First Always

PriorityFocusExamples
1st — AirwayIs the airway open?Stridor, obstruction, inability to speak
2nd — BreathingIs breathing adequate?RR <12 or >20, low SpO₂, apnea
3rd — CirculationIs circulation adequate?Hypotension, tachycardia, pallor, hemorrhage

🧠 NCLEX Tip: Always address airway before breathing, breathing before circulation. Life-threatening problems always take priority over non-life-threatening.
