Anticoagulant Lab Goals

  • Heparin: Monitor aPTT (goal 1.5–2.5× normal)
  • Warfarin: Monitor INR (goal 2–3)
  • Enoxaparin: Less monitoring needed

Insulin Types

  • Rapid (Lispro): onset 15 min — with meals
  • Regular: onset 30–60 min
  • NPH: onset 1–2 hr, has peak
  • Glargine: onset 1–2 hr, no peak (24 hr)

Pre-Op & Post-Op Core Concepts

Pre-Op
  • Consent before any sedatives
  • NPO 6–8 hr (general anesthesia)
  • Verify: signed consent, labs, allergies, warfarin status
  • Pre-op meds: antibiotics, Midazolam, Ondansetron, Atropine
  • If patient ate: notify surgeon — may delay surgery
Post-Op PACU
  • Priority #1: Airway & Breathing
  • Vitals q15 min × 4, then hourly
  • Urine output ≥30 mL/hr
  • Stridor = airway obstruction → call for help
  • Evisceration: moist sterile gauze, keep supine, call surgeon
  • Wound dehiscence: sterile dressing, notify provider

Orthopedic Post-Op

THR — 3 NO's
  • No adduction (cross midline)
  • No internal rotation
  • No hip flexion >90°
  • Use abduction pillow
TKR
  • CPM machine to prevent stiffness
  • Early weight bearing with assistive device
  • Early ambulation prevents DVT
All Ortho
  • Neurovascular checks (5 P's)
  • DVT prophylaxis: SCDs, enoxaparin, ambulation
  • Incentive spirometer
🚨 Compartment Syndrome: Severe pain unrelieved by meds + tight dressing + pulselessness → Emergency!

Wound Care & Infection Control

Drainage Types

  • Serous: clear/watery — normal
  • Sanguineous: bloody — active bleed
  • Serosanguineous: pink/watery — normal healing
  • Purulent: pus — infection ⚠️

Wound Healing Stages

  1. Inflammatory (0–3 days): redness, swelling
  2. Proliferative (3–21 days): granulation
  3. Maturation (weeks–months): scar

IV Fluids & Electrolyte Imbalances

IV Fluid Types

  • Isotonic (NS, LR): fluid replacement
  • Hypotonic (0.45% NS): cellular dehydration
  • Hypertonic (D10, 3% NS): severe hyponatremia

Key Electrolyte Alerts

  • ↓K⁺: cramps, arrhythmias
  • ↑K⁺: tall T waves, weakness
  • ↓Na⁺: confusion, seizures
  • ↓Ca²⁺: Chvostek/Trousseau signs
  • ↓Mg²⁺: tremors, arrhythmias

Blood Transfusion Safety

  • Reaction: STOP transfusion → NS through new tubing → notify HCP & blood bank
  • Stay with patient for first 15 minutes
  • Infuse within 4 hours
  • Use filtered Y-tubing with NS only
  • Reaction signs: chills, fever, back pain, hypotension, tachycardia

NCLEX Priority Rapid-Fire

  • RR <12 on opioids → hold dose, give Narcan
  • Family presses PCA → educate: only patient presses
  • Post-op urine <30 mL/hr → notify provider
  • Evisceration → moist sterile gauze, flat, call surgeon
  • Sudden SOB + chest pain post-op → PE — O₂, call provider NOW
  • Anaphylaxis → Epinephrine IM first
  • Septic shock → IV Antibiotics first
  • Stridor post-op → airway obstruction — call for help
  • Warfarin patient going to surgery → may delay — check INR
  • Compartment syndrome → fasciotomy — emergency
