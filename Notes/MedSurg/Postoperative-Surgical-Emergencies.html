Postoperative Surgical Emergencies & Complications

PACU assessment, ABCDES framework, complication recognition, and respiratory risks

1. The Postoperative Period

  • Immediate (PACU): First 1–2 hours — highest monitoring intensity
  • Early: First 24–48 hours
  • Late: Days to weeks after surgery
🚨 NCLEX Tip: The first 24 hours post-op is the highest risk for life-threatening complications!

2. Priority Post-Op Assessments — "ABCDES"

LetterFocusWhat to Check
AAirwayEnsure patent airway; monitor for obstruction
BBreathingRespiratory rate, breath sounds, O₂ saturation
CCirculationBP, HR, capillary refill
DDisability (Neuro)LOC, orientation, pupil response
EExposureHypothermia, surgical site bleeding
🧠 NCLEX Tip: First priority post-op = Airway & Breathing! Opioids and anesthesia can depress respiration!

5. Urinary Output Monitoring

  • Normal: minimum 30 mL/hr
  • Less than 30 mL/hr → Assess for dehydration or kidney injury
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Low urine output <30 mL/hr → Assess first, then notify provider.
