Preoperative & Postoperative Care

Surgical consent, NPO requirements, pre-op checklist, PACU monitoring, and post-op complication prevention

🧠 Key Principle: Pre-op = Prevent complications. Post-op = Monitor & Manage complications!

PREOPERATIVE CARE (Before Surgery)

2. Preoperative Nursing Responsibilities

  • Consent: Surgeon explains procedure | Nurse verifies understanding & signature | Must be done BEFORE sedatives
  • NPO:
    • General anesthesia → NPO 6–8 hours (prevents aspiration)
    • Local anesthesia → light meal may be allowed
  • IV Access: for hydration and medication administration
  • Pre-op Medications:
    • Antibiotics → prevent infection (before incision)
    • Sedatives (Midazolam, Lorazepam) → calm anxiety
    • Antiemetics (Ondansetron) → prevent nausea
    • Anticholinergics (Atropine) → reduce secretions
  • Patient Education: incentive spirometry, leg exercises, early ambulation, pain control options
🚨 NCLEX Tip: If a patient eats before surgery → notify the surgeon — surgery may need to be delayed!

3. Preoperative Checklist

  • Confirm NPO status (no food, water, gum, or smoking)
  • Remove jewelry, dentures, contacts, prosthetics
  • Ensure signed consent is in the chart
  • Check for allergies (especially latex, shellfish, iodine)
  • Verify lab results: PT/INR, electrolytes, CBC, pregnancy test
  • Have patient void (empty bladder before anesthesia)
🚨 NCLEX Tip: If a patient took warfarin (Coumadin), surgery may be delayed due to bleeding risk!

POSTOPERATIVE CARE (After Surgery)

4. Immediate Post-Op Priorities (PACU — First 1–2 Hours)

  • Airway (Top Priority!): ensure breathing after anesthesia | suction if needed | watch for stridor
  • Vital Signs: every 15 min × 4, then hourly — BP, HR, O₂ sat
  • Neuro Assessment: ensure proper wake-up from anesthesia | monitor confusion/disorientation
  • Monitor Complications:
    • Bleeding: check surgical site, dressing, drains
    • Pain: IV meds first, then oral later
    • N/V: give antiemetics as ordered
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Noisy breathing (stridor) → possible airway obstruction → call for help immediately!

5. Post-Op Nursing Assessment Summary

Airway & BreathingO₂ levels, breath sounds, signs of distress
CirculationBP, HR, skin color, temperature

Pain ManagementOpioids: risk of respiratory depression
GI FunctionNausea, vomiting, bowel sounds, diet advancement
Urinary OutputMinimum 30 mL/hr (less = possible kidney injury)
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Low urine output <30 mL/hr → assess for dehydration or kidney injury!
