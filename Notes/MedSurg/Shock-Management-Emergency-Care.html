Shock Management & Emergency Care

Types of shock, ABCS framework, specific treatments, and nursing monitoring

🧠 Core Concept: Shock = Decreased Blood Flow → Low Oxygen → Organ Failure!

2. Types of Shock & Their Causes

TypeMechanismExamples
HypovolemicLoss of blood or fluids → not enough blood for circulationHemorrhage, dehydration, burns
CardiogenicHeart failure → heart cannot pump effectivelyMI, heart failure, valve disease
DistributiveBlood vessels dilate → blood pools, not circulatingSepsis, anaphylaxis, neurogenic (spinal cord injury)
ObstructivePhysical blockage prevents blood flowPE, cardiac tamponade, tension pneumothorax
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Septic, Anaphylactic, & Neurogenic Shock = Vasodilation → BP drops!

3. Signs & Symptoms of Shock

Early Signs (Compensated Shock — Body Adapting)

  • Tachycardia (trying to maintain blood flow)
  • Hypotension (low BP due to poor circulation)
  • Cool, clammy skin (blood diverted to vital organs)
  • Restlessness & anxiety (low O₂ to brain)

Late Signs (Decompensated Shock — Organs Failing)

  • Severe hypotension (dangerously low BP)
  • Weak or absent peripheral pulses
  • Slow, shallow breathing (respiratory failure)
  • Loss of consciousness (brain not getting O₂)
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Shock starts with HIGH heart rate (compensation) → progresses to LOW BP & organ failure if untreated!

4. Shock Management — "ABCS First!"

StepAction
A — AirwayEnsure airway is open; intubation if unable to breathe properly
B — BreathingProvide 100% O₂ via non-rebreather mask or ventilator; monitor RR
C — CirculationIV Fluids (hypovolemic/septic); Vasopressors (distributive); Blood transfusion (hemorrhagic)
S — Stop the causeTreat underlying cause (infection, allergy, bleeding, blockage)
🚨 NCLEX Tip: Shock = Give Oxygen, Fluids, and Vasopressors if BP is dangerously low!

5. Specific Shock Treatments & Medications

Shock TypeTreatmentKey Medications
HypovolemicIV Fluids (NS, LR); Blood transfusion if neededIV Fluids (first-line), Packed RBCs
CardiogenicImprove heart pumping (avoid excess IV fluids!)Dobutamine (↑ contractility), Dopamine (↑ BP & HR), Diuretics
SepticTreat infection + support BPIV Antibiotics (FIRST!), IV Fluids, Norepinephrine, Dopamine
AnaphylacticReverse the allergic reactionEpinephrine IM (FIRST!), Diphenhydramine, Steroids, IV Fluids
NeurogenicStabilize BP, prevent further injuryDopamine, Norepinephrine, IV Fluids, Atropine (for low HR)
ObstructiveRemove the obstructionChest tube (tension pneumothorax), Surgery (tamponade/PE)
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Septic Shock → First priority: Give IV Antibiotics ASAP!
