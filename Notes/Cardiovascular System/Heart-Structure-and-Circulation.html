Cardiovascular System

Heart Structure & Circulation

Chambers, valves, pulmonary & systemic circulation, and blood flow pathway

Heart Chambers

Right Side (Receives Deoxygenated Blood)

  • Right Atrium (RA) — receives deoxygenated blood from body via SVC & IVC
  • Tricuspid Valve — separates RA from RV (3 leaflets)
  • Right Ventricle (RV) — pumps blood to the lungs
  • Pulmonic Valve — opens to pulmonary artery

Left Side (Receives Oxygenated Blood)

  • Left Atrium (LA) — receives oxygenated blood from lungs via pulmonary veins
  • Mitral Valve (Bicuspid) — separates LA from LV (2 leaflets)
  • Left Ventricle (LV) — pumps oxygenated blood to entire body
  • Aortic Valve — opens to aorta
📌 Mnemonic: "Try Pulling My Aorta" → Tricuspid, Pulmonic, Mitral, Aortic (right-to-left order)

Blood Circulation Pathways

Pulmonary Circulation (Small Circuit)

Heart → Lungs → Heart

  • RV pumps deoxygenated blood → Pulmonary artery → Lungs
  • Gas exchange occurs in lung capillaries
  • Oxygenated blood returns via pulmonary veins → LA

Systemic Circulation (Large Circuit)

Heart → Body → Heart

  • LV pumps oxygenated blood → Aorta → Body
  • Oxygen delivered to all tissues
  • Deoxygenated blood returns via SVC & IVC → RA
Complete Blood Flow Pathway
1
Body tissues → deoxygenated blood enters Superior Vena Cava (SVC) or Inferior Vena Cava (IVC)
2
SVC/IVC → Right Atrium (RA)
3
RA → through Tricuspid Valve → Right Ventricle (RV)
4
RV → through Pulmonic Valve → Pulmonary Artery (only artery carrying deoxygenated blood)
5
Pulmonary Artery → Lungs (gas exchange: CO₂ out, O₂ in)
6
Oxygenated blood → Pulmonary Veins → Left Atrium (LA) (only veins carrying oxygenated blood)
7
LA → through Mitral Valve → Left Ventricle (LV)
8
LV → through Aortic Valve → Aorta → Body
📌 Key Rule: Arteries carry blood Away from the heart. Veins carry blood toward the heart. The pulmonary artery/vein are the exceptions to oxygenation.
Heart Layers

Three Layers of the Heart Wall

  • Epicardium — outer layer (visceral pericardium); covers heart surface
  • Myocardium — middle muscular layer; the actual pumping muscle
  • Endocardium — inner lining; smooth layer that lines chambers and valves

Pericardium

  • Double-layered sac surrounding the heart
  • Contains a small amount of lubricating fluid for frictionless movement
  • Excess fluid → Cardiac Tamponade
Coronary Arteries (Heart's Own Blood Supply)

Coronary Artery Anatomy

  • Left Coronary Artery (LCA) branches into:
    • LAD (Left Anterior Descending) — supplies anterior wall of LV & 2/3 of interventricular septum → "Widow Maker"
    • LCX (Left Circumflex) — supplies lateral wall of LV
  • Right Coronary Artery (RCA) — supplies right ventricle and posterior wall; branches into Posterior Descending Artery (PDA)
