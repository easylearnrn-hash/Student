Cardiovascular Medications

Antihypertensives, antianginals, heart failure drugs, anticoagulants, antiplatelets, thrombolytics

Antihypertensives
ClassExamplesMechanismKey Nursing Note
ACE Inhibitors Lisinopril, Captopril, Enalapril Block angiotensin-converting enzyme → ↓ angiotensin II → vasodilation Dry cough (common SE) → switch to ARB. Monitor K+ (hyperkalemia). Avoid in pregnancy
ARBs Losartan, Valsartan, Candesartan Block angiotensin II receptor → vasodilation No cough (vs ACE-I). Monitor K+. Avoid in pregnancy. Better tolerated
Beta-Blockers Metoprolol, Atenolol, Carvedilol Block β₁ adrenergic receptors → ↓ HR and contractility Hold if HR <60. Monitor for bradycardia, fatigue, bronchospasm. Do NOT stop abruptly
Calcium Channel Blockers Amlodipine, Diltiazem, Verapamil, Nifedipine Block Ca²⁺ channels → vasodilation (Amlodipine), ↓ HR (Diltiazem/Verapamil) Monitor for peripheral edema, reflex tachycardia (Nifedipine). No grapefruit
Thiazide Diuretics Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ), Chlorthalidone Block Na⁺ reabsorption in DCT → ↑ urine output → ↓ plasma volume Monitor K+ (hypokalemia), glucose (hyperglycemia), uric acid (gout risk)
Antianginals

Nitrates (NTG)

  • SL NTG Sublingual tablet or spray — onset 1–2 min
  • Dilates coronary arteries and veins → ↓ preload and ↑ O₂ supply
  • Protocol: 1 tablet q5 min × 3 doses. If no relief after 3rd dose → call 911 / STEMI protocol
  • Side effects: headache, hypotension, reflex tachycardia
  • Contraindicated with PDE5 inhibitors (Sildenafil/Viagra) — severe hypotension
  • Store in dark glass container, keep cool

Beta-Blockers & CCBs (Antianginal)

  • Metoprolol/Atenolol: reduce HR and workload → ↓ O₂ demand
  • First-line for stable angina prophylaxis
  • Diltiazem/Verapamil: also reduce HR + vasospasm (Prinzmetal's angina)
  • Ranolazine (Ranexa): alternative for chronic angina, ↓ late Na⁺ current
Heart Failure Medications

Digoxin (Cardiac Glycoside)

  • Positive inotrope (↑ contractility) + negative chronotrope (↓ HR)
  • Used in HF with A-fib (rate control)
  • Narrow therapeutic index: 0.5–2 ng/mL
  • Toxicity signs: halos around lights, nausea/vomiting, bradycardia, dysrhythmias
  • Hold if HR <60
  • Antidote: Digibind (Digoxin-specific Fab)

Diuretics in HF

  • Furosemide (Lasix): Loop diuretic → ↓ fluid volume → ↓ preload
  • Monitor K+ (hypokalemia) and daily weight
  • Weight gain rule: 2 lbs in 24 hrs or 5 lbs in 1 week → call provider
  • Spironolactone: K+-sparing diuretic, also improves HF survival
  • ACE-I/ARBs + Beta-blockers: cornerstone of HFrEF therapy
Anticoagulants

Heparin

  • Monitor: aPTT (therapeutic: 45–80 sec)
  • Antidote: Protamine Sulfate
  • Watch for HIT (thrombocytopenia)

Warfarin (Coumadin)

  • Monitor: INR (therapeutic: 2–3)
  • Antidote: Vitamin K
  • Consistent Vitamin K intake; avoid NSAIDs; frequent INR checks
Antiplatelets

Aspirin

  • Irreversibly inhibits COX-1 → ↓ thromboxane A₂ → ↓ platelet aggregation
  • 325 mg acute MI; 81 mg daily maintenance
  • Give immediately in suspected MI
  • Side effects: GI upset, bleeding; take with food

Clopidogrel (Plavix)

  • P2Y12 receptor blocker → ↓ platelet aggregation
  • Used post-PCI: Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT) with Aspirin × 1 year
  • Hold 5–7 days before surgery
  • Ticagrelor (Brilinta) alternative
Thrombolytics

tPA (Alteplase) — Clot Busters

  • Activates plasminogen → fibrinolysis → dissolves clot
  • STEMI: use if PCI >120 min away; must be given within 3–4.5 hours of symptom onset
  • Ischemic stroke: within 4.5 hours of onset
  • Contraindications: recent surgery (<3 months), active bleeding, hemorrhagic stroke, severe HTN
  • Monitor for bleeding closely during infusion
Statins

HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors

  • Examples: Atorvastatin (Lipitor), Rosuvastatin (Crestor), Simvastatin (Zocor)
  • Reduce LDL → stabilize plaque → prevent MI/stroke
  • Give at bedtime (cholesterol synthesis highest at night)
  • Monitor: liver enzymes, CK (rhabdomyolysis risk)
  • Side effects: myalgia, myopathy — report muscle pain immediately
  • No grapefruit (↑ serum levels)
NCLEX Priorities: Hold beta-blocker if HR <60. Hold Digoxin if HR <60. NTG → check BP first (hold if systolic <90). Never stop beta-blockers abruptly (rebound hypertension/MI).
