Blood Pressure — Detailed

BP Classification (JNC)

  • Normal: <120/<80
  • Elevated: 120–129 / <80
  • HTN Stage 1: 130–139 / 80–89
  • HTN Stage 2: ≥140 / ≥90
  • Hypertensive Crisis: >180/>120
  • Hypotension: <90/60

MAP — Mean Arterial Pressure

  • Formula: MAP = (SBP + 2×DBP) ÷ 3
  • Example: 120/80 → (120 + 160) ÷ 3 = 93 mmHg
  • Normal: 70–100 mmHg
  • MAP <60 mmHg = organ hypoperfusion (brain, kidneys, heart)
  • MAP ≥65 = minimum goal in shock management
Why MAP matters: MAP reflects actual perfusion pressure to organs. A normal SBP doesn't guarantee adequate perfusion — MAP gives the true picture.

Temperature — Clinical Significance

Fever

  • Fever threshold: 38°C / 100.4°F
  • Low-grade: 37.3–38°C
  • High fever: >39°C / 102.2°F
  • Hyperpyrexia: >41°C / 105.8°F (emergency)
  • Causes: infection, inflammation, malignancy, medications
  • Nursing: antipyretics, cooling measures, cultures before antibiotics

Hypothermia

  • Definition: <36°C / 96.8°F
  • Mild: 32–36°C — shivering
  • Moderate: 28–32°C — no shivering (dangerous)
  • Severe: <28°C — cardiac arrhythmias, arrest risk
  • Targeted Temperature Management: 32–36°C post-ROSC
  • Do not declare death until rewarmed (NCLEX: "not dead until warm and dead")
SpO₂ — Oxygen Saturation

SpO₂ Reference

  • Normal: 95–100%
  • Mild hypoxia: 91–94% — supplemental O₂
  • Moderate hypoxia: 86–90% — higher flow O₂
  • Severe hypoxia: <86% — emergency (mechanical ventilation may be needed)
  • COPD patients: target SpO₂ 88–92% (hypoxic drive)
  • Post-cardiac arrest: target 94–98% (avoid hyperoxia)
Organ Perfusion Priorities

Brain

  • MAP >60 needed for autoregulation
  • Ischemia within 4–6 minutes
  • Irreversible damage after 10 min

Heart

  • Needs diastolic BP for coronary perfusion
  • Ischemia in minutes without perfusion
  • HR >100 = less filling time → less CO

Kidneys

  • MAP >65 needed for filtration
  • UO goal: >0.5 mL/kg/hr
  • <30 mL/hr = oliguria → assess volume status
NCLEX Critical Values to Know: BP <90/60 = hypotension → assess. HR <60 = hold Digoxin/BB. HR >100 = assess cause. RR <12 = opioid overdose? → Narcan. Temp >38°C = fever → cultures before antibiotics. SpO₂ <95% → supplemental O₂.
