Ischemic Heart Disease

IHD, coronary artery territories, angina types, STEMI/NSTEMI, nitroglycerin protocol

IHD Overview

What Is IHD?

  • Group of cardiac conditions caused by reduced blood flow to the myocardium
  • Caused by atherosclerosis (most common), coronary vasospasm, thrombosis
  • Spectrum: Silent ischemia → Stable angina → Unstable angina → NSTEMI → STEMI
  • Risk factors: HTN, DM, hyperlipidemia, smoking, obesity, family history, male >45/female >55
Coronary Arteries & Territory

LAD (Left Anterior Descending)

  • Supplies: Anterior wall, septum
  • "Widow Maker" — largest territory
  • Occlusion → anterior STEMI (leads V1–V4)
  • High risk of complete LV failure

LCX (Left Circumflex)

  • Supplies: Lateral wall, SA node (in some)
  • Occlusion → lateral MI (leads I, aVL, V5–V6)
  • Less common; often non-dominant

RCA (Right Coronary Artery)

  • Supplies: Right ventricle, inferior wall, AV node (80%)
  • Occlusion → inferior STEMI (leads II, III, aVF)
  • AV blocks common with RCA MI

STEMI vs NSTEMI

STEMI

  • Complete occlusion of coronary artery
  • Transmural (full-thickness) infarction
  • EKG: ST elevation + Q waves (later)
  • Troponin elevated
  • Treatment: Primary PCI within 90 minutes (door-to-balloon)
  • If PCI unavailable → thrombolytics within 30 min

NSTEMI

  • Partial occlusion or severe vasospasm
  • Subendocardial infarction (partial thickness)
  • EKG: ST depression and/or T-wave inversion (no ST elevation)
  • Troponin elevated
  • Treatment: Anticoagulation + dual antiplatelet; early PCI within 24–72 hrs
Key Difference: STEMI = ST elevation = complete blockage = 90-min PCI deadline. NSTEMI = no ST elevation = partial blockage = less urgent but still needs intervention.
Nitroglycerin (NTG) Protocol

Sublingual NTG — Angina Treatment

1
Check BP — hold if systolic <90 mmHg. Have patient sit down.
2
Give 1 SL tablet or 1 spray under tongue. Wait 5 minutes.
3
If pain not relieved → 2nd dose. Wait 5 more minutes.
4
If still no relief → 3rd dose. Wait 5 minutes.
!
After 3 doses with no relief → Call 911 / Activate STEMI protocol immediately
NTG Storage: Keep in original dark glass bottle. Store at room temperature. Replace every 6 months or when burning sensation on tongue disappears (indicates loss of potency).
Acute MI Management — MONA

MONA (First-Line)

  • Morphine — pain relief, ↓ preload (IV, caution with hypotension)
  • Oxygen — only if SpO₂ <94% (avoid hyperoxia)
  • Nitroglycerin — vasodilate, ↓ angina
  • Aspirin — 325 mg chewed immediately

Additional ACS Treatment

  • 12-lead EKG within 10 min of presentation
  • Clopidogrel + Aspirin (DAPT)
  • Heparin (anticoagulation)
  • Beta-blocker (↓ workload, ↓ re-infarction risk)
  • ACE inhibitor (↑ survival post-MI)
  • Statin therapy
