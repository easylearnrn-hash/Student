Cardiovascular System

Cardiac Biomarkers

Troponin, BNP, CK-MB, Myoglobin — values, timing, and clinical significance

Why Biomarkers?

Clinical Purpose

  • Biomarkers are proteins released into blood when cardiac cells are damaged
  • Used to diagnose MI, heart failure, and assess cardiac stress
  • Serial values every 3–6 hrs confirm or rule out MI
  • Rising AND falling pattern = MI (not just one elevated value)
Troponin — Gold Standard for MI

Troponin I

  • Normal: < 0.04 ng/mL
  • Rises: 3–6 hours post-injury
  • Peaks: 12–24 hours
  • Normalizes: 7–10 days
  • Highly specific to cardiac muscle

Troponin T

  • Normal: < 0.01 ng/mL
  • Rises: 3–6 hours post-injury
  • Peaks: 12–24 hours
  • Normalizes: 10–14 days
  • High-sensitivity Troponin T (hsTnT) detects smaller MIs
🏆 NCLEX Priority: Troponin is the MOST specific and MOST sensitive biomarker for MI. It stays elevated the LONGEST (up to 14 days), which can confirm a recent MI even days after the event.

BNP — B-Type Natriuretic Peptide

BNP Key Facts

  • Released by ventricular myocytes when walls are stretched (volume overload)
  • Normal: < 100 pg/mL
  • High BNP = Heart Failure indicator
  • Used to differentiate cardiac from pulmonary dyspnea
  • NT-proBNP: normal < 300 pg/mL (longer half-life)

BNP Clinical Significance

  • BNP 100–400: possible HF
  • BNP >400: likely HF exacerbation
  • Guides diuretic therapy and hospital discharge
  • Higher BNP = worse prognosis
  • Does NOT indicate MI — it indicates fluid overload
Myoglobin

Myoglobin Facts

  • First biomarker to rise after MI — 1–3 hours
  • Normalizes quickly: returns to normal within 24 hours
  • NOT cardiac-specific (found in skeletal and cardiac muscle)
  • Clinical use: Rule-OUT MI in early presentation (if normal at 3 hrs, MI less likely)

Other Relevant Labs

BUN / Creatinine

  • BUN: 8–20 mg/dL
  • Creatinine: 0.6–1.2 mg/dL
  • ↑ in reduced cardiac output → renal hypoperfusion

ABG / Lactate

  • Metabolic acidosis in shock/MI
  • Lactate >2 mmol/L = tissue hypoperfusion
  • Used to assess shock severity

Lipid Panel

  • Total chol <200 mg/dL desired
  • LDL <70 mg/dL in high-risk patients
  • HDL >60 = protective
NCLEX Priorities

Must Know

  • Troponin = most specific, most sensitive, longest duration — gold standard
  • Serial Troponin q3–6h to diagnose MI (single value not enough)
  • CK-MB: use when checking for re-infarction (returns to normal faster than Troponin)
  • BNP: rule out/confirm heart failure cause of dyspnea
  • Myoglobin: earliest but non-specific — used only for early RULE-OUT
