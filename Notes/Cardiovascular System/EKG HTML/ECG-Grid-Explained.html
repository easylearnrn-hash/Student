Understanding ECG Paper

ECG paper is divided into a grid of small and large boxes. Each box represents a precise interval of time, allowing clinicians to measure cardiac intervals and calculate heart rate with accuracy.

Paper Speed: 25 mm/sec 1 Small Box = 1 mm Standard Gain: 10 mm/mV

Grid Measurements at a Glance
Unit 1 — Smallest 0.04 s 1 Small Box

Width: 1 mm at 25 mm/sec
The fundamental time unit of ECG paper.

Unit 2 — Large Box 0.20 s 5 Small Boxes

5 × 0.04 s = 0.20 s
Normal PR interval = 3–5 large boxes.

Unit 3 — One Second 1.00 s 5 Large Boxes

5 × 0.20 s = 1.00 s
Used to calculate heart rate from R-R intervals.

Clinical Tip — Heart Rate Calculation

There are two widely used methods to calculate heart rate from an ECG strip. Choose based on whether the rhythm is regular or irregular.

Method 1 — Regular Rhythm 300 ÷ Large Boxes HR = 300 ÷ (R-R large boxes)

Count the number of large boxes between two consecutive R-wave peaks, then divide 300 by that number.

Example: 4 large boxes → 300 ÷ 4 = 75 bpm
Method 2 — Irregular Rhythm QRS Count × 10 HR = QRS count (6 sec) × 10

Mark a 6-second strip (30 large boxes). Count every QRS complex within that window, then multiply by 10.

Example: 7 QRS in 6 sec → 7 × 10 = 70 bpm
Method 3 — Quick Estimate Memorization Rule 300 → 150 → 100 → 75 → 60 → 50

Count large boxes between R-waves and match to this sequence: 1 box = 300, 2 = 150, 3 = 100, 4 = 75, 5 = 60, 6 = 50 bpm.

Fastest bedside estimate for regular rhythms
