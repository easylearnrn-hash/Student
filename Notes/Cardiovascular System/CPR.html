CAB Sequence
C

Compressions — First Priority

Begin chest compressions immediately. Do NOT delay for airway or breathing.

A

Airway

Head-tilt chin-lift (no trauma). Jaw thrust if cervical spine injury suspected.

B

Breathing

Look-listen-feel for breathing ≤10 seconds. If absent → 2 rescue breaths.

Old ABC → New CAB (2010 AHA Update): Compressions first because oxygenation fails without circulation. Initiate compressions within 10 seconds of recognizing arrest.
Adult CPR Specifications

Compressions

  • Depth: at least 2 inches (5 cm)
  • Rate: 100–120 compressions/min
  • Hand position: center of chest, lower half of sternum
  • Allow full chest recoil between compressions
  • Minimize interruptions (<10 sec)
  • Switch compressors every 2 minutes to prevent fatigue

Ventilations

  • Ratio: 30 compressions : 2 breaths (1 or 2 rescuers, adult)
  • Each breath: over 1 second, visible chest rise
  • Avoid hyperventilation (↑ intrathoracic pressure → ↓ CO)
  • With advanced airway (ET tube): 1 breath q6 sec continuous compressions

AED — Automated External Defibrillator

AED Steps

  • 1. Power ON the AED
  • 2. Attach pads: right clavicle / left lateral chest
  • 3. Let AED analyze (stop CPR, don't touch patient)
  • 4. If shockable → deliver shock → resume CPR immediately
  • 5. If no shock advised → resume CPR, reanalyze q2 min
Shockable vs Non-Shockable Rhythms

SHOCKABLE — Defibrillate

  • Ventricular Fibrillation (V-fib) — chaotic, no QRS
  • Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia (V-tach)
  • AED/defibrillator will advise shock
  • Biphasic: 120–200 J; Monophasic: 360 J

NON-SHOCKABLE — CPR + Epinephrine

  • Asystole ("flat line")
  • Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA)
  • Treat reversible causes (5 H's and 5 T's)
  • Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO q3–5 min
5 H's and 5 T's (Reversible Causes)

5 H's

  • Hypovolemia
  • Hypoxia
  • Hydrogen ion (Acidosis)
  • Hypo/Hyperkalemia
  • Hypothermia

5 T's

  • Tension pneumothorax
  • Tamponade (cardiac)
  • Toxins
  • Thrombosis (pulmonary)
  • Thrombosis (coronary)

ROSC — Return of Spontaneous Circulation

Signs of ROSC

  • Pulse palpable
  • Spontaneous breathing
  • SpO₂ detectable on pulse oximetry
  • ETCO₂ sudden rise >40 mmHg during CPR
  • BP detectable on arterial line
Post-ROSC Care: 12-lead EKG, oxygen titration (SpO₂ 94–98%), avoid hyperthermia. Targeted Temperature Management (TTM) if remains unconscious — 32–36°C for 24 hours. Transfer to PCI if STEMI on EKG.
🆕 New CPR Rule: Continue CPR even if rib fractures occur — the priority is maintaining circulation. Broken ribs can be repaired; death from no CPR cannot be reversed.
