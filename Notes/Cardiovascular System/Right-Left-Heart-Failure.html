Right & Left Heart Failure

Pathophysiology, symptoms, and treatment of right-sided vs left-sided heart failure

Heart Failure Overview

What Is Heart Failure?

  • Heart unable to pump sufficient blood to meet body's metabolic needs
  • Results in backward congestion of fluid and/or inadequate forward output
  • HFrEF: Reduced ejection fraction (<40%) — dilated, weak heart
  • HFpEF: Preserved ejection fraction (>50%) — stiff, non-compliant heart
  • EF normal: 55–70%; HF diagnosis if symptoms present regardless of EF
Left-Sided Heart Failure
❤️ Left Side = Lungs (Pulmonary Congestion)

Causes

  • Mitral valve disease (regurgitation/stenosis)
  • Aortic valve disease
  • MI (especially anterior/LAD territory)
  • Hypertension (chronic pressure overload)
  • Cardiomyopathy

Pathophysiology

  • LV fails → blood backs up into pulmonary veins
  • Pulmonary venous pressure ↑ → fluid leaks into alveoli
  • Result: pulmonary edema and respiratory compromise

Left HF Symptoms — "PULMONARY"

  • Pulmonary edema — pink frothy sputum (severe)
  • Dyspnea on exertion — first symptom
  • Orthopnea — can't lie flat, needs 2+ pillows
  • Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea (PND) — wakes up gasping at night
  • Crackles (rales) in lung bases bilaterally
  • Cough (especially at night)
  • S3 gallop (ventricular filling sound) — hallmark of HF
  • Fatigue, decreased exercise tolerance
Right-Sided Heart Failure
💙 Right Side = Body (Systemic Congestion)

Causes

  • Left-sided HF (most common cause — backup from left)
  • Tricuspid valve disease
  • Pulmonary hypertension (cor pulmonale)
  • Right ventricular MI
  • Chronic pulmonary disease (COPD, PE)

Pathophysiology

  • RV fails → blood backs up into venous system
  • Systemic venous pressure ↑ → fluid accumulates in body
  • Result: peripheral congestion

Right HF Symptoms — "PERIPHERAL"

  • Jugular Venous Distension (JVD) — elevated venous pressure in neck
  • Peripheral pitting edema — bilateral ankle/leg edema
  • Weight gain — rapid fluid retention (1 kg = 1 L)
  • Ascites — fluid in abdomen (severe right HF)
  • Hepatomegaly — liver congestion → right upper quadrant pain
  • Nausea, anorexia (GI congestion)
  • Fatigue, weakness

Treatment

Both Sides — General Treatment

  • Diuretics (Furosemide) — reduce fluid overload
  • ACE inhibitors / ARBs — reduce afterload, improve survival
  • Beta-blockers (Carvedilol, Metoprolol) — ↓ workload
  • Daily weight monitoring (2 lbs/day or 5 lbs/week = call provider)
  • Sodium restriction (<2 g/day)
  • Fluid restriction in severe HF

Left HF Specific

  • Elevate HOB (high Fowler's) → ↓ preload
  • Supplemental O₂; BiPAP for refractory pulmonary edema
  • Digoxin for A-fib + HF rate control
  • Aldosterone antagonists (Spironolactone)
  • LVAD or heart transplant if refractory HFrEF
NCLEX Memory: Left = Lungs. Right = Retention (fluid). If lungs are wet → left HF. If legs are swollen + JVD → right HF. Left HF often CAUSES right HF (the backup travels from lungs to right heart).
Weight Rule: Teach patient: weigh daily, same time, same scale. Report >2 lb gain in 24 hrs or >5 lb gain in 1 week — signs of fluid retention requiring medication adjustment.
