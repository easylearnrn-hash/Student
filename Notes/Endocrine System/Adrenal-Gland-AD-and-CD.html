Adrenal Gland — Addison's & Cushing's

Adrenal cortex hormones, Addison's disease (↓ cortisol) vs Cushing's disease (↑ cortisol)

Adrenal Gland Anatomy

Adrenal Gland Structure

  • Cortex (outer): Produces steroid hormones — regulated by ACTH from anterior pituitary
  • Medulla (inner): Produces catecholamines (Epinephrine, Norepinephrine) — NOT regulated by ACTH

⬆ Cushing's Disease (Too Much Cortisol)
Cause: Tumor in adrenal cortex or small-cell lung cancer → excess cortisol production. Also from prolonged steroid use (Cushing's Syndrome). NCLEX wants cortisol to be HIGH for this disease.

Symptoms ( Everything)

  • Weight gain: trunk obesity with purple striae (stretch marks)
  • Moon face (rounded face)
  • Buffalo hump (fat on back of neck)
  • Thin legs (disproportionate fat)
  • Hirsutism (excess hair growth)
  • ↑ BP, ↑ HR
  • Hyperglycemia (↑ blood sugar)
  • Hypernatremia, Hypokalemia
  • Metabolic alkalosis
  • Osteoporosis (cortisol pulls Ca from bones)
  • Hypercalcemia
  • Acne, gynecomastia (men)
  • Butterfly rash

Treatment

  • Partial adrenalectomy (one adrenal gland removed)
  • Full adrenalectomy (both adrenal glands)
  • After surgery: long-term hormone replacement (Prednisone)
  • NEVER stop hormones abruptly
  • For thyroid storm component → Dexamethasone

Cushing's Syndrome (vs Disease)

  • Cushing's Disease: caused by a pituitary or adrenal tumor → excess ACTH → excess cortisol
  • Cushing's Syndrome: caused by prolonged external steroid use (iatrogenic) — reversible with treatment
⬇ Addison's Disease (Too Little Cortisol)
Cause: Adrenalectomy, autoimmune attack on adrenal glands, genetic factors → insufficient adrenal hormone production. "Everything goes down."

Symptoms ( Everything)

  • Weight loss, thin appearance
  • Fatigue, decreased motivation
  • Bronze skin (hyperpigmentation)
  • Dehydration, dry skin
  • Bad mood, depression
  • ↓ BP, ↓ HR
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Hyponatremia, Hyperkalemia
  • Metabolic acidosis
  • Hypoxia (low oxygen)
  • HYPOvolemic HYPOnatremia
  • Stress intolerance

Treatment

  • Steroids (Prednisone IV for crisis; oral in morning with food)
  • Adequate sodium intake (table salt)
  • High protein, low potassium diet
  • Plenty of water
  • Increase steroid dose during physical/emotional stress
  • NEVER stop steroids abruptly
🚨 Addison's Crisis (Adrenal Crisis)

Extreme Emergency

  • Triggered by: emotional or physical stress, infection, missed steroids
  • BP can drop to 70/50 mmHg
  • Treatment: IV corticosteroids (hydrocortisone), IV fluids, close monitoring
  • Instruct patients: NEVER miss a steroid dose; carry medical alert ID

NCLEX Must-Know

Top NCLEX Points

  • Cushing's = high cortisol; Addison's = low cortisol
  • Bronze skin = Addison's disease hallmark
  • Moon face + buffalo hump + striae = Cushing's hallmarks
  • Adrenal crisis → IV steroids + IV fluids immediately
  • NEVER stop hormone replacement abruptly (both conditions)
  • Increase steroid dose during stress/surgery (Addison's patients)
  • Cushing's: HYPERvolemic HYPERnatremia; Addison's: HYPOvolemic HYPOnatremia
  • Aldosterone manages Na/K/water — key for BP and fluid balance
