🍭 Diabetes Medications
Insulin (All Types)

Used for: Type 1 DM, Type 2 DM (uncontrolled), DKA

Types: Regular (R), Lispro/Aspart (rapid-acting), NPH (intermediate), Glargine (long-acting)

⚠️ Side effects: Hypoglycemia, weight gain, lipodystrophy at injection sites

Metformin (Biguanide)

Used for: Type 2 DM — first-line oral medication

Key rule: HOLD 24 hrs before contrast dye or surgery; restart 48 hrs after

⚠️ Side effects: GI upset, lactic acidosis (rare but serious)

Sulfonylureas — Glyburide, Glipizide

Used for: Type 2 DM — stimulate pancreas to release more insulin

Caution: Sulfa allergy — cross-reaction risk

⚠️ Side effects: Hypoglycemia, weight gain

DPP-4 Inhibitors — Sitagliptin (Januvia)

Used for: Type 2 DM — incretin-based, glucose-dependent insulin release

⚠️ Side effects: Pancreatitis, joint pain

SGLT2 Inhibitors — Canagliflozin (Invokana)

Used for: Type 2 DM — increases glucose excretion in urine

⚠️ Side effects: UTIs, genital fungal infections, dehydration, weight loss

🦋 Thyroid Medications
Levothyroxine (L-Thyroxine, Synthroid)

Used for: Hypothyroidism (Hashimoto's)

Timing: Take at 5 AM on an empty stomach; onset ~6 weeks

Key rule: Do NOT stop abruptly; never skip doses

⚠️ Side effects (overdose): Insomnia, weight loss, palpitations, tachycardia — notify MD, dose may need lowering

Propylthiouracil (PTU) & Methimazole

Used for: Hyperthyroidism, Graves' disease

PTU advantage: Safe in pregnancy and for patients under 18 (Methimazole is NOT)

⚠️ Side effects: Agranulocytosis (report sore throat immediately → CBC), liver damage, rash

Radioactive Iodine (RAI)

Used for: Hyperthyroidism, thyroid storm (Toxic Goiter), thyroid cancer

NOT for: Pregnancy, patients under 18

Precautions: Limit visitors; nurses use barriers; flush toilet twice; no close contact with pregnant/children

⚠️ Side effects: Dry mouth, post-RAI hypothyroidism (may need Levothyroxine)

🧠 Adrenal Medications
Prednisone / Hydrocortisone (Corticosteroids)

Used for: Addison's disease (adrenal insufficiency), inflammation, autoimmune conditions

Key rule: Take with food to prevent GI upset; NEVER stop abruptly (adrenal crisis risk)

Increase dose during: Stress, infection, surgery (body needs more cortisol)

⚠️ Side effects: ↑ blood sugar, ↑ infection risk, mood changes, osteoporosis, Cushing's syndrome with long-term use

Fludrocortisone (Florinef)

Used for: Addison's disease — replaces aldosterone (mineralocorticoid replacement)

⚠️ Side effects: Fluid retention, hypertension, hypokalemia (low potassium)

🦴 Parathyroid / Bone Medications
Calcium Supplements

Used for: Hypocalcemia, osteoporosis prevention, post-thyroidectomy

⚠️ Side effects: Constipation, kidney stones (high doses)

Vitamin D

Used for: Enhances intestinal calcium absorption; given with calcium supplements

⚠️ Side effects: Rare; toxicity in high doses (hypercalcemia)

Calcitonin (Miacalcin)

Used for: Hypercalcemia, osteoporosis; lowers serum calcium

⚠️ Side effects: Flushing, GI upset

Cinacalcet (Sensipar)

Used for: Hyperparathyroidism, dialysis patients — decreases PTH production

⚠️ Side effects: Hypocalcemia, muscle cramps

Alendronate (Bisphosphonate — Fosamax)

Used for: Osteoporosis — prevents bone resorption

Administration: Take on empty stomach with full glass of water; sit upright for 30 minutes

⚠️ Side effects: Esophagitis, esophageal ulcer (if patient lies down after taking)

NCLEX Must-Know

Top NCLEX Points

  • Metformin → HOLD before contrast/surgery (lactic acidosis risk)
  • Sore throat on PTU/Methimazole → agranulocytosis → hold drug, get CBC stat
  • PTU safe in pregnancy; Methimazole is NOT
  • Levothyroxine overdose signs = tachycardia, insomnia, weight loss
  • Corticosteroids → NEVER stop abruptly; increase dose with stress
  • Alendronate → stay upright 30 min (esophagitis prevention)
  • Cinacalcet lowers PTH; monitor for hypocalcemia
  • RAI precautions: flush twice, limit visitors, no pregnant/child contact
