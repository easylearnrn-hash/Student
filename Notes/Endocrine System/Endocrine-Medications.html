Thyroid Medications
Hypothyroidism
MedicationUseNCLEX Safety
Levothyroxine Main treatment for hypothyroidism Take in morning on empty stomach, 30–60 min before breakfast; monitor TSH/T4
Liothyronine Synthetic T3; faster acting More cardiac stimulation; use carefully
Levothyroxine — signs of too much dose: tachycardia, chest pain, palpitations, nervousness, sweating, insomnia, weight loss. Levothyroxine treats hypothyroidism by replacing thyroid hormone and is usually taken once daily on an empty stomach before breakfast.
Hyperthyroidism
MedicationUseNCLEX Safety
Methimazole Decreases thyroid hormone production Report fever/sore throat = possible agranulocytosis
Propylthiouracil / PTU Decreases thyroid hormone production; also blocks T4 to T3 conversion Risk of severe liver injury; often preferred in 1st trimester pregnancy or thyroid storm
Radioactive iodine Destroys thyroid tissue Avoid pregnancy; radiation precautions; may cause hypothyroidism
Lugol solution / SSKI Iodine solution used before thyroid surgery or thyroid storm Give after antithyroid medication, not before
Beta blockers (Propranolol, Atenolol) Controls symptoms: tachycardia, tremor, anxiety Symptomatic relief; does not lower thyroid hormone
PTU: can cause severe liver injury — report jaundice, dark urine, fever, nausea, or right upper abdominal pain immediately.
Thyroid Emergency Medications
EmergencyMedications
Thyroid storm Beta blocker + PTU or methimazole + iodine solution + corticosteroid + cooling
Myxedema coma IV levothyroxine + hydrocortisone + warming + airway/ventilation support
NCLEX order for thyroid storm: give antithyroid medication first, then iodine. If iodine is given first, it can provide more substrate for thyroid hormone production.
Adrenal Medications
Addison's Disease / Adrenal Insufficiency
MedicationUseNCLEX Safety
Hydrocortisone Replaces cortisol Take with food; never stop abruptly; increase dose during stress/illness per provider
Prednisone Glucocorticoid replacement or anti-inflammatory Hyperglycemia, infection risk, osteoporosis, Cushingoid features
Dexamethasone Potent glucocorticoid Used in many endocrine tests/treatments
Fludrocortisone Replaces aldosterone Monitor BP, edema, sodium, potassium
Adrenal insufficiency is treated by replacing missing adrenal hormones. Adrenal crisis: immediate IV corticosteroids + IV saline + dextrose.
Corticosteroid NCLEX Safety
ClassExamplesNCLEX Point
Glucocorticoids Prednisone, Hydrocortisone, Methylprednisolone, Dexamethasone Never stop suddenly; causes hyperglycemia, infection risk, fluid retention, osteoporosis
Mineralocorticoid Fludrocortisone Retains sodium/water, loses potassium; monitor BP and edema

Patient Teaching — Corticosteroids

  • Take with food
  • Do not stop abruptly
  • Report fever or infection
  • Monitor blood glucose
  • Avoid sick contacts
  • Wear medical alert identification if chronic adrenal insufficiency

Pituitary / ADH Medications
Diabetes Insipidus
MedicationUseNCLEX Safety
Desmopressin / DDAVP Replaces ADH effect; decreases urine output Monitor for hyponatremia and water intoxication
Vasopressin ADH replacement; also vasoconstrictor Monitor BP, urine output, sodium
Thiazide diuretics Nephrogenic DI sometimes Paradoxically decreases urine output
NCLEX response to DDAVP working: urine output decreases, urine specific gravity increases, serum sodium normalizes.

Hypercalcemia / Hyperparathyroidism
MedicationUseNCLEX Safety
CalcitoninQuickly lowers calciumShort-term effect; monitor calcium
Bisphosphonates (Alendronate, Ibandronate, Risedronate, Zoledronic acid)Prevent bone breakdownTake oral forms with full glass of water and stay upright
CinacalcetDecreases PTH and calciumWatch for hypocalcemia

Alendronate: must be taken on empty stomach with plain water; stay upright 30–60 min to avoid esophageal injury. Cinacalcet: signals the body to produce less PTH and lowers calcium.

Super High-Yield NCLEX Medication Rules

Top NCLEX Points

  • Metformin → hold for surgery/contrast per policy; monitor eGFR; lactic acidosis risk
  • Sulfonylureas → hypoglycemia risk; take with food
  • SGLT2 inhibitors → glucose leaves through urine; UTI/genital infection/dehydration/euglycemic DKA risk
  • Insulin → check glucose, know peak, watch hypoglycemia and hypokalemia
  • Regular insulin → only insulin used IV
  • NPH → cloudy, roll gently, can mix
  • Glargine / Detemir / Degludec → do not mix
  • Levothyroxine → morning, empty stomach, lifelong, monitor for hyperthyroid symptoms
  • Methimazole / PTU → report fever/sore throat; PTU has liver risk
  • DDAVP → for DI; monitor for hyponatremia
  • Tolvaptan → for SIADH/hyponatremia; sodium must not rise too fast
  • Steroids → never stop suddenly; infection and hyperglycemia risk
  • Fludrocortisone → monitor BP, edema, sodium, potassium
  • Alendronate → take with water, stay upright, empty stomach
  • Calcium gluconate → acute hypocalcemia/tetany
  • Calcitonin → lowers calcium
  • Cinacalcet → lowers PTH and calcium
