Endocrine System

Diabetes Mellitus

Pancreatic function, Type 1 vs Type 2, hyperglycemia vs hypoglycemia, 3 Ps, treatment ladder

Pancreatic Function

Alpha Cells

  • Produce glucagon
  • Released during hypOglycemia
  • Raises blood sugar by releasing glycogen from the liver

Beta Cells

  • Produce insulin
  • Released during hypERglycemia
  • Drives glucose from blood into cells for energy

Pancreatic Enzymes (Exocrine)

  • Amylase — breaks down carbohydrates → glucose
  • Lipase — breaks down fats → lipids (fatty acids)
  • Protease — breaks down proteins → amino acids
  • Released through the ductus pancreaticus into the duodenum

HypERglycemia — The 3 Ps + More

Classic Symptoms (3 Ps)

  • Polydipsia — extreme thirst
  • Polyuria — increased urination (large volumes)
  • Polyphagia — excessive hunger

Additional Signs

  • Dry mucous membranes
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Headache
  • Confused LOC (altered mental status)
  • Nausea & vomiting (severe)
HypOglycemia — Cold & Clammy Needs Candy

Symptoms

  • Diaphoresis (sweating), cold/clammy skin
  • Trembling / shaking hands
  • Tachycardia / palpitations
  • Dizziness, lightheadedness
  • Hunger (sudden, intense)
  • Irritability, mood changes
  • Confusion, difficulty concentrating
  • Blurred vision
  • Pale skin
  • Numbness/tingling (lips, tongue, fingers)
  • Seizures (severe)

Treatment Protocol

IF ALERT:

  1. Give 15 g simple carbs immediately: ½ cup OJ, ½ can regular soda, ½ cup milk, 3–4 glucose tablets
  2. Wait 15 minutes
  3. Check blood glucose (BG)
  4. If still low → give complex carbs + protein (protein slows carb absorption, sustains glucose longer)

IF NOT ALERT / UNCONSCIOUS:

  1. Glucagon injection — IM, IV, or SubQ
  2. Dextrose 50% (D50W) — IV push
"Rule of 15": 15 g carbs → wait 15 min → recheck BG. Repeat if still <70 mg/dL. Do NOT over-treat hypoglycemia.
Insulin Injection Sites & Technique

Subcutaneous (SQ) Injection

  • Abdomen: 1.5–2 inches (5 cm) away from the umbilicus (navel) — fastest absorption
  • Thighs — outer, upper thigh
  • Buttocks
  • Upper arms — under the biceps
  • Rotate sites daily — prevents lipodystrophy (abnormal fat deposits from repeated use)
Treatment Ladder

Type 1 DM

  1. Insulin — always required immediately

Type 2 DM

  1. Diet and exercise
  2. Metformin — first-line oral medication
  3. Insulin — if Metformin fails (HbA1c >7%)
Complications of Uncontrolled DM

Microvascular

  • Diabetic retinopathy — blindness
  • Nephropathy — kidney disease
  • Neuropathy — peripheral nerve damage

Macrovascular

  • Atherosclerosis — excess glucose stored as plaque
  • MI, stroke, PVD
  • Diabetic foot ulcers → amputation

Acute

  • DKA — Type 1: ketones + acidosis
  • HHS — Type 2: extreme hyperglycemia, no ketones
  • Hypoglycemia from over-medication
NCLEX Must-Know

Top NCLEX Points

  • Alpha cells → glucagon (↑ BG in hypoglycemia); Beta cells → insulin (↓ BG in hyperglycemia)
  • Normal fasting BG: 70–110 mg/dL
  • HbA1c reflects past 3 months average; diabetic target <7%
  • 3 Ps = Polydipsia, Polyuria, Polyphagia → hyperglycemia
  • Hypoglycemia: cold, clammy, shaky → Rule of 15
  • Unconscious hypoglycemic → glucagon IM or D50W IV (NOT oral glucose)
  • Abdomen = fastest insulin absorption; rotate sites daily to prevent lipodystrophy
  • Type 1 treatment = insulin only; Type 2 = diet → Metformin → insulin
  • DKA = Type 1; HHS = Type 2 (no ketones)
