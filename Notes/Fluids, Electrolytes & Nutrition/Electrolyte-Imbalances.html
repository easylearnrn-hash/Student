Electrolyte Imbalances

Electrolytes control fluid balance, nerve signals, muscle contractions, and heart rhythm — all high-yield NCLEX topics

Na⁺ = Brain K⁺ = Heart Ca²⁺ = Muscles/Nerves Mg²⁺ = Reflexes

1. Sodium (Na⁺) 135–145 mEq/L — Controls Fluid & Nerves

⬇ Hyponatremia <135

Too much water / diluted sodium

Causes:
  • Excess water intake
  • Heart failure, SIADH
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, sweating
Symptoms:
  • Confusion, headache (brain swells)
  • Seizures (severe)
  • Muscle cramps, weakness
Treatment:
  • Mild: restrict water intake
  • Severe: 3% NS (hypertonic) — correct slowly!
  • Monitor for seizures
⬆ Hypernatremia >145

Too little water / concentrated sodium

Causes:
  • Dehydration
  • Diabetes insipidus
  • Excessive sodium intake
Symptoms:
  • Intense thirst
  • Dry mouth, swollen tongue
  • Confusion, seizures (brain shrinks)
Treatment:
  • 0.45% NS (hypotonic)
  • Encourage oral fluids
  • Monitor mental status
NCLEX: Sodium imbalances = BRAIN symptoms (confusion, seizures). Correct sodium slowly — rapid correction → osmotic demyelination (hyponatremia) or cerebral edema (hypernatremia).

2. Potassium (K⁺) 3.5–5.0 mEq/L — Controls Heart & Muscles

⬇ Hypokalemia <3.5

Muscles slow down

Causes:
  • Diuretics (Furosemide/Lasix) — kidney excretes K⁺
  • Vomiting, diarrhea
  • Hyperaldosteronism
Symptoms:
  • Muscle weakness, cramps
  • Constipation (slowed GI)
  • ECG: flat T-waves, U-waves
  • Arrhythmias → cardiac arrest risk
Treatment:
  • Oral or IV K+ — NEVER IV push
  • K+-rich foods: bananas, oranges, spinach
  • Continuous ECG monitoring
⬆ Hyperkalemia >5.0

Muscles overreact

Causes:
  • Kidney failure
  • Burns, tissue damage (K⁺ releases)
  • Excessive K+ supplements
Symptoms:
  • Muscle twitching → weakness → paralysis
  • Diarrhea
  • ECG: peaked T-waves → cardiac arrest
Treatment (in order):
  1. Calcium gluconate — stabilize heart first
  2. Insulin + Dextrose — shift K⁺ into cells
  3. Kayexalate — remove K⁺ via stool
  4. Dialysis if renal failure
⚠️ NCLEX: High K⁺ = Cardiac emergency! Watch ECG for peaked T-waves. NEVER give K⁺ IV push — causes fatal arrhythmia.

3. Calcium (Ca²⁺) 8.5–10.5 mg/dL — Bones, Muscles & Nerves

⬇ Hypocalcemia <8.5

Overactive nerves

Causes:
  • Hypoparathyroidism (↓ PTH)
  • Vitamin D deficiency
  • Chronic Kidney Disease
Classic Signs:
  • Muscle spasms, tetany
  • Trousseau's Sign — hand spasm when BP cuff inflated
  • Chvostek's Sign — facial twitch when cheek tapped
  • Seizures (severe)
Treatment:
  • IV Calcium Gluconate (emergency)
  • Oral calcium + Vitamin D
⬆ Hypercalcemia >10.5

Slow muscles

Causes:
  • Hyperparathyroidism (↑ PTH)
  • Bone cancer (calcium leaks)
  • Excess calcium supplements/antacids
Symptoms:
  • Muscle weakness (bones, stones, groans)
  • Kidney stones (calcium deposits)
  • Constipation, N/V
  • Confusion, depression
Treatment:
  • IV fluids + loop diuretics (flush Ca out)
  • Calcitonin (lowers calcium)
NCLEX: Low Ca²⁺ = Twitching/tetany (Trousseau + Chvostek). High Ca²⁺ = Weak muscles + kidney stones. Remember: "Bones, Stones, Groans, Psychic Moans" = hypercalcemia.

4. Magnesium & Phosphorus

ElectrolyteNormalLow (Deficit)High (Excess)
Magnesium (Mg²⁺) 1.5–2.5 mg/dL Hypomagnesemia: Hyperreflexia, seizures, torsades de pointes → Give MgSO₄ Hypermagnesemia: Weak muscles, ↓ reflexes, ↓ BP, respiratory depression → Calcium gluconate
Phosphorus (PO₄³⁻) 2.5–4.5 mg/dL Hypophosphatemia: Weakness, confusion, bone pain Hyperphosphatemia: Same as low calcium — muscle spasms, cramps (inversely related)
Ca²⁺ and PO₄³⁻ are inversely related: When phosphorus ↑, calcium ↓ and vice versa. CKD causes ↑ phosphorus → ↓ calcium → bone disease.

5. NCLEX Memory Anchors

  • ✅ Na⁺ = Brain (confusion, seizures in both hypo and hyper)
  • ✅ K⁺ = Heart (arrhythmias, peaked T-waves in hyper; flat T + U-waves in hypo)
  • ✅ Ca²⁺ = Muscles (twitching when low; weakness when high)
  • ✅ Mg²⁺ = Reflexes (hyperactive when low; absent when high)
  • ✅ Ca²⁺ and PO₄³⁻ are inversely related
  • ✅ Trousseau's sign = BP cuff → hand spasm; Chvostek's = tap cheek → face twitch (both = low Ca)
  • ✅ Kayexalate + insulin/dextrose = hyperkalemia treatment (NOT K⁺ push)
🔑 Key Takeaway: Na⁺ = Brain. K⁺ = Heart (peaked T = too high; never push IV). Ca²⁺ = Muscles (Chvostek + Trousseau = low). Mg²⁺ = Reflexes. Ca²⁺ and phosphate = inversely related. All electrolyte imbalances can cause cardiac and neurological emergencies.
