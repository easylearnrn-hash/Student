IV Fluids, Electrolytes & Medications

IV fluid types, fluid balance imbalances, electrolyte medications, acid-base overview, and IV complications

Isotonic = Stays in Vessels Hypotonic → Into Cells Hypertonic → Out of Cells NEVER IV Push K+

1. IV Fluids — Types, Uses & Effects

💧 Isotonic

Stays in bloodstream — no fluid shift between compartments

  • 0.9% Normal Saline (NS)
  • Lactated Ringer's (LR)
  • D5W (5% dextrose in water)

Uses: Dehydration, blood loss, surgery

⚠️ Risk: Fluid overload if given too fast

💧 Hypotonic

Moves fluid into cells — cells swell

  • 0.45% NS (Half NS)

Uses: DKA, hypernatremia (high sodium)

⚠️ Risk: Cerebral edema, cell swelling — avoid in ICP or burns

💧 Hypertonic

Pulls fluid OUT of cells into bloodstream

  • 3% NS
  • D10W (10% dextrose in water)
  • D5NS, D5LR

Uses: Cerebral edema, severe hyponatremia

⚠️ Risk: Fluid overload, high BP, seizures if rapid

2. Fluid Balance Imbalances

ImbalanceCausesSigns & SymptomsTreatment
Fluid Volume Deficit
(Dehydration)		 Vomiting, diarrhea, burns, diabetes insipidus, hemorrhage Thirst, dry mouth, low BP, tachycardia, ↓ urine output, skin tenting Isotonic fluids (NS, LR)
Fluid Volume Excess
(Overload)		 Heart failure, kidney failure, excessive IV fluids, cirrhosis Edema, crackles in lungs, ↑ BP, weight gain, SOB, bounding pulse Diuretics (furosemide/Lasix), fluid restriction
Daily Weight = Best fluid balance indicator. 1 kg weight change = ~1 L of fluid. Weigh same time, same scale, same clothing every day.

3. Electrolyte Medications Quick Reference

🧠
Sodium (Na⁺) | Normal: 135–145 mEq/L
Hyponatremia (<135) → confusion, seizures → Give 3% NS (hypertonic) — correct slowly to prevent osmotic demyelination
Hypernatremia (>145) → dry mouth, thirst, confusion → Give 0.45% NS (hypotonic)
❤️
Potassium (K⁺) | Normal: 3.5–5.0 mEq/L
Hypokalemia (<3.5) → weakness, arrhythmias → Give oral/IV K+ (NEVER IV push — fatal!)
Hyperkalemia (>5.0) → peaked T-waves, cardiac arrest risk →
  • Kayexalate — removes K⁺ via stool
  • Insulin + Dextrose — pushes K⁺ into cells
  • Calcium gluconate — protects heart (does NOT lower K⁺)
🦴
Calcium (Ca²⁺) | Normal: 8.5–10.5 mg/dL
Hypocalcemia → twitches, tetany, Chvostek/Trousseau signs → IV/oral calcium + Vitamin D
Hypercalcemia → weakness, kidney stones, constipation → IV fluids + calcitonin
⚡
Magnesium (Mg²⁺) | Normal: 1.5–2.5 mg/dL
Hypomagnesemia → seizures, hyperreflexia, torsades de pointes → Magnesium sulfate
Hypermagnesemia → weakness, ↓ reflexes, ↓ BP → Calcium gluconate (antidote)
🔋
Phosphorus (PO₄³⁻) | Normal: 2.5–4.5 mg/dL
Hypophosphatemia → weakness, confusion
Hyperphosphatemia → same as low calcium (cramps, tingling) — inversely related to Ca²⁺

4. Acid-Base Balance Overview

ConditionpHPrimary CauseTreatment
Respiratory Acidosis<7.35 + ↑CO₂CO₂ retention — COPD, opioids, hypoventilationImprove ventilation, treat cause
Respiratory Alkalosis>7.45 + ↓CO₂CO₂ loss — anxiety, hyperventilation, feverCalm breathing, rebreathe CO₂
Metabolic Acidosis<7.35 + ↓HCO₃Too much acid or HCO₃ loss — DKA, diarrhea, renal failureIV fluids, insulin (DKA), bicarb if severe
Metabolic Alkalosis>7.45 + ↑HCO₃Acid loss or too much base — vomiting, antacids, diureticsStop diuretics, replace fluids/electrolytes
ROME Mnemonic: Respiratory = Opposite (pH ↓ when CO₂ ↑), Metabolic = Equal (pH ↓ when HCO₃ ↓)

5. IV Therapy Complications

ComplicationSigns & SymptomsNursing Action
InfiltrationSwelling, cool/pale skin, discomfort at siteStop IV, elevate limb, warm compress, restart in new site
PhlebitisRedness, warmth, vein feels hard/cordlikeStop IV, warm compress, document, rotate sites
ExtravasationBurning, blistering, tissue damage (vesicant)Stop IV (don't flush), aspirate if possible, antidote if ordered, notify provider
Air EmbolismSudden SOB, chest pain, cyanosis, confusionLeft lateral Trendelenburg (trap air in right atrium), O₂, call provider stat
Fluid OverloadCrackles, SOB, ↑ BP, weight gain, edemaSlow IV rate, diuretics (Lasix), notify provider
⚠️ Rotate IV sites every 72–96 hours. Always use aseptic technique — clean ports with alcohol. Site red/swollen/painful = STOP IV immediately.

6. NCLEX Must-Knows

  • ✅ Isotonic = stays in vessels (NS, LR, D5W); Hypotonic → into cells; Hypertonic → out of cells
  • ✅ NEVER give K⁺ IV push — fatal cardiac arrhythmia
  • ✅ Air embolism → left side + Trendelenburg
  • ✅ Daily weight = most accurate fluid balance monitor (1 kg = 1 L)
  • ✅ Calcium gluconate = antidote for hypermagnesemia AND cardiac protection in hyperkalemia
  • ✅ ROME: Respiratory Opposite, Metabolic Equal
  • ✅ Hypertonic solutions → monitor for fluid overload + lung sounds
🔑 Key Takeaway: Match IV fluid to clinical need — isotonic for dehydration/blood loss, hypotonic for DKA/hypernatremia, hypertonic for cerebral edema/severe hyponatremia. Never push K⁺ IV. Air embolism = left side Trendelenburg. Daily weights track fluid balance best.
