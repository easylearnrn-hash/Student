Acid-Base Balance & ABG Interpretation

Lungs regulate CO₂ (respiratory). Kidneys regulate HCO₃ (metabolic). ROME mnemonic guides ABG interpretation.

Normal pH 7.35–7.45 CO₂ 35–45 = Respiratory HCO₃ 22–26 = Metabolic ROME: R=Opposite M=Equal

1. Normal ABG Values

ParameterNormal RangeWhat It MeasuresControlled By

Quick Rule: pH <7.35 = Acidosis. pH >7.45 = Alkalosis. CO₂ = Respiratory. HCO₃ = Metabolic.

2. The Four Acid-Base Disorders

Respiratory Acidosis
pH ↓ <7.35 CO₂ ↑ >45 HCO₃ Normal

Cause: CO₂ retention / hypoventilation

  • COPD, emphysema
  • Opioid/sedative overdose
  • Pneumonia, atelectasis
  • Neuromuscular disease

S/S: Confusion, drowsiness, shallow breathing, ↓ BP

Tx: Improve ventilation, treat cause, bronchodilators

🔄 Compensation: Kidneys retain HCO₃⁻ (↑HCO₃) to raise pH — takes hours to days
Respiratory Alkalosis
pH ↑ >7.45 CO₂ ↓ <35 HCO₃ Normal

Cause: CO₂ blown off / hyperventilation

  • Anxiety, panic attack
  • Pain, fever
  • Mechanical ventilation (over-breathing)
  • Early sepsis

S/S: Dizziness, tingling in fingers/lips, rapid breathing, muscle cramps

Tx: Treat underlying cause, calm breathing, rebreathing techniques

🔄 Compensation: Kidneys excrete HCO₃⁻ (↓HCO₃) to lower pH
Metabolic Acidosis
pH ↓ <7.35 CO₂ Normal HCO₃ ↓ <22

Cause: Too much acid or HCO₃ loss

  • DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis)
  • Kidney failure (can't excrete H⁺)
  • Diarrhea (loss of HCO₃)
  • Lactic acidosis (sepsis, shock)

S/S: Kussmaul breathing (deep, rapid), confusion, N/V, weakness

Tx: IV fluids, insulin (DKA), sodium bicarbonate if severe

🔄 Compensation: Lungs hyperventilate to blow off CO₂ (↓CO₂) — rapid response
Metabolic Alkalosis
pH ↑ >7.45 CO₂ Normal HCO₃ ↑ >26

Cause: Acid loss or excess HCO₃

  • Vomiting (loss of HCl)
  • Excess antacids or bicarb
  • Diuretics (loss of Cl⁻ and H⁺)
  • Hyperaldosteronism

S/S: Muscle twitching, dizziness, slow breathing, hypokalemia

Tx: Stop diuretics, replace fluids/KCl, treat cause

🔄 Compensation: Lungs retain CO₂ by hypoventilating (↑CO₂) to lower pH

4. Step-by-Step ABG Interpretation

1
Look at pH — <7.35 = Acidosis; >7.45 = Alkalosis; 7.35–7.45 = Normal/Compensated
2
Look at CO₂ — >45 = CO₂ retained (acid); <35 = CO₂ blown off (base) → this is the respiratory component
3
Look at HCO₃ — <22 = base deficit (acid); >26 = base excess (base) → this is the metabolic component
4
Apply ROME — Which value matches the pH direction?
Respiratory = Opposite (pH ↓ with CO₂ ↑)
Metabolic = Equal (pH ↓ with HCO₃ ↓)
5
Assess compensation — Is the "other" system moving to fix the pH? If yes → compensating. If pH is back to 7.35–7.45 → fully compensated.
Example: pH 7.28 (acidosis) + CO₂ 52 (elevated) + HCO₃ 24 (normal) → CO₂ matches acidosis direction (OPPOSITE relationship) → Respiratory Acidosis, Uncompensated

5. ROME Mnemonic

R = Respiratory → Opposite

  • pH ↓ (acidosis) + CO₂ ↑ (acid) = opposite direction → Respiratory
  • pH ↑ (alkalosis) + CO₂ ↓ (base) = opposite direction → Respiratory

ME = Metabolic → Equal

  • pH ↓ (acidosis) + HCO₃ ↓ (acid) = same direction → Metabolic
  • pH ↑ (alkalosis) + HCO₃ ↑ (base) = same direction → Metabolic

6. NCLEX Must-Knows

  • ✅ Normal pH: 7.35–7.45; CO₂: 35–45; HCO₃: 22–26
  • ✅ ROME: Respiratory = Opposite, Metabolic = Equal
  • ✅ Respiratory Acidosis = hypoventilation (COPD, opioids) → lungs can't blow off CO₂
  • ✅ Respiratory Alkalosis = hyperventilation (anxiety, pain, fever)
  • ✅ Metabolic Acidosis = DKA, kidney failure, diarrhea → Kussmaul breathing is compensation
  • ✅ Metabolic Alkalosis = vomiting, antacids, diuretics
  • ✅ Compensation: Respiratory disorders → kidneys compensate (slow). Metabolic disorders → lungs compensate (fast)
  • ✅ Fully compensated = pH 7.35–7.45 but CO₂ or HCO₃ still abnormal
🔑 Key Takeaway: CO₂ = lungs (respiratory). HCO₃ = kidneys (metabolic). pH <7.35 = acidosis; >7.45 = alkalosis. Use ROME to identify type. Compensation: the "other" system adjusts to bring pH back toward normal. Kussmaul breathing = metabolic acidosis compensation. Respiratory acidosis → COPD/opioids.
