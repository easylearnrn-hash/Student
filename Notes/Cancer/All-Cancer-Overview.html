Hematologic (Blood) Cancers

CancerCell OriginKey SymptomsRisk FactorsTreatment
Leukemia WBCs (bone marrow) Frequent infections, fatigue/anemia, easy bruising/bleeding, bone pain, fever/night sweats Radiation, chemo history, benzene, Down syndrome, HTLV-1/EBV Chemo (induction→consolidation→maintenance), stem cell transplant, targeted therapy (imatinib for CML)
Lymphoma Lymphocytes (lymph nodes) Painless swollen lymph nodes, night sweats, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, pruritus without rash EBV, HIV, immunosuppression, age >60 CHOP regimen (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone), rituximab (NHL)
Multiple Myeloma Plasma cells (bone marrow) CRAB: Calcium ↑ (kidney stones), Renal failure, Anemia, Bone lesions (fractures) Radiation, pesticides, African American, age >65 Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor), stem cell transplant, supportive care
Lymphoma Key Distinction

Hodgkin's Lymphoma: Reed-Sternberg cells (owl-eye appearance) — better prognosis
Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma (NHL): No Reed-Sternberg cells — more common, worse prognosis overall

B symptoms for both: Fever, Night sweats, Weight loss (>10% body weight)

General Cancer Treatments

TreatmentMechanismKey Nursing Points
Surgery Remove tumor (curative) or relieve symptoms (palliative) Post-op: respiratory, bleeding, infection, DVT prevention
Chemotherapy Kills rapidly dividing cells Give ondansetron before; monitor CBC (nadir 7–14 days); neutropenic precautions; oral care
Radiation External beam or brachytherapy (internal seeds) External: protect skin field, no lotion/harsh soap. Brachytherapy: time/distance/shielding precautions
Immunotherapy Boosts immune response against cancer cells Pembrolizumab (PD-1), trastuzumab (HER2+); watch for immune-related AEs (colitis, pneumonitis)
Targeted Therapy Attacks specific cancer cell markers Imatinib (CML), rituximab (NHL), bortezomib (myeloma), sorafenib (liver), sunitinib (kidney)
