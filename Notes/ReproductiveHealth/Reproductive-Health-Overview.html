Reproductive Health — NCLEX Overview

High-yield summary: anatomy, cycle, STIs, contraception, BPH, complications, emergencies

Topic Quick Reference

Anatomy

Female: ovaries → fallopian tubes → uterus. Male: testes → epididymis → vas deferens → urethra. Fertilization in tube; implantation in uterus.

Menstrual Cycle

Day 1 = menstruation. Day 14 = LH surge → ovulation. Days 15–28 = luteal phase (corpus luteum → progesterone). Fertilized? hCG saves corpus luteum.

Infertility

>12 months trying (<35 yo); >6 months (≥35). Female: ovulation, tubes, endometriosis. Male: sperm count/motility, varicocele. Clomid = stimulates ovulation.

Reproductive Emergencies

Ectopic Pregnancy

Unilateral pain + bleeding + positive hCG. Treat: Methotrexate (unruptured) or surgery. Monitor hCG to zero.

Placental Abruption

Painful dark red bleeding + rigid uterus = emergency delivery. Life-threatening for mother & baby.

Eclampsia

Preeclampsia + seizures. Give magnesium sulfate. Antidote = calcium gluconate. Only cure = delivery.

Placenta Previa

Painless bright red bleeding. NO vaginal exam. Bed rest. Plan C-section.

Testicular Torsion

Sudden severe scrotal pain + absent cremasteric reflex. Surgery within 6 hours to save testicle.

Priapism

Erection >4h = urological emergency. Sickle cell common cause. Aspirate + phenylephrine injection.

BPH & TURP Summary

  • BPH symptoms: Hesitancy, weak stream, nocturia, incomplete emptying, frequency
  • Tamsulosin (alpha-blocker) = relaxes urethra. Finasteride = shrinks prostate (6+ months)
  • TURP post-op: 3-way Foley + CBI. Pink urine = normal; bright red = hemorrhage
  • TURP syndrome = hyponatremia → confusion, seizures (from irrigant absorption)
  • Retrograde ejaculation after TURP = harmless but may affect fertility
