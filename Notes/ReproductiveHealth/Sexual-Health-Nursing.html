Sexual Health in Nursing

Nursing role in sexual health, cultural competence, contraception overview, STI prevention

Sexual Health — Definition

Sexual health includes physical, emotional, mental, and social well-being related to sexuality. It is not just the absence of disease — it encompasses respectful, safe, and positive sexual experiences.

Nursing Role in Sexual Health

Core Nursing Responsibilities
  • Assess: Ask about sexual activity, protection use, STI risks using open-ended, nonjudgmental questions
  • Educate: Provide accurate information about contraception, STIs, and consent
  • Support: Be affirming of all gender identities and sexual orientations
  • Advocate: Ensure patient privacy and access to appropriate services

Always assess readiness to learn before teaching. Use therapeutic communication. Provide privacy for sexual health discussions.

Cultural Competence in Nursing

Cultural competence = understanding and respecting diverse values, beliefs, and practices, and integrating them into care without imposing your own.

Culturally Competent Care Includes

  • Asking about patient preferences (food, modesty, spiritual practices, same-gender caregiver requests)
  • Avoiding assumptions and stereotypes — assess each patient individually
  • Using certified interpreters only (never family members or children)
  • Respecting traditional or alternative medicine alongside standard treatment

Key Concepts

  • Acculturation: Adapting to a new culture while maintaining aspects of original culture
  • Ethnocentrism: Believing one's own culture is superior — avoid
  • Cultural imposition: Forcing one's own beliefs on patients — never appropriate
🧠 LEARN Model — Culturally Competent Communication
  • L — Listen to the patient's perspective
  • E — Explain your perception of the situation
  • A — Acknowledge differences and similarities
  • R — Recommend care options
  • N — Negotiate an agreement

STI Prevention Teaching

  • Abstinence = 100% effective; condoms reduce but do not eliminate all STI risk
  • Regular STI screening — especially for high-risk individuals (multiple partners, IV drug use, MSM)
  • Vaccinations: HPV (Gardasil), Hepatitis B
  • Partner notification and treatment to prevent reinfection
  • Avoid sexual activity during active herpes outbreaks
  • Avoid alcohol with metronidazole (Flagyl) — severe vomiting reaction
  • After IUD insertion: report fever, pain, and discharge (infection signs)
