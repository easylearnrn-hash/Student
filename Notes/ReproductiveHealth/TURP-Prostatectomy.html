Reproductive Health

TURP — Transurethral Resection of the Prostate

Post-op CBI care, complications, patient teaching, NCLEX priorities

What Is TURP?

TURP = Transurethral Resection of the Prostate. Surgical removal of excess prostate tissue through the urethra using a resectoscope — no external incision. Most common surgery for BPH refractory to medication.

Post-op: 3-way Foley catheter inserted for Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI).

Post-Op CBI Nursing Care

Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI)

Sterile saline continuously runs into the bladder via the 3-way Foley to flush blood and prevent clot formation and catheter blockage.

  • Expected urine color: light pink (not dark red or bright red)
  • Urine becomes clear → slow irrigation rate
  • Bright red urine or clots → increase CBI rate; notify provider (possible hemorrhage)
  • Decreased output or bladder spasms → check for kinks or blockage; flush per protocol
🚨
Report Immediately

Bright red urine + clots = possible hemorrhage. Tachycardia + hypotension + clots = hemorrhagic emergency — notify provider STAT.

Post-Op Complications

ComplicationSignsAction
HemorrhageBright red urine, clots, tachycardia, hypotensionReport immediately; increase CBI; prepare for transfusion
Infection (UTI/Sepsis)Fever, cloudy/foul urine, suprapubic tendernessCultures; antibiotics; monitor vitals
Urinary retention (post-catheter)Inability to void after catheter removalAssess first void; straight catheterization if needed
TURP Syndrome (rare)Confusion, bradycardia, nausea, seizures = hyponatremia from irrigant absorptionMonitor serum Na⁺; restrict fluids; report immediately
Bladder spasmsUrge to void with catheter in place; crampingAntispasmodics: oxybutynin or belladonna-opium suppository

Patient Teaching

TopicTeaching
CBI purposePrevents clots and maintains catheter patency
Urine colorLight pink is expected; dark or bright red = report
Bowel habitsUse stool softeners (docusate) — straining raises abdominal pressure, risks bleeding
Activity restrictionsNo heavy lifting or sexual activity for 4–6 weeks
Kegel exercisesHelp regain urinary control after catheter removal
Temporary incontinenceCommon post-op — reassure patient; bladder training helps
Retrograde ejaculationSemen enters bladder instead of exiting — harmless but may affect fertility; counsel patient
HydrationDrink 2–3 L fluids daily (unless restricted) to flush urinary tract
🧠 Mnemonic: TURP CARE
  • T — Three-way Foley with CBI
  • U — Urine should be pink (not bright red)
  • R — Report clots, ↓ output, or dark red urine
  • P — Pain/bladder spasms → check catheter & treat
  • C — Continue fluids to prevent clots
  • A — Avoid straining/lifting post-op
  • R — Remove catheter as ordered; monitor first void
  • E — Educate on retrograde ejaculation & healing

Key Lab Monitoring

  • Hematocrit/Hemoglobin: Detect post-op bleeding
  • Serum Sodium (Na⁺): Monitor for TURP syndrome (hyponatremia)
  • Urinalysis/Culture: Rule out infection
  • BUN/Creatinine: Assess renal function post-urinary obstruction
📝
NCLEX Summary

CBI = nursing priority post-TURP. Pink urine = normal; bright red/clots = emergency. TURP syndrome = hyponatremia → confusion + seizures. Bladder spasms + decreased output = check catheter. No straining or heavy lifting. Retrograde ejaculation = harmless but affects fertility. Temporary incontinence = expected — teach Kegels.
