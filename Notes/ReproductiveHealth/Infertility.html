Reproductive Health

Infertility

Definition, female & male causes, diagnostic tests, treatments — NCLEX overview

Definition

Infertility = inability to conceive after:

  • 12 months of regular, unprotected intercourse (age <35)
  • 6 months of regular, unprotected intercourse (age ≥35)

Causes of Infertility

Female Factors
  • Ovulatory dysfunction (irregular/absent ovulation)
  • Blocked fallopian tubes (e.g., from PID)
  • Endometriosis (uterine tissue outside uterus)
  • Age (fertility declines significantly after 35)
  • Hormonal imbalance (thyroid, prolactin, PCOS)
  • Uterine fibroids or structural anomalies
Male Factors
  • Low sperm count (oligospermia) or absent sperm (azoospermia)
  • Poor sperm motility or abnormal morphology
  • Varicocele (enlarged scrotal veins — heats testes)
  • Ejaculation disorders or erectile dysfunction
  • Hormonal deficiencies (low testosterone)

Diagnostic Tests

TestPurpose
Ovulation testing (LH surge, progesterone)Confirm ovulation is occurring
Hysterosalpingogram (HSG)Dye test to check fallopian tube patency
Semen analysisEvaluate sperm count, motility, morphology
Pelvic ultrasoundAssess uterus and ovarian follicles
Hormone levelsFSH, LH, estrogen, prolactin, TSH, AMH
Endometrial biopsyAssess uterine lining

Always start with a full assessment of both partners.

Treatments

Medical / Pharmacological
  • Clomiphene citrate (Clomid): Stimulates ovulation — most commonly used first-line
  • Metformin: For PCOS-related infertility (improves insulin resistance → restores ovulation)
  • Gonadotropins (FSH/LH injections): Directly stimulate follicle development
Assisted Reproductive Technology
  • IUI (Intrauterine Insemination): Sperm placed directly into uterus around ovulation
  • IVF (In Vitro Fertilization): Eggs retrieved, fertilized in lab, embryo transferred to uterus
  • Surgery: Remove adhesions, fibroids, or treat endometriosis

Nursing Considerations

  • Assess both partners without blame or assumptions
  • Provide emotional support — infertility causes significant psychological distress
  • Teach basal body temperature charting and ovulation predictor kits
  • Optimal intercourse timing: around ovulation (Days 10–14 of a 28-day cycle)
  • Support patient through multiple treatment cycles (emotional rollercoaster)
  • Discuss adoption or other family-building options without judgment
📝
NCLEX Must-Knows

Infertility = 12 months trying (<35), 6 months (≥35). Assess both partners. HSG = dye test for fallopian tubes. Clomid = stimulates ovulation. Metformin = PCOS infertility. IUI = sperm into uterus. IVF = fertilization outside body. Endometriosis and PID (from chlamydia/gonorrhea) are common causes of blocked tubes.
