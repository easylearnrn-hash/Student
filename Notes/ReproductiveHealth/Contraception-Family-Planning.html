Estrogen Contraindications

Estrogen-containing contraceptives are contraindicated in:

  • Smokers older than 35 years
  • History of DVT or PE (blood clots)
  • Migraine with aura
  • History of breast cancer
  • Liver disease

Hormonal Contraceptive Teaching

  • Take pill at same time daily to maintain hormone levels
  • If a pill is missed: Take ASAP; use backup method if >2 pills missed
  • Common side effects: nausea, breast tenderness, mood changes, breakthrough bleeding
  • Depot-Provera (injection): every 3 months; may cause bone density loss — monitor calcium/Vit D
  • Nexplanon (implant): inserted subdermally in upper arm; lasts up to 3 years
⚠️ ACHES — Hormonal Contraceptive Warning Signs (Report Immediately)
  • A — Abdominal pain (liver, ectopic pregnancy, gallbladder)
  • C — Chest pain (PE — pulmonary embolism)
  • H — Headache (stroke)
  • E — Eye problems (vision changes — stroke, HTN)
  • S — Severe leg pain (DVT — deep vein thrombosis)

IUD Teaching

  • Slight cramping and spotting are normal immediately after insertion
  • Check strings monthly (placed to confirm IUD is in position)
  • Report if string is missing (may indicate expulsion or migration)
⚠️ PAINS — IUD Danger Signs
  • P — Period late (possible pregnancy)
  • A — Abdominal pain
  • I — Infection (fever, foul discharge)
  • N — Not feeling well
  • S — String missing

Menopause & HRT

Menopause = cessation of menses for 12 consecutive months. Caused by decline in estrogen.

Symptoms: Hot flashes, mood swings, vaginal dryness, sleep disturbances, osteoporosis risk.

HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy): Estrogen ± progesterone. Risks: blood clots, stroke, breast cancer. Contraindicated with history of breast cancer, liver disease, or clotting disorders.

Non-pharmacological: Weight-bearing exercise, calcium + Vitamin D supplementation, avoid smoking and alcohol.

NCLEX Priorities

Assess patient preference, lifestyle, and medical history before recommending contraception. Estrogen contraindications are heavily tested. ACHES = report immediately. PAINS = IUD red flags. Plan B within 72h. Copper IUD = most effective emergency method within 5 days. Informed consent required for permanent methods.
