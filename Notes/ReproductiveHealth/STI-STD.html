STIs / STDs — NCLEX Overview

Bacterial, viral, parasitic STIs — symptoms, treatments, nursing priorities

Bacterial STIs (Curable with Antibiotics)

STIOrganismSymptomsComplicationsTreatment
Chlamydia Chlamydia trachomatis Often asymptomatic; burning urination, abnormal discharge, pelvic pain PID → infertility; ectopic pregnancy; newborn eye infection Azithromycin (single dose) or Doxycycline × 7 days
Gonorrhea Neisseria gonorrhoeae Thick yellow-green discharge, dysuria PID, infertility, joint infections, newborn blindness Ceftriaxone IM + Azithromycin (treat both partners)
Syphilis Treponema pallidum Stage 1: painless chancre. Stage 2: rash on palms & soles. Stage 3: organ damage Neurosyphilis, blindness, congenital birth defects Penicillin G (drug of choice)
🚨
Chlamydia + Gonorrhea Often Co-Occur → Treat for Both

Syphilis has 3 stages — painless chancre first. Rash on palms/soles = Stage 2 (highly distinctive). Brain damage = Stage 3 (neurosyphilis).

Viral STIs (No Cure — Manageable)

STICauseSignsComplicationsManagement
HPV Human Papillomavirus Painless genital warts; abnormal Pap smear Cervical, anal, throat cancers Vaccine (Gardasil) — ages 11–12; warts treated with cryotherapy
Herpes (HSV-2) Herpes Simplex Virus Painful blisters on genitals or mouth; flu-like symptoms with first outbreak Neonatal herpes (severe in newborns); recurring outbreaks Acyclovir / Valacyclovir — reduces outbreaks; no cure; avoid sex during outbreaks
Hepatitis B Hepatitis B Virus Jaundice, fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain Liver failure, cirrhosis, liver cancer Vaccine prevents; antiviral therapy for chronic disease
HIV/AIDS HIV Flu-like symptoms initially; weight loss; recurrent infections AIDS (CD4 <200), opportunistic infections, death ART (Antiretroviral Therapy) — slows disease, no cure; test with ELISA → Western blot to confirm

Parasitic STIs (Curable)

STICauseSignsTreatment
Trichomoniasis Trichomonas vaginalis Foul-smelling, frothy, green discharge; itching; burning; strawberry cervix Metronidazole (Flagyl) — treat both partners; NO alcohol
Pubic Lice ("Crabs") Pthirus pubis Severe genital itching, visible lice in pubic hair Permethrin cream; wash clothes/bedding in hot water

Nursing Care

  • Educate on safe sex practices (condoms reduce risk; only abstinence is 100% effective)
  • Partner notification and treatment — prevents reinfection
  • Complete full antibiotic course even if symptoms resolve
  • Metronidazole (Flagyl) + alcohol = severe vomiting — always teach this
  • Methotrexate is teratogenic — handle with gloves
  • Reportable STIs: Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, Syphilis, HIV → report to health department
  • All pregnant women screened for STIs to protect the newborn
📝
NCLEX Summary

Chlamydia & Gonorrhea often co-occur — treat both. Syphilis = painless chancre → rash on palms/soles → brain damage. HPV = warts → cervical cancer → Gardasil prevents. Trich = green frothy discharge + strawberry cervix = Flagyl (no alcohol). HIV = ART, no cure. Flagyl + alcohol = vomiting. Reportable: Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, Syphilis, HIV.
