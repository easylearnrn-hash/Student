Reproductive Anatomy

Female & male structures, hormones, fertilization pathway — NCLEX overview

Female Reproductive Anatomy

External Genitalia (Vulva)

PartFunction
Mons pubisFatty tissue over pubic bone; cushions during intercourse
Labia majora / minoraProtect inner structures
ClitorisSensitive erectile tissue for sexual stimulation
Urethral openingUrine exits here (not reproductive)
Vaginal openingMenstrual flow, intercourse, childbirth

Internal Structures

OrganFunction
VaginaMuscular canal for intercourse, birth, and menstrual flow
CervixLower uterus; opens into vagina; dilates to 10 cm during labor
UterusMuscular organ where fetus grows
EndometriumInner uterine lining; sheds during menstruation
Fallopian tubesTransport egg from ovary → uterus; site of fertilization
OvariesStore eggs (ova); produce estrogen & progesterone

Male Reproductive Anatomy

External
  • Penis: Delivers sperm and urine
  • Scrotum: Holds testes; keeps them cooler than body temp for sperm production
Internal
  • Testes: Produce sperm & testosterone
  • Epididymis: Stores and matures sperm
  • Vas deferens: Transports sperm to urethra
  • Seminal vesicles: Add fluid → semen
  • Prostate gland: Adds fluid; can enlarge (BPH)
  • Urethra: Carries urine and semen through penis

Sperm pathway: Testes → Epididymis → Vas deferens → Urethra → out. Vasectomy = cutting the vas deferens.

HormoneSourceAction

EstrogenOvariesBuilds uterine lining; develops female secondary sex traits
ProgesteroneCorpus luteum / placentaMaintains uterine lining for pregnancy

NCLEX Must-Knows

Fertilization = fallopian tube. Implantation = uterus. Cervix dilates to 10 cm for delivery. FSH & LH = from pituitary gland. Prostate enlargement (BPH) blocks urine flow. Vasectomy cuts the vas deferens.
