Respiratory System

Chronic Bronchitis — "Blue Bloater"

Mucus-obstructed bronchi, persistent productive cough, COPD subtype — NCLEX review

Definition
Chronic Bronchitis: Inflammation and obstruction of the bronchi by excessive mucus — often caused by smoking. Defined clinically as a productive cough for ≥3 months in 2 consecutive years. Part of COPD. Nickname = "Blue Bloater" (cyanosis + edema).
Classic "Blue Bloater" Profile

The Blue Bloater

  • Blue (cyanotic) — low O₂, poor compensation
  • Bloated — peripheral edema from cor pulmonale (right heart failure)
  • Overweight appearance
  • Productive cough with thick sputum
  • Crackles and wheezing on auscultation
  • Clubbing of fingers (chronic hypoxia)
Symptoms

General COPD Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath (SOB)
  • Chest tightness NCLEX Keyword
  • Hypoxia (low O₂)
  • Tachypnea

Specific to Chronic Bronchitis

  • Productive cough — persistent, mucus-producing
  • Cyanosis — bluish skin (NCLEX keyword: "BLUE")
  • Crackles & wheezes
  • Peripheral edema (Cor pulmonale)
  • Night sweats & fever
  • Clubbing of fingers — chronic hypoxia
  • Tripoding position — leaning forward on hands/knees
Nursing Actions

Priority Nursing Interventions

  1. Increase fluid intake — 2–3 liters/day to loosen and thin mucus
  2. Humidifier at bedside — moisten the air to aid mucus clearance
  3. Chest physiotherapy (CPT) — percussion and postural drainage to clear secretions
  4. Huff/Half cough technique — deep breath → hold → cough halfway to mobilize mucus without excessive strain
  5. Suction device at bedside — for mucus removal when patient cannot clear independently
  6. High Fowler's or tripod position — maximizes lung expansion
  7. Low-flow O₂ — 1–2 L/min; target SpO₂ 88–92%

Precautions & Patient Teaching

Oxygen Safety

  • Post "Oxygen In Use" sign on door
  • No smoking in the home
  • Keep O₂ tank 2–3 meters away from gas sources
  • Avoid open flames near O₂ equipment

Vaccinations

  • Annual flu vaccine — prevents exacerbations
  • Pneumococcal vaccine — prevents bacterial pneumonia
  • COVID-19 booster as recommended
NCLEX Tip: The huff cough / half cough technique is tested! Patient takes a deep breath, holds it, then coughs halfway — moves mucus UP without excessive strain or bronchospasm.
Cor Pulmonale — Complication

Right-Sided Heart Failure from COPD

  • Chronic hypoxia → pulmonary vasoconstriction → pulmonary hypertension
  • Right ventricle works harder → eventually fails
  • Signs: peripheral edema, JVD, hepatomegaly, ascites
  • Treatment: Treat the underlying COPD + diuretics for fluid overload
NCLEX Must-Know

Key NCLEX Points

  • Chronic Bronchitis = BLUE Bloater (cyanosis + edema)
  • Low-flow O₂ always — never high-flow in COPD
  • Productive cough ≥ 3 months × 2 consecutive years = definition
  • Chest tightness is the classic NCLEX keyword
  • Tripoding = patient is in respiratory distress
  • Cor pulmonale = right heart failure caused by lung disease
  • Vaccinate! Flu + pneumonia vaccines prevent exacerbations
