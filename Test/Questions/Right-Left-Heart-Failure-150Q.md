**1.** A nurse is caring for a patient with left-sided heart failure. Which pathophysiologic mechanism directly explains this patient's pulmonary edema?

A. Right ventricular failure causes blood to back up into the pulmonary veins  
B. Left ventricular failure causes blood to back up into the pulmonary veins, increasing pulmonary venous pressure  
C. Decreased pulmonary vascular resistance allows excess fluid into the alveoli  
D. Elevated systemic venous pressure drives fluid into lung tissue  

**Correct Answer: B**  
*Rationale: In left-sided HF, the LV fails to pump forward, causing backward congestion into pulmonary veins → ↑ pulmonary venous pressure → fluid leaks into alveoli.*

**2.** A patient with heart failure reports waking up gasping for air approximately 2 hours after falling asleep. The nurse recognizes this as which clinical manifestation?

B. Dyspnea on exertion  
C. Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea  
D. Pulmonary hypertension  

**Correct Answer: C**  
*Rationale: PND is a classic left HF symptom — the patient wakes suddenly at night gasping due to fluid redistributing into the lungs when supine.*

*Rationale: HFrEF is defined as EF <40%, indicating a dilated, weak heart that cannot pump effectively.*
