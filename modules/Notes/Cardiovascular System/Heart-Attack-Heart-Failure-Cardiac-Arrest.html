Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)

Causes

  • Atherosclerosis (plaque buildup)
  • Clot formation → blocks coronary artery
  • Risk: HTN, smoking, high cholesterol, diabetes, sedentary lifestyle

Symptoms

  • Crushing chest pain — "elephant on chest"
  • Radiates to left arm, jaw, neck, back
  • Diaphoresis (sweating), nausea
  • SOB, anxiety ("feeling of doom")
  • Women: atypical — fatigue, epigastric pain

Treatment

  • STEMI: PCI within 90 min (door-to-balloon)
  • NSTEMI: Anticoagulants + cardiac cath
  • MONA: Morphine, O₂, Nitrates, Aspirin
  • Thrombolytics if PCI not available within 30 min
STEMI vs NSTEMI: STEMI = ST elevation + complete occlusion. NSTEMI = ST depression + partial occlusion. Both = Troponin elevated.
Heart Failure

Causes

  • Post-MI (scar tissue)
  • Hypertension (long-term)
  • Valve disease
  • Cardiomyopathy
  • COPD → right-sided HF

Symptoms by Side

  • Left HF: pulmonary edema, crackles, SOB, orthopnea, PND
  • Right HF: JVD, peripheral edema, ascites, weight gain

Treatment

  • Diuretics (Furosemide) — remove excess fluid
  • ACE inhibitors / ARBs — reduce workload
  • Beta-Blockers — reduce HR
  • Digoxin — strengthen contraction
  • Low Na⁺ diet, fluid restriction
Cardiac Arrest

Causes (Rhythms)

  • Shockable: V-Fib, Pulseless V-Tach
  • Non-shockable: Asystole, PEA
  • Triggered by MI, electrolyte imbalance, drowning, drug OD

Signs

  • Sudden loss of consciousness
  • No pulse, no breathing
  • Cyanosis (blue lips/nails)

Treatment (CAB)

  • C — Compressions (hard, fast, 2 in. depth, 100–120/min)
  • A — Airway (head-tilt chin-lift)
  • B — Breathing (rescue breaths)
  • AED → Defibrillate if V-Fib/V-Tach
  • Epinephrine every 3–5 min
🚨 Key Difference: Heart attack patient is awake and in pain. Cardiac arrest patient is unresponsive. Both require IMMEDIATE action — but different responses.
📌 NCLEX Rule: No pulse = start CPR first, then check rhythm. Do NOT delay compressions to check rhythm.
