Maternal Health Medications

Prenatal, antepartum, intrapartum, and postpartum pharmacology

Prenatal Medications

Folic Acid

  • 400–800 mcg daily (start before conception!)
  • Prevents neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly)
  • Increased to 4 mg if prior NTD pregnancy
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

RhoGAM must be given within 72 hours of delivery/any Rh-sensitizing event. Does NOT help if mother is already sensitized.

Nausea & Vomiting of Pregnancy

Mild Nausea (First-Line)

  • Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine) + Doxylamine
  • Dietary changes (small frequent meals)

Hyperemesis Gravidarum

  • Ondansetron (Zofran) — IV/oral
  • Promethazine — sedation, EPS risk
  • Thiamine (Vitamin B1) — prevent Wernicke's encephalopathy with prolonged vomiting
  • IV fluids + electrolyte replacement

Hypertension in Pregnancy

DrugUseKey Notes

Nifedipine (oral)Chronic HTN in pregnancyCalcium channel blocker; safe for use; also used as tocolytic
MethyldopaChronic HTN in pregnancyCentral alpha agonist; safest for long-term use

Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO₄)

  • Prevents eclamptic seizures in preeclampsia
  • Also used as tocolytic for preterm labor
  • Toxicity signs: ↓ respiratory rate, ↓ reflexes (check patellar reflex!), flushing, lethargy
  • Monitor RR (hold if <12), DTRs, urine output (>25 mL/hr), Mg levels (4–7 mEq/L therapeutic)
  • Antidote: Calcium gluconate — keep at bedside!
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Loss of patellar reflex = early MgSO₄ toxicity. Always keep calcium gluconate at bedside when giving MgSO₄ IV.

Labor Medications

Oxytocin (Pitocin)

  • Induce or augment labor
  • Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) prevention
  • SE: hyperstimulation (contractions <2 min, >90 sec) → STOP infusion
  • Water intoxication (antidiuretic effect)
  • Uterine rupture (rare)

Cervical Ripening

  • Misoprostol (Cytotec) — prostaglandin
  • Dinoprostone (Cervidil) — cervical softening
  • Monitor contractions/FHR after administration

Tocolytics (Stop Preterm Labor)

  • Terbutaline — beta-2 agonist; SE: maternal tachycardia (monitor HR!)
  • Nifedipine (CCB) — most commonly used tocolytic
  • Indomethacin — used <32 weeks
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Uterine hyperstimulation from oxytocin → stop infusion, reposition left lateral, O₂, notify provider.

Postpartum Medications

Methylergonovine (Methergine)

  • PPH — uterine atony
  • Causes strong uterine contraction
  • AVOID in preeclampsia → causes vasoconstriction + ↑ BP

Oxytocin (Pitocin)

  • First-line for PPH after delivery
  • IM or IV infusion

Iron Supplements

  • Postpartum anemia
  • Take with Vitamin C (absorption)
  • SE: black/dark stools, constipation

NCLEX Must-Know

  • Folic acid: start BEFORE conception for NTD prevention
  • RhoGAM (Rho(D) immune globulin): within 72 hr; for Rh-negative mothers only
  • MgSO₄ toxicity: ↓ RR, ↓ reflexes; antidote = Calcium gluconate (keep at bedside)
  • Oxytocin hyperstimulation: STOP, left lateral, O₂
  • Methergine CONTRAINDICATED in preeclampsia (↑ BP)
  • Terbutaline SE: maternal tachycardia — monitor HR
  • Betamethasone: given to mother to accelerate fetal lung maturity (surfactant) before 34 wks
