HEAD-TO-TOE ASSESSMENT

📌 What is a Head-to-Toe Assessment?

A head-to-toe assessment is a systematic, organized physical examination that evaluates the patient's overall health status.

Types of Assessments

  • Comprehensive: Complete admission or initial examination
  • Focused: Specific system or problem assessment
  • Ongoing: Shift assessment or monitoring changes

NCLEX SEQUENCE RULE

Standard Order: INSPECT → PALPATE → PERCUSS → AUSCULTATE

⚠️ EXCEPTION: Abdomen = Inspect → Auscultate → Percuss → Palpate

STEP-BY-STEP ASSESSMENT GUIDE

1GENERAL SURVEY (Before Touching Patient)

  • Appearance: Age, hygiene, grooming, signs of distress
  • Behavior: Alertness, facial expression, mood
  • Body Structure: Posture, build, deformities
  • Mobility: Gait, use of assistive devices
  • Speech: Clear, appropriate, slurred

💡 NCLEX Tip: The general survey starts the moment you see the patient - before any physical contact!

2VITAL SIGNS

  • Temperature: Oral, tympanic, temporal, axillary
  • Pulse: Rate, rhythm, strength, symmetry
  • Respirations: Rate, rhythm, depth, effort
  • Blood Pressure
  • Oxygen Saturation
  • Pain Assessment: Location, intensity, quality, duration

3HEAD & FACE

  • Inspect: Symmetry, lesions, swelling
  • Palpate: Scalp tenderness, temporal arteries, sinuses

💡 NCLEX Tip: Asymmetry may indicate stroke or Bell's palsy

4EYES

  • Inspect: Conjunctiva (pink, moist), sclera (white), pupil size
  • PERRLA: Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation
  • Visual Fields & Acuity: Snellen chart if needed

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Jaundice (yellow sclera), anisocoria (unequal pupils), nystagmus (involuntary eye movement)

5EARS

  • Inspect: External ear for lesions, drainage
  • Hearing Check: Whisper test

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Otorrhea (ear drainage), impacted cerumen, hearing loss

6NOSE & SINUSES

  • Inspect: Alignment, mucosa color, septum
  • Check Patency: Test both nostrils
  • Palpate: Sinuses for tenderness

7MOUTH & THROAT

  • Inspect: Lips, oral mucosa, teeth, gums, tongue, tonsils
  • Check Gag Reflex: Cranial nerves IX & X

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Cyanosis, leukoplakia (white patches), thrush, tonsillar swelling

8NECK

  • Inspect: Symmetry, masses, jugular vein distention (JVD)
  • Palpate: Lymph nodes, trachea alignment
  • Check ROM: Range of motion
  • Auscultate Carotids: For bruits (if >middle age or suspected vascular disease)

9RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

  • Inspect: Chest shape, symmetry, effort of breathing
  • Palpate: Chest expansion, tenderness
  • Percuss: Resonance (normal), dullness (fluid/consolidation)
  • Auscultate: Lung sounds - anterior, posterior, lateral

Normal Sounds: Vesicular, bronchovesicular, bronchial

⚠️ Abnormal Sounds: Crackles, wheezes, rhonchi, stridor

10CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM

  • Inspect: Precordium for lifts/heaves
  • Palpate: Apical impulse (PMI - Point of Maximal Impulse)
  • Auscultate: Heart sounds (Aortic → Pulmonic → Erb's point → Tricuspid → Mitral)

Normal Sounds: S1 ("lub"), S2 ("dub")

⚠️ Abnormal Sounds: Murmurs, S3, S4, rubs

Peripheral Pulses: Radial, brachial, femoral, popliteal, posterior tibial, dorsalis pedis

  • Pulse Grading: 0-4+ (2+ is normal)
  • Capillary Refill: <2 seconds is normal

11ABDOMEN SPECIAL ORDER!

⚠️ REMEMBER: Inspect → Auscultate → Percuss → Palpate

(Auscultate BEFORE palpation to avoid altering bowel sounds)

  • Inspect: Contour (flat, rounded), scars, distention
  • Auscultate: Bowel sounds in all quadrants (5-30/min normal)
  • Percuss: Tympany (normal), dullness (fluid/mass)
  • Palpate: Light then deep for tenderness/masses

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Absent bowel sounds (ileus), guarding, rigidity

12GENITOURINARY SYSTEM

  • Inspect: External genitalia for lesions, discharge
  • Assess Urination: Frequency, urgency, dysuria, color
  • Palpate Bladder: If distention suspected

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Hematuria (blood in urine), oliguria (<400 mL/day)

13MUSCULOSKELETAL SYSTEM

  • Inspect: Muscle bulk, symmetry, deformities
  • Palpate: Joints for swelling, tenderness
  • ROM: Active and passive range of motion
  • Strength Testing: 0-5 scale

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Atrophy, contractures, crepitus

14NEUROLOGICAL SYSTEM

  • Level of Consciousness (LOC): Alert, lethargic, obtunded, stuporous, comatose
  • Orientation: Person, place, time, situation
  • Cranial Nerves: I-XII assessment
  • Motor & Sensory: Strength, sensation, reflexes (0-4+)
  • Coordination: Finger-to-nose, heel-to-shin

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Weakness, paresthesia (numbness/tingling), abnormal reflexes

15SKIN, HAIR, NAILS

  • Inspect: Color (pink, cyanosis, pallor, jaundice), lesions, rashes
  • Palpate: Temperature, moisture, turgor (hydration)
  • Check Nails: Clubbing, capillary refill

⚠️ Abnormal Findings: Pressure injuries, petechiae (small red spots), poor turgor

NCLEX HIGH-YIELD TIPS

  • Always compare bilaterally (left vs right) for symmetry
  • Abnormal findings in general survey may guide you to do a focused assessment
  • In the abdomen, always auscultate before palpation to avoid altering bowel sounds
  • If patient condition changes → do an immediate focused reassessment
  • Document findings clearly and promptly

MEMORY TRICKS

  • I-P-P-A: "I Play Piano Awesomely" = Inspect, Palpate, Percuss, Auscultate
  • PERRLA: "Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation"
  • Abdomen Exception: "Always Auscultate Abdomen Before Palpating"
  • Bilateral Assessment: "Left vs Right = Look for Light differences"
