Female Reproductive Cycle, Fertilization & Implantation

Menstrual phases, hormonal control, ovulation, zygote development, implantation, hCG

The Female Reproductive Cycle (Avg 28 Days)

Day 1
Menstruation
↓ Estrogen & progesterone → endometrium sloughs off
Days 1–13
Follicular Phase
FSH → follicles grow → estrogen rises → endometrium thickens
Day ~14
Ovulation
LH surge → dominant follicle ruptures → egg released
Days 15–28
Luteal Phase
Corpus luteum → progesterone → prepares uterus for implantation

Hormonal Control

Hypothalamus → GnRH → Anterior pituitary → FSH & LH

  • Low–moderate estrogen: negative feedback → suppresses FSH
  • High estrogen (Day 12–13): positive feedback → LH surge → triggers ovulation
  • Remaining follicle becomes the corpus luteum → secretes progesterone
  • No fertilization → corpus luteum degenerates → progesterone drops → menstruation

Fertilization

  • 200–500 million sperm enter the vagina; ~500 reach the fallopian tube infundibulum
  • Only 1 sperm penetrates the zona pellucida of the egg
  • Sperm + egg DNA combine → Zygote
  • Zygote divides: 2 → 4 → 8 → 16 cells (~Day 3–4) = Morula
  • Morula → Blastocyst (~Day 5–6):
    • Embryoblast (inner cell mass) → forms baby
    • Trophoblast (outer cells) → forms placenta

Implantation (Days 6–10)

  • Blastocyst implants in the uterine wall (endometrium)
  • Trophoblast secretes hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)
  • hCG signals corpus luteum to keep producing progesterone → maintains lining
  • hCG is detected in urine ~1–2 weeks after fertilization (positive pregnancy test)
Placental Takeover (~Week 8–12)

hCG levels peak then decline. The placenta becomes fully functional and takes over producing estrogen and progesterone for the remainder of pregnancy.

NCLEX Must-Knows

Fertilization = fallopian tube. Implantation = uterus. hCG = confirms pregnancy and maintains corpus luteum. Positive pregnancy test = hCG in urine. LH surge = ovulation. Corpus luteum produces progesterone until placenta takes over at 8–12 weeks.
