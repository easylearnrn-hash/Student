Pediatric Medication Administration

Safe dose calculation, routes, and NCLEX essentials

Why Pediatrics Is Different

  • Immature liver and kidneys → altered drug metabolism and excretion
  • Higher body water ratio → different drug distribution
  • All doses are weight-based — never use adult doses

Dose Calculation Steps

  1. Convert weight: lbs ÷ 2.2 = kg
  2. Calculate dose: Dose (mg) = ordered mg/kg × weight (kg)
  3. Compare to safe dose range from drug reference
  4. Calculate volume if needed: Volume = Dose ÷ Concentration
Example

Child weighs 44 lbs. Order: 10 mg/kg of amoxicillin. Drug on hand: 250 mg/5 mL.

  1. 44 ÷ 2.2 = 20 kg
  2. 10 mg/kg × 20 kg = 200 mg
  3. Volume: 200 ÷ (250/5) = 200 ÷ 50 = 4 mL

Routes of Administration

RouteKey Points
OralUse oral syringe (not kitchen spoon); aim at inner cheek; do NOT mix in full bottle; never call "candy"
IM (Intramuscular)Infants → vastus lateralis (thigh); Toddler+ → deltoid if large enough; limit <1 mL in infants; Z-track not used in children
SubcutaneousRotate sites (abdomen, thigh, upper arm); small gauge needle
IVAlways use infusion pump; monitor for infiltration, phlebitis, and extravasation
Otic (ear drops)<3 years → pull pinna DOWN and back; ≥3 years → pull pinna UP and back
OphthalmicPull lower eyelid down to create pouch; do NOT touch dropper tip to eye or lashes
RectalUse when child is vomiting or unable to take orally
🔴
Otic Drop Trick — Easy to Mix Up on NCLEX

Under 3 → Down and back. Over 3 → Up and back. Think: children under 3 are "down" (smaller); older kids have "up" ears.

NCLEX Must-Know Reminders

  • Always use kg, not lbs for dose calculation
  • Vastus lateralis = safest IM injection site in infants
  • Use an oral syringe for small or liquid doses
  • Never call medicine "candy" — safety and honesty issue
  • Always check allergies before administering
  • Watch decimal points: 0.1 ≠ 1.0 (leading zeros, no trailing zeros)
  • Report adverse reactions immediately; document everything
  • Verify ID with at least 2 patient identifiers before any medication
📝
NCLEX Safety Tip

When in doubt about dose safety, do NOT give and call the provider. Double-check with another nurse for high-alert meds (insulin, digoxin, heparin). Document the reason for any withheld dose.
