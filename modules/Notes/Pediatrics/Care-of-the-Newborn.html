Thermoregulation

Newborns lose heat rapidly due to: thin skin, large body surface area, no shivering mechanism, limited brown fat stores.

Methods of heat loss: Evaporation (wet skin), Conduction (cold surface), Convection (air current), Radiation (nearby cold objects)

Nursing actions: Dry immediately after birth, skin-to-skin contact, warm blankets, knit cap, radiant warmer.

🔴
Thermoregulation = Top Priority

Cold stress → hypoglycemia + increased O₂ demand + respiratory distress. Thermoregulation is the #1 priority at birth on NCLEX.

Immediate Medications

Vitamin K (phytonadione)

IM injection into vastus lateralis. Prevents Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding (VKDB). Given within first hour of birth. Newborns lack gut flora to produce Vitamin K.

Erythromycin Eye Ointment

Applied to both eyes within 1 hour of birth. Prevents ophthalmia neonatorum from gonorrhea and chlamydia. Wipe excess after 1 minute; do not flush from eyes.

Feeding

  • Breastfeed within first hour of birth (establishes supply, colostrum provides antibodies)
  • Breastfeed q2–3h (8–12 times in 24 hours)
  • Normal to lose up to 10% of birth weight in first few days; regain by 2 weeks
  • Formula-fed: q3–4h; follow mixing instructions exactly (correct concentration)

Suspect Hirschsprung's disease or cystic fibrosis. Report to provider.

Umbilical Cord Care

  • Keep dry and clean; fold diaper below cord stump
  • No tub baths until cord falls off naturally (1–2 weeks)
  • Do not pull or remove forcefully
  • Report: redness, foul odor, drainage = signs of omphalitis (infection)

Newborn Screening Tests

  • Heel stick (metabolic screen): PKU, congenital hypothyroidism, galactosemia — after 24h of feeding (false negative if done too early)
  • Hearing screen: OAE or ABR before discharge
  • CCHD screen: Pulse oximetry on right hand + foot (detects critical heart defects) — done at 24h or before discharge
  • Bilirubin: If jaundice suspected (transcutaneous or serum)

Danger Signs — Report Immediately

  • Poor feeding or refusal to eat
  • Lethargy or difficult to arouse
  • Jaundice in first 24 hours (pathological) or rapid rise
  • Fever ≥100.4°F (38°C) in newborn = always report
  • Cyanosis (especially central — lips, tongue)
  • Green/bilious vomiting (suggests obstruction)
  • Seizure activity

Safe Sleep

  • Back to sleep — always on back, never prone
  • Firm, flat mattress in own crib/bassinet
  • No pillows, stuffed animals, bumper pads, or loose blankets
  • Room-share (not bed-share) for first 6–12 months (AAP recommendation)
📝
NCLEX Key Points

Jaundice in first 24h = pathological (report); Thermoregulation = priority #1 at birth; Meconium delayed >24h = suspect Hirschsprung's/CF; Vitamin K prevents VKDB; Erythromycin = eye prophylaxis for STI; PKU screen after 24h of feeding; APGAR 0–3 = emergency resuscitation. Breastfeed every 2–3 hours (8–12×/day); formula every 3–4 hours.
