VEAL CHOP — FHR Patterns

Maternal Health · Fetal heart rate deceleration types, causes, and nursing interventions

VEAL CHOP Mnemonic

Match each FHR pattern (VEAL) to its cause and nursing action (CHOP). Normal FHR = 110–160 bpm.

VEAL — FHR Pattern
CHOP — Cause + Nursing Action
V = Variable Decelerations
Abrupt V/U/W-shaped dips, variable in timing
C = Cord Compression 🟡
Reposition mom, O₂, amnioinfusion if ordered
E = Early Decelerations
Gradual dip, mirrors contraction exactly
H = Head Compression 🟢
Benign vagal response — no action needed
A = Accelerations
Brief FHR rise above baseline, ≥15 bpm for ≥15 sec
O = OK — Reassuring 🟢
No action needed — healthy oxygenation sign
L = Late Decelerations
Gradual dip that begins AFTER contraction peak
P = Placental Insufficiency 🔴
Left lateral, STOP oxytocin, O₂, IV fluids, notify provider

Late Decels = Emergency!

Late decels = fetus NOT getting adequate O₂ through the placenta. Act immediately: 1) Left lateral position → 2) Stop oxytocin → 3) O₂ 8–10 L/min via face mask → 4) Increase IV fluids → 5) Notify provider → 6) Prepare for delivery.

FHR Deceleration Types — In Detail

Variable Decels
  • Abrupt drop ≥15 bpm
  • Variable timing — not tied to contractions
  • Cause: Cord compression (umbilical)
  • Mild = usually benign; severe/prolonged = urgent
  • Action: Reposition, O₂, amnioinfusion
Early Decels
  • Gradual, uniform drop
  • Mirrors contraction (same shape, same timing)
  • Cause: Head compression against cervix
  • Benign vagal response — normal in active labor
  • Action: None required
Accelerations
  • Rise ≥15 bpm above baseline
  • Lasts ≥15 seconds
  • Same as reactive NST criteria
  • Sign of intact fetal CNS and good oxygenation
  • Action: None — reassuring!
Late Decels ⚠️
  • Gradual drop beginning AFTER contraction peak
  • Nadir (lowest point) after contraction ends
  • Cause: Placental insufficiency / uteroplacental compromise
  • Non-reassuring — requires immediate action
  • Action: Left lateral, stop oxytocin, O₂, IV fluids, notify

Prolonged Deceleration

FHR drop ≥15 bpm lasting 2–10 minutes. Causes include cord prolapse, maternal hypotension, uterine hyperstimulation, or abruptio placenta. Treat the cause urgently.

Amnioinfusion

Infusion of sterile saline into the amniotic cavity via an intrauterine pressure catheter (IUPC).

IndicationGoal
Severe variable decelerations (cord compression)Cushion the cord to relieve compression
Oligohydramnios (low amniotic fluid)Restore fluid volume to protect cord
Thick meconium-stained fluidDilute meconium to reduce aspiration risk

NCLEX Tip

Amnioinfusion is used for variable decelerations (cord compression), NOT late decelerations (placental insufficiency). Late decels require repositioning, stopping oxytocin, O₂, IV fluids, and provider notification.
