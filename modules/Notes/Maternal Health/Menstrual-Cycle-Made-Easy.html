The 28-Day Cycle at a Glance

🩸 Menstrual
Days 1–5
  • Endometrium sheds (stratum functionalis)
  • ↓ Estrogen & Progesterone from previous cycle trigger bleeding
  • FSH begins to rise
🌱 Proliferative (Follicular)
Days 6–13
  • FSH stimulates follicles to mature → one dominant Graafian follicle
  • Follicle produces ↑ Estrogen → rebuilds endometrium
  • Cervical mucus becomes thin, clear, stretchy ("egg white")
  • Estrogen peak → LH surge
💥 Ovulation
Day 14
  • LH surge → egg released
  • Egg viable ~12–24 hours
  • Sperm viable ~3–5 days
  • Fertile window: Days 10–17
🌙 Secretory (Luteal)
Days 15–28
  • Corpus luteum forms → ↑ Progesterone
  • Endometrium thickens/prepares for implantation
  • If no pregnancy: corpus luteum degenerates → ↓ progesterone → menstruation
  • If pregnancy: hCG maintains corpus luteum

The KEY Hormonal Sequence — Know This Cold

GnRH (hypothalamus) → FSH + LH released → Follicle matures → ↑ Estrogen → LH Surge (Day 14) → Ovulation → Corpus Luteum → ↑ Progesterone

Uterine Cycle vs Ovarian Cycle

CyclePhasesWhat Happens
Uterine (Endometrial) Menstrual → Proliferative → Secretory Endometrium sheds → rebuilds under estrogen → thickens/prepares under progesterone
Ovarian Follicular → Ovulation → Luteal Follicle matures under FSH → egg released at LH surge → corpus luteum forms/degenerates

Note: These two cycles happen simultaneously and are driven by the same hormones. Estrogen drives both follicular growth AND endometrial proliferation.

Fertility Signs: Cervical Mucus & Basal Body Temperature

SignNon-FertileFertile / Ovulation
Cervical Mucus Dry, thick, sticky, opaque — blocks sperm Wet, slippery, clear, stretchy — "egg white" consistency (spinnbarkeit) — allows sperm to travel
Basal Body Temp (BBT) Normal baseline (~97.2–97.7°F) Slight dip just before ovulation → then rises 0.4–0.6°F after ovulation (progesterone effect) — stays elevated if pregnant
Cervical Position Low, firm, closed High, soft, open ("SHOW" — Soft, High, Open, Wet)

👶 GTPAL — Pregnancy History

A tool used to assess a woman's pregnancy history — high-yield for NCLEX & maternity nursing.

LetterTermMeaning
GGravidaTotal number of times pregnant (includes current pregnancy, miscarriages, ectopics)
TTerm birthsBirths at ≥37 weeks (twins = 1 birth, not 2)
PPreterm birthsBirths between 20–36 weeks (twins count as 1)
AAbortions / MiscarriagesAll pregnancy losses before 20 weeks (elective or spontaneous)
LLiving childrenCount each living child individually (twins = 2)

NCLEX Example

A 26-year-old is currently 26 weeks pregnant. She had a miscarriage at 10 weeks 5 years ago and has a 3-year-old born at 39 weeks.

LetterAnswerWhy
G3Current pregnancy + miscarriage + term delivery
T1One birth at 39 weeks (≥37 wks)
P0No preterm births
A1One miscarriage at 10 weeks (<20 wks)
L1One living 3-year-old

GTPAL = G3 T1 P0 A1 L1

  • Always include the current pregnancy in Gravida (G)
  • Twins/triplets = 1 birth for T or P — but count each for L
  • Abortion/Miscarriage (A) = any loss before 20 weeks
