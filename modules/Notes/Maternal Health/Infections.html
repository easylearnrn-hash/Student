Vaginal & Maternal Infections

Candidiasis (Yeast Infection)
  • Cause: Candida albicans overgrowth
  • Triggers: Antibiotics, pregnancy, diabetes, HIV, tight clothing, hormonal changes
  • Signs: Thick white cottage-cheese discharge, vulvar itching, burning, redness, swelling
  • Diagnosis: Wet mount (budding yeast + pseudohyphae)
  • Tx: Antifungals (fluconazole, miconazole) — topical or oral
  • Teaching: Cotton underwear, avoid douching, complete full treatment even if symptoms improve
Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)
  • Cause: Overgrowth of anaerobic bacteria (↓ lactobacilli) — NOT an STI
  • Signs: Thin grayish-white discharge, fishy odor (especially after intercourse), minimal/no itching
  • Diagnosis: Clue cells on microscopy; positive whiff test (KOH + discharge = fishy odor)
  • Tx: Metronidazole or clindamycin
  • Avoid alcohol with metronidazole (disulfiram reaction)
  • Nursing: No need to treat partner; refrain from sex until treatment complete
Trichomoniasis
  • Cause: Trichomonas vaginalis (protozoan, STI)
  • Signs: Frothy yellow-green discharge, foul odor, strawberry cervix
  • Diagnosis: Wet mount (motile trichomonads)
  • Tx: Metronidazole — treat BOTH partners
  • No alcohol 24–72 hrs after metronidazole
Chlamydia & Gonorrhea
  • Cause: Bacterial STIs — Chlamydia trachomatis / Neisseria gonorrhoeae
  • Often asymptomatic → screen sexually active under 25
  • Signs (when present): purulent discharge, pelvic pain, dyspareunia, dysuria, cervical friability
  • Diagnosis: NAAT (Nucleic Acid Amplification Test)
  • Chlamydia: Doxycycline (or azithromycin) | Gonorrhea: Ceftriaxone IM + doxycycline (dual therapy)
  • Complications: PID, infertility, ectopic pregnancy, neonatal conjunctivitis/pneumonia
  • Nursing: Screen high-risk individuals; notify and treat partners; educate on condom use
Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)
  • Cause: Often from untreated Chlamydia or Gonorrhea — infection ascends from vagina → uterus → fallopian tubes → ovaries
  • Signs: Fever/chills, lower abdominal/pelvic pain, cervical motion tenderness, purulent discharge
  • Complications: Infertility, chronic pelvic pain, ectopic pregnancy, tubo-ovarian abscess
  • Tx: IV antibiotics (broad-spectrum); hospitalization if severe
  • Nursing: Rest, pain management; monitor for complications; complete antibiotics; abstain from sex
Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)
  • Cause: Toxin-producing S. aureus; linked to high-absorbency tampons (>4–6 hrs), wounds, nasal packing
  • Signs: Sudden high fever, hypotension (shock), sunburn-like rash (can peel), vomiting, diarrhea, multisystem organ failure
  • Action: Remove source (tampon), IV fluids, vasopressors, IV antibiotics
  • Nursing: Monitor VS, urine output, mental status; teach tampon safety (lowest absorbency, change every 4–6 hrs); never use tampons if history of TSS
