Ovarian Cycle Phases

Follicular Phase · Day 1–13
  • FSH stimulates follicles to grow
  • Dominant Graafian follicle matures
  • Rising estrogen → positive feedback → LH surge
  • Granulosa + theca cells produce estrogen
Ovulation · Day 14
  • LH surge → follicle ruptures → egg released
  • Egg swept into fallopian tube by fimbriae
  • Fertilization site: ampulla of fallopian tube
  • Egg lives ~24 hours
Luteal Phase · Day 15–28
  • Ruptured follicle → corpus luteum
  • Corpus luteum secretes progesterone + estrogen
  • If no fertilization → corpus luteum dies → cycle restarts
  • If fertilization → hCG maintains corpus luteum

NCLEX Tips

  • Estrogen = Proliferative phase | Progesterone = Secretory phase
  • LH surge = Ovulation | Corpus luteum = Progesterone factory
  • Fertilization = ampulla of fallopian tube
  • Most fertile window: Day 9–16 | Sperm survives up to 5 days
  • Menstruation = drop in progesterone + estrogen

GTPAL Example

A woman is currently 26 weeks pregnant, had a miscarriage at 10 weeks, and has a 3-year-old born at 39 weeks:

G3 T1 P0 A1 L1

  • G = 3 (current + miscarriage + term birth)
  • T = 1 (baby at 39 weeks ≥ 37 weeks)
  • P = 0 (none born 20–36 weeks)
  • A = 1 (miscarriage at 10 weeks)
  • L = 1 (one living 3-year-old)

Menstrual Disorders

DisorderDefinitionKey CauseTreatment
Dysmenorrhea
Painful periods		 Primary: excess prostaglandins (no disease)
Secondary: pelvic pathology (endo, fibroids, PID)		 Prostaglandins → uterine contractions → ischemia → pain NSAIDs (↓ prostaglandins), heat, OCPs; secondary → laparoscopy
Amenorrhea
No period		 Primary: no period by age 15
Secondary: previously had periods, now gone ≥3 months		 Pregnancy, PCOS, hypothalamic dysfunction, menopause, thyroid Always check pregnancy first! Then treat cause
Menorrhagia
Heavy periods		 Periods >7 days or soaking ≥1 pad/hour Anovulation, fibroids, polyps, coagulopathy, IUD, thyroid NSAIDs, OCPs, Mirena IUD, tranexamic acid; surgery if severe
PCOS
Polycystic Ovary		 Anovulation + hyperandrogenism + polycystic ovaries on US ("string of pearls") ↑ LH → excess androgens; insulin resistance worsens production OCPs (regulate cycle), metformin (insulin resistance), weight loss
🚨 NCLEX Red Flag: Pain that starts later in life, gets worse over time = suspect secondary dysmenorrhea. Amenorrhea: always rule out pregnancy FIRST!
