TORCH Infections, Maternal Infections & Medications

Maternal Health · TORCH, STIs, yeast, PID, key medications in pregnancy

TORCH Infections

TORCH = group of infections that can cross the placenta and cause congenital defects (teratogenic).

T
Toxoplasmosis
  • Cause: Toxoplasma gondii (parasite)
  • Transmission: Undercooked meat, contaminated soil, cat litter box
  • Fetal effects: Hydrocephalus, chorioretinitis, intracranial calcifications
  • Teaching: Avoid litter boxes, raw meat, unwashed produce during pregnancy
O
Other (Syphilis, Varicella, HIV, Hep B)
  • Syphilis: Treponema pallidum → transplacental → stillbirth, congenital syphilis (rash, snuffles, hepatosplenomegaly). Tx: Penicillin
  • Screened with VDRL/RPR in prenatal care
R
Rubella (German Measles)
  • Fetal effects: Congenital Rubella Syndrome = deafness, cataracts, heart defects (PDA), microcephaly
  • Live vaccine CONTRAINDICATED during pregnancy
  • Women should receive MMR vaccine ≥1 month before pregnancy
  • If non-immune → vaccinate postpartum
C
Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  • Transmission: Saliva, urine, sexual contact, blood, transplacental
  • Fetal effects: Microcephaly, hearing loss, seizures, periventricular calcifications
  • No vaccine available
  • Teaching: Avoid contact with young children's saliva/urine (e.g., daycares); hand hygiene
H
Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)
  • Mainly transmitted during vaginal delivery if active lesions present
  • Fetal effects: Neonatal herpes (fatal), seizures, lethargy, vesicular lesions
  • C-section if active lesions at delivery
  • Acyclovir used before labor to reduce outbreak risk

TORCH High-Yield Tips

  • Rubella vaccine CONTRAINDICATED during pregnancy (live virus)
  • Toxoplasmosis = avoid cats & raw meat
  • HSV = C-section if active lesions at delivery
  • CMV = hearing loss, no treatment, handwashing key
  • Syphilis = treat with penicillin (safe in pregnancy)

Maternal Infections

Candidiasis (Yeast Infection)
  • Cause: Candida albicans overgrowth
  • Triggers: Antibiotics, pregnancy, diabetes, HIV
  • Signs: Thick white cottage-cheese discharge, vulvar itching, redness
  • Diagnosis: Wet mount (budding yeast + pseudohyphae)
  • Tx: Antifungals (fluconazole, miconazole)
  • Teaching: Cotton underwear, avoid douching, complete full treatment
Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)
  • Cause: Overgrowth of anaerobes (↓ lactobacilli); NOT an STI
  • Signs: Thin, grayish-white discharge, fishy odor (especially post-intercourse), minimal itching
  • Diagnosis: Clue cells on microscopy; positive whiff test (KOH + fishy smell)
  • Tx: Metronidazole or clindamycin
  • Avoid alcohol with metronidazole (disulfiram reaction)
Trichomoniasis
  • Cause: Trichomonas vaginalis (protozoan, STI)
  • Signs: Frothy yellow-green discharge, foul odor, strawberry cervix
  • Diagnosis: Wet mount (motile trichomonads)
  • Tx: Metronidazole — treat BOTH partners
  • No alcohol 24–72 hrs after metronidazole
Chlamydia & Gonorrhea
  • Often asymptomatic → screen sexually active under 25
  • Signs (when present): purulent discharge, pelvic pain, dyspareunia, dysuria, cervical friability
  • Diagnosis: NAAT (Nucleic Acid Amplification Test)
  • Chlamydia: Doxycycline | Gonorrhea: Ceftriaxone IM + doxycycline
  • Complications: PID, infertility, ectopic pregnancy, neonatal conjunctivitis
Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)
  • Cause: Toxin-producing S. aureus; linked to high-absorbency tampons (>4–6 hrs), wounds, nasal packing
  • Signs: Sudden high fever, hypotension (shock), sunburn-like rash, vomiting, multisystem organ failure
  • Action: Remove source (tampon), IV fluids, vasopressors, IV antibiotics
  • Teaching: Change tampons every 4–6 hours

Key NCLEX Medication Summary

  • MgSO₄ = monitor reflexes + respiratory rate; antidote = calcium gluconate
  • Oxytocin = watch for uterine hyperstimulation
  • RhoGAM = Rh-negative moms (28 weeks + postpartum)
  • Methergine = AVOID in preeclampsia (causes HTN)
  • Terbutaline = hold if HR >120 bpm

Neonatal Priorities

Thermoregulation

Newborns lose heat easily due to large surface area relative to body size.

InterventionPurpose
Skin-to-skin (kangaroo care)Maintains warmth, promotes bonding
Warm blanketsPrevents heat loss immediately after birth
Hat on headHead = major heat loss area
Radiant warmerUsed for at-risk or premature newborns

Cold Stress → Hypoglycemia!

Cold stress forces the newborn to burn glucose for heat → hypoglycemia → can lead to respiratory distress. Prevent cold stress as the priority!
