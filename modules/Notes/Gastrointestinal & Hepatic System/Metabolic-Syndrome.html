What Are Nutritional Disorders?

Occur when the body doesn't get the right balance of nutrients. Caused by:

  • Deficiencies — not enough vitamins, minerals, or calories
  • Excesses — too many unhealthy foods → obesity/metabolic issues
  • Malabsorption — body can't absorb nutrients properly (celiac, Crohn's)

Metabolic Syndrome — Must Have 3 of 5

A cluster of conditions that increase risk of heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. Diagnosis requires ≥3 of the 5 criteria:

1
Abdominal Obesity — Waist >40 in (men) or >35 in (women) → "apple-shaped" body
2
High Blood Pressure — BP >130/85 mmHg → headaches, dizziness, blurry vision
3
High Blood Sugar (Insulin Resistance) — Fasting glucose >100 mg/dL → thirst, frequent urination, fatigue
4
High Triglycerides — >150 mg/dL → usually no symptoms, ↑heart disease risk
5
Low HDL ("Good" Cholesterol) — <40 mg/dL (men) / <50 mg/dL (women) → no symptoms, ↑clogged artery risk
🧠 Quick recall: High Blood Sugar + High Cholesterol + High BP + Obesity = Metabolic Syndrome

Causes of Metabolic Syndrome

  • Unhealthy diet (too much sugar & fat)
  • Obesity (especially belly fat)
  • Lack of exercise
  • Genetics (family history of diabetes or heart disease)

Complications

  • Type 2 diabetes (can't use insulin properly)
  • Heart attack & stroke (blocked arteries)
  • Kidney disease (high BG + BP damages kidneys)

Overnutrition & Obesity

Complications of Obesity (BMI ≥ 30)

  • Heart disease (↑cholesterol & BP)
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Joint pain (extra weight)
  • Sleep apnea
  • Fatty liver disease (NAFLD)

Treatment

  • Healthy diet (low-calorie, nutrient-dense)
  • Exercise ≥150 min/week
  • Medications: Orlistat, Liraglutide (if lifestyle fails)
  • Bariatric surgery (gastric bypass) for severe obesity

⚡ NCLEX Priorities

  • Metabolic syndrome = ≥3 of 5: high BG, high BP, high TG, low HDL, abdominal obesity
  • BMI ≥ 30 = obesity → increased risk for DM2, heart disease
  • Celiac = no gluten (wheat, barley, rye)
  • Lactose intolerance = no dairy (or lactase supplements)
  • Gout = low-purine diet + allopurinol; avoid alcohol & red meat
  • High-fiber diet = best for diabetes, heart health, & weight loss
  • Encourage small, sustainable lifestyle changes over fad diets
