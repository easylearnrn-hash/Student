Digestive System

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · Anatomy & Function

Overview

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients for energy and building blocks, then expels indigestible waste.

Two parts: (1) Alimentary canal (hollow tube mouth → anus) & (2) Accessory organs (liver, pancreas, gallbladder)

Accessory Organs

Liver

  • Produces bile (fat digestion)
  • Processes nutrients & detoxifies substances
  • Hepatic artery, portal vein, bile ducts
  • Liver itself does NOT cause pain; liver area pain = gallbladder

Gallbladder

  • Stores bile from the liver
  • Releases bile → duodenum to digest fats

Pancreas

  • Produces digestive enzymes + bicarbonate
  • Bicarbonate neutralizes stomach acid in duodenum
  • Located behind the stomach

6 Functions of the Digestive System

  1. Ingestion — taking in food
  2. Secretion — releasing enzymes (break down macros) & mucus (protects lining)
  3. Mixing & Propulsion — peristalsis moves food through tract
  4. Digestion — mechanical (chewing) + chemical (enzymes)
  5. Absorption — nutrients into bloodstream; fats → lymphatic system
  6. Excretion — waste expelled via defecation

Detailed Pathway of Digestion

1
Mouth — chewing + salivary amylase begins carbohydrate digestion
2
Esophagus — bolus travels via peristalsis through UES and LES
3
Stomach — HCl + enzymes break down food; absorbs alcohol, some meds, water
4
Duodenum — pancreatic enzymes + bile released; absorbs Fe, Ca, Mg, fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K
5
Jejunum & Ileum — absorbs glucose, amino acids, fatty acids, vitamins, minerals, water
6
Large Intestine — absorbs water, vitamins, electrolytes; forms & stores stool
7
Rectum / Anus — waste expelled via defecation

Stomach Key Facts

Stomach Acid (HCl)

  • pH: 1.5–3.5
  • Breaks down food, kills bacteria, activates enzymes
  • Mucus layer protects stomach lining from acid

Stomach Layers

  • Mucus — protects against acid
  • Submucosal — blood vessels & nerves
  • Muscularis — stomach movement
  • Serosa — outer lining
Metabolic Alkalosis: Can occur if stomach acid is lost through vomiting or NG tube suction (↑ pH, ↓ HCl).

Physical Examination Order

Abdomen exam sequence: Inspection → Auscultation → Percussion → Palpation (Palpation LAST — can cause pain & alter bowel sounds)

Post-Op & Clinical Tips

  • Bowel sounds may be absent for up to 1 day post-surgery — normal
  • Walking (ambulation) helps stimulate intestinal activity
  • Appendix: immune function, helps repopulate gut bacteria after diarrhea
