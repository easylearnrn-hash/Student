Celiac Disease

Gastrointestinal & Hepatic System · note_gi_24

Definition

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disease triggered by gluten (a protein in wheat, barley, rye, and oats).

When a person with celiac disease eats gluten, the immune system attacks the small intestinal villi → villous atrophy → malabsorption of nutrients.

The only treatment is a strict, lifelong gluten-free diet.

Symptoms

GI Symptoms

  • Diarrhea (chronic, often fatty/pale)
  • Bloating & abdominal pain
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Weight loss
  • Steatorrhea (fatty stools)

Systemic Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Iron-deficiency anemia (most common)
  • Dermatitis herpetiformis (itchy blistering rash)
  • Bone/joint pain (Ca malabsorption)
  • Delayed growth in children
  • Infertility

Pathophysiology

  • Gluten intake → immune-mediated damage to intestinal villi
  • Villi flatten (villous atrophy) → decreased absorptive surface area
  • Results in malabsorption of: fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K), iron, calcium, B12, folate
Damaged villi = nutrient malabsorption — explains ALL downstream complications.

Complications (if Untreated)

ComplicationMechanism
Osteoporosis↓calcium & Vitamin D absorption
InfertilityMalnutrition, hormonal disruption
Neurologic issuesPeripheral neuropathy, ataxia (B12/folate deficiency)
Intestinal lymphomaChronic inflammation → T-cell lymphoma risk
AnemiaIron and/or B12/folate malabsorption

Dietary Management

Foods to AVOID (Contains Gluten)

  • Wheat (bread, pasta, flour)
  • Barley
  • Rye
  • Oats — unless certified gluten-free (cross-contamination risk)
  • Beer (barley-based)
  • Soy sauce (usually contains wheat)
Remember: W.B.R.O. — Wheat, Barley, Rye, Oats

Safe Foods (Gluten-Free)

  • Rice
  • Corn / Maize
  • Potatoes
  • Quinoa
  • Vegetables & fruits
  • Plain meat, fish, eggs, dairy
  • Certified gluten-free products

Nursing Teaching

  • Strict lifelong gluten avoidance — even trace amounts can cause damage
  • Read all food labels carefully
  • Dietitian referral for education and meal planning
  • Advise about cross-contamination risks (shared cooking surfaces, utensils)
  • Monitor for nutritional deficiencies: iron, Ca, Vit D, B12, folate
  • Supplement as needed (calcium + Vit D, iron, B vitamins)
  • Symptoms resolve and villi regenerate within months of strict gluten-free diet
Diagnosis confirmed by: serologic testing (anti-TTG IgA, anti-endomysial IgA) + small bowel biopsy (gold standard).
