Lower Gastrointestinal Obstruction

SBO vs LBO quick-reference with postoperative bowel sound guidelines

No Opioids NPO + NG Tube Absent BS >3d = Ileus Avoid Laxatives in LBO

1. SBO vs LBO — Quick Reference

Feature SBO (Paralytic Ileus) LBO (Large Bowel Obstruction)
Cause Paralytic ileus (post-op, stroke) Absolute constipation (tumor, volvulus)
Onset Abrupt Gradual
Symptoms Pain, distention, no flatus, early vomiting (may be bilious) Pain, distention, no flatus, constipation, late vomiting
Bowel Sounds Hypoactive → Absent Hypoactive → Absent
Associated Condition Hypokalemia, metabolic acidosis Hyponatremia, metabolic alkalosis
Treatment NPO, NG tube decompression, IV fluids, avoid opioids Diet mod, stool softeners, enema if needed, avoid opioids

2. Postoperative Bowel Sound Timeline

  • < 2 days post-op: Absent bowel sounds = normal — expected due to anesthesia
  • > 3 days post-op: Absent bowel sounds = suspect paralytic ileus → intervene

3. Treatment Details

SBO / Paralytic Ileus

  • NPO status — nothing by mouth
  • NG tube — manage vomiting and decompress
  • IV fluids — maintain hydration
  • Pain management — avoid opioids (worsen constipation)

LBO

  • Diet modification — NPO
  • Stool softener — ease passage of stool
  • Enema — may be used
  • Pain management — avoid opioids
  • Surgery often required for tumors, volvulus, strictures

4. NCLEX Tips

  • ✅ NG tube for both SBO and LBO → relieves distention and vomiting
  • ✅ Correct electrolytes — hypokalemia in SBO, hyponatremia in LBO
  • ✅ Monitor for signs of perforation: abdominal rigidity, fever, ↑ WBC
🚫 Never give laxatives or stimulate peristalsis in complete obstruction — perforation risk
🚫 Avoid opioid medications — slow gut motility and worsen obstruction
🔑 Key Takeaway: SBO = functional (post-op/stroke), abrupt, hypokalemia + acidosis. LBO = mechanical (tumor/volvulus), gradual, hyponatremia + alkalosis. Both → NPO + NG tube. Absent BS >3 days post-op = paralytic ileus.
