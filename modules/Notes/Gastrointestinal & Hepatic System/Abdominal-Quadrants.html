The Four Quadrants

RUQ — Right Upper Quadrant
  • Liver (right lobe)
  • Gallbladder
  • Duodenum
  • Head of the pancreas
  • Right kidney & adrenal gland
  • Hepatic flexure of the colon
  • Parts of ascending & transverse colon

⚕ Gallstones/cholecystitis · Hepatitis · Pancreatitis · Right kidney stones

LUQ — Left Upper Quadrant
  • Stomach
  • Spleen
  • Left lobe of the liver
  • Body and tail of the pancreas
  • Left kidney & adrenal gland
  • Splenic flexure of the colon
  • Parts of transverse & descending colon

⚕ Gastric ulcers/gastritis · Splenomegaly/rupture · Pancreatitis · Left kidney stones

RLQ — Right Lower Quadrant
  • Cecum
  • Appendix
  • Ascending colon
  • Right ovary & fallopian tube (♀)
  • Right ureter
  • Lower right kidney
  • Part of small intestine

⚕ Appendicitis · Ovarian cysts/torsion (♀) · Kidney/ureteral stones · IBD

LLQ — Left Lower Quadrant
  • Descending colon
  • Sigmoid colon
  • Left ovary & fallopian tube (♀)
  • Left ureter
  • Lower left kidney
  • Part of small intestine

⚕ Diverticulitis · Ovarian issues (♀) · Kidney/ureteral stones · IBD

Physical Exam Order (Abdomen)

I → A → P → P

  • Inspection
  • Auscultation — before tapping/touching to avoid altering bowel sounds
  • Percussion
  • Palpation — done last (can cause pain/tenderness, altering other findings)
⚠️ Unlike other body systems, auscultation comes before percussion and palpation in the abdomen!

⚡ NCLEX Priorities

  • RLQ pain → think appendicitis first (McBurney's point)
  • LLQ pain + elderly + fever → think diverticulitis
  • RUQ pain after fatty meal → cholecystitis (gallbladder)
  • Abdominal exam order = I → A → P → P (unique to abdomen)
  • Kehr's sign (left shoulder pain) after trauma → splenic rupture (referred pain from LUQ blood)
