Mixing of Insulin Guidelines

NR–RN mnemonic (Air-Air-Fluid-Fluid), sliding scale, food tray timing for mixed insulin

Overview
Mixing insulin is done for patient convenience — combining two insulin types in one syringe = one injection instead of two.
General Mixing Rules

CANNOT Mix

  • Long-acting insulin (Glargine/Lantus, Detemir) CANNOT be mixed with any other insulin
  • Mixing changes the pH → alters absorption profile

CAN Mix

  • Regular (R) insulin can be mixed with NPH (N) insulin
  • This is the only safe, commonly tested combination
Sliding Scale Protocol

Step 1: Check Blood Glucose First

  • Check the blood sugar reading using fingerstick glucose meter
  • Based on the reading, determine the dosage per the sliding scale chart
  • Example: BG 150–200 → inject 4 units of Regular (R) + 6 units of NPH (N)
  • Sliding scale ensures dose is proportional to current glucose level
Mixing Sequence — Mnemonic: NR–RN (Air-Air-Fluid-Fluid)
NR–RN = N gets Air first, R gets Air second, then R drawn first, N drawn last.
"Inject air into N, then air into R. Draw R first, then N." Think: Clear before Cloudy.
N Air
Step 1: Draw 6 units of air → inject air into the N (NPH cloudy) vial. Prevents vacuum buildup. Do NOT draw fluid yet.
R Air
Step 2: Draw 4 units of air → inject air into the R (Regular clear) vial without removing the syringe.
R Draw
Step 3: With syringe still in the R vial → aspirate 4 units of R (Regular) insulin into the syringe. (Draw clear first)
N Draw
Step 4: Insert syringe into the N (NPH) vial → aspirate 6 units of N insulin. (Draw cloudy last)

Why This Order Matters

  • Injecting air first prevents vacuum in vials (makes drawing easier, prevents contamination)
  • Drawing Regular (clear) FIRST prevents contaminating the Regular vial with NPH
  • If NPH accidentally mixes into Regular → Regular is contaminated → discard and start over
Visual Step-by-Step
💨
1. Inject air into N (NPH) vial — 6 units of air. Remove syringe without drawing fluid.
💨
2. Inject air into R (Regular) vial — 4 units of air. Keep syringe in vial.
💉
3. Draw R (Regular/Clear) insulin — 4 units. Clear insulin drawn first.
💉
4. Draw N (NPH/Cloudy) insulin — 6 units. Cloudy drawn last. Total in syringe = 10 units.
Memory: "Clear before Cloudy" — Always draw the clear (Regular) insulin before the cloudy (NPH) insulin into the syringe.
Food Tray Timing for Mixed Insulin

First Food Tray

  • 2 hours after injecting the mixed insulin
  • Matches the peak of Regular insulin (short-acting component)

Second Food Tray

  • 4–6 hours after injecting the mixed insulin
  • Aligns with the peak of NPH insulin (intermediate component)
Errors & Troubleshooting

If NPH is Drawn Before Regular (Wrong Order)

  • NPH contaminates the Regular insulin vial → changes its effectiveness
  • Discard the syringe, discard the contaminated Regular vial
  • Start the entire process over with a new syringe and fresh Regular vial
NCLEX Must-Know

Top NCLEX Points

  • Long-acting insulin (Lantus/Glargine) CANNOT be mixed with anything
  • Only R (Regular) + N (NPH) can be mixed
  • Mnemonic: NR–RN = Air into N first, then Air into R; Draw R first, then N
  • "Clear before Cloudy" — always draw Regular before NPH
  • First food tray = 2 hours after mixed injection (Regular peak)
  • Second food tray = 4–6 hours (NPH peak)
  • If syringe contaminated with NPH → discard, start over
  • Total syringe units = sum of both insulin doses
