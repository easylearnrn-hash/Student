Thyroid Gland & Hormones

T3/T4 production, HPT axis, hyperthyroidism vs hypothyroidism, Graves' vs Hashimoto's

Location & Structure

Thyroid Gland Facts

  • Located in the neck, wrapped around the trachea (windpipe)
  • Butterfly shape — two lobes, one on each side of trachea
  • Between the Adam's apple (laryngeal prominence) and suprasternal notch
  • Made of follicles (tiny sacs) lined with follicular cells
  • Follicular cells make thyroglobulin (protein precursor)
  • Inside follicles: colloid — where hormones are made and stored
How Thyroid Hormones Are Made

Requirements: Iodine + Thyroglobulin

  • Iodine sources: salt, eggs, seafood — converted to iodide (I⁻)
  • Iodide enters cells via sodium-iodide symporter → moves into colloid → oxidized with H₂O₂
  • Thyroid peroxidase attaches iodide to thyroglobulin:
  • 1 iodine = MIT (Monoiodotyrosine) → T₃ = MIT + DIT (3 iodine atoms)
  • 2 iodine = DIT (Diiodotyrosine) → T₄ = DIT + DIT (4 iodine atoms)
  • T₄ is inactive; enzyme deiodinase (needs selenium) converts T₄ → T₃
  • Most of T₄ (80%) converts to T₃; T₃ is the active hormone
T₄ inactive → T₃ active. T₃ needs selenium for conversion. Only free T₃ (1%) enters cells and acts on DNA.

T₃ and T₄

Negative Feedback

  • High T₃/T₄ → stops TRH and TSH release → keeps hormone levels balanced
  • Low T₃/T₄ → stimulates TRH → TSH rises → thyroid makes more hormones
  • Hypothyroidism: T₃/T₄ ↓, TSH ↑ (pituitary compensating)
  • Hyperthyroidism: T₃/T₄ ↑, TSH ↓ (feedback shuts it down)

Hyperthyroidism vs Hypothyroidism

Hyperthyroidism (Graves' Disease)

  • Autoimmune — body attacks thyroid → excess hormone
  • Weight loss (hypermetabolism)
  • Heat intolerance, diaphoresis (sweating)
  • Hyperactive, insomnia
  • Diarrhea, hair thinning (hirsutism)
  • ↑ BP, ↑ HR, tachycardia
  • ↑ cardiac output
  • Exophthalmos (bulging eyes)
  • T₃/T₄ ↑, TSH ↓
  • Always HOT (hyperactivity)
  • Goiter (enlarged thyroid)

Hypothyroidism (Hashimoto's)

  • Autoimmune — attacks thyroid → insufficient hormone
  • Weight gain (hypometabolism)
  • Cold intolerance, fatigue
  • Lethargy, sleepy
  • Constipation, hair loss
  • ↓ BP, ↓ HR, bradycardia
  • ↓ cardiac output
  • Periorbital edema, dry skin
  • T₃/T₄ ↓, TSH ↑
  • Always COLD (hypoactivity)
  • Symptoms may mimic depression
Treatment

Hyperthyroidism Treatment

  1. Methimazole — anti-thyroid; NOT for pregnancy or under 18
  2. PTU — safe for pregnancy and under 18
  3. Radioactive Iodine (RAI) — destroys overactive tissue; NOT for pregnancy/under 18
  4. Thyroidectomy — surgery; patient positioned semi-Fowler's after

Hypothyroidism Treatment

  • Levothyroxine (L-Thyroxine) — hormone replacement
  • Take at 5 AM on empty stomach; onset ~6 weeks
  • NEVER stop abruptly
  • Signs of overdose = tachycardia, insomnia — notify MD
Complications

Thyroid Storm (Hyperthyroid Crisis)

  • Life-threatening; requires urgent hospital care
  • Tx: anti-thyroids + beta-blockers + IV fluids + cooling
  • Dexamethasone may be given
  • Untreated → deadly

Myxedema Coma (Hypothyroid Crisis)

  • Critical; triggered by infection, cold, trauma, missed meds
  • Marked decrease in metabolic rate
  • Treatment: IV Levothyroxine, warming, supportive care
Post-Thyroidectomy Monitoring

Key Complications to Watch After Thyroid Surgery

  • Hypocalcemia — parathyroid glands may be accidentally removed; look for Trousseau's and Chvostek's signs
  • Airway obstruction — swollen neck; watch for wheezing → prepare for intubation, notify MD
  • Laryngeal damage — hoarseness or voice loss; assess vocal cord function
  • Position: semi-Fowler's post-op
NCLEX Must-Know

Top NCLEX Points

  • T₄ = inactive; T₃ = active (selenium needed for conversion)
  • Graves = hyper; Hashimoto's = hypo
  • Hyper: T₃/T₄ ↑, TSH ↓ | Hypo: T₃/T₄ ↓, TSH ↑
  • PTU safe in pregnancy; Methimazole is NOT
  • RAI: flush toilet twice, limit visitors, no pregnancy contact
  • Post-thyroidectomy: watch for hypocalcemia, airway, voice changes
  • Levothyroxine overdose = tachycardia + insomnia → lower dose
  • Thyroid storm = emergency → anti-thyroids + beta-blockers
  • Myxedema coma = emergency → IV Levothyroxine
