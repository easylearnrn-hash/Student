Urinary Catheters & CBI

Types, CAUTI prevention, continuous bladder irrigation (CBI) management, and post-TURP care

CAUTI Prevention — Remove ASAP CBI: True UO = Total Out − Irrigation In Bag Always Below Bladder Post-TURP = Light Pink Urine

1. Types of Urinary Catheters

Foley (Indwelling)

Remains in bladder; balloon inflated to hold in place; closed drainage system; highest CAUTI risk

Intermittent (Straight)

Inserted → drains → removed; used for retention, neurogenic bladder; lower CAUTI risk

Suprapubic

Surgically inserted through abdominal wall into bladder; used when urethral access not possible

Condom (Texas)

External catheter for incontinent men; no insertion into urethra; lowest risk; check skin daily

3-Way (CBI)

Three lumens: irrigation in, urine out, balloon port; used for continuous bladder irrigation after prostate surgery

Coudé Catheter

Curved tip; used for BPH or urethral stricture where straight catheter cannot pass

2. CAUTI Prevention

Priority #1: Remove the catheter as soon as possible — every day with a catheter = increased infection risk
  • ✅ Maintain a closed drainage system — do NOT disconnect
  • ✅ Keep drainage bag BELOW bladder level at all times (prevents backflow)
  • ✅ Keep drainage tubing free of kinks and loops
  • ✅ Perform pericare daily and after bowel movements — clean catheter from meatus outward
  • ✅ Use aseptic technique during insertion
  • ✅ Do NOT irrigate unless specifically ordered
  • ✅ Empty drainage bag when ½–¾ full; use separate container per patient
  • ✅ Reassess catheter necessity daily — remove ASAP
Bag Position: Never raise the bag above bladder level — gravity flows backward into bladder = contamination and infection

3. Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI)

Used after TURP (transurethral resection of the prostate) to prevent blood clots from obstructing the catheter lumen.

  • Uses a 3-way Foley catheter: irrigation in, urine + irrigation out, balloon inflate
  • Irrigant: Normal Saline (NS) — non-hypotonic, doesn't cause electrolyte absorption
  • Bags are hung on IV pole; continuous flow in and out

True Urine Output = Total Drainage − Total Irrigation Instilled

CBI Troubleshooting

FindingAction
Bright red urine / large clotsIncrease CBI rate — flush out clots; notify provider if no improvement
Light pink urineNormal post-TURP — can decrease CBI rate
No output / possible obstructionCheck tubing for kinks → manually irrigate with syringe per order → notify provider if unresolved
Bladder spasmsNormal; may order antispasmodics (oxybutynin, belladonna & opium suppositories)
Post-TURP Expected: Light pink urine is NORMAL. Bright red urine is NOT normal — indicates active bleeding → increase CBI rate + notify provider

4. Catheter Insertion — Key Steps

  1. Gather supplies; perform hand hygiene; explain procedure
  2. Position patient: women = dorsal recumbent; men = supine
  3. Open sterile kit using sterile technique; set up sterile field
  4. Clean urethral meatus: women = labial separation, clean front to back; men = circular motion (retract foreskin)
  5. Lubricate catheter tip; insert gently until urine flows
  6. Inflate balloon with sterile water (per label size, e.g., 10 mL) — ONLY after urine returns
  7. Connect to closed drainage bag; secure tubing to inner thigh
  8. Position bag below bladder level
NEVER inflate balloon before urine returns — may be in urethra, not bladder → causes urethral trauma

5. NCLEX Nursing Priorities

  • ✅ Drainage bag always BELOW bladder
  • ✅ Closed system — do not disconnect for specimen; use sampling port
  • ✅ Remove catheter ASAP — reassess daily
  • ✅ CBI formula: True UO = total drainage − total irrigation
  • ✅ Bright red CBI output → increase irrigation rate
  • ✅ Post-TURP: light pink = normal; bright red = abnormal
  • ✅ Balloon inflated only AFTER urine return confirmed
  • ✅ CAUTI prevention = standard precautions + aseptic insertion + remove ASAP
🔑 Key Takeaway: 6 catheter types — 3-way = CBI. CAUTI prevention = remove ASAP + closed system + bag below bladder. CBI: True UO = total drainage − irrigation input. Bright red clots → increase CBI rate. Post-TURP: light pink is okay, bright red is not.
