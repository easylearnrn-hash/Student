Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Progressive, irreversible loss of kidney function lasting ≥ 3 months — ultimately leading to ESRD

IRREVERSIBLE Stage 5 = ESRD → Dialysis Watch K+ → Cardiac EPO → RBC Production

1. GFR Stages of CKD

Stage 1
≥ 90
Normal GFR with kidney damage
Kidney damage markers present (proteinuria, hematuria) but GFR normal. Often asymptomatic. Control underlying disease (DM, HTN).
Stage 2
60–89
Mildly Decreased
Slight GFR reduction with damage markers. Usually asymptomatic. Lifestyle modifications, BP/blood sugar control.
Stage 3
30–59
Moderately Decreased
Anemia and bone disease may begin. Start EPO, Vit D. Avoid nephrotoxins. Monitor K+ and phosphorus.
Stage 4
15–29
Severely Decreased
Uremia symptoms appear. Prepare for dialysis/transplant education. Phosphate binders. Fluid restriction may begin.
Stage 5
< 15
Kidney Failure (ESRD)
Dialysis or kidney transplant required. All late-stage symptoms present.

2. Lab Changes in CKD

LabDirectionReason / Consequence
BUN / Creatinine↑ ↑Kidneys can't excrete waste → azotemia → uremia (confusion, metallic taste)
Potassium (K+)↑Kidneys can't excrete K+ → risk of fatal cardiac arrhythmias
Phosphorus↑Kidneys can't excrete → bone weakness (renal osteodystrophy)
Calcium↓Phosphorus binds calcium; less Vit D activation → bone disease + fractures
Hemoglobin (Hgb)↓ (Anemia)↓ EPO production → ↓ RBC production → fatigue, pallor
GFR↓ (Progressive)Core marker of CKD severity — used for staging
Classic Late CKD triad: Anemia (↓ Hgb) + ↓ Calcium + ↑ Phosphorus = renal disease with bone involvement

3. Signs & Symptoms (Stage 4–5)

  • Fatigue and weakness (anemia)
  • Edema — legs, ankles, face (fluid retention)
  • Hypertension (↑ fluid volume + renin)
  • Itchy / dry skin (uremia deposits on skin — uremic frost in ESRD)
  • Muscle cramps (electrolyte imbalance)
  • Metallic taste, anorexia, N/V (uremia)
  • Confusion / drowsiness (uremic encephalopathy)
  • Pallor (anemia from ↓ EPO)

4. Treatment

Medications

DrugPurpose
ACE inhibitors / ARBsBP control; reduce proteinuria; slow CKD progression (especially in DM and HTN)
DiureticsReduce edema in early CKD (loop diuretics like furosemide)
Erythropoietin (EPO / epoetin alfa)Stimulates RBC production — treat anemia of CKD; target Hgb 10–12 g/dL
Phosphate BindersTaken WITH MEALS — bind dietary phosphate in GI tract before absorption
Active Vitamin D (calcitriol)Kidney can't activate Vit D → supplement to prevent bone disease + low calcium
⚠️ Avoid: NSAIDs (reduce renal perfusion), IV contrast (nephrotoxic), aminoglycosides (nephrotoxic). These accelerate CKD progression.

Diet for CKD

  • Low protein — reduces BUN/waste buildup
  • Low potassium — prevent cardiac arrhythmias
  • Low phosphorus — prevent bone disease
  • Low sodium — reduce fluid retention and BP
  • Fluid restriction (if on dialysis or severe overload)
  • High calorie — prevent protein catabolism (muscle wasting)

5. Patient Education

  • Monitor daily weight and blood pressure at home
  • Report sudden weight gain (>2 lbs/day), swelling, or shortness of breath
  • Adhere strictly to low K+/phosphorus/sodium diet
  • Take phosphate binders with meals — not at bedtime
  • Keep all follow-up lab appointments (BMP, CBC, GFR monitoring)
  • Signs of uremia to report: confusion, bad breath, nausea, fatigue worsening
  • Begin planning for dialysis or transplant well before Stage 5

6. NCLEX Must-Knows

  • ✅ CKD is irreversible — goal is to slow progression
  • ✅ Stage 5 (GFR <15) = ESRD → dialysis or transplant
  • ✅ Watch K+ → cardiac monitoring; hyperkalemia = arrhythmias
  • ✅ Anemia + ↓ Ca + ↑ Phosphorus = classic late CKD
  • ✅ EPO treats anemia (not iron unless iron deficient)
  • ✅ Phosphate binders WITH meals
  • ✅ NO NSAIDs / contrast dye in CKD
  • ✅ ACE inhibitors / ARBs = first-line BP + kidney-protective
🔑 Key Takeaway: CKD = irreversible kidney failure over ≥3 months. Staged by GFR (Stage 1–5). Classic late findings: anemia + ↓Ca + ↑Phosphorus + ↑BUN/Cr + hyperkalemia. Treat with EPO, phosphate binders (with meals), Vit D, ACE/ARBs. Low protein/K/phosphorus/Na diet. Stage 5 GFR <15 = ESRD → dialysis.
