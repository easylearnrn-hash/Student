Rubeola (Measles)

Rubeola (Measles)

Highly contagious airborne paramyxovirus — Koplik spots are pathognomonic

Key Facts

  • Caused by Morbillivirus (Paramyxovirus family)
  • Extremely contagious — one of the most transmissible viruses known
  • Transmission: Airborne (droplet nuclei <5 µm; can linger in air for 2 hours)
  • Incubation: 7–14 days
  • Contagious: 4 days before → 4 days after rash onset

Prodrome (3 Cs)

  • Cough
  • Coryza (runny nose)
  • Conjunctivitis (red, watery eyes)
  • High fever (>104°F / 40°C)
⭐ Koplik Spots (PATHOGNOMONIC)

Small white/bluish-white spots on buccal mucosa (inside cheeks), appear 2 days before rash. Only seen in measles — tell the NCLEX examiner you know this!

🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Koplik spots = measles. If you see "white spots on buccal mucosa" → select Rubeola. No other disease causes them.

Rash Pattern

  • Erythematous maculopapular rash — confluent, brick-red
  • Starts at hairline/face → spreads downward → trunk → extremities
  • Lasts 5–7 days; fades in same pattern (head to toe)

Complications

  • Otitis media (most common complication)
  • Pneumonia — #1 cause of measles death (especially in malnourished children)
  • Encephalitis (post-infectious)
  • Subacute sclerosing panencephalitis (SSPE) — fatal, years later
  • Blindness (Vitamin A deficiency → corneal ulcers)
  • Croup (laryngotracheobronchitis)

Treatment

  • Supportive care (antipyretics, fluids, rest)
  • Vitamin A supplementation — reduces severity and complications, especially in malnourished/developing countries (WHO recommends for ALL children with measles)
  • No specific antiviral
  • Antibiotics only if secondary bacterial infection
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Vitamin A for measles — especially children in developing countries. Reduces mortality significantly.

Isolation

  • Airborne precautions
  • Negative-pressure room, door CLOSED
  • N95 respirator for all healthcare workers entering room
  • Only immune HCW should care for patient
  • Isolate for 4 days after rash onset (immunocompromised = entire illness duration)
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

Measles = N95 (NOT surgical mask). Room must be negative-pressure. Surgical mask is NOT adequate.

Prevention

  • MMR vaccine — live attenuated; 95%+ effective after 2 doses
  • Dose 1: 12–15 months; Dose 2: 4–6 years
  • Post-exposure prophylaxis: MMR within 72 hours OR IGIV within 6 days
  • Contraindicated: pregnancy, severely immunocompromised

NCLEX Must-Know

  • 3 Cs prodrome: Cough + Coryza + Conjunctivitis
  • Koplik spots on buccal mucosa = pathognomonic for measles
  • Rash spreads head → toe (maculopapular, confluent)
  • Airborne precautions, N95, negative-pressure room
  • Vitamin A supplementation for treatment
  • Pneumonia = most common cause of death from measles
  • Contagious 4 days before and 4 days after rash
