Rubella (3-Day Measles)

German measles — mild viral illness with devastating teratogenic effects in pregnancy

Key Facts

  • Caused by Rubella virus (Togavirus family)
  • Transmission: droplet (respiratory secretions)
  • Incubation: 14–21 days
  • Contagious: 7 days before → 7 days after rash
  • Called "3-day measles" — rash lasts ~3 days

Signs & Symptoms

Classic Triad:

  • Low-grade fever
  • Maculopapular rash — begins on face, spreads downward, lasts 3 days
  • Postauricular & suboccipital lymphadenopathy (lymph nodes behind ears/neck)

Also: arthralgia (especially in adult women), conjunctivitis, mild URI symptoms

Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS)

If rubella is contracted in the 1st trimester, fetal damage is most severe:

  • Cataracts (classic eye finding)
  • Sensorineural deafness (most common CRS manifestation)
  • Congenital heart defects (patent ductus arteriosus, pulmonary artery stenosis)
  • Microcephaly, intellectual disability
  • Glaucoma, retinopathy, "blueberry muffin" rash (petechiae)
  • Risk highest before 12 weeks gestation
🧠
NCLEX KEY:

CRS triad = cataracts + deafness + congenital heart defects. Any pregnant woman suspected of rubella → report immediately.

Diagnosis

  • Rubella IgM antibody → recent/current infection
  • Rubella IgG titer → immunity (vaccine or past infection)
  • Prenatal screening: check rubella titer at first prenatal visit

Treatment & Isolation

Treatment:

  • Supportive only (no antivirals)
  • Acetaminophen for fever/pain
  • Rest, fluids

Isolation:

  • Droplet precautions
  • Isolate for 7 days after rash onset
  • Keep away from pregnant women

Prevention

  • MMR vaccine (Measles-Mumps-Rubella) — live attenuated
  • Dose 1: 12–15 months; Dose 2: 4–6 years
  • Contraindicated in pregnancy — avoid pregnancy for 28 days post-vaccination
  • Screen rubella titer at first prenatal visit → vaccinate postpartum if non-immune
  • Contraindicated: immunocompromised

NCLEX Must-Know

  • 3-day rash + postauricular lymph nodes + low fever = rubella
  • CRS: cataracts, deafness, heart defects (1st trimester exposure)
  • Check rubella titer at prenatal visit; vaccinate postpartum if negative
  • DO NOT give MMR during pregnancy
  • Droplet precautions for 7 days after rash onset
  • Keep rubella-infected persons away from pregnant patients
