Orthopedic Post-Op Care

THR, TKR, DVT prophylaxis, neurovascular checks, and assistive device safety

1. General Postoperative Orthopedic Priorities

  • Neurovascular checks: circulation, movement, sensation, capillary refill, color, temperature, pulses
  • Pain management: analgesics (opioids or NSAIDs)
  • Preventing complications: DVT, atelectasis, infection
  • Patient positioning: prevent joint dislocation or pressure injuries
  • Early ambulation: walk ASAP to prevent clots and restore function

2. Total Hip Replacement (THR) — Post-Op Care

Goal: Prevent hip dislocation and promote safe healing.

Key Precautions (THE 3 NO's)

  • No adduction — don't let the leg cross the midline
  • No internal rotation — don't twist the leg inward
  • No hip flexion > 90° — don't bend the hip past a right angle
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Use an abduction pillow to maintain correct leg alignment.

Positioning

  • Keep leg abducted (moved away from midline)
  • Do NOT elevate HOB > 45°
  • Avoid low chairs or toilets
  • Turn toward unaffected side (or logroll if needed)

3. Total Knee Replacement (TKR) — Post-Op Care

Goal: Regain mobility and prevent contractures.

  • CPM machine (Continuous Passive Motion) — keeps knee moving to prevent stiffness
  • Weight bearing: may begin same day or next day with assistive devices
  • Follow provider orders for progression
🧠 NCLEX Tip: Early movement is encouraged — delays increase risk of DVT and joint stiffness.

4. DVT Prophylaxis

Orthopedic surgery patients are at high risk for VTE due to immobility and vessel injury.

  • Sequential compression devices (SCDs) or compression stockings
  • Low molecular weight heparin (enoxaparin)
  • Early ambulation
  • Leg exercises: ankle pumps, foot circles
🚨 NCLEX Priority: Sudden chest pain + shortness of breath = Pulmonary Embolism → Call provider immediately.

5. Pulmonary Hygiene

  • Use incentive spirometer (encourages deep breaths)
  • Encourage coughing & deep breathing exercises
  • Monitor for: ↓ O₂ saturation, fever, crackles

6. Neurovascular Checks

Assess distal to the surgical site (the "5 P's + Color + Temperature"):

AssessmentNormal / Red Flag
ColorPink = normal | Pale = poor perfusion ⚠️
WarmthWarm = normal | Cool = poor circulation ⚠️
Capillary refill<2 seconds = normal
PulsesPresent & strong = normal | Absent/weak = 🚨
Movement & SensationIntact = normal | Loss = nerve damage or compartment syndrome 🚨
🚨 NCLEX Priority: Severe pain unrelieved by meds + tight cast/dressing + loss of pulse = Compartment Syndrome → Emergency!

7. Assistive Devices & Safety Teaching

  • Teach how to use walker or crutches safely (3-point or 4-point gait)
  • Fall prevention: clear pathways, install grab bars, avoid rugs
  • Do NOT bend forward, cross legs, or twist torso (especially after THR)
