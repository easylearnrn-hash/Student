Ocular Pharmacology

Complete guide to ophthalmic drugs — mechanisms, indications, and nursing considerations

Glaucoma Medications
Timolol (Timoptic) — Beta-Blocker

Mechanism: ↓ aqueous humor production by ciliary epithelium. Available 0.25% and 0.5% solution; gel (once daily). First-line for open-angle glaucoma.

⚠️ Absorbed systemically via nasolacrimal duct → can cause bradycardia, bronchospasm. CI: asthma, COPD, heart block, bradycardia, heart failure. Teach nasolacrimal occlusion to minimize systemic absorption.
Latanoprost (Xalatan) — Prostaglandin Analog

Mechanism: ↑ uveoscleral outflow. Gold-standard once-daily dosing (PM). Reduces IOP by 25–35%.

⚠️ Iris color change (heterochromia) — irreversible. Eyelash growth (hypertrichosis). Mild conjunctival hyperemia. Store in refrigerator (before opening).
Brimonidine (Alphagan P) — Alpha-2 Agonist

Mechanism: ↓ aqueous production AND ↑ uveoscleral outflow. Twice-daily dosing.

⚠️ Avoid in children (CNS depression, apnea). Contraindicated with MAOIs. Allergy common (follicular conjunctivitis).
Acetazolamide (Diamox) — Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitor (Systemic)

Mechanism: Inhibits CA II/IV in ciliary body → ↓ aqueous production. Used systemically for acute angle-closure crisis. Dorzolamide/Brinzolamide = topical CAIs for chronic use.

⚠️ Sulfonamide allergy CI. Metabolic acidosis, hypokalemia, paresthesias, GI upset, nephrolithiasis. Monitor electrolytes.
Pilocarpine — Miotic (Cholinergic)

Mechanism: Muscarinic agonist → miosis → opens trabecular meshwork angle. Used in acute angle-closure emergencies.

⚠️ Brow ache (ciliary spasm), myopia, blurred vision. Not used in open-angle (angle already open). Avoid in retinal detachment risk.
Anti-VEGF Agents (Intravitreal)
Ranibizumab (Lucentis) / Aflibercept (Eylea) / Bevacizumab (Avastin)

Mechanism: VEGF inhibition → ↓ neovascularization and vascular permeability. Injected directly into vitreous. Indications: wet AMD, DME, CRVO, PDR.

⚠️ Endophthalmitis (most serious — pain + vision loss post-injection). Subconjunctival hemorrhage, transient IOP spike. Systemic: rare thromboembolic events. Monitor vision and pain post-injection.
Mydriatics / Cycloplegics
Tropicamide / Phenylephrine — Mydriatics

Mechanism: Tropicamide (antimuscarinic) → mydriasis + cycloplegia. Phenylephrine (alpha-1 agonist) → mydriasis only. Used for dilated fundus exams and pre-operative dilation.

⚠️ CONTRAINDICATED in angle-closure glaucoma — dilation blocks aqueous drainage, triggers acute attack. Warn patient: blurred near vision and light sensitivity for 4–6 hours. Do not drive.
Atropine / Cyclopentolate — Long-Acting Cycloplegics

Atropine: longest duration (days). Cyclopentolate: shorter (24h). Used in children for refraction, amblyopia treatment, and uveitis (prevents posterior synechiae).

⚠️ Systemic anticholinergic toxicity (children most at risk): fever, tachycardia, flushing, dry mouth, urinary retention. CI: angle-closure glaucoma.

Topical Antivirals
Trifluridine (Viroptic) / Ganciclovir gel

Indication: HSV keratitis. Trifluridine: 9× daily initially. Ganciclovir gel: 5× daily. Systemic acyclovir/valacyclovir for severe HSV or prophylaxis.

⚠️ Do NOT use steroids with HSV keratitis — corneal perforation risk. Instruct patient: do not wear contacts during treatment.
Lubricants / Artificial Tears

Dry Eye Products

  • Artificial tears (CMC, hyaluronate, PVA) — symptom relief; use PRN to q1h
  • Preservative-free preferred for frequent dosing (>4× daily)
  • Cyclosporine 0.05% (Restasis) / Lifitegrast (Xiidra) — for chronic dry eye (inflammation-mediated)
  • Punctal plugs — conserve natural tears; non-pharmacologic
Nursing: Eye Drop Administration
1
Wash hands thoroughly before and after
2
Tilt head back; pull lower lid down to form pocket (conjunctival sac)
3
Instill 1 drop into conjunctival sac — NOT directly on cornea
4
Apply nasolacrimal occlusion (press inner canthus) × 1–2 min — reduces systemic absorption
5
If multiple drops: wait 5 min between different medications
6
Do not touch dropper tip to eye, lids, or any surface
⚠️ Multiple Medications: If patient uses both eye drops and ointment — drops FIRST, ointment LAST. Always wait 5 minutes between different drop medications.
👁 Contact Lens Rule: Remove contact lenses BEFORE instilling any eye drops. Wait at least 15 minutes before reinserting — preservatives in drops can damage lens material and cause corneal irritation.
