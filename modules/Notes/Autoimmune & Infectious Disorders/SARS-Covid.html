SARS & COVID-19

Coronavirus · airborne + contact · PCR diagnosis · supportive care · N95 + AIIR · SARS vs COVID-19 comparison

What Is SARS?

SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) is caused by SARS-CoV, a coronavirus first identified in 2003 in Southeast Asia. It causes severe pneumonia with potential progression to ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome). COVID-19 is caused by SARS-CoV-2, a closely related but distinct coronavirus that emerged in 2019 and caused a global pandemic.

Transmission

How It Spreads
  • Airborne transmission — infectious particles can travel >3 feet and linger in air; SARS-CoV-2 highly efficient airborne spread
  • Droplet transmission — respiratory droplets from coughing, sneezing, talking
  • Contact transmission — touching contaminated surfaces (fomites) then touching face; virus survives on surfaces hours to days
  • Incubation: SARS = 2–10 days; COVID-19 = 2–14 days (average 5 days)
  • Contagious during incubation period (pre-symptomatic spread) — key feature of SARS-CoV-2

Signs & Symptoms — Disease Progression

Clinical Course
PhaseSymptoms
Early (Days 1–5) Fever (hallmark — often >38°C / 100.4°F), dry cough, myalgia, fatigue, headache; may have chills, sore throat, loss of taste/smell (COVID-19)
Worsening Respiratory (Days 5–10) Progressive dyspnea, hypoxia (SpO2 <94%), worsening pneumonia (bilateral infiltrates on CXR/CT); tachypnea
Severe / Critical ARDS (acute respiratory distress syndrome), respiratory failure, mechanical ventilation; multi-organ dysfunction; septic shock; cytokine storm (COVID-19)

Diagnosis

Diagnostic Tests
  • PCR (RT-PCR) of nasopharyngeal swab — gold standard and best test for early diagnosis; detects viral RNA
  • Rapid antigen test — less sensitive than PCR; useful for point-of-care screening
  • Chest X-ray: bilateral patchy infiltrates; ground-glass opacities
  • CT chest: bilateral ground-glass opacities (more sensitive than CXR)
  • Labs: leukopenia (low WBC), lymphopenia; elevated CRP, ferritin, D-dimer, LDH (markers of inflammation/severity)
  • Pulse oximetry: SpO2 — monitor closely; <94% = significant hypoxia
NCLEX Tip: PCR (nasopharyngeal swab) is the best and most accurate test for early SARS/COVID-19 diagnosis.

Treatment

Management — Primarily Supportive
  • Oxygen therapy — O2 by nasal cannula, non-rebreather mask, high-flow nasal cannula, or mechanical ventilation for ARDS
  • IV fluids — careful fluid management; avoid fluid overload in ARDS
  • Antipyretics — acetaminophen for fever (avoid NSAIDs in some guidelines)
  • Prone positioning — improves oxygenation in ARDS (prone for 16+ hours/day)
  • Corticosteroids (dexamethasone) — for severe COVID-19 requiring O2; reduces mortality
  • COVID-19 antivirals: Remdesivir (hospitalized), Paxlovid/nirmatrelvir-ritonavir (outpatient, high-risk)
  • SARS: no proven specific antiviral (ribavirin experimental; not established)
  • Thromboprophylaxis — VTE prevention (COVID-19 increases clotting risk)

Infection Control

😷
Airborne + Contact Precautions
  • Airborne precautions — N95 respirator (fit-tested) for ALL healthcare workers entering room
  • Contact precautions — gown + gloves
  • Eye protection — goggles or face shield (aerosol-generating procedures)
  • AIIR (negative pressure room) — especially for aerosol-generating procedures (intubation, bronchoscopy, suctioning)
  • Surgical mask on patient during transport outside room
  • Limit visitors and personnel entering room
  • Hand hygiene — soap + water or alcohol-based sanitizer
NCLEX Tip: SARS and COVID-19 require Airborne + Contact precautions. Use N95 (not just surgical mask) + face shield + gown + gloves.

NCLEX Must-Know Summary

⭐
Key Takeaways
  • SARS = SARS-CoV; COVID-19 = SARS-CoV-2 (both coronavirus, both respiratory)
  • Transmission: airborne + contact; survives on surfaces hours–days
  • Hallmark early symptom: fever + dry cough; COVID may have anosmia (loss of smell)
  • Best early diagnostic test: PCR (nasopharyngeal swab)
  • Severe disease: ARDS; bilateral ground-glass opacities on CT/CXR
  • Treatment: supportive (O2, prone positioning, dexamethasone for severe COVID-19)
  • Precautions: Airborne + Contact; N95 + gown + gloves + eye protection; AIIR room
  • SARS: ~10% mortality, lower spread, no vaccine; COVID-19: ~1–2% mortality, higher spread, vaccine available
