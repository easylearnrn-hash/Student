NCLEX-focused — post-op atelectasis prevention · deep breathing · airway clearance techniques

What Is Incentive Spirometry (IS)?

An incentive spirometer is a handheld device that guides patients to take slow, deep sustained breaths to:

Primary Goal
Prevent atelectasis (collapse of alveoli) — common post-surgery or during prolonged immobility
Secondary Goals
Improve lung expansion · promote oxygenation · prevent post-op pneumonia
🧠
NCLEX #1 Fact: Incentive = INHALE

The patient inhales through the device — not exhales. If a patient exhales into it, correct the technique immediately.

Benefits of Incentive Spirometry

  • ✅ Prevents post-operative pneumonia
  • ✅ Reduces risk of atelectasis (alveolar collapse)
  • ✅ Promotes lung re-expansion
  • ✅ Improves oxygenation and SpO₂
  • ✅ Helps mobilize secretions when followed by coughing

Correct IS Technique — Step by Step

1
Position: Sit upright — High Fowler's or sitting on edge of bed; best position for maximum lung expansion
2
Exhale normally first — empty lungs before placing mouthpiece
3
Seal lips tightly around mouthpiece
4
Inhale slowly and deeply — raise the piston/ball to the goal marker
5
Hold breath 3–5 seconds at the top — allows alveoli to fully expand
6
Exhale slowly through mouth
7
Cough afterward — mobilizes secretions loosened during deep inhalation
8
Repeat 10 times per hour while awake
💡
Pain Management Before IS

Post-surgical patients should splint the incision with a pillow before deep breathing/coughing — reduces pain and encourages compliance.

Nursing Priorities for IS

  • 📋 Teach technique before surgery — easier to learn when not in pain
  • 🕐 Use every hour while awake — 10 breaths/hour
  • 🪑 Position upright (High Fowler's or sitting)
  • 🤧 Encourage coughing after each session
  • 📝 Document effort, volume achieved, and patient response
  • 🩺 Auscultate lung sounds before and after — assess improvement
  • ⚠️ Contraindications: Active hemoptysis; untreated pneumothorax

Pulmonary Hygiene (Pulmonary Toileting)

Pulmonary hygiene is a broad set of techniques used to clear airway secretions and optimize lung function — especially in patients who cannot clear secretions on their own.

When Is It Essential?

Post-Surgical Patients
Especially abdominal or thoracic surgery — pain inhibits deep breathing
Neurologically Impaired or Intubated
Cannot initiate or complete effective cough
COPD, CF, Pneumonia
Excess secretion production + impaired clearance
Prolonged Immobility or Sedation
Secretions pool in dependent lung zones

Pulmonary Hygiene Techniques

TechniqueDescriptionKey NCLEX Points
Incentive Spirometry Device-guided deep inhalation; 10x/hour while awake INHALE — not exhale; hold 3–5 sec
Coughing & Deep Breathing (C&DB) Take 2–3 deep breaths, then forceful cough to mobilize mucus Splint incision with pillow for post-op patients
Chest Physiotherapy (CPT) Rhythmic percussion (clapping) and vibration over chest wall to loosen secretions Perform before meals; avoid over bony prominences, spine, sternum, or recent surgical wounds
Postural Drainage Positioning patient so gravity drains specific lung segments Position depends on affected segment; Trendelenburg for lower lobes
Suctioning Mechanical removal of secretions via catheter (oral, nasotracheal, or trach) Sterile for trach/ET; clean for oral; limit <15 sec; preoxygenate first
Nebulizer Therapy Aerosolized medication or saline to loosen/thin secretions Used before CPT/postural drainage for max effect
Humidified Air/O₂ Keeps airways moist to prevent secretions from thickening Important with supplemental O₂ >4 L/min (add humidifier)

NCLEX High-Yield Tips

  • ✅ Post-op decreased breath sounds? → First action: incentive spirometry
  • ✅ SOB + secretions? → Encourage deep breathing and coughing
  • ✅ Aspiration risk? → Elevate HOB and perform oral suctioning
  • ✅ Avoid CPT over: fractured ribs, surgical wounds, osteoporosis, bleeding disorders
  • ✅ Suctioning: preoxygenate → suction → oxygenate again (limit each pass to <15 sec)
  • ✅ IS is INHALE — most common NCLEX distractor is saying patient should exhale into it
  • ✅ Teach IS preoperatively — patient compliance is better when pain-free
